This metric quantifies how effectively a platform, website, or campaign persuades its audience to complete a specific goal. The "desired action" can vary widely depending on the objective.

For instance, if a website aims to generate leads, a conversion might be a user filling out a contact form. If an e-commerce store's goal is sales, a conversion is a successful purchase. The calculation is straightforward:
$$ \text{Conversion Rate} = \left( \frac{\text{Number of Conversions}}{\text{Total Visitors or Interactions}} \right) \times 100\% $$

An example usage: "The new landing page design resulted in a 15% increase in the conversion rate for newsletter sign-ups."

This term is predominantly employed in digital marketing, e-commerce, web analytics, and performance analysis, providing insight into the effectiveness of strategies and user experience.
