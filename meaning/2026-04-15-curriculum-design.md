This involves determining what knowledge, skills, and attitudes students should acquire, structuring the content logically, and identifying appropriate teaching methods and assessment strategies. The process often begins with defining learning objectives, followed by selecting and organizing subject matter, and finally outlining how learning will be evaluated to ensure objectives have been met. Effective curriculum design considers the needs of learners, available resources, and the broader educational context.

For example, a curriculum designer might outline a unit on fractions for fifth graders, detailing the concepts to be covered, the hands-on activities students will engage in, and the types of problems they will solve to demonstrate understanding.

This term is commonly used in education, educational administration, instructional design, and academic program development.
