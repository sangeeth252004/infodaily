MOOC stands for Massive Open Online Course. The term "massive" refers to the large number of participants that can enroll, often numbering in the thousands. "Open" signifies that enrollment is typically free and accessible to a broad audience without prerequisites. "Online" indicates that the course content, lectures, assignments, and discussions are delivered over the internet.

These courses are usually structured like traditional university courses, featuring video lectures, readings, quizzes, and projects. They are frequently developed by universities and educational institutions, though companies and organizations also offer them.

For instance, a student might enroll in a MOOC on Python programming offered by a prominent technology university to gain practical coding skills.

MOOCs are commonly found on platforms dedicated to online learning, such as Coursera, edX, and FutureLearn, and are widely used for professional development, academic enrichment, and lifelong learning.
