This initial capital injection is typically used to cover the very first expenses incurred by a new business. These costs often include market research, product prototyping, team recruitment, and initial legal or administrative fees. It is essential for proving a concept's viability before the company seeks larger, subsequent investment rounds.

Sources for seed funding commonly include the founders' own capital, contributions from friends and family, individual angel investors, or startup incubators and accelerators. It helps a company establish its preliminary foundation and demonstrate potential for growth.

For example, "The new tech venture successfully raised seed funding to hire its first engineers and develop a minimum viable product." This type of financing is a critical step within the startup ecosystem and venture capital landscape.
