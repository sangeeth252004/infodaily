This concept signifies that a company has identified a sizable group of customers who genuinely value its product, perceiving it as a solution to a problem or a way to fulfill a desire. When a product achieves this fit, it experiences strong demand, high user retention, and organic growth, indicating that the market is pulling the product rather than the product pushing into the market. It represents a point where a product's features, pricing, and value proposition align perfectly with what its target audience requires.

For example, a new streaming service might achieve product market fit when its unique content library and competitive pricing lead to rapid subscriber acquisition and low churn rates.

This term is predominantly used in the contexts of startups, entrepreneurship, venture capital, and product management, often cited as a crucial milestone for business growth and investment.
