This skill set involves understanding how to navigate and interact with the vast amount of information available online and through various digital devices. It includes being able to locate relevant content, discern its accuracy and reliability, and then apply that knowledge or utilize digital tools to produce new content or communicate ideas.

For instance, a person with strong digital literacy can research a topic for a school project by using search engines, identifying credible sources like academic journals or reputable news outlets, and then synthesizing that information into a written report or presentation.

The term is frequently encountered in educational settings, professional development, and discussions about public access to information and technology.
