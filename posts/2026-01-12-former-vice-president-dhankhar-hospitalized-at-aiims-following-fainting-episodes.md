Former Vice President Dhankhar has been admitted to the All India Institute of Medical Sciences (AIIMS) in New Delhi for comprehensive medical evaluation. The hospitalization follows reports indicating that he experienced two fainting episodes within the past week. Medical professionals at the institution are preparing for an MRI scan as part of a thorough diagnostic process to ascertain the underlying cause of his recent health concerns.

The admission of a prominent national figure such as a former Vice President to a major medical facility invariably garners public and political attention, underscoring the general interest in the health status of leaders and public servants. While official details regarding the precise cause of the recent fainting spells have not yet been officially disclosed, the medical team at AIIMS is engaged in conducting a comprehensive series of diagnostic tests to evaluate his condition.

AIIMS, recognized as a premier medical institution in India, is renowned for its advanced diagnostic and treatment capabilities. It frequently serves as the facility entrusted with the medical care of high-profile individuals, including current and former government officials. The decision to perform an MRI scan suggests a focused approach towards detailed neurological or internal assessments, which are crucial in identifying the root cause of symptoms such as recurrent fainting. These types of evaluations are considered standard medical practice when patients present with such symptoms, as they can be indicative of a range of underlying conditions that necessitate meticulous investigation.

Fainting, medically known as syncope, can result from various factors, including temporary reductions in blood flow to the brain, cardiac issues, neurological conditions, or even severe dehydration. The diagnostic process typically involves a combination of physical examinations, blood tests, cardiac monitoring, and imaging studies like MRI to pinpoint the specific cause. The medical team's current objective is to ensure an accurate diagnosis, which is fundamental for developing an effective treatment plan.
