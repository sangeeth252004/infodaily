The third round, often a critical phase, serves to fill any remaining vacant seats from the previous two rounds, offering another opportunity for candidates who have not yet secured a preferred institution or specialization.

The schedule outlines several important phases for Round 3, which typically include:

*   **Fresh Registration:** Commencement of new registrations for eligible candidates who have not registered in previous rounds or wish to upgrade.
*   **Choice Filling and Locking:** A specific window during which registered candidates can select their preferred colleges and courses, followed by locking these choices.
*   **Seat Allotment Processing:** The period during which the MCC processes the choices and NEET PG 2025 ranks to allot seats.
*   **Publication of Results:** The official announcement of Round 3 seat allotment results on the MCC website.
*   **Reporting to Allotted Colleges:** A designated period for candidates to report to their assigned institutions for document verification and admission formalities.

The NEET PG counselling process is designed to ensure transparency and fairness in the allocation of postgraduate medical seats across the country. The MCC, operating under the Directorate General of Health Services (DGHS), Ministry of Health & Family Welfare, Government of India, plays a pivotal role in managing this complex admission procedure.
