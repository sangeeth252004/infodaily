On March 10, 2026, the Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP) secured a decisive victory in the Pimpri-Chinchwad Municipal Corporation (PCMC) elections, marking a significant shift in the political landscape of the crucial industrial twin-city near Pune. The results, declared across all wards, indicated a substantial lead for the BJP, consolidating its presence in the region. Conversely, the Nationalist Congress Party (NCP), historically a dominant force in Pimpri-Chinchwad and closely associated with the influential Pawar family, experienced a notable setback, failing to secure its anticipated number of seats and losing its majority.

The BJP's performance in the 2026 PCMC elections surpassed pre-election projections, with the party comfortably crossing the majority mark required to form the municipal council. Initial reports suggest the BJP secured approximately 87 out of the total 128 contested seats, representing a clear mandate from the electorate. This victory grants the BJP full control over the civic body, enabling it to pursue its developmental agenda for the rapidly growing urban centre. Party leaders, including Maharashtra BJP President Chandrashekhar Bawankule, attributed the success to effective campaign strategies focusing on local issues, good governance, and the popularity of state and national leadership.

The party, which had previously commanded a strong presence in the corporation, saw its seat count diminish considerably. While specific figures are still being consolidated, preliminary tallies indicate the NCP secured around 35 seats, a substantial reduction from its previous performance. This result is being widely interpreted as a blow to the political stronghold of the Pawar family in the Pune district's urban belt. Analysts suggest factors such as anti-incumbency, internal party dynamics, and effective opposition campaigning contributed to the NCP's reduced tally.

Pimpri-Chinchwad is a vital industrial and IT hub within the Pune Metropolitan Region, known for its rapid urbanization and diverse electorate. Control over the PCMC is highly coveted due to the substantial budget, developmental projects, and administrative authority it wields.
