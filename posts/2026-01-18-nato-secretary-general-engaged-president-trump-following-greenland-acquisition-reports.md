In August 2019, NATO Secretary General Jens Stoltenberg engaged with then-U.S. President Donald Trump, initiating a phone call and subsequently planning a meeting to discuss issues pertinent to the Arctic region. This diplomatic outreach occurred amidst widespread reports confirming President Trump's interest in the United States acquiring Greenland, an autonomous territory of Denmark. While specific details of their discussion and the upcoming agenda remained undisclosed at the time, the engagement underscored the geopolitical significance of the Arctic and the imperative for coordinated allied strategy.

President Trump had openly confirmed his administration's consideration of purchasing Greenland, citing its strategic importance and natural resources. This proposal, initially reported by The Wall Street Journal, was met with a firm rejection from Danish officials. Denmark's Prime Minister, Mette Frederiksen, called the idea "an absurd discussion," emphasizing that Greenland was not for sale. This stance led to the abrupt cancellation of a planned state visit by President Trump to Denmark in early September 2019, highlighting the diplomatic tensions spurred by the acquisition reports.

Greenland holds substantial strategic value, primarily due to its geographic position between the North Atlantic and Arctic oceans. The U.S. maintains a crucial military presence on the island, notably at Thule Air Base, which is vital for missile warning, space surveillance, and Arctic defense. Beyond its military significance, Greenland possesses vast untapped reserves of rare earth minerals, oil, gas, and fresh water, resources increasingly coveted by global powers. The melting Arctic ice cap further opens new shipping routes and access to these resources, intensifying international interest and competition in the region.

NATO's involvement, spearheaded by Secretary General Stoltenberg, underscored the alliance's recognition of the Arctic as a critical strategic domain. The region's security dynamics are evolving rapidly, particularly with increased military activity from Russia and the growing economic interests of China. Stoltenberg's direct engagement with President Trump was perceived as an effort to ensure alignment within the transatlantic alliance regarding Arctic policy and to manage potential diplomatic fallout from the Greenland reports. The alliance emphasizes freedom of navigation, responsible resource management, and regional stability, advocating for the Arctic to remain a low-tension area.
