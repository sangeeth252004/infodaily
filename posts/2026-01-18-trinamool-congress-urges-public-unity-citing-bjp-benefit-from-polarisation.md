The statement specifically linked polarisation to the BJP's political strategy, suggesting that such an environment would be conducive to the opposition party's objectives.

This appeal comes amidst a backdrop of heightened political competition and ideological debates across the country. The TMC, led by Chief Minister Mamata Banerjee, has frequently positioned itself as a bulwark against what it describes as divisive politics. The party's public communication often emphasizes secularism and social harmony as foundational principles.

Key aspects of the TMC's message included:
*   **Call for Social Cohesion:** Emphasis on the importance of different communities remaining united.
*   **Warning Against Divisive Tactics:** Alerting the public to narratives or actions that could create rifts along religious, caste, or regional lines.
*   **Strategic Implication:** Directly stating that such divisions are perceived to benefit the BJP politically, framing it as a calculated electoral strategy.
*   **Preservation of Diversity:** Highlighting India's diverse social fabric as a strength that should not be undermined.

The statement by the TMC is consistent with its long-standing rhetoric in West Bengal, where it has historically campaigned on platforms of inclusive governance and resistance to what it terms "communal politics." The political rivalry between the TMC and the BJP in West Bengal has intensified in recent years, particularly following the 2019 general elections and the 2021 state assembly elections, which saw the BJP emerge as the primary opposition force.

In the context of the TMC's statement, "polarisation" typically refers to the process by which political opinion becomes increasingly divided into opposing factions, often along socio-religious lines. Political analysts frequently discuss how such divisions can consolidate specific vote banks. The TMC's public address attempts to counter this by advocating for a unified civic response.
