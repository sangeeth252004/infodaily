On August 19, 2022, then-Brazilian President Jair Bolsonaro engaged in a telephone conversation with Indian Prime Minister Narendra Modi. The call centered on reaffirming the strategic partnership between India and Brazil and addressing a range of global and regional issues of mutual concern. Both leaders emphasized their commitment to strengthening bilateral ties across various sectors and coordinating positions on international platforms.

The discussion served to review the extensive bilateral relationship between the two nations, highlighting progress and identifying areas for further collaboration. India and Brazil, both large developing economies and members of significant global groupings, share a commitment to fostering a multipolar world order and addressing common challenges. The call underscored the importance of sustained high-level dialogue in achieving these objectives.

Key points of discussion during the telephone conversation included:

*   **Bilateral Cooperation:** The leaders reviewed the ongoing cooperation in trade, investment, defence, agriculture, and other key sectors. Both nations have been working towards expanding economic engagements and diversifying their trade basket.
*   **Multilateral Forums:** The importance of close coordination in multilateral forums such as the United Nations (UN), G20, and BRICS was reiterated. India and Brazil frequently collaborate on global governance issues, climate change, sustainable development, and trade negotiations within these frameworks.
*   **Global Challenges:** Discussions also touched upon pressing global challenges, including concerns regarding food and energy security. The leaders exchanged views on how their respective countries could contribute to global stability and resilience in these critical areas, especially in the context of prevailing international geopolitical dynamics.
*   **Brazil's Bicentennial:** Prime Minister Modi extended congratulations to President Bolsonaro on the occasion of Brazil's bicentennial of independence, which was celebrated on September 7, 2022.
*   **G20 Presidency:** Prime Minister Modi expressed his anticipation for Brazil's upcoming G20 Presidency, scheduled for 2024, signaling India's support for Brazil's leadership in the prestigious international forum. This highlighted the continuous rotation of leadership within the G20, with India holding the presidency in 2023.

The reaffirmation of the strategic partnership through this high-level engagement underscored the enduring nature of India-Brazil relations. The two nations continue to work towards enhancing their cooperation, not only for mutual benefit but also to contribute to global peace, stability, and sustainable development. Moving forward, both countries are expected to maintain active dialogue and cooperation within bilateral and multilateral frameworks to implement the outcomes of such high-level discussions.
