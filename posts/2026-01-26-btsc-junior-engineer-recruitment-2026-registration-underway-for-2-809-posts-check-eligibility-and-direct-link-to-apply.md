The Bihar Technical Service Commission (BTSC) announced a significant recruitment drive in May 2022 for 2,809 Junior Engineer (JE) positions, a process referred to as "BTSC Junior Engineer recruitment 2026." At the time of the announcement, online registration for these posts was underway, commencing on May 18, 2022. Prospective candidates were provided a window of opportunity to submit their applications, with the final deadline set for June 12, 2022.

This recruitment initiative aimed to fill a substantial number of technical roles within various departments under the Bihar government. The demand for qualified engineers, particularly those holding diplomas in specialized fields, was addressed through this extensive hiring process. The selection methodology was primarily based on academic performance at the diploma level and relevant work experience, according to the official notification from the commission.

The application procedure was conducted entirely online, requiring candidates to access the official BTSC website or the dedicated application portal. This digital approach streamlined the process for a wide pool of eligible applicants from across the state and beyond. Successful candidates were expected to contribute to the state's ongoing infrastructure and development projects.

Key details regarding the BTSC Junior Engineer recruitment 2026 included:

*   **Commission Responsible**: Bihar Technical Service Commission (BTSC)
*   **Total Posts Advertised**: 2,809 Junior Engineer positions
*   **Application Period**: May 18, 2022, to June 12, 2022
*   **Application Mode**: Online
*   **Official Websites for Application**: btsc.bih.nic.in and pariksha.nic.in
*   **Disciplines Covered**: Civil Engineering, Mechanical Engineering, and Electrical Engineering
*   **Minimum Educational Qualification**: Candidates were required to possess a Diploma in the respective engineering discipline from an institution recognized by the All India Council for Technical Education (AICTE).
*   **Age Limit**: For general male candidates, the age limit was set between 18 and 37 years as of August 1, 2018. Relaxations applied to specific categories, with women and BC/EBC male candidates eligible up to 40 years, and SC/ST male and female candidates up to 42 years.

The recruitment drive represented a crucial opportunity for diploma holders seeking entry into government service. The substantial number of vacancies underscored the state's efforts to bolster its technical workforce. Following the conclusion of the application period, the commission proceeded with the evaluation and selection process, ultimately aiming to induct qualified individuals into public service roles. The outcomes of this recruitment were integral to enhancing technical expertise and capacity within Bihar's governmental framework.
