India's diplomatic presence in Yemen observed its 75th Republic Day on January 26, 2024, marking the first such celebration held within the conflict-affected country in over a decade. This event signaled a measured return of public diplomatic activity by India in a nation where its embassy had previously scaled back operations significantly due to severe security concerns. The resumption of this traditional ceremony underscores India's enduring commitment to its relationship with Yemen and the welfare of the Indian community residing there.

The decade-long hiatus in public Republic Day celebrations directly correlates with the severe deterioration of the security situation in Yemen since the mid-2010s. From late 2014, the country plunged into a complex civil conflict, characterized by widespread fighting, displacement, and a profound humanitarian crisis. This instability led numerous foreign diplomatic missions, including India's, to suspend or significantly scale back their on-the-ground operations and relocate personnel. India's embassy in Sana'a had effectively ceased full-scale public functions for many years, though efforts were consistently made to maintain essential consular services and monitor the welfare of Indian nationals through a network of contacts and remote operations. During this period, India notably undertook 'Operation Raahat' in 2015, a large-scale evacuation effort that successfully rescued thousands of Indian citizens, alongside foreign nationals, from the conflict zones, demonstrating its commitment even during the peak of hostilities. The resumption of the Republic Day ceremony now signals a cautious re-evaluation of the operational environment, potentially indicating localized improvements in stability or a strategic decision to re-engage more visibly.

The flag-hoisting ceremony, held on January 26, 2024, was reportedly organized under strict security protocols, reflecting the persistent challenges in the region. Attendees included members of India's diplomatic contingent in Yemen and a segment of the Indian expatriate community still residing in the country. While specific details regarding the presence of Yemeni government officials were not publicly disclosed, the event itself serves as a powerful symbol of India's enduring commitment to its historical relations with Yemen and its steadfast support for the well-being of its diaspora. During the ceremony, the senior-most Indian diplomatic representative present is understood to have unfurled the national flag and read excerpts from the President of India's address to the nation, a standard diplomatic protocol observed globally on Republic Day.
