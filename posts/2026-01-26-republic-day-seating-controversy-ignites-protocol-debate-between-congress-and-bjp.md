New Delhi witnessed a political dispute following the 74th Republic Day parade on January 26, 2023, as the Indian National Congress (INC) questioned the seating arrangements for its top leaders. Congress President Mallikarjun Kharge and former party president Rahul Gandhi were seated in the third row at the Kartavya Path event, a placement the INC described as a breach of protocol and a sign of disrespect, sparking a sharp retort from the ruling Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP).

The INC contended that the seating of its senior leaders, particularly the then Leader of Opposition (LoP) Mallikarjun Kharge, did not align with established government protocol for such a significant national event. Party spokespersons highlighted that previous Leaders of Opposition and senior opposition figures had historically been accorded front-row or second-row seating. Congress leaders publicly stated their dissatisfaction, suggesting the arrangement was politically motivated and deviated from customary practice. They specifically pointed out that former Congress President Sonia Gandhi had previously been seated in the first row at similar events.

In response, the BJP and government officials defended the seating plan, asserting that it strictly adhered to extant protocol based on the constitutional positions of the attendees. BJP representatives clarified that the seating matrix for Republic Day parades is determined by a hierarchy that places constitutional functionaries, such as the President, Vice President, Prime Minister, and Chief Justice of India, in the front rows, followed by cabinet ministers, state chief ministers, and then the Leader of Opposition. According to government sources, the LoP is typically placed in the third row, a protocol that was reportedly followed in this instance for Mr. Kharge. Rahul Gandhi, as a Member of Parliament, was seated according to the protocol for elected legislators.
