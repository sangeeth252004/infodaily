The application window is typically open for several weeks, providing sufficient time for candidates to complete the online application form and submit necessary documents.

The examination process for the JPSC Civil Services Examination is structured into three distinct stages to comprehensively assess candidates' knowledge, aptitude, and personality. These stages are:

*   **Preliminary Examination (PT):** This is an objective-type examination designed to shortlist candidates for the main examination. It typically comprises two papers: General Studies Paper I and General Studies Paper II, focusing on general knowledge, current affairs, and aptitude.
*   **Main Examination:** This stage consists of written examinations with both compulsory and optional papers. It evaluates candidates' in-depth knowledge, analytical ability, and writing skills across various subjects relevant to public administration and general studies.
*   **Interview/Personality Test:** Candidates who qualify the main examination are invited for an interview. This stage assesses the candidate's mental aptitude, critical thinking, communication skills, and suitability for a career in public service.

Prospective applicants must meet specific eligibility criteria, including educational qualifications and age limits, as detailed in the official notification. Generally, a bachelor's degree from a recognized university is a fundamental requirement. Age relaxation provisions are typically available for candidates belonging to reserved categories, as per government rules.
