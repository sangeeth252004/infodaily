A passenger who intentionally jumped 100 feet from Royal Caribbean's Symphony of the Seas cruise ship into the Atlantic Ocean near Grand Bahama Island in November 2023 has publicly recounted the experience, describing the impact and the subsequent 45 minutes he spent in the water before being rescued. The individual, identified as Nick Naydev, was reportedly banned for life from Royal Caribbean cruises following the incident, which was captured on video by his companions.

The event unfolded while the Symphony of the Seas was sailing in international waters off the coast of Grand Bahama. Naydev, reportedly under the influence, made the deliberate decision to leap from his cabin balcony, which was approximately 100 feet above the water line. He later described the water's surface upon impact as feeling "like cement," a common sensation reported by individuals falling from significant heights into water. Naydev also stated his immediate fear of "shark-infested waters," a perception that heightened the urgency of his situation.

Upon witnessing the jump, the cruise ship's crew initiated emergency protocols. The vessel was reportedly brought to a halt, and a life raft was deployed to assist Naydev. After approximately 45 minutes in the open ocean, Naydev was successfully retrieved by the ship's rescue team. Following his rescue, he was reportedly handed over to Bahamian authorities.

*   **Incident Date:** November 2023
*   **Vessel:** Royal Caribbean's Symphony of the Seas
*   **Location:** Near Grand Bahama Island
*   **Height of Jump:** Approximately 100 feet
*   **Time in Water:** Approximately 45 minutes
*   **Consequence for Passenger:** Banned for life from Royal Caribbean cruises.

Cruise lines maintain strict policies against such actions, citing significant safety risks to both the individual involved and the ship's operations. Jumping overboard, regardless of intent, necessitates an immediate halt to the ship, the deployment of rescue assets, and potential deviation from its scheduled itinerary, leading to delays for thousands of passengers. Such incidents place an undue burden on crew members and can involve local authorities and coast guard resources.

The maritime industry consistently warns passengers about the dangers of irresponsible behavior onboard, emphasizing that actions like jumping from a moving vessel can lead to severe injury, drowning, or legal consequences. Cruise operators typically implement a zero-tolerance policy for such violations, which often includes lifetime bans and financial penalties. This specific incident serves as a reinforcement of the critical importance of adhering to safety guidelines and responsible conduct while at sea, mitigating risks for all individuals aboard.

Authorities and cruise lines continue to stress that passenger safety remains paramount, and any actions compromising this safety are met with serious repercussions to ensure the well-being of all on board.
