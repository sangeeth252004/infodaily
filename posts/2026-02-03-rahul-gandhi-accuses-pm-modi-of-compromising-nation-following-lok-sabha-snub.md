On March 11, 2022, Congress leader Rahul Gandhi made a significant political accusation outside the Parliament House in New Delhi, alleging that Prime Minister Narendra Modi had "compromised and sold our nation." Gandhi's remarks followed an incident in the Lok Sabha where he reported being denied permission by the Speaker to speak on the government's foreign policy, specifically concerning the ongoing conflict between Russia and Ukraine.

He expressed his intent to provide context and details of his concerns had he been allowed to speak inside the legislative chamber.

The parliamentary incident involved Mr. Gandhi attempting to raise his points when Lok Sabha Speaker Om Birla reportedly did not permit him to do so. Following this, Mr. Gandhi addressed a press conference, where he reiterated his frustration over what he described as the government's unwillingness to engage in an open debate on a matter of significant national and international importance. He emphasized that the right to discuss crucial foreign policy issues is fundamental to democratic functioning.

Parliamentary Affairs Minister Pralhad Joshi swiftly responded to Mr. Gandhi's allegations, refuting the claims made by the Congress leader. Minister Joshi stated that Mr. Gandhi was not specifically denied permission to speak, but rather that the Lok Sabha session had been adjourned at the time of his attempt to address the House. Mr. Joshi further clarified that Mr. Gandhi's previous remarks against the government had been expunged from the parliamentary records, implying that such actions are consistent with established parliamentary rules and procedures. The Minister underscored that discussions on foreign policy issues are typically scheduled through proper parliamentary channels and protocols, often after consultations by the Business Advisory Committee.
