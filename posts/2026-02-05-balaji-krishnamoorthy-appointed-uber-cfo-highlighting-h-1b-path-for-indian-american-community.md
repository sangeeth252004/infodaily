The appointment of Balaji Krishnamoorthy as Chief Financial Officer (CFO) of Uber Technologies Inc. in late 2022 continues to be a point of significant recognition within the Indian-American community, particularly given his journey as a former H-1B visa holder. This development emerges amid ongoing national discussions regarding skilled immigrant visas and U.S. immigration policy.

Krishnamoorthy's promotion to the CFO role at the global ride-sharing and food delivery giant was announced by Uber, elevating him from his previous position as Head of Global Finance Operations. Prior to joining Uber, Mr. Krishnamoorthy held significant financial leadership roles across major technology companies. His career trajectory, from being a recipient of the H-1B non-immigrant visa to assuming a top executive position at a multi-billion dollar corporation, resonates deeply with many within the Indian-American community.

The H-1B visa program allows U.S. employers to temporarily employ foreign workers in specialty occupations that require theoretical or technical expertise in specialized fields, such as IT, engineering, mathematics, science, and medicine. India is consistently one of the leading countries of origin for H-1B visa beneficiaries, with many professionals migrating to the U.S. to work in the technology sector. The journey of moving from a temporary work visa status to securing U.S. permanent residency and eventually reaching executive leadership positions is often viewed as an aspiration within the community.
