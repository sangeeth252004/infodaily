The minister's remarks included a specific reference to violence in the state, stating, "'Bengal mein bomb chalta hai'" (bombs prevail in Bengal).

During her parliamentary address, Minister Sitharaman articulated grave concerns regarding the security environment in West Bengal. She cited instances of violence, encapsulated by the phrase referencing the prevalence of bombs, which she presented as indicative of a pervasive issue of lawlessness directly impacting daily life. The minister further extended her critique to the safety of women in the state, alleging that conditions in certain areas were such that women felt compelled to remain indoors after nightfall. She presented this situation as indicative of a systemic failure in maintaining public order under the administration led by West Bengal Chief Minister Mamata Banerjee. This direct challenge from a high-ranking Union minister highlights the ongoing federal-state tensions, particularly with states governed by opposition parties.
