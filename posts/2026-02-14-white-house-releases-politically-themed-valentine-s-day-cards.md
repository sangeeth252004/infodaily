The collection, disseminated via official White House digital channels, included lighthearted messages directed at entities such as Venezuelan President Nicolas Maduro and the Danish territory of Greenland, marking a departure from traditional holiday greetings. This initiative represents a unique approach by the current administration to engage the public and deliver messages through a less formal, humorous lens.

The cards, designed in a classic Valentine's Day aesthetic, repurposed common romantic phrases to reference political figures and geopolitical topics. One notable card depicted an illustration of Nicolas Maduro, accompanied by the caption, "Maduro, you captured my heart." Another featured an illustration of Greenland, with the message, "Greenland, I'm sweet on you." These specific references recall past discussions and diplomatic exchanges involving the U.S. government, particularly a 2019 report of a U.S. presidential interest in purchasing Greenland and ongoing U.S. policy regarding the Venezuelan government. The collection also extended to other themes, including domestic infrastructure projects and climate initiatives, adopting a similar playful tone.

This style of public engagement leverages a holiday known for expressing affection to comment on serious topics in a more accessible manner. By utilizing humor and popular culture references, the administration appears to aim for broader public reach and engagement, particularly among audiences on social media platforms. While the specific intent behind each card's message is open to interpretation, the overall approach suggests an effort to humanize policy discussions and to subtly reaffirm certain U.S. stances or priorities through a seemingly innocuous medium. This method contrasts with traditional diplomatic communiques and public statements, offering a different avenue for messaging.
