New Delhi, India â€“ India is pursuing self-reliance in critical minerals and aims to commence domestic production of rare-earth permanent magnets by the end of the current year. Union Minister G. Kishan Reddy announced this strategic objective, highlighting the current global market dynamics where "some countries have monopolized them," referring to essential critical minerals. The initiative underscores India's push to reduce import dependency and bolster its domestic manufacturing capabilities in key technological sectors.

The minister's statement signals a significant step in India's broader "Atmanirbhar Bharat" (self-reliant India) vision, particularly in areas crucial for advanced industries and national security. Critical minerals, including rare earths, are indispensable components in a wide array of high-technology products, ranging from electric vehicles (EVs) and renewable energy systems to consumer electronics and advanced defense applications. India currently relies heavily on imports for many of these strategic materials and their processed derivatives.

The focus on rare-earth permanent magnets is particularly noteworthy. These magnets are vital for manufacturing components such as electric motors, wind turbine generators, and sophisticated electronic devices. Developing an indigenous supply chain for these magnets is expected to provide a strategic advantage, ensuring a stable supply for India's burgeoning EV market and its expanding renewable energy infrastructure. The government's stated timeline targets initial production before the close of the year, indicating an accelerated push to establish this capability.

Key details of the initiative include:
*   **Strategic Goal:** Achieve self-reliance in critical minerals to reduce foreign dependency.
*   **Production Target:** Commence rare-earth permanent magnet production within India by the year-end.
*   **Driving Factor:** Address the global concentration of critical mineral supply chains, which Minister Reddy described as being monopolized by certain nations.
*   **Economic Impact:** Support domestic manufacturing across critical sectors like electric vehicles, electronics, and defense.
*   **National Security:** Enhance India's strategic autonomy by securing access to vital industrial inputs.

The move comes amidst increasing global competition for critical mineral resources and a growing recognition of their geopolitical significance. Nations worldwide are evaluating their supply chains to mitigate risks associated with disruptions and monopolistic control. For India, establishing a domestic capacity for rare-earth permanent magnets and securing critical mineral resources is projected to fortify its industrial base, support technological innovation, and contribute to long-term economic stability and strategic independence. The implementation details and further timelines for scaling up production are anticipated to emerge as the year progresses.
