Recently, a Delhi court delivered strong remarks against members of the Indian National Congress (INC) for conducting a "shirtless" protest during an Artificial Intelligence (AI) Summit. The court asserted that such actions "imperil the Republic's image" and bring disrepute to the nation on an international platform. The observations were made during a legal proceeding, underscoring judicial concern over public conduct at events involving global participation.

The judiciary's direct commentary on the conduct of political protests, particularly those occurring at international technology gatherings, highlights growing concerns regarding public decorum and its potential impact on India's global standing. The court's remarks emphasize the importance of maintaining a dignified national image, especially in venues hosting foreign delegates and showcasing technological advancements. This judicial stance signals a broader focus on the appropriateness of protest methods in sensitive national and international contexts.

During the hearing, the court reportedly condemned the protest as an act that could negatively affect international perceptions of India. While the specific grievances the protesters aimed to highlight through their "shirtless" demonstration were not the subject of the court's direct criticism, the observations focused primarily on the manner of the protest and its potential implications for the nation's reputation. The judicial body emphasized that actions perceived as undignified could undermine efforts to project India as a responsible and modern republic.

The protest, involving members of the Indian National Congress, occurred at an AI Summit in Delhi. Such summits are typically platforms for showcasing technological innovation, fostering international collaboration, and attracting investment. The court's comments suggest that the disruption or perceived lack of decorum at such an event could inadvertently detract from its core objectives and present an unfavorable image of the country to a global audience. The nature of the protest, involving participants without shirts, was specifically noted in the court's disapproval.

*   **Court's Observation:** The court's direct commentary included statements suggesting that such acts amounted to "hooliganism" and "imperil the Republic's image," causing "disrepute."
*   **Event Context:** The incident took place during an Artificial Intelligence (AI) Summit, an event typically aimed at promoting technological advancement and international engagement.
*   **Parties Involved:** Members of the Indian National Congress carried out the protest.
*   **Focus of Remarks:** The court's criticism was directed at the conduct and presentation of the protest, rather than the political motives behind it.
