The Delhi Subordinate Services Selection Board (DSSSB) has recently released the Tier I examination results for the position of Laboratory Attendant, under Post Code 35/21. Concurrently, the Board has commenced the process of inviting e-dossiers from candidates who have qualified for various posts within the Delhi Jal Board, marking a significant step in the recruitment cycle for government vacancies in the National Capital Territory of Delhi.

Candidates who appeared for the Tier I examination for Laboratory Attendant can now access their scores and check their qualification status via the official DSSSB website. The release of these results allows successful candidates to proceed to the next stage of the selection process, which typically involves the submission of required documents and potentially further examination stages. The DSSSB notification specifies the qualifying marks and criteria for progression.

For several positions within the Delhi Jal Board, the DSSSB has opened the window for e-dossier submission. This digital submission process requires provisionally shortlisted candidates to upload their academic, professional, and personal documents for verification. This stage is crucial for confirming the eligibility of candidates based on their applications and performance in previous examination stages. The positions for which e-dossiers are being invited include:

*   **Filter Supervisor** (Post Code 3/21)
*   **Junior Engineer (Civil)** (Post Code 13/20)
*   **Assistant Engineer (E&M)** (Post Code 14/20)
*   **Junior Engineer (E&M)** (Post Code 15/20)

Candidates provisionally shortlisted for these Delhi Jal Board posts are advised to refer to the official DSSSB notification for precise details regarding the e-dossier submission window, required documents, and specific instructions. Failure to upload the necessary documents within the stipulated timeframe may lead to disqualification. The e-dossier serves as a primary method for the Board to scrutinize the credentials of applicants before final selection.

The DSSSB is responsible for recruiting candidates to various departments, local bodies, and autonomous organizations under the Government of the National Capital Territory of Delhi. These announcements are critical for thousands of job seekers aspiring to secure public sector employment. The selection process, comprising written examinations and document verification, aims to ensure a transparent and merit-based recruitment system.

The next steps for successful candidates in both the Laboratory Attendant recruitment and the Delhi Jal Board posts will involve the thorough verification of all submitted e-dossier documents. Following this, the Board will proceed with the preparation of final merit lists and subsequent offer letters to selected candidates, pending any further stages of examination or interview as per the specific post's recruitment rules. Candidates are encouraged to regularly monitor the official DSSSB website for all subsequent updates and notifications related to these recruitment drives.
