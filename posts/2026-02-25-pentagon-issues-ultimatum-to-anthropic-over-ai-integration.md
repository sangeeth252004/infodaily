U.S. Secretary of Defense Pete Hegseth has issued a direct ultimatum to Dario Amodei, CEO of leading artificial intelligence firm Anthropic, demanding heightened cooperation with government initiatives or risk significant federal action. The directive, which underscores escalating national security concerns regarding advanced AI capabilities, was made public following an official company announcement from Anthropic. The nature of the ultimatum, described as a clear call to "get on board" with government objectives, signals a pivotal moment in the relationship between the defense sector and pioneering AI developers.

The ultimatum from Secretary Hegseth reportedly focuses on the integration of Anthropic's sophisticated AI models, such as its Claude series, into national defense frameworks, alongside calls for increased transparency and collaboration on AI safety protocols relevant to national security. Pentagon leadership has increasingly voiced the need for access to cutting-edge AI technologies for strategic advantage, while simultaneously emphasizing the importance of mitigating potential risks associated with powerful AI systems. This demand highlights the U.S. government's proactive stance in shaping the trajectory of AI development, particularly within companies at the forefront of the field.

Key details surrounding the ultimatum suggest several areas of concern and proposed collaboration:

*   **National Security Integration:** The Pentagon seeks Anthropic's expertise and technology for defensive and intelligence applications, aiming to leverage advanced AI for data analysis, strategic planning, and operational support.
*   **AI Safety and Ethics:** A central theme involves establishing shared frameworks for responsible AI development, ensuring that Anthropic's commitment to "Constitutional AI" aligns with governmental standards for safe and ethical deployment in sensitive contexts.
*   **Access to Research and Development:** The ultimatum implies a demand for greater insight into Anthropic's proprietary research and development processes, facilitating government oversight and potentially accelerating the adaptation of commercial AI for defense purposes.
*   **Data Security and Sovereignty:** Concerns over data handling, security vulnerabilities, and ensuring U.S. control over critical AI infrastructure are also understood to be significant components of the directive.
