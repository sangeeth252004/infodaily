The incident, which involved comments made on a "hot mic" during what appears to be a virtual interaction, has prompted an immediate investigation by the college administration.

The recordings captured the professor making statements deemed racist regarding the academic capabilities and presence of Black students. While the precise context of the remarks is subject to the ongoing investigation, the audio quickly circulated, drawing widespread attention and condemnation across the university community and beyond. The content of the remarks sparked immediate concerns regarding the learning environment and institutional commitment to diversity and inclusion.

Following the disclosure of the audio, Hunter College administration confirmed that the professor had been placed on administrative leave, effective immediately. This action signifies the institution's initial response to the allegations, allowing for a full and impartial inquiry into the incident. Hunter College President Ann Kirschner, or relevant college spokespersons, typically issue statements in such situations reaffirming the institution's commitment to fostering an inclusive and respectful environment for all students and condemning discriminatory behavior. Such statements often emphasize a zero-tolerance policy for racism and discrimination.
