India's Supreme Court has voiced strong criticism concerning the National Council of Educational Research and Training (NCERT) over a chapter on graft, delivering remarks that underscored serious judicial apprehension regarding educational content. During recent proceedings, the court stated, "They fired the gun and judiciary is bleeding," signaling profound displeasure with the educational body. These observations emerged amid a series of pointed statements from the apex court, highlighting the perceived impact of certain curriculum choices on the integrity of the judiciary.

The remarks from the Supreme Court carry significant weight, impacting a key institution responsible for developing and publishing textbooks for schools across India. NCERT's curriculum forms the backbone of the national education system, making any judicial scrutiny of its content a matter of national importance. The court's sharp language reflects an unusual level of concern, suggesting that the "graft chapter" in question may have touched upon sensitive issues related to corruption in a manner deemed problematic by the judiciary.

The specific context of the Supreme Court's criticism revolved around content within a chapter focusing on "graft," or corruption. While the exact details of the chapter's content or the specific actions by NCERT that prompted the judicial intervention were not immediately detailed, the court's pronouncements indicate a perception of significant misrepresentation or adverse implications for the judicial system. The reported "top 10 quotes" from the Supreme Court's observations emphasize the intensity and gravity of its concerns.

*   Reports indicate that these remarks were part of a series of strong statements, collectively referred to as "top 10 quotes," made by the court during its proceedings.
*   The judicial observations highlight a potential conflict between educational content dissemination and the court's view on the portrayal or impact on institutional integrity.

While NCERT has yet to issue a formal response to the Supreme Court's specific remarks, the judicial observations are expected to prompt internal discussions and potentially lead to re-evaluation of relevant chapters to align with judicial concerns, ensuring educational materials uphold public confidence in national institutions.
