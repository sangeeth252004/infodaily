Indian benchmark equity indices commenced trading on February 27, 2026, with a notable decline. The Nifty50 opened below the 25,450 mark, while the S&P BSE Sensex registered a drop of over 250 points from its previous close, indicating a cautious start to the day's session on Dalal Street, Mumbai.

At the market opening, the NSE Nifty50 began trading at approximately 25,430 points, having concluded the previous session around the 25,500 level. Concurrently, the S&P BSE Sensex initiated trading at approximately 75,200 points, following its close at around 75,450 points on February 26. This early downturn reflected a prevalent selling pressure across key segments of the market.

The decline in Indian equities mirrored broader cautious sentiment observed in global markets, with investors closely monitoring international economic data and geopolitical developments. Factors such as shifts in crude oil prices, fluctuations in the U.S. dollar index, and movements in global bond yields often influence investor decisions in emerging markets like India. Domestic economic indicators, including inflation figures and industrial output data, also play a significant role in shaping market direction.

Specific sectors, including financial services and information technology, observed early selling activity, contributing to the overall downward trajectory of the indices. Market participants were noted to be evaluating upcoming corporate earnings announcements and any policy statements that could impact corporate profitability and economic growth projections.
