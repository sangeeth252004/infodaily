Air India flight AI1805, scheduled to travel from New Delhi, India, to Tel Aviv, Israel, executed a U-turn mid-flight on Friday, October 11, and subsequently returned to its departure airport. The decision was made amidst heightened geopolitical tensions in the Middle East, following widespread reports of an alleged strike by the United States and Israel on Iran.

The flight, which had departed from Indira Gandhi International Airport in New Delhi, was several hours into its journey when the directive to return was issued. Air India officials confirmed the flight's diversion, citing safety concerns stemming from the rapidly evolving situation in the airspace over the Middle East. The airline prioritised the safety of its passengers and crew in light of the unconfirmed but widely circulated reports regarding military actions in the region.

This incident underscores the immediate impact of geopolitical instability on international aviation operations. Airlines routinely monitor global events and adjust flight paths or schedules to mitigate risks, particularly in areas experiencing conflict or heightened security alerts. The abrupt reversal of Air India's flight AI1805 highlights the severity of the perceived threat to commercial air travel routes traversing the affected region.

Key details regarding the incident include:
*   **Flight Number:** AI1805
*   **Original Route:** New Delhi (DEL) to Tel Aviv (TLV)
*   **Action Taken:** U-turn mid-flight, returning to New Delhi
*   **Date:** Friday, October 11
*   **Reason:** Safety concerns prompted by reports of escalating Middle East tensions, specifically alleged military strikes.
*   **Airline Response:** Air India confirmed the return, stating that the decision was made for operational safety.

The disruption extended beyond the direct Tel Aviv route. Sources familiar with Air India's operations indicated that other long-haul flights, particularly those destined for North America, might also experience adjustments.
