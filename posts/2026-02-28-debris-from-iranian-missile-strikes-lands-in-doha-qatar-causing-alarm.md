On January 15, 2024, debris from missiles launched by Iran's Revolutionary Guard Corps (IRGC) reportedly fell over Doha, the capital of Qatar. The incident occurred as the IRGC conducted missile strikes against targets in Syria and Iraq. While no casualties or significant damage were immediately reported, the falling debris triggered alarm among residents in Doha.

The missile launches by the IRGC were described as a retaliatory measure following twin bombings in Kerman, Iran, on January 3, 2024, which resulted in over 90 fatalities. The IRGC announced its intentions to target "terrorist headquarters" in Syria and an "espionage headquarters" in Iraq's Kurdistan region. The debris falling in Qatar is considered an unintended consequence of these operations, with no indication that Qatar was an intended target of the strikes.

Residents in Doha reported witnessing debris falling from the sky, leading to widespread concern and the circulation of related videos on social media platforms. Qatari authorities have not issued detailed official statements specifically addressing the debris incident at the time of reporting. The event highlights the potential for broader regional impact from ongoing military actions in the Middle East.

*   **Intended Targets of Strikes:** "Terrorist headquarters" in Syria and an "espionage headquarters" in Iraq's Kurdistan region.
*   **Location of Debris Fall:** Over Doha, Qatar.
*   **Reported Impact:** Caused panic among residents; no immediate reports of casualties or damage.
