Car batteries, while robust, are designed for specific applications and typically contain lead-acid chemistry, which requires careful handling.

This experiment highlights growing interest in do-it-yourself (DIY) energy solutions and large-scale power storage, but also underscores significant safety considerations. Connecting a large number of batteries can create considerable hazards, including:

*   **Thermal Runaway:** Overcharging or short-circuiting lead-acid batteries can lead to excessive heat generation, potentially causing fires or explosions.
*   **Acid Spills:** Damage to battery casings can release corrosive sulfuric acid, posing chemical burn risks.
*   **Gassing:** Charging lead-acid batteries produces hydrogen gas, which is highly flammable and explosive when mixed with air. In confined spaces or without proper ventilation, this can accumulate to dangerous levels.
*   **High Current/Voltage Arcs:** Large electrical installations carry the risk of dangerous arcs if connections are faulty or mishandled, leading to severe burns or electrocution.
