In early October 2022, Israel's then-Intelligence Minister, Eli Cohen, issued a stark warning directed at Iran's future supreme leader. Cohen stated that if the incoming leader were to threaten Israel's existence, they would be "targeted for elimination." The declaration was made during an interview with the Saudi news site Elaph, published on Friday, October 7, 2022, and subsequently reported by various international news outlets.

The warning emerged amidst sustained high tensions between Israel and Iran, both of whom are regional rivals with deeply entrenched and often conflicting strategic interests. The statement by a high-ranking Israeli official underscored Israel's assertive posture regarding potential future leadership in Iran, particularly given the advanced age and reported health issues of Iran's current Supreme Leader, Ayatollah Ali Khamenei, who was 83 at the time.

Cohen's remarks elaborated on Israel's core security doctrines concerning Iran. He explicitly stated, "The next supreme leader, whoever he may be, needs to know that if he threatens Israel's existence, he will be targeted for elimination." This strong language indicated Israel's perceived red lines and its readiness to act against what it deems existential threats.

Beyond the direct threat against a future leader, Cohen outlined broader Israeli strategic objectives regarding Iran:
*   **Nuclear Program:** He reiterated Israel's unwavering commitment to preventing Iran from acquiring nuclear weapons, asserting that Israel "will never allow Iran to obtain nuclear weapons."
*   **Regional Entrenchment:** Cohen emphasized Israel's determination to prevent Iran's "regional entrenchment," referring to Tehran's military and political influence across the Middle East through proxies and allied forces.
*   **International Pressure:** He also called for a "concert of nations" to form a united front against Iran, advocating for sustained international pressure, including sanctions, to counter Iran's nuclear ambitions and support for militant groups. Cohen further criticized the 2015 Iran nuclear deal, arguing that it provided Iran with funds that could be channeled towards supporting terrorism.

Iran, for its part, routinely issues statements calling for the destruction of Israel. Concurrently, Tehran maintains that its nuclear program is exclusively for peaceful purposes and that its regional presence is defensive in nature. The public warning from Israel's Intelligence Minister highlights the ongoing covert and overt conflict, intelligence operations, and strategic posturing that characterize the relationship between the two nations.

The minister's statement serves as a clear signal of Israel's long-term strategic planning and intelligence focus on Iranian leadership succession. It underlines the continued volatility of the Middle East geopolitical landscape and the persistent challenges posed by the deep-seated rivalry between Israel and Iran. The implications of such a public warning are expected to resonate within the intelligence and foreign policy circles of both nations and their international allies, suggesting an enduring period of elevated vigilance and potential for escalation.
