New Delhi officially conveyed its condolences to Tehran following the death of Iranian President Ebrahim Raisi and Foreign Minister Hossein Amir-Abdollahian in a helicopter crash. Indian External Affairs Minister S. Jaishankar engaged in a telephonic conversation with Iranian Acting Foreign Minister Ali Bagheri Kani, extending India's sympathies and reaffirming the commitment to bilateral relations. This diplomatic exchange occurred in May 2024, shortly after the tragic incident in Iran's East Azerbaijan province.

The incident, which saw President Raisi's helicopter crash in rugged terrain, also claimed the lives of several other high-ranking officials. India's swift and formal response underscores the significance of its long-standing diplomatic and economic ties with Iran, particularly amidst evolving regional geopolitical dynamics. The relationship holds strategic importance for India, encompassing energy security, trade routes, and regional stability.

Prime Minister Narendra Modi had earlier expressed profound sadness and condolences via social media on May 20, remembering President Raisi's contributions to strengthening India-Iran relations. The subsequent call between EAM Jaishankar and Acting FM Kani served to formalize these sentiments, with Jaishankar conveying "heartfelt condolences" on behalf of the Indian government and its people. He also reiterated India's steadfast commitment to fostering closer bilateral cooperation across various sectors.

*   **Fatalities:** President Ebrahim Raisi, Foreign Minister Hossein Amir-Abdollahian, East Azerbaijan Governor Malek Rahmati, and Ayatollah Mohammad Ali Ale-Hashem (Supreme Leader's representative in East Azerbaijan province) were among the nine individuals who perished in the crash.
*   **Incident Circumstances:** The helicopter, transporting President Raisi and his delegation, crashed on May 19, 2024, in the mountainous Dizmar forest near Varzaqan city, in the East Azerbaijan province. The accident occurred during adverse weather conditions, including heavy fog and rain, making search and rescue operations challenging.
*   **National Mourning:** India observed a day of national mourning on May 21. As a mark of respect, the national flag was flown at half-mast on all government buildings nationwide, reflecting the diplomatic gravity of the loss and the depth of India's solidarity with Iran.
*   **Bilateral Engagement:** During the phone conversation, both foreign ministers also discussed the trajectory of India-Iran bilateral cooperation. Key areas of focus include economic partnerships and regional connectivity projects. Notably, India has made significant investments in the development of the Chabahar Port, which serves as a crucial transit hub for trade with Afghanistan and Central Asian countries, bypassing Pakistan. The port remains a cornerstone of India's strategic outreach in the region.

Following the death of President Raisi, Iran has initiated procedures for snap presidential elections, which are constitutionally mandated to be held within 50 days of the president's demise. The elections are currently scheduled for June 28, 2024, to select Raisi's successor. First Vice President Mohammad Mokhber has assumed interim presidential duties and is overseeing this transition period. India's consistent diplomatic outreach, including its expressions of condolence and reaffirmation of bilateral ties, underscores its strategic interest in maintaining stable and constructive relations with Iran, regardless of internal political transitions. This approach ensures continued engagement on critical matters such as trade, energy security, and the advancement of shared regional infrastructure initiatives like the Chabahar Port project.
