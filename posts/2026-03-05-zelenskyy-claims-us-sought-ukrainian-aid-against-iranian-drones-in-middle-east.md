Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelenskyy announced on February 10, 2024, that the United States had approached Ukraine with a request for assistance in countering Iranian-manufactured drones in the Middle East. Speaking during a press conference in Kyiv, President Zelenskyy indicated that the request stemmed from Ukraine's extensive experience in intercepting these aerial vehicles during the ongoing conflict with Russia.

The statement highlights the evolving landscape of global drone warfare and Ukraine's emerging role as a key player in anti-drone strategies. Ukraine has been a primary target for Iranian-designed Shahed-131 and Shahed-136 loitering munitions, often referred to as "kamikaze" drones, supplied by Iran to Russia. Kyiv's forces have developed various tactics, including mobile fire teams and electronic warfare systems, to combat these threats effectively, claiming a high success rate in intercepts.

Zelenskyy did not specify the exact nature or scope of the assistance requested by the US, nor did he confirm whether Ukraine would provide such aid. However, his remarks underscored the recognition of Ukraine's combat-tested expertise in dealing with drone technology that has proliferated beyond the Ukrainian theater.

Key details surrounding the situation include:
*   **Drone Type:** The drones in question are primarily Shahed-131 and Shahed-136 loitering munitions. These unmanned aerial vehicles are known for their relatively low cost and ability to strike targets at long ranges.
*   **Iranian Proliferation:** Iranian-made drone technology has been observed in various conflict zones. Besides their use by Russia in Ukraine, similar drones have reportedly been supplied to non-state actors in the Middle East, including Houthi rebels in Yemen.
*   **Middle East Context:** The US and its allies have faced increasing drone and missile attacks in the Red Sea and surrounding regions. Iran-backed Houthi forces have utilized such weaponry against commercial shipping and naval vessels, leading to intensified US and international military operations to protect maritime routes and regional stability.
*   **Ukrainian Expertise:** Since Russia's full-scale invasion in February 2022, Ukraine has become a proving ground for counter-drone technologies and tactics. Its forces have adapted to defend against sustained drone attacks targeting critical infrastructure and military positions.

The potential for Ukraine to share its anti-drone knowledge or provide other forms of support suggests a new dimension in international defense cooperation. While no official confirmation or details regarding the US request or Ukraine's response have been released by either government, Zelenskyy's public statement introduces the possibility of a direct exchange of tactical intelligence or technological solutions developed in the context of the Russo-Ukrainian war being applied to challenges in another geopolitical region. The statement underscores the persistent and global challenge posed by the proliferation of advanced drone technology.
