Dubai, UAE – Emirates and Etihad Airways have initiated a phased resumption of limited flight services following recent geopolitical developments that prompted temporary adjustments to airspace and flight paths across parts of the Middle East. Concurrently, major Indian carriers, including IndiGo and Air India Express, have announced the addition of extra flights to key Gulf destinations, responding to sustained passenger demand and adapting to evolving operational landscapes.

The decisions by the UAE-based flag carriers come after a period of heightened caution, during which airlines temporarily rerouted or suspended services to ensure passenger and crew safety. Emirates, headquartered in Dubai, and Etihad Airways, based in Abu Dhabi, are progressively reinstating services on select routes. This careful approach aims to balance operational stability with safety protocols, utilizing revised flight corridors where necessary. The resumption is expected to alleviate some of the travel disruptions experienced by passengers connecting through or travelling to the region.

Indian airlines have swiftly responded to the operational changes by augmenting their capacity. This move is particularly significant given the substantial air traffic between India and the Gulf Cooperation Council (GCC) countries. The additional flights are designed to accommodate increased demand, which often surges when traditional routes face adjustments.

*   **IndiGo:** India's largest carrier, has announced plans to introduce new flights and increase frequencies on existing routes connecting major Indian cities with destinations like Dubai, Abu Dhabi, and Sharjah. This expansion aims to ensure continued connectivity for business and leisure travellers, as well as the significant expatriate Indian population in the Gulf.
*   **Air India Express:** The low-cost arm of Air India, is also enhancing its services to the Middle East. The airline is adding flights to bolster its network, providing more options for travellers affected by previous route adjustments or seeking immediate travel.
*   **Vistara:** A full-service Indian carrier, is reportedly monitoring the situation closely and making its own adjustments to flight schedules to optimize connectivity and capacity where feasible.

Airline spokespersons for the respective carriers have emphasized that operational decisions are being made with paramount importance placed on safety and adherence to international aviation guidelines. Flight paths are continuously monitored and adjusted in real-time, leveraging advanced air traffic control technologies to navigate regional airspace effectively. Passengers are advised to regularly check their airline’s official channels for the most current flight status and any potential schedule modifications.

The aviation sector continues to demonstrate its capacity for swift adaptation in dynamic environments. While limited services are being reinstated and additional capacities introduced, the airlines remain vigilant. Future operational decisions will likely depend on the continued assessment of regional conditions and the stability of designated flight corridors. Travellers planning journeys through or within the affected regions are encouraged to consult airline advisories for the latest updates on flight schedules and travel guidance.
