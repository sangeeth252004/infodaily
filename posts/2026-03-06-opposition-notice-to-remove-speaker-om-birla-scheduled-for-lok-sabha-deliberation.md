Opposition parties, primarily members of the Indian National Developmental Inclusive Alliance (INDIA) bloc, formally submitted a notice for a resolution seeking the removal of Lok Sabha Speaker Om Birla. This parliamentary procedure was scheduled to be taken up for deliberation during the Lok Sabha session on Monday, July 22, 2024. The move signals a significant procedural challenge within the Indian Parliament, bringing the conduct and authority of the Speaker under direct scrutiny.

The notice was filed following heightened tensions between the government and opposition benches over various issues, including parliamentary decorum, the suspension of Members of Parliament, and the handling of legislative business. The Speaker, as the presiding officer of the Lok Sabha, is responsible for maintaining order, interpreting rules, and ensuring the smooth functioning of the House. A motion for their removal is a rare but constitutionally provided mechanism for members to express discontent with the Speaker's performance or impartiality.

According to the Rules of Procedure and Conduct of Business in Lok Sabha, specifically Rule 18, a Member may give notice of a resolution for the removal of the Speaker. Such a notice must be supported by at least 50 Members of the House. Once the notice is accepted by the Secretary-General, it is placed before the House. If the motion is admitted, a date is fixed for its discussion and a vote. The Speaker does not preside over the House when a resolution for their own removal is under consideration. In such instances, the Deputy Speaker or another member from the panel of chairpersons officiates.

Key details regarding the motion:
*   **Target:** Lok Sabha Speaker Om Birla.
*   **Initiators:** Opposition parties, prominently from the INDIA bloc.
*   **Procedure:** Governed by Rule 18 of the Lok Sabha Rules of Procedure.
*   **Requirement:** Support from a minimum of 50 Lok Sabha Members.
*   **Outcome:** If adopted by a majority of all the then members of the House, the Speaker vacates their office.

The submission of this notice underscores the escalating political dynamics within the Lok Sabha. While motions of no-confidence against the government are more frequent, a resolution for the removal of the Speaker is less common, making this development particularly noteworthy. Such a motion tests the numerical strength of the opposition and can significantly impact the legislative agenda and future conduct of parliamentary sessions.

The deliberation on Monday, July 22, 2024, commenced with procedural formalities. The outcome of the discussion and potential vote holds implications not only for Speaker Om Birla's tenure but also for the broader relationship between the treasury and opposition benches. Further parliamentary proceedings will determine the trajectory of this resolution and its ultimate impact on the functioning of the Lok Sabha.
