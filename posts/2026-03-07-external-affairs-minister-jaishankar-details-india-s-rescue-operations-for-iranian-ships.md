External Affairs Minister S. Jaishankar recently affirmed India's consistent provision of assistance to Iranian ships seeking aid within Indian maritime zones. Speaking on a recent occasion, Minister Jaishankar highlighted instances where Iranian vessels in distress specifically requested entry into Indian harbours, an appeal consistently met with India's humanitarian response. This public acknowledgement underscores India's active and impartial role as a maritime responder in the vital Indian Ocean region.

Minister Jaishankar elaborated that these vessels "wanted to come into our port," indicating a clear and deliberate appeal for help from the Iranian side. India's response to such pleas aligns with its long-standing policy of extending humanitarian assistance and disaster relief (HADR) at sea, a principle upheld irrespective of the nationality or flag of the distressed vessel. The assistance provided typically encompasses a range of crucial services:

*   **Safe Harbour:** Offering secure anchorage and berthing facilities in Indian ports.
*   **Technical and Mechanical Support:** Providing engineers and equipment for essential repairs to propulsion systems, navigation equipment, or other critical ship components.
*   **Provision of Supplies:** Supplying fuel, fresh water, provisions, and spare parts.
*   **Medical Aid:** Attending to sick or injured crew members, including facilitating medical evacuations when necessary.

The minister's remarks shed light on the practical aspects of India's comprehensive maritime outreach. Strategically positioned along critical international shipping lanes, India's coastline and maritime jurisdiction frequently encounter situations requiring such interventions. Its robust naval and coast guard assets are routinely deployed to render assistance to vessels of all flags experiencing mechanical failures, medical emergencies, severe weather damage, or other forms of maritime distress. These operations are conducted in strict adherence to international maritime law, which obligates states to render aid to those in peril at sea.

This consistent provision of aid extends beyond operational assistance, contributing significantly to diplomatic goodwill between India and Iran. Both nations share historical ties and maintain active maritime trade, making safe passage and cooperation essential. India's readiness to assist Iranian vessels reinforces its image as a reliable and responsible stakeholder in global maritime safety and security, particularly within its immediate neighbourhood. Such acts of aid demonstrate a tangible commitment to international maritime conventions and contribute positively to regional stability and enhanced bilateral relations.

The EAM's statement, delivered during a recent address, did not specify the exact number of incidents or the precise dates of these rescues. However, it confirmed the recurrence of such appeals from Iranian ships, indicating a consistent pattern of humanitarian engagement. This highlights India's operational readiness and its unwavering commitment to maritime safety principles. India's extensive coastal infrastructure and highly trained personnel play a crucial role in managing such emergencies, ensuring that distressed vessels receive timely and effective support in accordance with established international protocols.

India's broader maritime doctrine places significant emphasis on being a "first responder" and a "net security provider" in the Indian Ocean. This philosophy guides its actions in extending help to commercial, fishing, and other vessels, including those from nations with complex international relations. The recent remarks by Minister Jaishankar serve to publicly acknowledge and document these humanitarian efforts, which, by their nature, often occur away from widespread public scrutiny.

As maritime traffic continues to grow in the Indian Ocean region, the imperative for robust search and rescue capabilities and comprehensive humanitarian assistance remains paramount. India's ongoing efforts to support distressed vessels, including those from Iran, reinforce its strategic position and diplomatic approach in maintaining a secure, cooperative, and safe maritime environment. These actions are expected to continue shaping India's role as a key player in regional maritime security and humanitarian outreach, fostering goodwill and adherence to international maritime norms.
