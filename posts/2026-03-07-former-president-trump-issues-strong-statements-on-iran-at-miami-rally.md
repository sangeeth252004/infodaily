Former U.S. President Donald Trump made a series of forceful statements regarding Iran during a campaign rally in Miami, Florida, on October 20, 2023. Addressing supporters, Mr. Trump asserted that "Iran has surrendered," claimed credit for what he described as Tehran's apology to its neighbors, and hinted at the possibility of targeting more leaders. His remarks included the declaration, "'Today, Iran will be hit very hard!'"

The former president's comments centered on recent developments and past U.S. actions concerning Iran. Mr. Trump stated that Iran's foreign minister had issued an apology to neighboring nations for recent events, an action for which Mr. Trump took credit. He connected this perceived apology to a shift in Iran's stance, suggesting it indicated a capitulation. The statement "Iran has surrendered" underscored this assessment of the nation's current position.

During the rally, Mr. Trump also alluded to past operations, specifically referencing the January 2020 killing of General Qassem Soleimani, then the commander of the Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps' Quds Force. He reiterated his responsibility for the operation, stating that Soleimani was "gone forever." This historical reference served as a backdrop to his current remarks about potential future actions, specifically hinting at the targeting of "more leaders." The phrase "'Today, Iran will be hit very hard!'" followed his allusions to such measures, suggesting an immediate and severe response.

His rhetoric reflects a firm and assertive stance against the Iranian government, consistent with his administration's approach during his presidency.
