Former India captain Sunil Gavaskar has publicly advocated for the exclusion of young batter Abhishek Sharma from India's playing XI for the upcoming T20 World Cup final. Gavaskar's comments, made ahead of the crucial match against South Africa, specifically highlighted Sharma's repeated dismissals due to similar shot selections, suggesting a failure to adapt or learn from previous mistakes.

Gavaskar articulated his stance following India's dominant semi-final victory over England in Guyana. While acknowledging the importance of player form, the cricketing legend emphasized the need for strategic team selection in a high-stakes encounter like a World Cup final. His comments underscore a focus on consistent performance and tactical awareness, particularly in top-order batting positions.

Key details from Gavaskar's remarks include:
*   **Concern over Dismissal Pattern:** Gavaskar pointed out that Abhishek Sharma has been dismissed in a consistent manner, indicating a lack of adjustment to specific bowling strategies or conditions. This suggests a perceived technical vulnerability that could be exploited by opposition bowlers.
*   **"Hasn’t learned from his dismissals":** This direct quote encapsulates Gavaskar's primary criticism, implying a lack of progression in Sharma's approach to batting after being dismissed similarly in multiple instances.
*   **Call for Replacement:** While Gavaskar did not explicitly name a replacement, his call implies a preference for a player who demonstrates greater technical solidity or adaptability in high-pressure situations. Potential alternatives in the squad often considered for batting roles include Sanju Samson or Axar Patel, depending on tactical requirements.
*   **Timing of Comments:** The remarks come at a critical juncture, just days before the T20 World Cup final on June 29, 2024, at Kensington Oval in Barbados. This timing places additional scrutiny on the team management's final selection decisions.

Abhishek Sharma has featured in recent matches, including the semi-final, as India sought to finalize their batting combinations. His role, often as an aggressive top-order batter, carries expectations of rapid scoring. However, the scrutiny from a figure of Gavaskar's stature brings an additional layer of pressure on the player and the team selectors.

The Indian team management, led by Captain Rohit Sharma and Head Coach Rahul Dravid, will now face a significant decision regarding the playing eleven for the final. While player form and match-ups are typically paramount, external commentary from respected former players can sometimes fuel public debate and add to the strategic considerations. The focus will now shift to whether the team retains its recent combinations or makes tactical adjustments in response to perceived vulnerabilities, with the ultimate goal of securing the T20 World Cup title.
