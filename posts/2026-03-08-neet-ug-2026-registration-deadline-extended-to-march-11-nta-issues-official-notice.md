New Delhi – The National Testing Agency (NTA) has officially extended the registration deadline for the National Eligibility cum Entrance Test (Undergraduate) [NEET UG] 2026. Aspiring medical students now have until March 11 to complete their applications for the highly anticipated examination. This extension was formally announced through a public notice issued by the NTA, providing an additional window for candidates across the country.

NEET UG serves as the primary gateway for admissions to a wide array of undergraduate medical and allied health science courses in India. These include Bachelor of Medicine, Bachelor of Surgery (MBBS), Bachelor of Dental Surgery (BDS), and various Bachelor of Ayurvedic Medicine and Surgery (BAMS), Bachelor of Homeopathic Medicine and Surgery (BHMS), and Bachelor of Unani Medicine and Surgery (BUMS) programs offered by approved medical, dental, and AYUSH colleges, as well as deemed universities nationwide.

The decision to extend the deadline comes as a measure to ensure that all eligible candidates have ample opportunity to register for the competitive examination. This move is particularly beneficial for students who may have faced technical difficulties, connectivity issues, or required more time to gather necessary documentation during the initial application period. The NTA consistently aims to facilitate a smooth and inclusive application process for the lakhs of students who aspire to pursue careers in healthcare.

Key details regarding the NEET UG 2026 registration process and the extended deadline include:
*   **New Registration Deadline:** March 11
*   **Issuing Authority:** National Testing Agency (NTA)
*   **Examination:** National Eligibility cum Entrance Test (Undergraduate) [NEET UG] 2026
*   **Application Portal:** The registration process is conducted exclusively online through the official NTA NEET website. Candidates are required to visit the portal to fill out the application form, upload necessary documents, and complete the fee payment.
*   **Purpose:** Admission to undergraduate courses in medicine, dentistry, and AYUSH disciplines across government and private institutions in India.
*   **Impact:** The extension provides crucial additional time, potentially accommodating a larger pool of applicants for the upcoming examination cycle.

Candidates are strongly advised to utilize this extended period responsibly by thoroughly reviewing their application forms before final submission to avoid any discrepancies. The NTA has also provided specific guidelines and instructions on its official website regarding the application procedure, required documents, and eligibility criteria. Applicants are encouraged to refer to these resources for accurate information.

Following the definitive closure of the registration window on March 11, the NTA is expected to proceed with subsequent stages of the examination cycle. These typically include the opening of correction windows, issuance of admit cards, and ultimately, the conduct of the NEET UG 2026 examination on its predetermined schedule. Aspiring students should remain vigilant for further announcements from the NTA concerning these upcoming phases.
