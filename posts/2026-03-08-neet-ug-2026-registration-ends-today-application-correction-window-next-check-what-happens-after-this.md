The National Eligibility cum Entrance Test (Undergraduate) 2026, commonly known as NEET UG 2026, registration period officially concludes today. Aspiring candidates for undergraduate medical, dental, and AYUSH courses across India were required to complete their application submissions through the official website of the National Testing Agency (NTA) by the stipulated deadline. This marks a critical juncture for millions of students seeking admission to MBBS, BDS, and other allied medical programs in government and private institutions nationwide.

The NTA, the organizing body for NEET UG, has managed the online application process, which commenced several weeks ago. The extensive application form requires candidates to provide personal details, academic qualifications, contact information, and preferred exam centers, alongside uploading necessary documents such as photographs and signatures. The payment of the application fee also forms an integral part of the submission process, with varied fee structures for different candidate categories. The closure of the registration window signifies the end of the initial application phase, preparing the groundwork for subsequent stages of the examination cycle.

Following the culmination of the registration process, the NTA is anticipated to activate the application correction window. This critical phase allows candidates to review and amend specific details within their submitted forms. The correction facility is designed to address minor errors or discrepancies that may have occurred during the initial application filling, ensuring the accuracy of candidate data before the final processing stages.

Key details regarding the application correction window and subsequent steps are outlined below:

*   **Application Correction Window:** The NTA is expected to open a dedicated correction window in the coming days. This period is typically short, often lasting a few days, during which candidates can log in to their application portal to make necessary edits.
    *   **Amendable Details:** Candidates are generally permitted to correct personal information such as name spellings, parent's names, date of birth, gender, category (if proof is available), and domicile state. Additionally, details related to academic qualifications and communication addresses may also be editable.
    *   **Image Corrections:** Discrepancies in uploaded photographs and signatures, often related to size or clarity, are common issues addressed during this period. The NTA provides specific guidelines for image formats, which candidates must adhere to.
    *   **Non-Amendable Details:** Certain critical details, such as the registered mobile number and email ID, are typically not changeable as they are used for essential communication throughout the examination process. Candidates are advised to consult the official NTA notification for precise guidelines on what can and cannot be modified.

*   **Admit Card Release:** After the correction window closes and the NTA completes its verification processes, admit cards for NEET UG 2026 will be released. These e-admit cards, mandatory for entry into the examination hall, will contain crucial information including the candidate's roll number, examination center details, exam date, and reporting time. Candidates will be able to download their admit cards from the NTA NEET official website.

*   **Examination Date:** Based on previous examination cycles, the NEET UG 2026 examination is typically conducted in May. The exact date will be officially announced by the NTA well in advance. The examination is a single paper comprising multiple-choice questions from Physics, Chemistry, and Biology (Botany and Zoology), conducted in offline mode across various centers.

*   **Result Declaration and Counselling:** Post-examination, the NTA will release the provisional answer key, allowing candidates to raise objections. Following this, the final answer key and results will be declared. Qualified candidates will then participate in the NEET UG counselling process, conducted by the Medical Counselling Committee (MCC) for All India Quota seats and by respective state authorities for state quota seats, leading to admissions into medical and dental colleges.

Candidates who have submitted their applications are strongly advised to remain vigilant for official announcements from the National Testing Agency regarding the opening of the correction window, admit card release, and the final examination schedule. Regular checks of the NTA NEET website are crucial to ensure compliance with all deadlines and requirements.
