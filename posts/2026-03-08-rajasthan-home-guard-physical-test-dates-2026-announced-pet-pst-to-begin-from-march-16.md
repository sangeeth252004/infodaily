The Rajasthan Home Guard Recruitment Department has officially announced the schedule for the Physical Efficiency Test (PET) and Physical Standard Test (PST) for the 2026 recruitment cycle. These critical physical assessments are set to commence from March 16, 2026, across various designated centres within Rajasthan.

This announcement provides clarity and a definitive timeline for thousands of candidates who have applied for the Home Guard positions, marking a significant progression in the selection process. The PET and PST rounds are mandatory elimination stages designed to assess applicants' physical fitness, endurance, and adherence to prescribed physical measurements. Candidates must successfully clear these tests to advance to subsequent recruitment phases.

The Physical Standard Test (PST) typically involves measuring height, chest (for male candidates), and weight, ensuring candidates meet the minimum physical criteria established for the Home Guard force. The Physical Efficiency Test (PET) assesses various aspects of physical capability through a series of tasks. These commonly include running races over specified distances, long jump, and shot put, with varying standards and requirements for male and female applicants. Detailed information regarding the specific events, qualifying marks, and criteria will be provided in the official notification accompanying the admit cards.

Key details regarding the upcoming physical tests include:

*   **Test Commencement Date:** March 16, 2026.
*   **Nature of Tests:** Physical Efficiency Test (PET) and Physical Standard Test (PST).
*   **Admit Card Release:** Admit cards for the PET/PST are expected to be released digitally on the official recruitment portal several weeks prior to the commencement date. Candidates will be required to download and print their admit cards, which will contain specific details about their test venue, date, and reporting time.
*   **Required Documents:** Candidates must bring their printed admit card, a valid photo identification proof (such as Aadhaar card, voter ID, driving license, or passport), recent passport-sized photographs, and original copies of relevant educational qualification and reservation certificates to the test venue. Failure to produce these documents may result in disqualification.
*   **Preparation:** Applicants are advised to adhere to a disciplined physical training regimen and ensure they meet the health and fitness standards required for the tests. Regular checks of the official website for updates and instructions are recommended.

Following the successful completion of the PET and PST, qualified candidates will proceed to the next stages of the recruitment process. These typically include a written examination, a detailed medical examination, and thorough document verification. The final selection will be based on a comprehensive evaluation of performance across all stages. Applicants are advised to regularly visit the official website of the Rajasthan Home Guard for the latest updates, notifications, and to download their admit cards promptly once released.
