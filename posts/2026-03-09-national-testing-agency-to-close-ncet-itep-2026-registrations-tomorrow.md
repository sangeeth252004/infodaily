The National Testing Agency (NTA) is scheduled to conclude the online registration process for the National Common Entrance Test (NCET) for the Integrated Teacher Education Programme (ITEP) 2026 tomorrow. Prospective candidates aiming for admission into the four-year ITEP across various Central, State, and private universities/institutions must complete their applications before the deadline. The official application window will close at the end of the day tomorrow, with no extensions anticipated.

The NCET serves as the gateway for admissions to the Integrated Teacher Education Programme, a comprehensive four-year undergraduate course that combines a Bachelor of Arts (B.A.), Bachelor of Science (B.Sc.), or Bachelor of Commerce (B.Com.) degree with a Bachelor of Education (B.Ed.). This initiative aligns with the National Education Policy (NEP) 2020, which mandates ITEP as the minimum degree qualification for school teachers by 2030, aiming to professionalize the teaching sector and ensure quality education standards. The programme is designed to prepare teachers for the foundational, preparatory, middle, and secondary stages of schooling.

Candidates are required to apply through the NTA’s official website, ncet.samarth.ac.in. The online portal facilitates the entire application process, including registration, form filling, document upload, and payment of the applicable examination fees. Timely submission is crucial, as late applications will not be entertained by the agency. The NTA has advised all applicants to refer to the comprehensive information bulletin available on its website for detailed eligibility criteria, examination structure, participating institutions, and other essential guidelines before proceeding with their applications.

Key details regarding the NCET ITEP 2026 application process include:

*   **Application Mode:** Exclusively online via the official NTA portal.
*   **Deadline:** Registrations close at the end of the day tomorrow.
*   **Official Website:** ncet.samarth.ac.in is the designated platform for application submission and information access.
*   **Eligibility:** Candidates must have passed the Class 12th examination or equivalent with a minimum aggregate percentage, as specified in the information bulletin. Specific subject requirements may vary based on the chosen ITEP specialization (B.A. B.Ed., B.Sc. B.Ed., or B.Com. B.Ed.).
*   **Application Steps:**
    *   New registration using a valid email ID and mobile number.
    *   Filling out the detailed application form with personal and academic information.
    *   Uploading scanned copies of required documents, including photograph and signature, in specified formats.
    *   Payment of the application fee through various online modes (credit card, debit card, net banking, UPI).
    *   Reviewing and final submission of the application form.
    *   Printing the confirmation page for future reference.

The NCET 2026 examination structure typically includes multiple-choice questions (MCQs) covering General Awareness, Language, Teaching Aptitude, and Domain-Specific Subjects, tailored to assess candidates' readiness for the teaching profession. The exact syllabus and paper pattern are detailed in the information bulletin.

Following the closure of registrations, the NTA is expected to announce the schedule for admit card releases and the examination dates for NCET ITEP 2026. Applicants are strongly encouraged to regularly check the official NTA website for the latest updates and announcements regarding the examination timeline and any further instructions. Adherence to the stipulated deadlines and guidelines is paramount for all aspiring candidates.
