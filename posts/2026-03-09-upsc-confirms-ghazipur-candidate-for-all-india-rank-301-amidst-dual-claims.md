The Union Public Service Commission (UPSC) has issued a clarification regarding the rightful claimant to All India Rank (AIR) 301 in the recently declared results of the Civil Services Examination (CSE) 2022. Following public confusion over two candidates bearing the same name, Akanksha Singh, the commission confirmed that Akanksha Singh from Ghazipur, Uttar Pradesh, is the legitimate holder of the said rank.

The confusion arose after the UPSC released the final results for the CSE 2022, which saw thousands of candidates qualify for various civil services. Reports emerged of two individuals, both named Akanksha Singh, simultaneously claiming AIR 301. One candidate, hailing from Ghazipur, presented her roll number and other credentials to support her claim. Simultaneously, another candidate from Bihar, also named Akanksha Singh, reportedly put forward a different roll number, asserting her qualification for the same rank. This situation led to widespread media attention and questions regarding the accuracy of the results and the potential for administrative errors.

To address the growing ambiguity and uphold the integrity of its rigorous examination process, the UPSC released an official statement. The commission meticulously cross-referenced the details of both individuals against its definitive records. The investigation concluded that Akanksha Singh from Ghazipur, bearing roll number 1109060, was indeed the candidate assigned All India Rank 301. Her candidature details, including marks secured and preferences, were consistent with the official merit list.

Conversely, the UPSC statement clarified that the other individual, also named Akanksha Singh, who presented roll number 1103039, did not secure AIR 301 in the Civil Services Examination 2022. The commission's records indicated that roll number 1103039 was not associated with this specific rank in the final results published for the CSE 2022. This distinction was crucial in resolving the public uncertainty surrounding the result.

The UPSC is responsible for conducting one of the most competitive examinations globally, selecting candidates for prestigious roles such as the Indian Administrative Service (IAS), Indian Foreign Service (IFS), and Indian Police Service (IPS). The accurate dissemination of results is paramount to maintaining public trust and ensuring fairness to all aspirants. Incidents like these, while rare, underscore the commission's commitment to transparency and meticulous record-keeping. The prompt clarification by the UPSC was vital in dispelling misinformation and validating the efforts of the rightful candidate.

With this official confirmation, Akanksha Singh of Ghazipur can now proceed with the subsequent stages of the civil services induction process, including cadre allocation and training, free from any further doubt regarding her achievement. The incident serves as a reminder of the extensive verification processes involved in large-scale national examinations and the importance of official communications in rectifying discrepancies.
