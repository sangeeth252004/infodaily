The Union Public Service Commission (UPSC) has officially confirmed Akanksha Singh, daughter of Anil Kumar Singh from Ghazipur, Uttar Pradesh, as the legitimate holder of Rank 301 in the Civil Services Examination (CSE) 2022. This clarification definitively resolves a period of confusion that arose after two individuals, both named Akanksha Singh, publicly claimed the same rank following the declaration of results on May 23, 2023. The UPSC's conclusive statement aims to reassure the public and candidates about the accuracy and integrity of its examination process.

The unusual situation garnered significant media attention and prompted scrutiny into the rigorous UPSC examination system. Both candidates had presented themselves to various media outlets, each asserting their claim to Rank 301. The Ghazipur-based Akanksha Singh, bearing Roll Number 1521300, consistently maintained her rank was verified by official UPSC documents. Conversely, another individual, Akanksha Singh, daughter of B.P. Singh from Aligarh, also publicly claimed Rank 301, citing a different roll number, 0407130.

The discrepancy became evident through differing roll numbers and the marks obtained by the two individuals. The UPSC's subsequent verification process, including the release of official detailed mark sheets, confirmed the Ghazipur candidate's legitimate claim to the rank. The official data for Akanksha Singh (Roll No. 1521300) revealed a total of 960 marks, comprising 787 in the Main examination and 173 in the interview segment. In contrast, the Aligarh-based Akanksha Singh's claimed marks were 802 in Mains and 160 in the interview, totaling 962; however, her purported result document was later identified as a fabrication.

Key details regarding the confirmed candidate and the disputed claim include:

*   **Confirmed Candidate:** Akanksha Singh (daughter of Anil Kumar Singh, Ghazipur)
    *   UPSC Roll Number: 1521300
    *   Mains Examination Marks: 787
    *   Interview Marks: 173
    *   Total Marks: 960
    *   Officially Confirmed UPSC Rank: 301
*   **Disputed Candidate:** Akanksha Singh (daughter of B.P. Singh, Aligarh)
    *   Claimed UPSC Roll Number: 0407130
    *   Claimed Mains Examination Marks: 802
    *   Claimed Interview Marks: 160
    *   Claimed Total Marks: 962
    *   Basis of Claim: Forged document, refuted by UPSC

The Union Public Service Commission has not only affirmed the Ghazipur candidate's rightful rank but has also indicated its intention to investigate the circumstances surrounding the forged document presented by the Aligarh-based claimant. This incident underscores the critical importance of verifying examination results through official sources and highlights the Commission's commitment to maintaining transparency and fairness in its selection process. Further action, including potential legal proceedings against those responsible for the deceptive claim and fabrication of results, is expected to be pursued by the UPSC. The swift resolution of this matter aims to reinforce public trust in the reliability of the UPSC's rigorous evaluation system.
