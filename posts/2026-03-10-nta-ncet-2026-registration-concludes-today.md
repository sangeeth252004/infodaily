NEW DELHI – The National Testing Agency (NTA) today, June 15, 2025, marks the final day for candidates to register for the National Common Entrance Test (NCET) 2026. Aspiring students seeking admission to the 4-Year Integrated Teacher Education Programme (ITEP) must submit their applications via the official NTA examination portal, exams.nta.nic.in, before the deadline. This concludes the registration window that commenced on May 15, 2025.

The NCET is a national-level entrance examination crucial for admission into the innovative 4-Year ITEP across various central and state universities, IITs, NITs, and other government colleges. This program is designed to prepare future educators in alignment with the recommendations of the National Education Policy (NEP) 2020. The integrated structure of the ITEP aims to produce highly competent and multidisciplinary teachers for the Indian education system.

The significance of the NCET 2026 lies in its role as the sole gateway for entry into the ITEP for the academic session 2026-27. The programme integrates both pedagogical and subject-specific knowledge, offering BA B.Ed, BSc B.Ed, and BCom B.Ed degrees. This approach seeks to streamline teacher education, making it more robust and responsive to contemporary educational needs. Candidates who successfully qualify for the NCET will be eligible for admissions based on their ranks and the specific criteria set by participating institutions.

Key details regarding the NCET 2026 application process and subsequent examination:
*   **Application Window:** May 15, 2025, to June 15, 2025.
*   **Correction Window:** A specific period for application corrections is anticipated to open shortly after the registration deadline, typically spanning two to three days. Exact dates will be announced by NTA.
*   **Admit Card Release:** Admit cards for registered candidates are provisionally expected to be released in late July 2025, detailing examination centers and timings.
*   **Examination Date:** The NCET 2026 is tentatively scheduled to be conducted in early August 2025, with precise dates to be confirmed by the NTA. The examination will be a Computer Based Test (CBT).
*   **Eligibility:** Candidates must meet specific academic qualifications, usually involving completion of Class 12th or equivalent from a recognized board, with a minimum aggregate percentage. Age criteria may also apply as per institutional norms.
*   **Examination Structure:** The test typically comprises multiple-choice questions covering subjects such as Language, General Awareness, Teaching Aptitude, and specific domain subjects.

Following the closure of the registration window today, the NTA will proceed with processing the applications. Candidates are advised to regularly check the official NTA website, exams.nta.nic.in, for updates regarding the correction window, admit card release, examination schedule, and result announcements. The successful execution of NCET 2026 is critical for enrolling the next cohort of aspiring teachers into a program designed to elevate the standards of elementary and secondary education across the nation.
