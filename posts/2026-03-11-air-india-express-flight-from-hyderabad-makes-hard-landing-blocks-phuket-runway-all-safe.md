Phuket, Thailand – An Air India Express flight originating from Rajiv Gandhi International Airport in Hyderabad, India, experienced a hard landing at Phuket International Airport (HKT) on Friday, October 25, 2024. The incident, involving an Air India Express Boeing 737-800 aircraft, resulted in the plane becoming immobilized on the runway, prompting a temporary closure of the airport's primary airstrip. All 164 passengers and 6 crew members on board were reported safe and disembarked without injury.

The flight, identified as IX 248, departed Hyderabad at approximately 2:30 PM IST and was scheduled to arrive in Phuket by 7:10 PM ICT. According to an official statement released by Air India Express, the aircraft, registered VT-AXG, encountered an unusually firm touchdown upon landing, leading to its inability to taxi off the runway. Emergency services were immediately activated by Phuket International Airport authorities, who swiftly responded to the scene to ensure the safety of those on board and assess the situation. Passengers were subsequently transported to the terminal.

The hard landing and the subsequent immobilization of the aircraft led to significant operational disruptions at Phuket International Airport. The main runway was closed for several hours, causing multiple inbound flights to be diverted to nearby airports, including Krabi and Hat Yai, and outbound flights to be delayed or cancelled. Airport management confirmed efforts were underway to remove the aircraft and restore full operations as quickly as possible.

Key details surrounding the incident include:
*   **Aircraft:** Boeing 737-800, registration VT-AXG.
*   **Flight Route:** Hyderabad (HYD) to Phuket (HKT).
*   **Date of Incident:** Friday, October 25, 2024.
*   **Occupants:** 164 passengers and 6 crew members.
*   **Outcome:** All personnel reported safe with no immediate injuries.
*   **Impact:** Runway closure at Phuket International Airport, leading to flight diversions and delays.
*   **Source:** Official company announcement from Air India Express.

Both Air India Express and the relevant aviation authorities have initiated investigations into the cause of the hard landing. India's Directorate General of Civil Aviation (DGCA) is expected to coordinate with Thai aviation regulators, including the Civil Aviation Authority of Thailand (CAAT), to conduct a thorough inquiry. Preliminary assessments will focus on meteorological conditions at the time of landing, aircraft performance, and pilot actions. The airline has stated its full cooperation with all investigative bodies and has prioritized passenger safety and operational integrity. Further updates on the runway's reopening and the investigation's progress are anticipated as information becomes available.
