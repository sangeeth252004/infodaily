New Delhi – The Indian government has officially stated that the nation's crude oil supplies remain secure, while simultaneously announcing the prioritization of Liquefied Petroleum Gas (LPG) distribution for domestic households. This assurance comes amidst ongoing efforts to manage the distribution of petrol and diesel across the country, according to recent statements from government officials.

The Ministry of Petroleum and Natural Gas confirmed that strategic measures are in place to ensure a steady inflow of crude oil, crucial for meeting India's vast energy demands. The government's immediate focus for LPG, a primary cooking fuel, is to safeguard uninterrupted supply to its extensive base of domestic consumers. This prioritization aims to mitigate any potential disruption to essential household services, particularly benefiting beneficiaries under schemes such as the Pradhan Mantri Ujjwala Yojana, which now covers 22.5 crore beneficiaries nationwide.

While crude oil imports are stable, challenges have emerged in the domestic distribution of petrol and diesel. Government officials noted an increased demand for these fuels in recent months. Public sector Oil Marketing Companies (OMCs) – Indian Oil Corporation (IOC), Bharat Petroleum Corporation Ltd (BPCL), and Hindustan Petroleum Corporation Ltd (HPCL) – have reportedly increased their procurement to meet this rising demand. Concerns have been raised regarding certain private refiners allegedly prioritizing exports over domestic market supply, thereby placing additional pressure on public sector OMCs to cover the domestic deficit.

To address these challenges, the government has implemented several measures and is maintaining strict oversight:
*   **Daily Monitoring:** Senior officials are conducting daily meetings to monitor fuel availability and distribution networks across states, ensuring real-time assessment of the situation.
*   **Increased Imports:** Public sector OMCs have been directed to augment their imports of refined petroleum products to bridge any domestic supply gaps that may arise.
*   **No Rationing Plans:** The government has explicitly ruled out any plans for rationing petrol or diesel, affirming its commitment to maintaining open access for consumers.
*   **Fiscal Measures:** Previous government interventions include reductions in excise duty on petrol and diesel to ease price burdens on consumers. Furthermore, a targeted subsidy for cooking gas has been implemented for Ujjwala scheme beneficiaries.
*   **Ethanol Blending:** The government is also progressing with its ethanol blending program in petrol, a move aimed at reducing import dependence and promoting cleaner fuel.

The government continues to emphasize its commitment to India's energy security and ensuring stable fuel supplies for both domestic and industrial consumption. Officials reiterated that while the global energy landscape remains dynamic, strategic planning and proactive measures are being undertaken to maintain stability in the domestic market. Further updates on fuel supply and distribution are expected as monitoring efforts continue across the nation.
