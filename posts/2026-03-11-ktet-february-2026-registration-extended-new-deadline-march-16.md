The registration window for the Kerala Teacher Eligibility Test (KTET) February 2026 session has been extended, offering candidates additional time to complete their applications. Prospective teachers now have until March 16, 2024, to submit new applications and make necessary corrections to previously submitted forms. This extension provides a crucial opportunity for individuals aspiring to teaching positions in Kerala.

Organised by the Pareeksha Bhavan, Government of Kerala, the KTET is a mandatory examination for candidates seeking to become teachers in various schools across the state, including LP (Lower Primary), UP (Upper Primary), High School, and Specialist (Art, Craft, Physical Education) categories. Qualifying the KTET is a prerequisite for employment in government, aided, and un-aided schools in Kerala. The examination assesses a candidate's eligibility to teach specific classes based on their educational qualifications and chosen category.

The decision to extend the registration and correction deadline was announced by the authorities, moving the final date for submissions to March 16, 2024. This adjustment aims to ensure all eligible applicants have sufficient time to navigate the online application portal and rectify any errors in their submissions. The application process is entirely online, typically requiring candidates to register, fill out personal and educational details, upload necessary documents, and pay the examination fee through the designated official website.

The KTET is conducted across four categories, each catering to different teaching levels and qualifications:

*   **Category I:** For teachers of Lower Primary classes (Classes I-V).
*   **Category II:** For teachers of Upper Primary classes (Classes VI-VIII).
*   **Category III:** For teachers of High School classes (Classes IX-X).
*   **Category IV:** For Language teachers up to UP classes, Specialist teachers (Art & Craft), and Physical Education teachers.

Candidates must meet specific educational and professional qualifications relevant to their chosen category to be eligible for the examination. Detailed eligibility criteria are usually outlined in the official notification released prior to the registration period.

Candidates are strongly advised to utilize this extended period to finalize their applications and ensure all details are accurate before the March 16, 2024, deadline. Any discrepancies found after the closing of the correction window may lead to application rejection. Following the conclusion of the registration and correction period, the Pareeksha Bhavan is expected to announce further details regarding the release of admit cards and the official examination dates for the KTET February 2026 session. Applicants should regularly check the official KTET website, ktet.kerala.gov.in, for the latest updates and announcements. The extension underscores the state's commitment to facilitating access to teaching careers for qualified individuals and ensuring a smooth application process for this crucial eligibility test.
