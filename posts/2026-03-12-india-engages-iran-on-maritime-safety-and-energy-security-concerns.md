India's External Affairs Minister S. Jaishankar has engaged in three separate conversations with Iran's Foreign Minister, focusing on concerns regarding the safety of maritime shipping and the broader implications for energy security. These diplomatic discussions underscore India's proactive efforts to address increasing instability in key global sea lanes that are vital for international trade.

The series of high-level talks highlights New Delhi's commitment to safeguarding its economic and strategic interests amidst ongoing regional challenges. India, a major global economy with significant reliance on maritime trade for both its exports and imports, has consistently advocated for the protection of freedom of navigation and adherence to international law in critical shipping routes. The Ministry of External Affairs confirmed the frequency and nature of these engagements, emphasizing India's deep interest in the stability of maritime corridors, particularly those crucial for its energy imports.

India's concerns are multifaceted, stemming from its extensive maritime interests. The nation operates a substantial merchant fleet and is a significant contributor of seafarers to the global maritime industry, making the safety and security of personnel and vessels a primary consideration. Furthermore, a considerable portion of India's crude oil and natural gas imports traverses strategic waterways, including the Red Sea and the Gulf of Aden. Disruptions in these regions pose a direct threat to the country's energy security. Any sustained instability or heightened risks in these areas could lead to increased shipping costs, extended transit times, and potential supply chain disruptions, which would impact India's economy and consumer prices.

Key details of India's diplomatic outreach include:
*   **Three confirmed discussions** between EAM Jaishankar and Iran's Foreign Minister, signaling sustained high-level engagement.
*   **Emphasis on maritime safety**, directly addressing threats to commercial vessels and the security of seafarers.
*   **Focus on energy security**, acknowledging India's dependence on global oil and gas supplies transiting vulnerable maritime routes.
*   **Advocacy for de-escalation**, promoting dialogue and peaceful resolution to ensure uninterrupted international trade.

The discussions with Iran align with India's broader foreign policy objectives of protecting its economic and strategic interests in a volatile global environment. India has consistently called for respecting international maritime law and safeguarding the free movement of commercial shipping, which is critical for global supply chains. As a significant player in the Indo-Pacific, India maintains a vested interest in the security and stability of the wider maritime domain.

Looking ahead, India is expected to maintain its diplomatic engagements with both regional and international partners to promote stability in affected maritime zones. The Indian Navy has also enhanced its presence in critical areas to ensure the safety of Indian-flagged vessels and seafarers, while diplomatic channels remain active in seeking a peaceful resolution to ongoing threats to international shipping. India's commitment to ensuring the safety of its trade routes and the security of its energy supplies remains a central pillar of its foreign policy agenda.
