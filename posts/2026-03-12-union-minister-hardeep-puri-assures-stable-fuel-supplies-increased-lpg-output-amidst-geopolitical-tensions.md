New Delhi – Union Minister for Petroleum and Natural Gas, Hardeep Singh Puri, has assured the nation of stable fuel supplies and stepped-up liquefied petroleum gas (LPG) output. The minister's statement, made in the context of recent geopolitical developments in the Middle East, aimed to allay public concerns about potential disruptions and price volatility. He also critically addressed what he termed "rumour mongering" by the opposition regarding fuel availability.

The assurances come at a time when global crude oil markets are experiencing fluctuations due to heightened tensions in West Asia. India, as a major importer of crude oil, remains susceptible to international price movements and supply chain stability. Minister Puri emphasized that the country's energy infrastructure and strategic reserves are robust, ensuring continuous availability of essential fuels like petrol, diesel, and cooking gas.

Key points from the minister's statement and related context include:

*   **Continuous Fuel Supply:** Puri confirmed that the flow of petrol and diesel across the country remains uninterrupted. This stability is crucial for maintaining economic activity, transportation, and daily life.
*   **Increased LPG Production:** To meet domestic demand, the minister stated that LPG output has been significantly stepped up. This measure is intended to ensure ample availability of cooking gas cylinders for households, preventing shortages and price hikes for consumers.
*   **Addressing Concerns:** The minister directly addressed narratives that suggested impending fuel shortages or supply crises, attributing them to opposition parties. He underscored that such claims were unfounded and contributed to unnecessary public apprehension.
*   **Strategic Preparedness:** India's public sector oil marketing companies (OMCs) and refiners maintain diversified crude oil sourcing strategies and efficient distribution networks. These mechanisms are designed to mitigate risks associated with geopolitical events and ensure energy security.
*   **Global Context:** The Middle East region is a critical source of global crude oil, and any instability there can impact international prices and supply lines. The Indian government has consistently monitored the situation to protect national interests.

Maintaining energy security is a priority for India, given its economic growth trajectory and a large population dependent on affordable and accessible fuel. The government's focus on diversifying its energy basket and investing in domestic exploration and production, alongside maintaining strong international relationships, contributes to its ability to manage global energy market dynamics.

Looking ahead, the Ministry of Petroleum and Natural Gas is expected to continue monitoring international crude oil markets and geopolitical developments closely. The stated objective is to maintain supply stability, manage price impacts, and ensure that consumers do not face unwarranted disruptions in the availability of essential petroleum products and LPG. Public sector undertakings are mandated to ensure that distribution channels remain efficient and responsive to demand fluctuations.
