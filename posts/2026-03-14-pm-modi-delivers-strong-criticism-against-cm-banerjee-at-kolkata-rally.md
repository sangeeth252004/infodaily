Prime Minister Narendra Modi on Sunday, October 27, 2024, addressed a significant political rally in Kolkata, West Bengal, where he delivered strong criticism against Chief Minister Mamata Banerjee and the state's ruling Trinamool Congress (TMC) government. Speaking to a large gathering at the Brigade Parade Grounds, Prime Minister Modi invoked the Bengali phrase "Hisab liya jayega" (a reckoning will be had or accounts will be settled), signaling a focus on accountability regarding the state's administration and various alleged issues.

The rally marked a key moment in the ongoing political discourse between the Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP) and the TMC in West Bengal. Prime Minister Modi's address primarily centered on allegations of corruption, deficiencies in governance, and perceived obstacles to development within the state. He asserted that the central government's initiatives were not fully reaching the populace due to state-level impediments, a recurring theme in the BJP's criticism of the TMC administration. The phrase "Hisab liya jayega" was used to underscore the BJP's commitment to exposing and addressing these alleged failures.

During his speech, Prime Minister Modi highlighted several areas where he claimed the state government had faltered. These included issues related to the implementation of central welfare schemes, concerns over law and order, and the pace of industrial development and job creation. He contrasted the performance of the state government with his vision for a "developed India," suggesting that West Bengal was lagging behind other states. Specific points of contention included alleged irregularities in various public schemes and the purported politicization of state institutions.

The Prime Minister's visit to Kolkata and his robust address are seen as part of the BJP's sustained efforts to expand its political footprint in West Bengal, a state historically dominated by regional parties. The political rivalry between the BJP and the TMC has intensified over recent years, marked by frequent exchanges of accusations and a highly competitive electoral landscape. Kolkata, as the state capital, holds significant symbolic and strategic importance for both parties.

Key aspects highlighted during the rally included:

*   **Attendance:** Thousands of supporters gathered at the Brigade Parade Grounds, indicating significant public interest.
*   **Criticism Focus:** Accusations primarily revolved around alleged corruption, particularly regarding public funds and welfare schemes.
*   **Development Narrative:** Prime Minister Modi emphasized the central government's commitment to West Bengal's development, juxtaposed with claims of state government inaction.
*   **Law and Order:** Concerns were raised regarding the state's law and order situation, a frequent point of contention in political debates.
*   **Call for Accountability:** The repeated use of "Hisab liya jayega" served as a direct call for the state government to be held answerable for its actions and alleged omissions.

The rally concluded with Prime Minister Modi reiterating his party's dedication to the welfare of West Bengal's citizens and promising a future where the state would achieve its full potential. This strong message from the Prime Minister is anticipated to further fuel the political debate in West Bengal, particularly in the run-up to future electoral contests. Political analysts suggest that such high-profile rallies are designed to galvanize party cadres and influence public opinion, setting the stage for continued political engagement between the two prominent parties in the state. The Trinamool Congress is expected to issue a formal response to the Prime Minister's accusations in the coming days.
