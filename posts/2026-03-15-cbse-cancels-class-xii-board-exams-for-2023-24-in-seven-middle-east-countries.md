The Central Board of Secondary Education (CBSE) has announced the cancellation of Class XII board examinations for the academic year 2023-24 for students enrolled in CBSE-affiliated schools across seven Middle East countries. The decision, communicated by the board, means that students in these specific regions will not be required to appear for their final theoretical board exams as originally scheduled. The CBSE stated that a special result formula would be subsequently announced to evaluate these students.

This directive affects numerous Indian expatriate students pursuing their Class XII education in the designated Gulf nations and Iran. The move represents a significant departure from the standard examination procedure previously followed by the CBSE for its international schools in these territories. The cancellation applies specifically to the examination cycle for the current academic year, 2023-24, with no immediate impact on prior or future examination schedules.

The affected countries include Qatar, the United Arab Emirates (UAE), Saudi Arabia, Oman, Bahrain, Kuwait, and Iran. Educational institutions and students in these seven countries are now awaiting detailed guidelines regarding the alternative assessment mechanism from the CBSE headquarters in India. The board's communication indicated that this formula would serve as the basis for calculating the final marks for the current batch of Class XII students in the listed regions.

This decision marks a notable change in examination policy for CBSE schools operating abroad, particularly in a region with a substantial Indian diaspora. While the full reasons behind the board's decision were not explicitly detailed in the initial announcement, the immediate focus is on the fairness and transparency of the upcoming result formula. Educational bodies, parents, and students are anticipating a comprehensive framework that ensures equitable evaluation for students who have prepared for traditional board examinations.

As schools conclude their academic sessions, the immediate focus for the CBSE and the affected institutions will be on the swift and clear communication of the evaluation methodology. This clarity is crucial for students, parents, and teachers to understand the new assessment criteria and to ensure a smooth transition to higher education applications. The board is expected to release the specific result formula in due course, providing full details on the assessment process.
