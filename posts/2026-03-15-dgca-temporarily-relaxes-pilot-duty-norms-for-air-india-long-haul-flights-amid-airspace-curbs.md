NEW DELHI – The Directorate General of Civil Aviation (DGCA) has temporarily eased Flight Duty Time Limitations (FDTL) for pilots operating Air India's long-haul flights. This decision comes as a direct response to extended flight durations necessitated by ongoing airspace restrictions in parts of the Middle East, which compel airlines to adopt longer, circuitous routes. The temporary relaxation aims to provide Air India with operational flexibility to manage its international schedule while adhering to safety standards.

The measure specifically addresses the challenges presented by geopolitical tensions that have led to the closure or restriction of certain air corridors over the Middle East. These restrictions, in place for several months, have forced airlines like Air India to reroute flights, significantly increasing travel times and fuel consumption on key international sectors. For instance, flights typically traversing direct routes over affected regions now require additional hours, impacting crew scheduling and operational efficiency. The DGCA's intervention is designed to help Air India maintain its flight schedules without compromising the well-being of its flight crews.

Under standard regulations, FDTL norms are meticulously prescribed to prevent pilot fatigue, ensuring optimal cognitive function and safety throughout flight operations. These regulations dictate maximum flight duty periods, minimum rest periods, and cumulative flying hours over various durations. The temporary adjustment by the DGCA allows for a degree of flexibility within these strict parameters, acknowledging the exceptional circumstances faced by the airline. This flexibility is contingent upon Air India implementing enhanced monitoring protocols for pilot fatigue and ensuring adequate rest periods are still provided.

Key details of the temporary relaxation include:
*   **Targeted Relief:** The easing of norms applies exclusively to Air India's long-haul international flights that are directly impacted by the rerouting requirements.
*   **Temporary Measure:** The relaxation is not permanent and is subject to review based on the evolving geopolitical situation and the reopening of traditional air corridors.
*   **Operational Necessity:** The decision underscores the operational pressures faced by airlines when geopolitical events disrupt established global aviation routes.
*   **Safety Oversight:** The DGCA has reiterated that while flexibility is being offered, the paramount importance of aviation safety and crew welfare remains central to its oversight. Air India is expected to ensure comprehensive risk assessments and fatigue management systems are rigorously applied.

The longer flight times translate to increased operational costs for Air India, including higher fuel consumption and potentially additional crew requirements. The DGCA's move mitigates some of these challenges by allowing the airline to better utilize its existing pilot pool under the modified conditions. This strategic adjustment aims to sustain connectivity on critical international routes, which are vital for trade, tourism, and diplomatic travel.

This temporary adjustment by the DGCA underscores the adaptability required in the aviation sector to navigate global challenges. It reflects a balancing act between maintaining strict safety protocols and enabling operational continuity for national carriers under exceptional circumstances. The aviation regulator will continue to monitor the situation closely, and the eased norms will remain in effect only as long as the underlying airspace restrictions persist. Air India has confirmed its commitment to operating safely within the revised guidelines.
