India's External Affairs Minister (EAM) S. Jaishankar engaged in separate telephonic conversations with his Saudi Arabian and United Arab Emirates (UAE) counterparts in October 2023, addressing the escalating crisis in West Asia. The discussions underscored India's concerns regarding regional stability, de-escalation of violence, and the safety of its significant diaspora in the region.

The diplomatic outreach comes amidst heightened tensions in West Asia following recent developments, prompting international calls for restraint and humanitarian assistance. India, maintaining long-standing diplomatic ties with nations across the region, has reiterated its position advocating for a peaceful resolution and immediate de-escalation of the conflict.

During his call with Saudi Foreign Minister Prince Faisal bin Farhan Al Saud, EAM Jaishankar discussed the prevailing situation and its potential broader implications for the region. Both ministers reportedly exchanged views on the imperative of ensuring stability and minimizing civilian impact. Saudi Arabia, a key regional player, has been actively involved in diplomatic efforts to address the crisis.

Subsequently, EAM Jaishankar also spoke with UAE Foreign Minister Sheikh Abdullah bin Zayed Al Nahyan. The conversation focused on the need for de-escalation and humanitarian considerations. The UAE has consistently emphasized the importance of dialogue and peaceful means to resolve regional disputes, positioning itself as a strategic partner for India in West Asia.

Key points of the discussions included:
*   **De-escalation:** Emphasis on reducing tensions and preventing the conflict from spreading further within the region.
*   **Humanitarian Aid:** Addressing the urgent need for humanitarian assistance to affected populations.
*   **Regional Stability:** Collective efforts to safeguard peace and security in West Asia, a region critical for global energy supplies and trade routes.
*   **Diaspora Safety:** India's primary concern for the well-being and security of its large expatriate community residing in the Gulf nations.

India has consistently advocated for a two-state solution in the Israel-Palestine conflict, emphasizing the right of the Palestinian people to a sovereign state while acknowledging Israel's security concerns. The government has also provided humanitarian assistance to the region, including medical supplies and disaster relief materials, through established channels.

These high-level diplomatic engagements reflect India's commitment to active participation in global and regional affairs, particularly concerning areas with significant strategic and economic ties. The ongoing dialogue with Saudi Arabia and the UAE highlights the importance of multilateral cooperation in navigating complex geopolitical challenges. India continues to monitor the evolving situation closely, maintaining open channels for communication and advocating for peaceful resolutions.
