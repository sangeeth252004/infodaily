Balwinder Kaur, the mother of 26-year-old Gursimran Kaur, who died in a commercial oven at a Walmart Supercentre in Salmon Arm, British Columbia, on January 26, 2024, has publicly voiced her profound dissatisfaction with the ongoing police investigation into her daughter's death. Speaking recently, Ms. Kaur alleged that the Royal Canadian Mounted Police (RCMP) probe has been inadequate and expressed significant concerns regarding the circumstances of her daughter's demise.

The incident, initially deemed non-suspicious by authorities, has drawn renewed attention following Ms. Kaur's statements, which challenge the official narrative and call for a more comprehensive inquiry. Ms. Kaur has explicitly stated her belief that her daughter did not die by suicide, instead suggesting the possibility of foul play. She emphasized her frustration, stating, "I could not see her," referring to her daughter's body after the incident.

Gursimran Kaur, originally from Punjab, India, had resided in Canada since 2020, initially on a study visa before securing a work permit. She was employed as a supervisor within the bakery department of the Salmon Arm Walmart at the time of her death. Her family's current concerns revolve around the perceived lack of detail and rigor in the initial law enforcement response.

Ms. Kaur's principal grievances regarding the police investigation include:
*   Allegations that the scene of her daughter's death was not thoroughly examined by investigators for all potential evidence.
*   Claims that her daughter's body was not properly inspected for signs of struggle or other relevant forensic details.
*   Her personal inability to view her daughter's remains, which she indicates further compounded her distress and concerns about the investigation's transparency.

The family maintains that critical details may have been overlooked, pressing for a re-evaluation of the evidence and a more in-depth investigation into the events leading to Gursimran Kaur's death. This public challenge underscores the family's quest for clarity and justice, seeking answers that they believe have not yet been provided by the current investigative efforts.

Multiple agencies are involved in reviewing the circumstances surrounding Ms. Kaur's death. The British Columbia Coroners Service has confirmed an ongoing investigation, which typically examines sudden, unexpected, or unexplained deaths to determine the cause and manner of death. Additionally, WorkSafeBC, British Columbia's provincial health and safety regulator, is conducting its own inquiry into the workplace incident to ensure compliance with occupational safety standards and to identify any contributing factors related to the work environment.

The statements from Gursimran Kaur's mother place renewed pressure on Canadian authorities to address the family's concerns regarding the thoroughness of the investigation. The family's legal representatives are expected to continue pursuing avenues for a more detailed examination of the case, potentially leading to further reviews of the initial findings by the RCMP and other involved agencies. The outcomes of the ongoing investigations by the BC Coroners Service and WorkSafeBC are anticipated to provide additional clarity on the incident.
