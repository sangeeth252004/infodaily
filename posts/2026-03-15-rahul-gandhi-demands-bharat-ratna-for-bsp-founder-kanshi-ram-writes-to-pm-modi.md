Senior Congress leader Rahul Gandhi has formally written to Prime Minister Narendra Modi, urging the conferment of India's highest civilian honor, the Bharat Ratna, upon Bahujan Samaj Party (BSP) founder Kanshi Ram. The request was communicated in a letter addressed to the Prime Minister's Office, advocating for posthumous recognition of Kanshi Ram's work.

In his communication, Mr. Gandhi highlighted Kanshi Ram's significant and lasting contributions to social justice and the empowerment of marginalized communities across India. The letter emphasized Kanshi Ram's relentless efforts in advocating for the rights and upliftment of Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes, Other Backward Classes, and minorities, describing these efforts as instrumental in shaping the nation's political landscape towards greater inclusivity and equality.

Kanshi Ram, who passed away in 2006, was a prominent figure in Indian politics and a seminal leader for Dalit rights. He founded the Dalit Shoshit Samaj Sangharsh Samiti (DS4) in 1981, which subsequently led to the establishment of the Bahujan Samaj Party (BSP) in 1984. Under his leadership, the BSP emerged as a significant political force, particularly in Uttar Pradesh, focusing on mobilizing and empowering the 'Bahujan' (majority) community, which comprises various marginalized groups. His political philosophy centered on social equality and self-respect for historically oppressed communities.

The Bharat Ratna, instituted in 1954, is India's highest civilian award, granted "for the highest degrees of national service." It is awarded without distinction of race, occupation, position, or sex. The demand for Kanshi Ram to receive this prestigious award comes amidst a period of heightened political focus on the legacies of social reformers and leaders from diverse backgrounds.

Mr. Gandhi's letter specifically underscored Kanshi Ram's role not just as a political leader but as a social revolutionary who gave voice to millions who had historically been disenfranchised. The communication stated that Kanshi Ram's work led to increased political representation and awareness among the 'Bahujan' community, impacting policy and governance across multiple states. This move by the Congress leader is seen as a recognition of the enduring impact of Kanshi Ram's movement on Indian society and its political fabric.

As of the time of reporting, the Prime Minister's Office has not issued an official statement regarding Mr. Gandhi's request. The move is expected to generate further discussion within political circles and among civil society groups concerning the recognition of leaders who have championed social justice and equality. The demand also aligns with ongoing political realignments and preparations for upcoming electoral cycles, where the support of marginalized communities remains a crucial factor.
