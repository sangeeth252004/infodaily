A security incident occurred at a major port in the United Arab Emirates on the evening of October 27, 2023, prompting an immediate response from local authorities and raising concerns over maritime safety in the region. Despite the incident, an Indian-flagged oil tanker, the MT Prem Divya, successfully navigated out of the port area, reportedly sustaining no damage and with all crew members safe. The vessel's operating company, Mumbai-based Mercator Limited, confirmed the safe departure and the well-being of its personnel in an official statement.

The exact nature and target of the attack remain under investigation by UAE authorities. Initial reports indicated that the incident might have involved an unmanned aerial vehicle (UAV) or drone, though official confirmation has not been released. The affected area of the port was secured, and operations in other sections reportedly continued with heightened vigilance. No group or entity has yet claimed responsibility for the incident, which follows a pattern of security challenges in the strategically vital Gulf waterways.

The MT Prem Divya, a crude oil tanker, had completed its cargo operations and was preparing for its onward journey when the incident unfolded in a distinct part of the port complex. According to Mercator Limited's announcement, the vessel's master and crew swiftly implemented established emergency and security protocols. This adherence to procedure, combined with the ship's operational integrity, ensured its safe and timely departure from the potentially volatile zone. The Indian Ministry of External Affairs has acknowledged the incident and confirmed that it is in close contact with both UAE authorities and Mercator Limited to monitor developments.

The recent incident underscores the persistent and evolving security risks faced by international shipping in the Arabian Gulf and surrounding areas. This region is a critical artery for global oil and gas trade, making any disruption a potential concern for international markets and supply chains. Previous incidents, attributed to various actors, have consistently highlighted the need for robust maritime security measures and international cooperation to safeguard commercial vessels and their crews.

Key details regarding the incident include:
*   **Vessel Involved:** Indian-flagged crude oil tanker, MT Prem Divya, operated by Mercator Limited.
*   **Crew Status:** All crew members aboard the MT Prem Divya were reported safe and unharmed.
*   **Vessel Condition:** The tanker sustained no damage during the incident and maintained operational integrity.
*   **Location:** A significant port in the United Arab Emirates.
*   **Attack Type:** Under investigation; initial unofficial reports suggested a drone or UAV involvement.
*   **Official Response:** UAE authorities have launched an investigation; Indian government is monitoring the situation.

Investigations by UAE law enforcement and security agencies are actively underway to ascertain the specifics of the attack, identify the perpetrators, and prevent future recurrences. The international community is expected to reiterate calls for de-escalation and respect for maritime law to ensure the unimpeded flow of global commerce. Shipping companies operating in the region are advised to maintain elevated security postures and adhere strictly to all navigation and safety advisories issued by relevant authorities.
