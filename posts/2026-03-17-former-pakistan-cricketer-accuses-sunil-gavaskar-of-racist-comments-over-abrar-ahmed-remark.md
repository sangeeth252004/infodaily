Indian cricket legend and commentator Sunil Gavaskar has faced severe criticism from former Pakistan cricketer and television pundit Sikandar Bakht, who accused Gavaskar of making "racist comments" during a recent broadcast. The controversy stems from Gavaskar's remark concerning the name of Pakistani spinner Abrar Ahmed during a live cricket match commentary.

The incident occurred as Gavaskar provided commentary for the ongoing Test series between Pakistan and Australia. During the broadcast, Gavaskar made a comment about Abrar Ahmed's name, suggesting its etymology or meaning could be interpreted as "Alag-Ghar," which translates to "separate house" in Hindi. This remark quickly drew the ire of Sikandar Bakht.

Sikandar Bakht, who also works as a cricket analyst and TV pundit, publicly condemned Gavaskar's comments. Bakht voiced his strong disapproval on a television show, labelling Gavaskar's remarks as "vile stuff" and "unbelievable." He specifically stated, "Sunil Gavaskar, a legend, a great cricketer, but what kind of racist comments are you passing? This is unbelievable." Bakht further linked the comment to what he described as "sick mentality," implying a deeper issue with the nature of the remark.
