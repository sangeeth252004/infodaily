AHMEDABAD – The Gujarat Secondary and Higher Secondary Education Board (GSHSEB) has officially released the admit cards for the Gujarat Common Entrance Test (GUJCET) 2026. Candidates registered for the state-level examination can now download their hall tickets directly from the board's official website, gseb.org, by providing their application number and password or date of birth.

The release of the admit card marks a crucial phase for thousands of aspirants seeking admission to various undergraduate degree engineering and degree/diploma pharmacy courses across institutions in Gujarat. The GUJCET 2026 is scheduled to be conducted on Monday, April 7, 2026, across multiple examination centers throughout the state. The examination is mandatory for candidates aspiring for admission to these professional courses under various quotas.

The GUJCET examination typically comprises three sections: Physics, Chemistry, and either Mathematics or Biology, depending on the chosen stream. Each section carries equal weightage, and the test is administered in Gujarati, Hindi, and English mediums. Detailed instructions regarding the examination pattern, marking scheme, and syllabus were previously outlined in the official information brochure published by GSHSEB.

Candidates are strongly advised to download and print multiple copies of their admit card immediately upon release. It is imperative to thoroughly verify all personal details printed on the hall ticket, including the candidate's name, photograph, signature, roll number, examination center address, date, and time of the exam. Any discrepancies or errors identified should be promptly reported to the GSHSEB helpline or relevant authorities for rectification before the examination date.

The admit card is a mandatory document for entry into the examination hall. Without a valid admit card, candidates will not be permitted to appear for the GUJCET 2026. Along with the admit card, candidates are required to carry a valid photo identification proof (such as an Aadhar Card, Passport, Driving License, or PAN Card) to the examination center for verification purposes.

**Key Information for Candidates:**

*   **Official Website:** gseb.org
*   **Login Credentials:** Application Number and Password/Date of Birth
*   **Exam Date:** Monday, April 7, 2026
*   **Reporting Time:** Candidates must report to their assigned examination center at least 30 minutes prior to the commencement of the exam as indicated on their admit card.
*   **Documents Required:** Printed GUJCET 2026 Admit Card and a valid Photo ID proof.

**Steps to Download GUJCET 2026 Admit Card:**

1.  Navigate to the official website of the Gujarat Secondary and Higher Secondary Education Board: `gseb.org`.
2.  Locate the "GUJCET 2026 Admit Card Download" link on the homepage.
3.  Click on the designated link.
4.  Enter the required credentials, typically the candidate's application number and the password or date of birth used during registration.
5.  Submit the details.
6.  The GUJCET 2026 admit card will be displayed on the screen.
7.  Verify all information carefully.
8.  Download and print a hard copy of the admit card for future reference and examination day.

Candidates are strictly prohibited from carrying electronic gadgets such as mobile phones, smartwatches, calculators, or any study material into the examination hall. Only essential items like pens, pencils, and the required documents are typically permitted. Further specific instructions regarding exam day conduct will be detailed on the admit card itself.

Following the examination, GSHSEB will announce the provisional answer key, allowing candidates to raise objections if any. The final answer key and GUJCET 2026 results are expected to be released in May 2026, after which the admission counseling process will commence for eligible candidates. Aspirants are advised to regularly check the official website for all updates and announcements related to GUJCET 2026.
