Tehran, Iran – The Islamic Republic of Iran has officially confirmed the death of Ali Larijani, its national security chief, in a recent announcement. Following the confirmation, President Masoud Pezeshkian extended his condolences, acknowledging Larijani's extensive service and significance within the country's political and security establishment.

Ali Larijani, a prominent figure in Iranian politics for decades, held the crucial position responsible for overseeing and coordinating Iran's national security policies. His role as national security chief, often associated with the Secretary of the Supreme National Security Council (SNSC), placed him at the nexus of domestic and international security challenges facing the nation. During his tenure, he played a key part in discussions related to regional stability, strategic defense, and diplomatic engagements.

President Pezeshkian's message of condolence, issued shortly after the confirmation of Larijani’s passing, highlighted the official's dedication and contributions to the Iranian state. The statement underscored Larijani's long career, which spanned various high-level governmental roles before his appointment as national security chief. Such expressions from the newly inaugurated president typically acknowledge the legacy and impact of senior officials on the country's trajectory.

Larijani's career included significant periods as a parliamentary speaker, a presidential candidate, and a top nuclear negotiator. These roles established him as a key voice in Iran's strategic decision-making processes. His death creates a vacancy in a critical government portfolio during a period of complex regional dynamics and ongoing international relations.

Key aspects of Ali Larijani's government service include:
*   **National Security Chief:** Headed the body responsible for formulating Iran's security and defense policies.
*   **Senior Negotiator:** Played a pivotal role in sensitive international discussions.
*   **Parliamentary Leadership:** Served multiple terms as Speaker of the Majlis (Parliament), influencing legislative processes.
*   **Strategic Advisor:** Often provided counsel on critical state affairs to the highest levels of Iranian leadership.

The confirmation of Ali Larijani's death and the subsequent condolences from President Pezeshkian initiate a period of transition within Iran's security apparatus. The government is expected to announce a successor to the national security chief position in due course. This appointment will be closely watched for indications of continuity or potential shifts in Iran's security and strategic policy direction under the new presidential administration.
