On May 24, 2024, Prime Minister Narendra Modi held a telephone conversation with Kuwait's Crown Prince, Sheikh Mishal Al-Ahmad Al-Jaber Al-Sabah. During the call, Prime Minister Modi reiterated India's consistent stand on fostering regional peace and stability, emphasizing the nation's commitment to a secure and prosperous wider neighborhood. The exchange served to review the extensive bilateral relationship between India and Kuwait and explore avenues for its further enhancement.

The discussion between the two leaders covered the multifaceted aspects of the special and privileged partnership shared by India and Kuwait. Both sides acknowledged the deep-rooted historical and cultural ties that underpin their diplomatic relations. The conversation primarily focused on strengthening cooperation across various sectors, aligning with both countries' strategic interests and developmental goals.

Key areas highlighted for enhanced collaboration included:
*   **Trade and Investment:** Exploring opportunities to boost bilateral trade volumes and facilitate mutual investments, particularly in emerging sectors.
*   **Energy Security:** Reaffirming the ongoing partnership in the energy sector, which is critical for India's energy needs and Kuwait's economic diversification.
*   **Technology and Innovation:** Identifying potential for collaboration in digital technologies, research, and development.
*   **People-to-People Contacts:** Acknowledging the significant role of the Indian diaspora in Kuwait and reiterating the importance of their welfare. Prime Minister Modi conveyed his appreciation for the care extended to the Indian community by the Kuwaiti government.

Prime Minister Modi's reaffirmation of India's position on regional peace aligns with New Delhi's "Neighbourhood First" policy and its broader commitment to stability in the Gulf region. India views a stable and secure Gulf as essential for its economic prosperity and regional security. The conversation underscored mutual interests in upholding a rules-based international order and promoting peaceful resolution of conflicts.

Both Prime Minister Modi and Crown Prince Sheikh Mishal expressed satisfaction with the current trajectory of bilateral relations. They affirmed their commitment to maintaining regular high-level contacts to further deepen the comprehensive partnership. The leaders conveyed anticipation for future opportunities to engage directly and continue advancing cooperative initiatives, indicating a sustained focus on strengthening diplomatic and economic ties between India and Kuwait.
