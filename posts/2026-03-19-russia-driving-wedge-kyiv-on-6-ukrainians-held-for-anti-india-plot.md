Six Ukrainian citizens have been detained in India, accused by local authorities of involvement in an "anti-India plot" that includes the creation of "anti-India content and maps," some reportedly depicting distorted Indian territory. In response, the Ukrainian government has vehemently denied these allegations, characterizing them as "Russian disinformation" and a "provocation" designed to undermine relations between India and Ukraine.

The Security Service of Ukraine (SBU) stated on May 30, 2024, that the claims against its citizens are part of Russia's "hybrid warfare" tactics. Kyiv has called for a "fair and impartial probe" into the detentions, expressing "deep concern" over the accusations. Ukraine's Ambassador to India, Oleksandr Polishchuk, underscored his government's position, asserting that Russia is attempting to "drive a wedge" between the two nations.

The allegations against the detained Ukrainians reportedly involve activities such as creating and disseminating online content deemed hostile to India and producing maps that misrepresent India's geographical boundaries. Indian intelligence agencies are understood to be investigating these claims, which emerged amidst ongoing geopolitical sensitivities.

Key details surrounding Ukraine's response include:
*   **Categorical Denial:** The SBU has explicitly denied any involvement of Ukrainian state bodies or its citizens in anti-India activities.
*   **Accusation of Russian Influence:** Kyiv maintains that the situation is a deliberate "provocation" orchestrated by Russia to sow discord and exploit India's neutral stance on the conflict in Ukraine.
*   **Call for Investigation:** Ukraine has formally requested that Indian authorities conduct a thorough, transparent, and unbiased investigation into the allegations and the circumstances of the detentions.
*   **Historical Context:** Ukrainian officials referenced a similar incident in 2022, where they alleged Russia attempted a "false flag operation" to strain India-Ukraine ties.

The diplomatic incident comes at a time when India has maintained a largely neutral position on the Russia-Ukraine conflict, balancing its strategic partnership with Russia with its growing ties with Western nations and its humanitarian support to Ukraine. Kyiv's strong reaction indicates a concern that these allegations could disrupt the delicate balance of its international relations, particularly with a key global player like India.

Ukrainian officials anticipate cooperation from Indian authorities to clarify the facts surrounding the detentions. The ongoing diplomatic engagement aims to resolve the situation and address the allegations of foreign interference in India's internal affairs while safeguarding the rights of the detained Ukrainian citizens. The outcome of the requested investigation will be crucial in determining the path forward for bilateral relations.
