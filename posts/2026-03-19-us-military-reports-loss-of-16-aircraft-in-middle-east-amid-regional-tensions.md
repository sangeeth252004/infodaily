The United States military has lost 16 aircraft, including 10 MQ-9 Reaper drones, in the broader Middle East region since the beginning of the Gaza conflict on October 7, 2023. This information comes from a recent report by *Military Times*, an independent news organization focused on the U.S. armed forces, as cited by the Times of India. The losses underscore the heightened operational tempo and complex security environment in a region marked by escalating tensions involving Iran and its proxies.

The *Military Times* report indicates that beyond the ten Reaper drones, six other manned and unmanned aircraft were also lost during the same period. These incidents have occurred as the US military has increased its presence and surveillance activities across the Middle East in response to a surge in attacks on US personnel and assets, primarily attributed to Iran-backed groups. The accumulated losses represent a significant challenge to ongoing US operations in the region.

Key details from the report include:
*   **Total Aircraft Lost:** 16
*   **Drone Losses:** 10 MQ-9 Reaper drones
*   **Other Aircraft:** 6 additional manned and unmanned aircraft
*   **Timeline:** Since October 7, 2023
*   **Location:** Broader Middle East region
*   **Attribution of Report:** *Military Times*

The MQ-9 Reaper drone, manufactured by General Atomics, is a crucial asset for intelligence, surveillance, reconnaissance, and strike operations. Each Reaper drone carries an estimated cost of approximately $30 million. The loss of multiple such sophisticated unmanned aerial vehicles not only represents a substantial financial burden but also impacts the operational capabilities of US forces, potentially affecting their ability to monitor threats and conduct missions effectively.

According to the *Military Times* analysis, some of these aircraft losses are attributed to operational accidents rather than direct enemy fire. However, the report also specifies instances where drones were shot down by hostile actors. For example, Houthi rebels in Yemen claimed responsibility for shooting down a US Reaper drone in November 2023, an incident acknowledged by US officials. Another Reaper drone was reportedly lost in November 2023, though the specific cause was not immediately disclosed.

The increase in operational incidents and losses reflects the intensified and often contested airspace over parts of the Middle East. US forces continue to operate in areas where they face threats from various non-state actors equipped with anti-aircraft capabilities. The ongoing review of these incidents aims to understand the full scope of the losses and to mitigate future risks in a volatile operational environment. The US military maintains a significant presence in the region, conducting counter-terrorism operations, deterring aggression, and protecting its interests and allies.
