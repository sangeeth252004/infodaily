The manifesto outlines the TMC's vision and commitments across various sectors, aiming to address the needs and aspirations of the state's populace over the next term. The "10 Protigya" are presented as foundational pillars for Bengal's continued progress.

Key pledges reportedly highlighted within the manifesto include:
*   **Social Welfare Expansion:** Initiatives aimed at enhancing existing social security nets and introducing new schemes to benefit vulnerable sections of society.
*   **Employment Generation:** Programs focused on creating job opportunities for youth, fostering entrepreneurship, and supporting small and medium enterprises.
*   **Healthcare Infrastructure:** Commitments to further develop public health facilities, improve access to medical services, and strengthen healthcare delivery systems across the state.
*   **Educational Advancement:** Plans to improve the quality of education at all levels, from primary schooling to higher education, and to promote skill development.
*   **Agricultural Development:** Measures designed to support farmers, enhance agricultural productivity, ensure fair prices for produce, and strengthen rural economies.
*   **Women's Empowerment:** Policies aimed at advancing gender equality, ensuring safety for women, and providing economic opportunities for women.
*   **Industrial Growth:** Strategies to attract investment, boost industrial output, and create a conducive environment for business and manufacturing.
*   **Urban and Rural Development:** Projects focused on improving infrastructure, sanitation, and housing in both urban and rural areas.
*   **Cultural Preservation:** Initiatives to protect and promote West Bengal's rich cultural heritage, arts, and traditions.
*   **Good Governance:** Commitments to ensure transparent administration, efficient public service delivery, and upholding democratic principles.
