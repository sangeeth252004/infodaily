The Common Offer Acceptance Portal (COAP), a centralized platform for postgraduate engineering admissions to the Indian Institutes of Technology (IITs) and the Indian Institute of Science (IISc), has officially released its schedule for the 2026 academic year. According to the announcement, Round 1 of the offer acceptance process is set to commence on May 11, 2026. This development marks a significant step for thousands of candidates who have qualified the Graduate Aptitude Test in Engineering (GATE) and are aspiring for M.Tech programs.

COAP was established to streamline the admission process for GATE-qualified candidates, providing a single portal to view and manage admission offers from various participating institutions. This system allows candidates to accept, retain, or reject offers, thereby preventing multiple seat blockages and ensuring a more efficient allocation of coveted M.Tech seats across premier institutions. The structured release of the schedule aims to provide clarity and adequate time for candidates to make informed decisions regarding their academic future.

The portal's operational framework involves multiple rounds of offer dissemination and candidate responses. Each round has a specific window during which candidates must log in, review their offers, and take one of three actions: "Accept and Freeze," "Retain and Wait," or "Reject." The "Accept and Freeze" option confirms a candidate's acceptance of an offer, removing them from subsequent rounds. "Retain and Wait" allows candidates to hold an offer while remaining eligible for better offers in subsequent rounds. "Reject" declines the current offer.

Key dates and processes outlined in the COAP 2026 schedule include:
*   **Round 1:** Begins May 11, 2026. This initial round will allow candidates to respond to their first set of offers from participating institutions.
*   Subsequent rounds will follow, typically spaced to provide opportunities for candidates to receive and respond to new offers as seats become available from previous rounds' rejections or retained offers.

Candidates are required to register on the COAP portal using their GATE registration number, GATE score, and other personal details. It is mandatory for all GATE-qualified aspirants seeking M.Tech admissions in participating IITs and IISc to register on COAP to view and respond to their admission offers. Failure to register or respond within the stipulated deadlines for each round can lead to the forfeiture of offers.

This centralized, multi-round approach is critical for maintaining transparency and fairness throughout the M.Tech admission process. It minimizes administrative complexities for both applicants and admissions committees while maximizing the probability of filling all available seats with suitable candidates. The system's design helps ensure that deserving candidates secure their preferred programs efficiently.

Prospective M.Tech students are strongly advised to regularly monitor the official COAP 2026 website for the comprehensive, detailed schedule of all rounds, specific deadlines, and any potential updates or announcements. Adherence to these published dates and guidelines is paramount for successful admission to M.Tech programs through GATE scores.
