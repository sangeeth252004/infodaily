The Central Bureau of Investigation (CBI) has recently questioned three former senior executives of Anil Ambani's Reliance Group as part of an ongoing investigation into an alleged fraud linked to Yes Bank. The questioning occurred in the days leading up to early May 2024 and marks a significant development in a case concerning alleged financial irregularities involving several high-profile individuals and entities.

The probe focuses on an alleged ₹200 crore transaction connected to a larger fraud case involving Yes Bank and Dewan Housing Finance Corporation Ltd (DHFL). The CBI had initially registered a First Information Report (FIR) in March 2020, accusing former Yes Bank Managing Director and CEO Rana Kapoor and DHFL promoter Kapil Wadhawan, among others, of corruption and criminal conspiracy. The agency alleges a 'quid pro quo' arrangement, wherein Kapoor purportedly received kickbacks from Wadhawan in exchange for sanctioning substantial loans.

The three former executives questioned by the CBI are K. Anbalagan, former Chief Executive Officer of Reliance Asset Reconstruction Company (RARC); Ravindra Kumar, former Chief Financial Officer of Reliance General Insurance; and Sanjay Rastogi, former Chief Executive Officer of Reliance Home Finance. Their questioning is part of the CBI's effort to gather further details regarding the financial transactions central to the case.

Key details surrounding the investigation include:
*   **FIR Registration:** The CBI's investigation began with an FIR filed in March 2020, alleging criminal conspiracy and corruption under the Prevention of Corruption Act.
*   **Core Allegation:** Rana Kapoor is accused of receiving alleged kickbacks totaling approximately ₹600 crore from Kapil Wadhawan for sanctioning loans worth around ₹3,700 crore to DHFL.
*   **Alleged Transaction:** A portion of these alleged kickbacks, specifically ₹200 crore, was reportedly routed through RARC as an investment. This transaction is under scrutiny by the investigative agency.
*   **Parallel Investigation:** The Enforcement Directorate (ED) is also conducting a parallel money laundering investigation into the same allegations, further expanding the scope of the probe.
*   **Company Stance:** Reliance Group has consistently maintained that all its transactions were legitimate business dealings conducted in accordance with applicable laws and regulations.

The CBI's investigation continues to unfold, with further questioning and examination of financial records anticipated. The outcome of this probe could have significant implications for the individuals and entities involved, as regulatory bodies continue to scrutinize corporate governance and financial practices within the Indian business landscape. The legal proceedings in this multi-agency investigation are ongoing, with details emerging as the case progresses through the justice system.
