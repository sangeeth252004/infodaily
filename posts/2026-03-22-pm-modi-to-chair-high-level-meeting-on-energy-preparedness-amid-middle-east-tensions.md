Prime Minister Narendra Modi is scheduled to chair a high-level meeting to review India's energy preparedness and security strategy, with a particular focus on the implications of ongoing geopolitical tensions in the Middle East. The meeting, set to take place in New Delhi, aims to assess the nation's resilience against potential disruptions to global energy supplies and price volatility.

The gathering underscores India's proactive approach to safeguarding its energy interests amidst a volatile international environment. The Middle East remains a critical region for global energy supplies, with any instability having the potential to impact crude oil and natural gas prices and availability worldwide. India, a major energy consumer, imports approximately 85% of its crude oil requirements and nearly 50% of its natural gas needs, making its economy susceptible to fluctuations in the international energy market.

Key officials from several ministries are expected to attend the review, including those from Petroleum and Natural Gas, External Affairs, Defence, and Finance. The agenda is anticipated to cover a comprehensive assessment of the current global energy landscape and its direct relevance to India's strategic energy objectives.

Discussions during the meeting are expected to center on several critical areas:
*   **Crude Oil and Natural Gas Supplies:** Evaluating the current status of India's crude oil and natural gas procurement, including import contracts and supply chain logistics.
*   **Strategic Petroleum Reserves:** Reviewing the adequacy and management of India's Strategic Petroleum Reserves, which serve as a buffer against supply shocks.
*   **Diversification of Energy Sources:** Exploring strategies for further diversifying India's energy import sources to reduce reliance on any single region or supplier.
*   **Logistics and Shipping Routes:** Assessing the security of maritime trade routes, particularly those passing through the Middle East, which are vital for India's energy imports.
*   **Contingency Planning:** Developing and refining contingency plans to address potential scenarios such as supply disruptions or significant price spikes.

The ongoing tensions in the Middle East, including regional conflicts and their impact on crucial shipping lanes, present a complex challenge for energy-importing nations. These geopolitical factors have the potential to disrupt global trade flows and elevate commodity prices, directly affecting national economies.

The high-level review reflects the Indian government's commitment to ensuring stable and affordable energy access for its citizens and industries. By convening top officials, the government aims to consolidate a unified strategy to navigate the current global uncertainties and bolster India's energy security framework. The outcomes of this meeting are expected to guide future policy decisions related to energy procurement, infrastructure development, and international energy diplomacy.
