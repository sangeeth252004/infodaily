A Sikh student pursuing a Bachelor of Science degree at Government College University in Rawalpindi, Pakistan, has reportedly been subjected to a violent assault by fellow students on campus. Mahinder Pal Singh, a resident of Nankana Sahib, reported that he was kicked, punched, and had his turban forcibly removed during the incident. The alleged assault has prompted strong reactions from Sikh organizations and human rights advocates.

The incident occurred recently on the university premises in Rawalpindi. According to reports from the student, he was attacked by other students who reportedly removed his turban and pulled his hair, an act considered deeply offensive and sacrilegious in the Sikh faith. Singh sustained injuries as a result of the reported physical assault. The specific date of the incident has not been publicly confirmed, but reports emerged in late October.

*   **Victim:** Mahinder Pal Singh, a student at Government College University, Rawalpindi.
*   **Location:** Government College University campus, Rawalpindi, Pakistan.
*   **Allegations:** Singh reported being kicked, punched, and having his turban forcibly removed by other students.
*   **Impact:** The incident has been widely condemned, raising concerns about the safety and religious freedom of minority communities in Pakistan.

The alleged assault against Mahinder Pal Singh has drawn swift condemnation internationally. The Delhi Sikh Gurdwara Management Committee (DSGMC) issued a statement condemning the act and urged immediate intervention from both the Pakistan government and the Indian Ministry of External Affairs. Harmeet Singh Kalka, president of the DSGMC, highlighted the significance of the turban in Sikhism, describing its removal as a severe desecration.

The National Commission for Minorities in India has also taken cognizance of the incident. Reports indicate the commission has sought a detailed report from the Ministry of External Affairs regarding the matter. This development underscores diplomatic and human rights concerns, as the incident is viewed by some as an attack on religious identity and minority rights.

The broader context of minority rights in Pakistan has been a subject of international discussion. Advocacy groups and human rights organizations have periodically raised concerns about the treatment of religious minorities within the country. While specific details regarding potential motives or the identities of the alleged attackers have not been fully disclosed by official Pakistani sources, the nature of the assault, particularly the removal of the turban, has led to interpretations of it as religiously motivated persecution.

As of the current reporting, details on any official investigation by Pakistani authorities or arrests made in connection with the alleged assault are pending. The incident continues to generate calls for justice and enhanced protection for minority communities in Pakistan.
