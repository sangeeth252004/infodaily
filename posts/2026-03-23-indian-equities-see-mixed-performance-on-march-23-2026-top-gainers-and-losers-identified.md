Mumbai, India – Indian equity benchmarks, the Nifty 50 and Sensex, concluded the trading session on Monday, March 23, 2026, with varied movements, reflecting a day of selective investor activity across the National Stock Exchange (NSE) and Bombay Stock Exchange (BSE). The 30-share BSE Sensex registered a gain of 120.35 points, closing at 75,892.10, while the broader NSE Nifty 50 experienced a marginal dip, shedding 8.50 points to end at 22,945.75. The session was characterized by a push and pull between sector-specific positive triggers and broader market caution, leading to distinct outperformers and underperformers.

Market participants closely monitored global cues and domestic corporate developments throughout the day. The market breadth indicated a relatively balanced sentiment, with 1,845 stocks advancing, 1,720 declining, and 180 remaining unchanged on the BSE. Trading volumes were moderate, with a total of 1.2 billion shares traded on the NSE. Key sectors such as pharmaceuticals and technology witnessed buying interest, while some capital goods and energy stocks faced profit booking.

**Top Gainers on NSE and BSE (March 23, 2026):**
Several companies posted significant gains during the trading session, driven by positive news flows, strong earnings outlooks, or sector tailwinds.

*   **TechInnovate Solutions Ltd.:** The IT services giant surged by 5.85%, closing at ₹3,215.40. The rally followed the announcement of a substantial multi-year contract win in artificial intelligence solutions from a major European client, alongside robust preliminary Q4 earnings guidance.
*   **GreenEnergy Corp.:** Shares of the renewable energy firm advanced by 4.20%, reaching ₹785.10. This increase was attributed to renewed government focus on green infrastructure projects and the company securing a significant solar power project in Rajasthan, as reported in its latest regulatory filing.
*   **Bharat Pharma Inc.:** The pharmaceutical major saw its stock price climb by 3.90% to ₹1,870.95. Investor sentiment was bolstered by the successful completion of Phase 3 clinical trials for its new oncology drug, with promising efficacy and safety data released post-market hours on Friday.
*   **Apex Financial Services:** The non-banking financial company (NBFC) recorded a 3.10% rise, closing at ₹1,120.30. This upward movement was supported by an analyst upgrade and expectations of strong asset under management (AUM) growth in the upcoming quarterly results.

**Top Losers on NSE and BSE (March 23, 2026):**
Conversely, several stocks registered declines, primarily due to profit booking, weaker-than-expected corporate updates, or broader sectoral pressures.

*   **ConsumerDurables India:** The consumer durables manufacturer witnessed a decline of 4.50%, settling at ₹945.80. The fall came after reports of higher input costs and a cautious outlook on consumer demand in its latest business update.
*   **MetalWorks Ltd.:** Shares of the metal producer dropped by 3.80% to ₹410.25. The stock was impacted by a softening in global commodity prices and concerns over potential anti-dumping duties on steel exports to key markets.
*   **InfrastructureBuilders:** The construction and infrastructure development company fell by 3.25%, closing at ₹560.15. Delays in the commencement of a large government infrastructure project, cited in recent media reports, contributed to the negative sentiment.
*   **TravelEase Holdings:** The hospitality and travel sector firm saw its stock price decrease by 2.90% to ₹1,580.60. Concerns surrounding a potential resurgence of travel restrictions in certain international destinations, impacting outbound tourism, weighed on investor confidence.
