Tehran, Iran – Iranian officials have firmly rejected claims by former U.S. President Donald Trump regarding direct negotiations between the two nations, asserting that such claims are baseless and intended to "buy time" and mislead the public. The denials come after Trump reportedly suggested that Iran was eager to engage in direct talks, intensifying the long-standing diplomatic impasse between Washington and Tehran.

The statements from Tehran underscore the deep distrust and persistent friction characterizing U.S.-Iran relations, particularly since the U.S. withdrawal from the Joint Comprehensive Plan of Action (JCPOA), commonly known as the Iran nuclear deal, in 2018 under the Trump administration. Iranian spokespersons reiterated their consistent position that direct engagement with the current U.S. political establishment is not on their agenda, citing a lack of sincerity from Washington.

Key points from Iran's response include:
*   **Categorical Denial of Direct Talks:** Iranian officials explicitly stated that no direct negotiations have occurred between Tehran and the U.S. regarding a nuclear agreement or broader bilateral issues.
*   **Accusation of 'Buying Time':** The primary motive attributed to Trump's claims by Iranian authorities is an attempt to "buy time" or create a false narrative for political gain, particularly given the ongoing U.S. political landscape.
*   **Misleading the Public:** Iran contended that Trump's assertions are designed to mislead both the American public and the international community regarding the true state of U.S.-Iran diplomatic channels.
*   **Focus on U.S. Policy Reversal:** Tehran maintained that any meaningful progress would require a fundamental shift in U.S. policy, including a return to the JCPOA and the lifting of sanctions imposed by the Trump administration.

The re-emergence of such claims and subsequent denials highlights the complexities surrounding efforts to revive the JCPOA, which has seen limited progress through indirect negotiations involving European intermediaries. Iran has consistently demanded that the U.S. first honor its commitments under the original deal before any new direct talks could be considered. The U.S., on the other hand, has sought assurances from Iran regarding its nuclear program and regional activities.

Impact of these statements includes:
*   **Reinforced Stalemate:** The exchange further solidifies the current diplomatic stalemate, indicating little immediate prospect for direct bilateral discussions.
*   **Heightened Mistrust:** It deepens the existing mistrust between the two nations, complicating future attempts at de-escalation or conflict resolution.
*   **JCPOA Hurdles:** The public dispute adds another layer of complexity to the already challenging process of potentially restoring the nuclear agreement, emphasizing the wide chasm in expectations and demands.

Looking ahead, analysts suggest that without significant shifts in policy or leadership from either side, U.S.-Iran relations are likely to remain strained. The current public disagreement serves as a reminder of the enduring obstacles to any diplomatic breakthrough, with both nations maintaining firm positions that leave little room for immediate direct engagement. Indirect channels, primarily through European facilitators, are expected to remain the principal avenue for any future dialogue concerning the nuclear program and regional stability.
