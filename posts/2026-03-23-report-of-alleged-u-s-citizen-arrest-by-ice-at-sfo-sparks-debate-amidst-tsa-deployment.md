The initiative was linked to efforts by the former Trump administration to address airport operational challenges.

The alleged arrest at SFO drew immediate attention and scrutiny, raising questions about the scope of ICE's authority and its operational practices when deployed in roles typically handled by other federal agencies. The individual detained reportedly asserted their U.S. citizenship at the time of the arrest, prompting concerns among civil liberties advocates and some lawmakers regarding due process and the potential for overreach by immigration enforcement agencies.

During the Trump administration, specifically in early 2019 amidst a partial government shutdown, federal agencies faced staffing challenges. To mitigate the impact on airport security and operations, the administration reportedly explored and implemented measures, including the deployment of non-TSA federal personnel to assist at checkpoints. ICE agents, whose primary mission is immigration enforcement, were among those reported to be assigned to support airport functions. This deployment was framed as a temporary measure to address a "crisis" in TSA staffing, ensuring continued security checks and passenger flow during the period of reduced federal funding.
