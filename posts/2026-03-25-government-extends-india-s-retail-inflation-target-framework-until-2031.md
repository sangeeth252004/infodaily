The Government of India has extended the existing retail inflation target framework for the Reserve Bank of India (RBI) until March 31, 2031. This decision maintains the current target of 4% for the Consumer Price Index (CPI) based inflation, with a symmetrical tolerance band of +/- 2 percentage points, meaning the operational range for inflation will continue to be between 2% and 6%. The announcement underscores the government's commitment to price stability and policy continuity in managing India's economic landscape.

The inflation targeting framework was initially introduced in 2016 through an amendment to the Reserve Bank of India Act, 1934. It established a five-year mandate, setting the target from August 5, 2016, to March 31, 2021. Upon its expiry, the government reviewed the framework and extended it for another five years, from April 1, 2021, to March 31, 2026. The latest extension will now ensure this framework remains operational for an additional five years, covering the period from April 1, 2026, to March 31, 2031.

Under this framework, the RBI's primary objective is to maintain price stability while keeping in mind the objective of growth. The Monetary Policy Committee (MPC), a six-member body headed by the RBI Governor, is entrusted with the responsibility of setting the policy interest rate (repo rate) to achieve the inflation target. The committee convenes at least four times a year to assess economic conditions and make policy decisions.

Key operational aspects of the framework include:
*   **Target Mid-Point:** 4% retail inflation (CPI).
*   **Tolerance Band:** +/- 2 percentage points, setting the effective range at 2% to 6%.
*   **Accountability Mechanism:** If the average inflation remains outside the 2% to 6% band for three consecutive quarters, the RBI is required to provide an explanation report to the government. This report must detail the reasons for the failure, the remedial actions proposed, and an estimated timeframe within which inflation is expected to return to the tolerance level.

The continuity provided by this extension is expected to enhance predictability for businesses, investors, and consumers, fostering an environment conducive to long-term investment and economic planning. By explicitly defining the central bank's mandate and the government's expectations, the framework aims to anchor inflation expectations, which is crucial for macroeconomic stability. It also reinforces the institutional independence of the RBI in implementing monetary policy, while ensuring a clear accountability structure.

The extension signals a sustained focus on a robust monetary policy regime despite evolving global and domestic economic challenges. This consistent approach is intended to mitigate volatility and support sustainable economic growth by ensuring that inflation remains within manageable levels, preventing erosion of purchasing power and maintaining financial sector stability. The government's decision reaffirms its long-term commitment to a rule-based monetary policy approach.

The framework will continue to be subject to periodic review and assessment by both the government and the RBI to ensure its effectiveness in changing economic circumstances.
