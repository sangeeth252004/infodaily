Veteran Congress leader Sonia Gandhi was admitted to Sir Ganga Ram Hospital in New Delhi on Wednesday, December 26, 2023, due to a mild fever and a chest infection. Medical professionals at the hospital have confirmed that her condition is stable, and she is currently undergoing treatment.

According to Dr. Ajay Chauhan, Chairman (Medicine) at Sir Ganga Ram Hospital, Ms. Gandhi was admitted for observation and treatment related to a respiratory issue and fever. Her health is being closely monitored by a team of specialists. This hospitalization follows a period of routine health check-ups for the prominent political figure.

Sonia Gandhi, 77, holds significant standing in Indian politics. She served as the longest-serving president of the Indian National Congress party, leading it for over two decades from 1998 to 2017. Currently, she is the Chairperson of the Congress Parliamentary Party (CPP) and a Member of Parliament representing Rae Bareli constituency in Uttar Pradesh. Her health updates often garner attention given her central role in the opposition party's structure and history.

Key details regarding her current medical status and political context include:
*   **Admission Date:** Wednesday, December 26, 2023.
*   **Hospital:** Sir Ganga Ram Hospital, New Delhi.
*   **Reason for Admission:** Mild fever and chest infection.
*   **Medical Status:** Condition reported as stable by attending doctors.
*   **Family Presence:** Her children, Rahul Gandhi and Priyanka Gandhi Vadra, were reportedly present at the hospital following her admission.
*   **Political Role:** Continues to be an influential figure as Chairperson of the Congress Parliamentary Party.

The news of her hospitalization prompted well-wishes from across the political spectrum, underscoring her stature. Political leaders from various parties expressed hopes for her swift recovery. The Congress party officially acknowledged her admission, reiterating the doctors' assessment of her stable health.

Ms. Gandhi has faced health challenges previously and has been admitted to the hospital for routine check-ups and treatment on several occasions in recent years. Her public appearances have become less frequent, with her involvement in party affairs often managed from a less visible role. The current admission is being managed with standard medical protocols for such conditions.

Hospital authorities have indicated that she will remain under observation for a few days as her treatment progresses. Further updates regarding her discharge will be provided by the medical team as her condition warrants. The focus remains on her recovery and ensuring stable health before her return home.
