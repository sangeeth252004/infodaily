The Supreme Court of India has issued a strong rebuke to the Haryana Police for their "insensitive" approach in handling a child rape case, specifically criticizing the summoning of a three-year-old victim. During a recent hearing, the apex court labeled the police action as "shameful," underscoring significant lapses in adherence to established protocols for investigations involving child victims.

A Supreme Court bench expressed dismay over the police department's conduct, highlighting the profound insensitivity displayed towards a highly vulnerable individual. The court's remarks emphasized the critical need for law enforcement agencies to adopt victim-centric and trauma-informed approaches, particularly when dealing with minors in sensitive cases. The incident has brought into sharp focus the procedural deficiencies and lack of specialized training evident in the handling of such investigations.

The criticism stems from the Haryana Police issuing a summons to the three-year-old child in connection with the ongoing rape investigation. Such actions are in direct contravention of guidelines and legal precedents designed to protect child victims from further trauma and ensure their well-being throughout the judicial process. Legal experts indicate that established procedures typically involve conducting interviews in child-friendly environments, often through trained professionals, and with minimal direct interaction with the child victim unless absolutely necessary and conducted with extreme care.

Key issues highlighted by the Supreme Court's observations include:
*   **Violation of Child Protection Norms:** Summoning a three-year-old is seen as a clear disregard for legal provisions aimed at protecting child victims from secondary victimization.
*   **Lack of Sensitivity:** The court noted a stark absence of sensitivity and understanding of child psychology and trauma during the investigative process.
*   **Procedural Lapses:** The incident points to a failure in implementing proper investigative protocols specifically designed for cases involving young children.
*   **Need for Specialized Training:** The court's remarks implicitly call for enhanced training for police personnel on handling child abuse cases, focusing on victim care and legal compliance.

The Supreme Court's intervention underscores its role in ensuring that justice is not only served but also administered with empathy and adherence to human rights, especially for the most vulnerable sections of society. The court's stringent remarks are expected to prompt a review of investigative practices within the Haryana Police force and potentially other state police departments across India, particularly concerning child-related offenses.

Moving forward, the Supreme Court's directives are anticipated to mandate corrective measures, potentially including stricter guidelines for police investigations involving minors, compulsory specialized training modules for investigating officers, and a renewed emphasis on psychological support for child victims. This judicial oversight aims to prevent similar insensitive actions and ensure that child victims are treated with the dignity, sensitivity, and protection mandated by law.
