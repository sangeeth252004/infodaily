The Indian Navy has commenced "Op Urja Suraksha," deploying a contingent of five warships to guide cargo vessels transiting the Strait of Hormuz. This strategic deployment aims to enhance the safety and security of commercial shipping in the critical waterway, which has experienced heightened regional tensions and incidents affecting maritime trade.

The Strait of Hormuz, connecting the Persian Gulf to the Arabian Sea, represents a vital artery for global energy markets. Approximately one-fifth of the world's total petroleum consumption, alongside a significant portion of liquefied natural gas (LNG) shipments, passes through this narrow maritime chokepoint daily. Its strategic importance underscores the necessity for uninterrupted and secure passage for international trade, particularly for energy-importing nations.

The decision to launch "Op Urja Suraksha" follows a period of increased security concerns in the region. Various incidents impacting commercial vessels, including attacks and seizures, have prompted navies operating in the area to reinforce protective measures. The Indian Navy's deployment provides a visible security presence intended to deter potential threats and ensure the freedom of navigation for merchant shipping.

Key details of the operation include:
*   **Naval Assets:** Five Indian Navy warships are actively involved in the escort and surveillance mission.
*   **Mission Scope:** The operation focuses on guiding and protecting cargo vessels, including oil tankers and other commercial ships, as they navigate the Strait of Hormuz.
*   **Strategic Objective:** To safeguard India's maritime interests, ensure the uninterrupted flow of trade, and contribute to overall maritime stability in the region.

India, as a major consumer and importer of crude oil and natural gas, relies heavily on secure transit through the Strait of Hormuz for its energy supplies. A substantial percentage of the nation's energy imports originate from the Persian Gulf states and must traverse this waterway. Therefore, ensuring the safety of these passages directly contributes to India's energy security and economic stability.

"Op Urja Suraksha" reflects the Indian Navy's ongoing commitment to maritime security in international waters and its broader role in upholding maritime law and order. The naval assets involved are tasked with maintaining continuous surveillance, providing real-time intelligence, and responding promptly to any developing situations that could endanger commercial shipping. The duration of this specific operation will be determined by the evolving geopolitical and security landscape in the Gulf region.
