An avalanche recently struck the Zoji La mountain pass in Ladakh, resulting in the deaths of seven individuals and injuries to five others. The incident has led to the complete closure of the crucial Srinagar-Leh National Highway, a primary land route connecting the Kashmir Valley with Ladakh. Multi-agency rescue and recovery operations were immediately initiated by personnel from the Border Roads Organisation (BRO), the Indian Army, and local police.

The avalanche occurred during snow clearance operations, trapping a team working to open the strategic pass. Initial reports indicate that the deceased and injured were part of this team, which was engaged in removing accumulated snow from the highway. The Zoji La pass, situated at an altitude of approximately 11,575 feet (3,528 meters), is known for its challenging terrain, severe weather conditions, and high susceptibility to avalanches, particularly during periods of heavy snowfall or subsequent thaw. The loss of life highlights the inherent risks faced by personnel involved in maintaining connectivity in such extreme mountainous environments.

The closure of the Srinagar-Leh Highway has significantly disrupted traffic and the flow of essential supplies to Ladakh from the Kashmir Valley. This highway serves as a vital artery for both civilian travel and strategic military logistics. Efforts to reopen the pass are being complicated by the scale of the snow and debris from the avalanche, as well as ongoing unstable weather in the region. Authorities have issued advisories cautioning against travel to the area and urging vigilance for those already present in the high-altitude zones.

*   **Casualties:** Seven fatalities have been confirmed, with five individuals sustaining injuries requiring medical attention.
*   **Location:** The incident occurred at Zoji La pass, a critical point on the highway roughly 100 kilometers east of Srinagar, marking the gateway to Ladakh.
*   **Affected Personnel:** Victims were reportedly part of a snow clearance team, believed to be associated with the Border Roads Organisation (BRO), which manages the maintenance and opening of the highway.
*   **Highway Status:** The Srinagar-Leh National Highway (NH-1) remains entirely blocked to all forms of traffic.
*   **Rescue Operations:** Joint teams from BRO, the Indian Army, and local police and disaster response units are actively engaged in search, rescue, and recovery efforts.
*   **Operational Challenges:** The rugged mountainous terrain, fresh snowfall, and the persistent risk of further avalanches are posing significant hurdles for the ongoing operations.

Recovery efforts are continuing around the clock, with rescue teams utilizing specialized equipment to locate any remaining individuals and to clear the substantial volume of snow and debris. Authorities have stated that the highway will remain closed until a thorough assessment deems it safe for passage. This process is expected to take several days, contingent on improved weather conditions and the progress of clearing operations. Further updates and advisories regarding the reopening of the pass will be issued by official channels as circumstances evolve.
