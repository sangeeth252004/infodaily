The Directorate General of Civil Aviation (DGCA), India's primary aviation regulatory body, has recently issued a revised set of safety guidelines specifically targeting non-scheduled flights carrying Very Very Important Persons (VVIPs). This regulatory update comes following an incident, described as a crash, involving a non-scheduled flight carrying senior politician Ajit Pawar. The new directives, introduced in late 2022, aim to enhance operational safety, bolster oversight mechanisms, and mitigate potential risks associated with such high-profile air travel.

The move by the DGCA underscores a proactive approach to aviation safety, particularly concerning flights that operate outside the purview of scheduled commercial airline services but carry critical passengers. Non-scheduled flights, which include private jets and chartered aircraft, often face unique operational challenges and require stringent adherence to safety protocols to ensure the well-being of passengers and crew. The incident involving Mr. Pawar's flight prompted a comprehensive review of existing regulations and led to the formulation of these updated mandates, designed to prevent future occurrences and elevate safety standards across the board for VVIP non-scheduled operations.

The revised guidelines focus on several critical areas of flight operations, from pre-flight preparations to in-flight procedures and post-flight reporting. Operators of non-scheduled flights carrying VVIPs are now subject to more rigorous checks and balances, ensuring that every aspect of the flight is conducted with the highest degree of safety and compliance. The DGCA's intent is to create a robust framework that accounts for the specific demands and risks associated with VVIP movements, which often involve complex logistics and dynamic operational environments.

Key aspects of the new DGCA regulations include:

*   **Mandatory Pre-Flight Briefings:** Comprehensive briefings for all flight and cabin crew are now mandatory, covering route specifics, weather conditions, emergency procedures, and passenger-specific requirements.
*   **Enhanced Maintenance Oversight:** Operators must ensure stricter adherence to aircraft maintenance schedules and airworthiness directives, with increased documentation and regular audits.
*   **Flight Duty Time Limitations (FDTL):** More stringent enforcement of FDTL for pilots and cabin crew to prevent fatigue-related errors, with detailed record-keeping requirements.
*   **Crew Qualification and Training:** Emphasized requirements for pilot experience, recurrent training, and specialized instruction for operating VVIP flights, including emergency scenario drills.
*   **Detailed Flight Planning:** Submission of comprehensive flight plans and risk assessments to the DGCA prior to VVIP non-scheduled operations, including contingency plans for diversions or emergencies.
*   **Incident Reporting:** Strengthened protocols for reporting minor incidents, deviations, or technical snags during VVIP flights to facilitate continuous safety improvements and data analysis.

These new regulations are effective immediately and require all operators of non-scheduled VVIP flights to demonstrate full compliance. The DGCA has indicated that it will conduct regular audits and inspections to ensure the diligent implementation of these revised safety standards. This regulatory update represents a significant step towards reinforcing aviation safety in India's non-scheduled sector, particularly for high-stakes VVIP operations, aiming to prevent future incidents and maintain public confidence in air travel safety.
