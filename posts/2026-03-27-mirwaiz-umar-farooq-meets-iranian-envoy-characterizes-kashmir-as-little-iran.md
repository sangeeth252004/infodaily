During the discussions, Mirwaiz Umar Farooq is said to have characterized Kashmir as "little Iran," a statement that draws attention to the region's deep cultural and historical ties with Iran.

The meeting took place at the Mirwaiz’s residence and focused on a range of topics, including the shared heritage, cultural exchanges, and religious connections between Kashmir and Iran. According to reports, Ambassador Chegeni expressed Iran's keen interest in further strengthening cultural cooperation and trade relations with the region. The reference to Kashmir as "little Iran" by Mirwaiz Umar Farooq is widely understood to underscore these profound historical and cultural affinities, which include significant Persian influence on Kashmiri language, art, and Sufi traditions, alongside a notable Shia Muslim population in parts of the valley.

Mirwaiz Umar Farooq, who also serves as the chairman of the Hurriyat Conference, has historically been a key voice advocating for a resolution to the Kashmir issue. His engagement with a foreign diplomat, particularly from a nation with significant regional influence like Iran, holds geopolitical importance. India maintains diplomatic relations with Iran, and Kashmir is considered an integral part of Indian territory by the Indian government. Such interactions by non-state actors in politically sensitive regions are closely observed within diplomatic circles.

The discussions reportedly covered the historical ties that have shaped the cultural landscape of Kashmir, emphasizing centuries of shared intellectual and spiritual heritage.
