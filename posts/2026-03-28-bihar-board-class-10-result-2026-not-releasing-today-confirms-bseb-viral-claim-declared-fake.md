Patna, Bihar – The Bihar School Examination Board (BSEB) has officially confirmed that circulating online claims regarding the immediate release of Bihar Board Class 10 (Matric) results for the 2026 academic year are false. The Board issued a statement clarifying that the results are not being declared today, advising students and parents to disregard any unofficial announcements.

Reports of the 2026 Class 10 results being published "today" had gained traction across various social media platforms and unofficial messaging groups. These claims often cited unverified sources, causing confusion and anxiety among students awaiting future academic updates. The BSEB's swift denial aims to curb the spread of this misinformation and ensure that accurate information prevails.

BSEB officials emphasized that all official announcements related to examination results, admit cards, or any other significant academic updates are exclusively released through designated official channels. The Board highlighted the importance of verifying information solely through its official website and authenticated press releases to prevent students from falling victim to false narratives.

Key official channels for Bihar Board updates include:
*   **Official Website:** biharboardonline.bihar.gov.in
*   **Official Social Media Handles:** Verified accounts of BSEB on platforms like Twitter and Facebook.
*   **Reputable Media Outlets:** News published following official press conferences or statements from the Board.

The dissemination of premature or fabricated result dates is a recurring issue, often leading to unnecessary stress and confusion among the student community. The Board reiterated its commitment to transparency and timely communication, urging stakeholders to exercise caution when encountering information from unverified sources.

Students, educators, and parents are strongly advised to bookmark the official BSEB website, biharboardonline.bihar.gov.in, and regularly check it for authenticated news and updates concerning the Class 10 examinations and results for future academic years, including 2026. The Board will issue a formal notification well in advance of any actual result declaration, detailing the specific date, time, and procedure for accessing scorecards. The current focus remains on scheduled academic processes, with official result announcements to follow in due course according to established timelines.
