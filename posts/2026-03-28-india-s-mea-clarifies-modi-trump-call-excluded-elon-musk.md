New Delhi, India – India’s Ministry of External Affairs (MEA) has officially clarified that a past phone conversation between Prime Minister Narendra Modi and then-US President Donald Trump was a bilateral exchange, refuting recent claims suggesting the involvement of entrepreneur Elon Musk. The MEA’s statement underscores the precise nature of high-level diplomatic engagements and seeks to ensure accuracy in reports concerning international relations.

The clarification by the MEA addresses comments attributed to former US President Donald Trump, who, in a recent interview, reportedly mentioned Elon Musk being on a call with Prime Minister Modi. The MEA spokesperson, upon inquiry, affirmed that the phone call in question, which occurred during Trump's presidency, involved only Prime Minister Modi and President Trump. This direct refutation by the MEA emphasizes the importance of factual reporting regarding communications between heads of state.

The official response from the Indian government highlights several key aspects:
*   **Diplomatic Protocol:** Bilateral calls between national leaders adhere to strict diplomatic protocols, typically involving only the designated representatives and their immediate staff.
*   **Accuracy of Information:** The MEA's intervention serves to correct public discourse and maintain an accurate record of official interactions, especially concerning such prominent figures.
*   **Context of the Claim:** The original claim, reportedly made by Donald Trump, suggested a broader participation in a sensitive diplomatic conversation, which the MEA has now unequivocally denied.

The Ministry did not specify the exact date of the phone call, but it was confirmed to have taken place during the tenure of Donald Trump as the 45th President of the United States. Such direct communications are standard practice for leaders to discuss bilateral issues, regional developments, and global challenges. The inclusion of a third-party private citizen like Elon Musk, known for his ventures in technology and space, would be an unusual deviation from established diplomatic norms.

The MEA's swift and clear denial is consistent with its role in managing India's foreign policy and public diplomacy. It ensures that statements concerning India's international engagements are factually correct and aligned with official records. This episode underscores the ongoing global attention on high-profile political figures and the necessity for official channels to verify or refute unsubstantiated claims.

No further statements regarding this specific clarification are expected from the MEA, as the official position has been clearly articulated. The focus remains on accurate representation of diplomatic interactions and the maintenance of established communication protocols between nations.
