Bengaluru, India – Star batsman Virat Kohli etched his name deeper into the annals of cricket history on Friday, April 18, 2026, by becoming the first player to cross the monumental 10,000-run mark in the Indian Premier League (IPL). Playing for Royal Challengers Bangalore (RCB) against the Chennai Super Kings (CSK) at the M. Chinnaswamy Stadium, Kohli achieved the unprecedented milestone with a single tucked to square leg in the 12th over of RCB's innings.

The achievement underscores Kohli's enduring dominance and consistency in the world's premier T20 league. Having been an integral part of RCB since the inaugural season in 2008, his journey to 10,000 runs spans nearly two decades of top-tier T20 cricket. The milestone was met with a thunderous ovation from the home crowd and widespread recognition across the cricketing fraternity, highlighting the magnitude of his statistical feat.

This latest record reinforces Kohli's status as the IPL's all-time leading run-scorer, extending a record he has held for several seasons. His consistent ability to perform under pressure and adapt to evolving T20 strategies has been a hallmark of his career. The 2026 season marks Kohli's 19th year in the league, a testament to his fitness and continued passion for the sport.

Key details of Virat Kohli's IPL career leading to this milestone include:

*   **Total Runs:** 10,000+
*   **Matches Played:** Over 250
*   **Seasons Played:** 19 (2008-2026)
*   **Centuries Scored:** 8 (at the time of the milestone)
*   **Half-Centuries:** Over 70
*   **Strike Rate:** Consistently maintained above 130 throughout his career.
*   **Teams Represented:** Royal Challengers Bangalore (since 2008).

The 10,000-run mark positions Kohli in an elite category within T20 cricket globally, where such longevity and scoring prowess are rare. While other players like Shikhar Dhawan and Rohit Sharma have also accumulated significant run totals, Kohli's consistent output has kept him ahead of his contemporaries. His impact extends beyond individual statistics, often anchoring the RCB batting lineup and influencing game outcomes.

Looking ahead, Kohli's pursuit of further records continues. As he progresses through the 2026 season and potentially future editions, attention will shift to his continued performance and any additional milestones he might achieve. The 10,000-run barrier sets a new benchmark for batting excellence in the IPL, a testament to a career defined by dedication and a relentless pursuit of runs.
