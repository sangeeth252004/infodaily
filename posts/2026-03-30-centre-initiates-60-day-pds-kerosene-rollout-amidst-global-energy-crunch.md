New Delhi – The Indian government, referred to as the Centre, has announced a significant, temporary measure to counter rising domestic fuel costs, initiating a 60-day rollout of subsidised kerosene through its Public Distribution System (PDS). This proactive step aims to alleviate increasing financial pressure on household budgets due to surging Liquefied Petroleum Gas (LPG) prices and potential supply constraints. LPG is a primary cooking fuel for millions across the nation, making its affordability critical. The move comes amidst a wider "energy crunch" characterized by volatile global commodity markets, with factors including geopolitical events affecting critical transit routes like the Strait of Hormuz influencing prices. This temporary relief measure, effective immediately, seeks to provide immediate financial respite and ensure energy access for millions of Indian households.

The Centre's decision underscores its proactive response to the complexities of volatile international energy markets. Global crude oil and natural gas prices have experienced significant and unpredictable fluctuations in recent months, often exacerbated by geopolitical tensions, supply chain bottlenecks, and increased demand. The Strait of Hormuz, a critical chokepoint for approximately one-fifth of the world's total petroleum consumption, has been specifically cited as a key factor contributing to market uncertainty and price volatility. Given that India imports a substantial portion of its energy requirements, including a significant percentage of its LPG, these global dynamics directly impact domestic fuel costs. The elevated cost of imported LPG translates directly into increased expenses for Indian consumers, who have been grappling with sustained price hikes for cooking gas cylinders.

Kerosene, historically a widely used cooking and lighting fuel in India, particularly prevalent in rural and semi-urban areas lacking piped gas infrastructure, is being strategically reintroduced as a temporary relief measure. For years, government policy has aimed to reduce kerosene consumption, promoting cleaner fuels like LPG through schemes such as the Pradhan Mantri Ujjwala Yojana (PMUY) to improve health outcomes and environmental quality. However, the current energy crisis necessitates a tactical reversal. The robust PDS network, originally established to distribute essential commodities at subsidised rates, is well-equipped to facilitate the widespread reach of kerosene to eligible beneficiaries across the country. This provision aims to offer a more affordable and readily available energy source, enabling households to manage their cooking fuel needs effectively without solely relying on the comparatively more expensive LPG cylinders.

Key aspects of the 60-day relief push, targeting immediate consumer needs, include:
*   **Duration:** The emergency allocation is strictly set for a period of 60 days, underscoring its nature as a short-term intervention to address immediate price shocks and supply concerns.
*   **Distribution Network:** Kerosene will be efficiently distributed through the existing and well-established national network of fair price shops under the Public Distribution System, ensuring broad accessibility even in remote areas.
*   **Beneficiaries:** The scheme specifically targets eligible households holding PDS ration cards. These beneficiaries now have the option to procure subsidised kerosene as an alternative or supplementary cooking fuel, easing their financial burden.
*   **Allocation:** Specific quantities per household will be determined by individual state governments, taking into account local needs, existing kerosene consumption patterns, and overall availability, adhering to standard PDS operational guidelines.
*   **Government Objective:** Beyond immediate relief, the initiative seeks to provide a critical buffer against the direct and sudden impact of international price volatility on household budgets, thereby maintaining essential energy access for cooking purposes and preventing a potential revert to less clean, traditional cooking methods like biomass.

The Centre has affirmed its commitment to closely monitor both evolving international energy price trends and the domestic uptake and overall impact of this kerosene rollout throughout the designated 60-day period. Government officials have indicated that the situation will be thoroughly reviewed thereafter to assess the necessity of extending the measure, modifying its scope, or implementing alternative, more sustainable strategies. This temporary intervention underscores India's dynamic approach to managing its complex energy security landscape and ensuring affordability for its vast consumer base amidst a highly volatile and interconnected global energy market. It also highlights the government's responsiveness to immediate public needs while navigating broader energy transition goals.
