NEW DELHI – The Indian government, through the Ministry of Finance, has announced the interest rates applicable to various Post Office small savings schemes for the first quarter of the fiscal year 2026-27, spanning from April 1, 2026, to June 30, 2026. The announcement indicates that rates for several popular schemes, including the Public Provident Fund (PPF), National Savings Certificate (NSC), and Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana (SSY), will remain unchanged for this period.

These small savings schemes serve as crucial investment avenues for millions of citizens, particularly those seeking stable, government-backed returns. The rates are reviewed and adjusted by the government on a quarterly basis, typically linked to the yields of government securities (G-secs) of comparable maturities. The decision to maintain rates provides continuity for existing and prospective investors in these instruments.

The schemes covered by this announcement are integral to the nation's savings landscape, offering a range of options for different financial goals, from long-term retirement planning to specific savings for children's education or senior citizen welfare. The consistent interest rates aim to provide predictability for investors relying on these programs.

Key interest rates for the April-June 2026 quarter are as follows:

*   **Public Provident Fund (PPF):** The interest rate remains at 7.1% per annum. PPF has a lock-in period of 15 years, offering tax benefits under Section 80C of the Income Tax Act.
*   **National Savings Certificate (NSC):** The interest rate is maintained at 7.7% per annum, compounded annually but payable at maturity. The NSC has a maturity period of five years.
*   **Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana (SSY):** The scheme for girl children continues to offer an interest rate of 8.2% per annum. This scheme allows deposits for up to 15 years from the date of account opening.
*   **Senior Citizen Savings Scheme (SCSS):** Senior citizens will continue to earn 8.2% per annum on their deposits, payable quarterly. The maximum investment limit for SCSS is currently Rs 30 lakh.
*   **Kisan Vikas Patra (KVP):** The interest rate stands at 7.5% per annum, which will see the invested amount double in 115 months (9 years and 7 months).
*   **Monthly Income Account Scheme (MIS):** This scheme will continue to provide an interest rate of 7.4% per annum, payable monthly.
*   **Post Office Time Deposits:**
    *   1-year Time Deposit: 6.9% per annum
    *   2-year Time Deposit: 7.0% per annum
    *   3-year Time Deposit: 7.1% per annum
    *   5-year Time Deposit: 7.5% per annum
*   **5-Year Recurring Deposit (RD):** The interest rate is set at 6.7% per annum.

The Ministry of Finance's Department of Economic Affairs issues notifications regarding small savings scheme rates before the start of each new quarter. This quarterly review mechanism is in place to ensure that these government-backed savings instruments remain competitive and reflective of prevailing market interest rate conditions, while also supporting national savings objectives. For the upcoming quarter, the stability in rates aims to offer a predictable financial environment for investors across India.
