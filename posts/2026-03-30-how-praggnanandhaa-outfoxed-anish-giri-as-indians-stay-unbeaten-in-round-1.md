Indian Grandmaster R. Praggnanandhaa secured a notable victory against Dutch Grandmaster Anish Giri during the opening round of the prestigious Tata Steel Chess Tournament 2022. The match, which took place on January 15, 2022, in Wijk aan Zee, Netherlands, saw the young Indian prodigy emerge victorious, contributing to an undefeated start for Indian participants across the various sections of the elite international event.

Praggnanandhaa, then 16 years old, demonstrated strategic depth and tactical precision while playing with the white pieces. He systematically outmaneuvered Giri, who is consistently ranked among the world's top-10 players and known for his robust and solid style. The game, which unfolded over 64 moves, culminated in a decisive win for Praggnanandhaa, marking it as a significant upset and a highlight of the tournament's first day. Analysts noted the young Grandmaster's composure and ability to convert a subtle advantage against an experienced Super-Grandmaster, showcasing his rapidly evolving skill set on the global stage.
