Ball-tampering, a practice prohibited under cricket’s Laws, involves illegally altering the condition of the ball to gain an unfair advantage for bowlers. Such actions are viewed as a serious breach of sportsmanship and can undermine the competitive balance of a match.

*   **Rules and Regulations:** The International Cricket Council (ICC) and various domestic leagues, including the PSL, maintain stringent regulations against ball-tampering. These rules are designed to ensure fair competition and prevent any team or player from gaining an undue advantage through illicit means.
*   **Prohibited Methods:** Methods of ball-tampering can include scuffing the surface with fingernails or abrasive objects, applying foreign substances like saliva mixed with sugary substances, or deliberately roughing one side of the ball while keeping the other shiny. The objective is typically to generate more swing or seam movement, making it harder for batters to score.
*   **Penalties:** Penalties for ball-tampering can range from fines and match bans for players to disciplinary action against team management and officials, depending on the severity and frequency of the offence. The exact sanctions are determined by the relevant cricketing body following an investigation.
