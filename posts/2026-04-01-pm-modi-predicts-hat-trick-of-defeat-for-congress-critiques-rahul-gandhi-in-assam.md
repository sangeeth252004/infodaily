Prime Minister Narendra Modi, addressing a public rally in Assam, asserted that the Indian National Congress party was poised for a "hat-trick of defeat" in the ongoing general elections. During his speech, Mr. Modi also directed a "rajkumar" (prince) jibe at Congress leader Rahul Gandhi, framing his remarks within the broader context of the 2024 Lok Sabha election campaign. The Prime Minister's statements were delivered in a key electoral state as political parties intensify their campaigning efforts across the country.

Mr. Modi's prediction of a "hat-trick of defeat" refers to the Congress party's performance in successive general elections, following their outcomes in 2014 and 2019 where the party secured significantly fewer seats compared to previous elections. The Prime Minister's remarks suggest a belief that the upcoming results will mirror or worsen this trend for the opposition party. This commentary forms a central part of the Bharatiya Janata Party's (BJP) campaign strategy, which often highlights past electoral outcomes to project confidence in its own prospects.

The "rajkumar" reference aimed at Rahul Gandhi is a recurring theme in the BJP's rhetoric. It is typically employed to criticize what the party describes as dynastic politics within the Congress, implying a lack of grassroots connection or reliance on inherited political status rather than earned merit. This particular jibe seeks to draw a contrast between the BJP's leadership, often portrayed as emerging from humble backgrounds, and the Nehru-Gandhi family's long-standing influence within the Congress. Such critiques are designed to resonate with voters who may be wary of political dynasties.

Assam holds strategic importance for the BJP, having seen significant electoral gains in recent years, including forming the state government. Prime Minister Modi's presence and focused attack on the Congress in the region underscore the BJP's intent to consolidate its position and maximize its seat tally from the North Eastern states. The BJP currently governs Assam and seeks to maintain its dominance in the region, which contributes 14 Lok Sabha seats.
