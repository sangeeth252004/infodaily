The Reserve Bank of India (RBI) on Monday, August 29, 2022, tightened the regulations governing banks' participation in the Over-the-Counter (OTC) and exchange-traded currency derivatives market. The central bank issued a circular to all commercial banks, aiming to introduce greater discipline and curb speculative activities amidst ongoing volatility in the Indian rupee.

The new directives are designed to ensure that foreign exchange derivative contracts are primarily used for hedging genuine underlying currency exposures rather than for speculative purposes. This measure is part of the RBI's broader strategy to manage the rupee's fluctuations and maintain stability within the financial system. The central bank's action follows a period of significant depreciation of the rupee against major global currencies, particularly the US dollar.

Key provisions of the RBI's circular include:
*   **Underlying Exposure Requirement:** Banks are now mandated to ensure that their customers possess a verifiable underlying currency exposure before entering into any forex derivative contracts. This stipulation is intended to prevent transactions that are purely speculative in nature.
*   **Restrictions on Netting and Set-off:** The circular specifies that if a customer wishes to net off or set off a derivative contract before its maturity, such an action must lead to the actual delivery of the underlying currency. This aims to reinforce the principle that derivatives should correspond to physical currency transactions.
*   **Prevention of Mis-selling:** The RBI has reiterated banks' responsibility to assess the suitability of derivative products for their clients and to transparently explain the associated risks. This emphasis seeks to protect customers from entering into contracts that do not align with their financial objectives or risk appetite.
*   **Clarification on Foreign Currency Exposure (FCE):** The guidelines also provide clarity on how Foreign Currency Exposure (FCE) should be computed when customers engage in hedging activities. This ensures consistency and accuracy in risk management practices across banks.

The tightening of these norms comes as the Indian rupee has experienced notable depreciation, falling over 7 percent against the US dollar in the preceding months and briefly touching record lows around the 80-per-dollar mark. Market analysts indicate that the RBI's move seeks to address concerns regarding excessive speculative build-up in the currency derivatives market, which can amplify volatility and make the rupee more susceptible to external shocks. By reinforcing the need for genuine underlying exposure, the RBI aims to reduce instances where derivative positions are taken without a corresponding commercial need.

Going forward, banks operating in India's currency derivatives market will be required to strictly adhere to these revised guidelines. The RBI's objective is to foster a more stable and orderly foreign exchange market, reducing unwarranted volatility and enhancing the overall resilience of the financial system.
