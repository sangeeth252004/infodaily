Veteran radio presenter Scott Mills has issued a statement addressing his departure from the British Broadcasting Corporation (BBC), confirming that his contract was terminated following an internal review related to a decades-old police investigation into sex offence allegations. Mills broke his silence amid "rumour and speculation" surrounding the circumstances of his exit.

The statement by Mr. Mills clarifies that a police investigation into historical allegations of a sexual nature had taken place. He confirmed his full cooperation with both the police inquiry and the subsequent internal review conducted by the BBC. No charges were brought against him in connection with the police investigation.

Mills, known for his long tenure at BBC Radio 1 and later BBC Radio 2, stated he had always treated such allegations with extreme seriousness. His public comments mark the first time he has directly addressed the specific nature of the events leading to his contract's termination by the broadcaster. He indicated his intention to focus on his ongoing career and personal life following the resolution of these matters.

Key details surrounding the situation include:
*   **Nature of Allegations:** The allegations prompting the police investigation were described as "decades-old."
*   **Police Investigation:** A formal police inquiry was conducted into the allegations.
*   **Outcome of Investigation:** No charges were filed against Scott Mills following the police investigation.
*   **BBC's Action:** The BBC initiated an internal review in response to the allegations. This review ultimately led to the termination of Mills' contract.
*   **Mills' Stance:** He stated full cooperation with both the police and the BBC's internal processes.

The BBC has not publicly commented on the specifics of individual employment cases. Mills' departure from BBC Radio 2 occurred in August 2022, following which he transitioned to a role at Virgin Radio. His statement primarily addresses the historical allegations and the BBC's internal processes that concluded with his contract's termination.

The situation underscores the BBC's protocol for addressing serious allegations, including historical ones, through internal review processes when they involve current or former employees. Mills' public statement aims to provide clarity on the circumstances surrounding his departure from the national broadcaster, particularly concerning the long-standing speculation. He emphasized his commitment to moving forward with his professional endeavors.
