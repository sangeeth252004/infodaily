The Union Public Service Commission (UPSC) has officially released the admit cards for the National Defence Academy (NDA) and Naval Academy (NA) Examination (I), 2026. Aspiring candidates can now download their hall tickets and access critical examination instructions via the official UPSC website ahead of the scheduled April examination. This issuance is a mandatory step for all registered applicants preparing to appear for one of India's most prestigious defence entrance tests.

This release marks a significant step for thousands of candidates vying for admission to the Army, Navy, and Air Force wings of the NDA, as well as the Indian Naval Academy Course (INAC). The NDA & NA (I) Examination is a highly competitive national-level entrance test, pivotal for individuals aiming to embark on a career in India's defence services. The timely issuance of admit cards ensures candidates have ample opportunity to prepare logistically and familiarize themselves with examination protocols, which are crucial for a smooth examination experience.

Candidates are required to download their admit cards in PDF format from the designated section of the UPSC's online portal. The Commission has emphasized that no physical admit cards will be dispatched by post to any candidate. To access the document, applicants must log in using their registration ID or roll number along with their date of birth. It is imperative that candidates thoroughly review all details printed on their admit card, including their name, photograph, signature, examination centre, and reporting time, to ensure accuracy and prevent any discrepancies on exam day. Any errors should be reported to the UPSC helpdesk immediately.

The official notification accompanying the admit card also outlines several crucial instructions that candidates must adhere to:

*   **e-Admit Card Printout:** Candidates must bring a clear printout of the downloaded e-Admit Card to the examination venue. Digital copies will not be accepted.
*   **Photo Identity Proof:** A valid photo identity card (such as Aadhaar Card, Passport, Driving License, or Voter ID) must be carried in its original form, along with a photocopy, for verification purposes at the examination centre.
*   **Reporting Time:** Candidates are advised to report to the examination centre well in advance of the stipulated reporting time to allow for necessary verification and security procedures. Latecomers may be denied entry.
*   **Prohibited Items:** Electronic devices, including mobile phones, smartwatches, Bluetooth devices, and any other communication gadgets, are strictly prohibited inside the examination hall. Candidates are also barred from carrying textual material, rough sheets (other than those provided), or calculators.
*   **COVID-19 Protocols:** Candidates are expected to strictly adhere to all prevailing government-mandated health and safety protocols, including wearing masks and maintaining social distancing, throughout the examination process.
*   **Discrepancy Reporting:** Any discrepancy found on the admit card, such as incorrect personal details or examination centre allocation, should be immediately brought to the notice of the UPSC through the designated contact channels before the examination date.

The NDA & NA (I) Examination 2026 is scheduled to be conducted on an upcoming date in April. Candidates are advised to meticulously follow all instructions provided by the UPSC to ensure a smooth and fair examination process. Success in this examination opens avenues for a distinguished career path in India's armed forces, contributing to national service and leadership. Further updates regarding the examination will be communicated via the official UPSC website.
