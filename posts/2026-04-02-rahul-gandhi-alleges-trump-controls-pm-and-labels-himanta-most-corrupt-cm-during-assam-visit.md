Congress leader Rahul Gandhi, during a recent leg of his 'Bharat Jodo Nyay Yatra' in Assam, made significant allegations against Prime Minister Narendra Modi and the state's Chief Minister, Himanta Biswa Sarma. Speaking at various public gatherings across the state, Mr. Gandhi stated that "Trump controls PM" and labeled Mr. Sarma as the "most corrupt Chief Minister" in the country. These statements underscore an escalation in political rhetoric as national elections approach.

Mr. Gandhi’s remarks were delivered as part of his ongoing cross-country march, which has traversed multiple states before reaching Assam. The allegations against Prime Minister Modi regarding external control were presented without specific elaboration on the 'Trump' reference during the public address. Political observers noted the unusual nature of the specific reference, typically in Indian political discourse, allegations of external influence often pertain to domestic corporate entities. The Congress leader's broader message has consistently focused on perceived influences on government policy.

Addressing the issue of alleged corruption, Mr. Gandhi launched a direct assault on Chief Minister Himanta Biswa Sarma. He reiterated claims of widespread corruption within the Assam state administration, presenting Mr. Sarma as its primary figurehead. The Congress party has previously raised various allegations concerning state governance and financial practices in Assam, particularly in areas like land deals and public procurement. Mr. Gandhi emphasized these points during his public interactions in regions such as Nagaon and Jorhat.

The allegations come amidst a charged political climate, with both the Congress and the ruling Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP) actively campaigning ahead of the upcoming general elections. Mr. Gandhi's 'Bharat Jodo Nyay Yatra' is designed to mobilize party workers and connect with voters, highlighting issues such as economic disparities, social justice, and alleged governmental corruption. The strategy involves direct engagement with the public, often accompanied by sharp criticism of the incumbent BJP governments at both state and central levels.

*   **Key Allegations Made by Rahul Gandhi:**
    *   **Against PM Modi:** "Trump controls PM."
    *   **Against CM Himanta Biswa Sarma:** "Most corrupt CM."
    *   **Broader Theme:** Criticisms centered on alleged external influences on government and pervasive corruption.

The BJP, both at the central and state levels, is anticipated to issue formal responses to Mr. Gandhi's strong accusations. Such exchanges are characteristic of heightened political activity during election periods, where leaders from opposing parties frequently engage in direct confrontations over policy and personal integrity. The fallout from these statements is expected to further intensify the political discourse in Assam and potentially influence broader national narratives as the election cycle progresses. The Congress party is set to continue its yatra, with further public addresses and interactions planned across other regions.
