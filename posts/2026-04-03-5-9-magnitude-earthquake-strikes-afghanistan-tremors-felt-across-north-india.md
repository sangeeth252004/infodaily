A 5.9 magnitude earthquake originating in Afghanistan's remote Hindu Kush region sent significant tremors across northern India, including the Delhi-National Capital Region (NCR), on the evening of Thursday, March 21, 2024. The seismic event, which struck at approximately 9:32 PM Indian Standard Time (IST), prompted residents to evacuate buildings in several cities as ground shaking was reported over a wide geographical area.

According to data released by the National Centre for Seismology (NCS), India's primary agency for earthquake monitoring, the earthquake's epicenter was located at a latitude of 36.36 degrees North and a longitude of 70.73 degrees East. It registered at a substantial depth of 181 kilometers beneath the Earth's surface. Earthquakes originating at such depths often have their energy distributed over a larger area, leading to widespread but typically less intense surface tremors compared to shallower quakes of similar magnitude. The United States Geological Survey (USGS) corroborated the event, reporting similar parameters for the seismic activity.

The Hindu Kush mountain range is one of the world's most seismically active zones, situated at the convergence of the Indian and Eurasian tectonic plates. This geological setting frequently experiences earthquakes due to ongoing continental collision. While the immediate impact in Afghanistan and surrounding regions is being assessed, preliminary reports from India indicate no immediate casualties or significant structural damage. However, the unexpected jolt led to widespread alarm, with many individuals feeling the shaking for several seconds.

Tremors from the 5.9 magnitude quake were distinctly felt across numerous states and union territories in northern India, prompting various disaster management agencies to issue advisories and monitor the situation. Affected regions included:
*   Delhi and the National Capital Region (NCR)
*   Jammu and Kashmir
*   Punjab
*   Haryana
*   Rajasthan
*   Uttarakhand
*   Uttar Pradesh

Social media platforms saw a rapid surge in reports from individuals confirming the tremors, with many sharing experiences of swaying objects and building movements. Emergency services in Delhi and other major cities were placed on alert, though no calls reporting major damage or injuries were registered in the initial hours following the event. Authorities have advised citizens to remain vigilant and follow safety protocols in the event of further seismic activity.

Seismologists and disaster management authorities continue to monitor the region for potential aftershocks, which are common following moderate to strong earthquakes. While the immediate threat appears to have subsided, the incident serves as a reminder of the inherent seismic vulnerability of the region spanning from Afghanistan through Pakistan and into northern India. Local governments and disaster response teams remain prepared to address any emergent situations, with further assessments of the impact expected in the coming days.
