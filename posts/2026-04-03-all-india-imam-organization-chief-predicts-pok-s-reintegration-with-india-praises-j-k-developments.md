Dr. Imam Umer Ahmed Ilyasi, Chief of the All India Imam Organization, publicly stated in late 2022 his prediction that Pakistan-occupied Kashmir (PoK) would "soon" be reintegrated with India. The prominent religious leader also expressed strong praise for the prevailing conditions in Jammu and Kashmir (J&K) following the abrogation of Article 370, describing an environment of peace and development. These remarks, made during an interview, contributed to ongoing national discussions regarding the status of Kashmir.

Ilyasi's statements carry significance due to his position as a prominent voice within India's Muslim community, representing a large network of Imams across the country. His articulation of a vision for PoK's return to India aligns with a long-standing national objective for New Delhi. The pronouncements also provided an assessment of the situation in J&K, a region that has undergone significant administrative and political changes in recent years.

Key points from Dr. Ilyasi's statements include:
*   **"Ghar wapsi soon":** Ilyasi explicitly predicted the "homecoming" or reintegration of Pakistan-occupied Kashmir with the Indian Union.
*   **Observations in J&K:** He recounted a visit to Srinagar in September 2022, during which he observed "peace, happiness, brotherhood, and humanism." He further noted improvements in infrastructure, reporting on "good roads, development," and a booming tourism sector.
*   **Support for current administration:** Ilyasi stated his belief that "Kashmiris are happy with the present government" and indicated that the previous Article 370, which granted special status to J&K, was perceived as a "barrier."
*   **Call for Dialogue:** While predicting reintegration, he also advocated for dialogue between India and Pakistan as a means to achieve this outcome.

The Imam chief's statements were contextualized by other public engagements around the same period. In September 2022, Dr. Ilyasi held a meeting with Rashtriya Swayamsevak Sangh (RSS) chief Mohan Bhagwat at a mosque in Delhi. Following this meeting, Ilyasi referred to Bhagwat as "Rashtra-Rishi" (nation's sage) and "Rashtra-Pita" (father of the nation), remarks that generated considerable discussion across media and political circles.

Dr. Ilyasi frequently emphasized a message of national unity, asserting, "India is our country. We all are Hindustanis." He called upon citizens to resist "anti-national elements" attempting to foster division and urged collective effort to strengthen India. His observations on J&K and predictions regarding PoK contribute to the broader discourse within India concerning its territorial claims and regional stability.

While Dr. Ilyasi's remarks reflect a specific viewpoint on a complex geopolitical issue, the status of Pakistan-occupied Kashmir remains a subject of ongoing diplomatic and political discussion between India and Pakistan. Such public pronouncements from influential figures contribute to shaping national sentiment and reiterating India's position on the Kashmir dispute. The future trajectory of PoK's status continues to be a matter of international attention and bilateral relations.
