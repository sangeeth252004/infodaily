Former U.S. President Donald Trump recently made public statements concerning the Strait of Hormuz, suggesting that the United States possesses the capability to "easily open Hormuz, take oil & make fortune." Trump's statements directly address the strategic waterway situated between the Persian Gulf and the Gulf of Oman, through which a significant portion of the world's crude oil and liquefied natural gas (LNG) transits daily. The former president's specific phrasing, including the assertion to "take oil," could be interpreted within the context of asserting U.S. dominance or as a response to perceived threats to global energy supplies. Such claims by a prominent political figure, particularly one who has previously held and may again seek the presidency, carry geopolitical weight and are closely monitored by international observers, energy markets, and regional actors.

The Strait of Hormuz is a narrow passage that has long been a flashpoint for international tensions, particularly involving Iran, which borders the Strait to the north. Maintaining freedom of navigation through the Strait is a declared interest of numerous global powers, including the United States, which maintains a significant naval presence in the region to ensure the uninterrupted flow of maritime commerce. Statements implying unilateral actions, such as "opening" the Strait or "taking oil," can introduce uncertainty into international relations and potentially impact the stability of global energy markets.

*   **Global Oil Transit:** Approximately one-fifth of the world's total petroleum liquids consumption, or about 21 million barrels per day, passed through the Strait of Hormuz in 2018, according to the U.S. Energy Information Administration (EIA).
*   **Strategic Importance:** The Strait is a vital conduit for oil exports from Saudi Arabia, Iran, UAE, Kuwait, and Iraq, as well as LNG from Qatar.
*   **International Law:** International maritime law generally provides for freedom of navigation through such international straits, subject to specific rules of passage.
*   **Historical Tensions:** The region has witnessed numerous incidents over decades involving naval forces, commercial shipping, and regional geopolitical disputes.
