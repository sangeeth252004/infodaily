In early January 2020, Iran issued a warning to the United States, stating that American technology and energy companies, including Microsoft and Nvidia, faced "complete annihilation" if the U.S. harmed Iran. The warning, delivered by Brigadier General Ramezan Sharif, spokesman for Iran's Revolutionary Guard Corps (IRGC), came in response to threats made by then-U.S. President Donald Trump concerning potential strikes on Iranian infrastructure.

The statements intensified a period of heightened geopolitical strain between the two nations. General Sharif indicated that any U.S. military action against Iran would result in severe consequences, framing the potential U.S. experience as a "nightmare." The Iranian official's remarks directly followed President Trump's threats to target specific Iranian sites, including "bridges" and "power plants," as part of a broader response to Iranian actions.

The Iranian warning specifically named several prominent global companies, signaling a potential scope of retaliation that extended beyond conventional military engagement. The mention of major technology and energy corporations underscored the complex, interconnected nature of modern international disputes.

Key details from the warning included:
*   **Companies Cited:** Brigadier General Sharif referenced Microsoft, Nvidia, Intel, Oracle, Boeing, ExxonMobil, and Saudi Aramco.
*   **Context of U.S. Threats:** Former President Trump had indicated readiness to strike Iranian infrastructure, specifically mentioning bridges and power plants.
*   **Timing:** The warning was delivered on January 6, 2020, amidst escalating tensions following the U.S. drone strike that killed Iranian General Qassem Soleimani days earlier.

This exchange of threats highlighted the volatility of U.S.-Iran relations at the time. The implication of targeting major technology and energy firms raised questions about the potential for cyber or economic forms of retaliation, although the precise methods of "annihilation" were not specified in the public statements. The inclusion of technology giants like Microsoft and Nvidia in such a geopolitical warning underscored the integral role these companies play in the global economy and critical infrastructure.

The incident reflected a period where rhetorical escalation between Washington and Tehran was a prominent feature of their diplomatic and security interactions. While the immediate aftermath of these specific threats did not lead to the scenarios described, the warnings served as a stark indicator of the deep-seated animosity and potential for wide-ranging repercussions in the event of further conflict. The situation underscored how global corporations, regardless of their direct involvement in political disputes, can become points of reference in international rhetoric during periods of high tension.
