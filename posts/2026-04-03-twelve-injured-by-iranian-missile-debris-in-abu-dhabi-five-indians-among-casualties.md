Twelve individuals sustained injuries in Abu Dhabi after being struck by debris identified as originating from an Iranian missile. Among those affected, five were Indian nationals. The incident, confirmed through an official company announcement, occurred recently in the capital of the United Arab Emirates and prompted an immediate emergency response to aid the injured.

The event underscores a critical security concern in a region frequently impacted by various forms of missile and drone activity. While the official statement did not specify the precise location within Abu Dhabi where the debris fell, nor did it detail the exact nature or severity of the injuries sustained by each individual, it affirmed the total count of casualties at twelve. All injured parties received immediate medical attention from local emergency services and were transported to hospitals for further treatment and assessment. The presence of foreign nationals, particularly Indian citizens, among the injured highlights the broader impact of such incidents on expatriate communities residing and working in the Gulf states.

*   **Total Casualties:** 12 individuals reported injured.
*   **Nationalities:** Five of the injured have been identified as Indian nationals. The nationalities of the remaining seven injured individuals have not been publicly disclosed in the announcement.
*   **Origin of Debris:** The debris has been officially identified as originating from an Iranian missile.
*   **Location of Incident:** Abu Dhabi, United Arab Emirates.
*   **Source of Information:** An official company announcement provided the initial confirmation of the incident and its details.

The incident is understood to have occurred without prior warning, leading to the unexpected impact of the debris. Such occurrences necessitate robust air defense systems and rapid response protocols, which were reportedly activated swiftly by UAE authorities following the event. Security forces secured the area of impact, and investigations are expected to commence to determine the full circumstances surrounding the missile activity and the debris fall. This includes examining the trajectory, potential intended targets, and the mechanism by which the debris landed in a populated or industrial zone within Abu Dhabi.

The Indian Embassy in the UAE is expected to be in contact with local authorities to gather more information regarding the condition of its nationals and to provide necessary consular assistance. The safety and well-being of expatriate workers remain a priority for diplomatic missions and host governments alike in the region.

As the situation develops, further official statements from UAE governmental bodies, alongside additional details from the company involved, are anticipated. These statements are crucial for clarifying the complete sequence of events, outlining any ongoing security assessments, and addressing regional concerns regarding stability and civilian safety. The focus for now remains on the recovery of the injured and the ongoing efforts to understand and mitigate future risks associated with missile debris.
