New Delhi, India – External Affairs Minister S. Jaishankar recently affirmed India's resilience in navigating global economic disruptions, advocating a strategic approach of "hedge, de-risk, and diversify" to bolster the nation's economic stability. The Minister's remarks were made amidst ongoing discussions concerning geopolitical tensions in the Middle East, which have implications for global trade and supply chains.

Mr. Jaishankar highlighted India's robust performance in recent years despite significant international challenges. He noted that the country has "solidly" weathered various global shocks, demonstrating inherent strengths in its economic framework. This perspective underscores a confidence in India's ability to maintain its growth trajectory and adapt to an unpredictable global landscape.

The proposed "hedge, de-risk, diversify" strategy is a multi-faceted approach aimed at insulating India from future volatilities.
*   **Hedging** involves mitigating potential economic risks, particularly in areas like energy security and commodity prices, through strategic reserves and diversified sourcing.
*   **De-risking** focuses on reducing over-reliance on single markets or supply chains, thereby lessening vulnerability to geopolitical events or economic downturns in specific regions. This includes fostering domestic manufacturing capabilities and promoting a resilient industrial base.
*   **Diversification** entails expanding India's economic partnerships across various sectors and geographies. This involves forging new trade agreements, exploring alternative markets for exports and imports, and attracting investments from a wider array of international partners.

These strategic imperatives are particularly relevant in the current geopolitical climate. Tensions in the Middle East, including those involving Iran, have historically impacted global energy markets and maritime trade routes. India, as a major energy importer and a growing trading nation, is directly affected by disruptions in these areas. The Minister's call for this strategy reflects a proactive stance to secure India's interests in such environments.

India’s economic performance has shown consistent growth despite global headwinds. The International Monetary Fund (IMF) projected India's economy to grow by 6.8% in 2024 and 6.5% in 2025, solidifying its position as one of the fastest-growing major economies globally. This growth is underpinned by strong domestic demand, government investment in infrastructure, and reforms aimed at improving the ease of doing business. Efforts under initiatives such as 'Make in India' and Production-Linked Incentive (PLI) schemes further contribute to the de-risking and diversification of supply chains by boosting domestic manufacturing.

The External Affairs Minister’s statements signal a continued focus on economic diplomacy and strategic autonomy. By proactively building resilience and diversifying its economic engagements, India aims to safeguard its development agenda and enhance its role in the evolving global economic order. This approach is expected to guide India's foreign policy and economic decision-making in the immediate future, emphasizing strategic foresight in navigating complex international relations and market dynamics.
