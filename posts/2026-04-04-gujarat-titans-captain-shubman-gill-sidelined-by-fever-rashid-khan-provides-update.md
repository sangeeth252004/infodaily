Gujarat Titans' stand-in captain Rashid Khan has provided an update on the health of regular skipper Shubman Gill, confirming that the batter is currently sidelined due to illness. Khan's statement came after a recent Indian Premier League (IPL) match where the Titans suffered a loss to Rajasthan Royals, with Gill absent from the playing eleven.

Speaking to reporters, Rashid Khan indicated that Shubman Gill has been battling a fever for several days, necessitating his temporary withdrawal from the team. "He's been battling fever for the last three to four days," Khan stated, offering insight into the captain's condition. The Afghan all-rounder expressed hope for Gill's swift recovery and return, adding, "Hopefully he will be back soon."

Gill's absence marks a significant challenge for the Gujarat Titans, who rely on his consistent performance at the top of the batting order and his leadership. The 24-year-old was appointed captain ahead of the current IPL season following Hardik Pandya's transfer to Mumbai Indians. Gill had a stellar IPL 2023 season, finishing as the tournament's leading run-scorer and winning the Orange Cap. His role as both a prolific batter and the team's strategist is considered crucial to their campaign.

During the match against Rajasthan Royals, which the Titans lost, Rashid Khan stepped in to lead the side in Gill's absence. The team management has not yet provided a definitive timeline for Gill's return, underscoring the day-to-day assessment of his recovery. The focus remains on ensuring Gill's full health before he resumes his duties on the field.
