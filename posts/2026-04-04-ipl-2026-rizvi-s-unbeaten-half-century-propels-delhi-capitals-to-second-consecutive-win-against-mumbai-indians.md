New Delhi, India – The Delhi Capitals secured their second consecutive victory in the Indian Premier League (IPL) 2026 season on Wednesday, April 15, 2026, defeating the Mumbai Indians by six wickets at the Arun Jaitley Stadium. Young batsman Sameer Rizvi delivered a match-winning performance, scoring an unbeaten 87 runs to successfully chase down Mumbai's challenging total of 197.

Mumbai Indians, after winning the toss and electing to bat first, posted a competitive 197 for 5 in their allotted 20 overs. Opener Rohit Sharma top-scored for Mumbai with a fluent 68 runs off 42 balls, supported by Suryakumar Yadav, who contributed 45 runs from 29 deliveries. The Delhi Capitals' bowling attack, initially struggling, found some rhythm in the latter half of the innings, with Anrich Nortje being the standout bowler, claiming 2 wickets for 38 runs. Spinner Kuldeep Yadav also made an impact, picking up 1 wicket for 30 runs in his four overs, limiting Mumbai's scoring in the middle overs.

In response, the Delhi Capitals’ chase started cautiously, losing two early wickets within the powerplay. However, Sameer Rizvi, batting at number four, stabilized the innings with captain Rishabh Pant. Their partnership added 78 runs for the third wicket, setting a foundation for the chase. After Pant's dismissal for 35, Rizvi accelerated, showcasing a blend of controlled aggression and precise stroke play. He formed another crucial 65-run stand with Axar Patel, who contributed a quickfire 22 not out off 11 balls. Rizvi's innings, which came off just 47 deliveries, included 9 boundaries and 4 sixes, concluding the match with 10 balls to spare.

The victory marks a significant momentum shift for the Delhi Capitals, who had a mixed start to their IPL 2026 campaign. This consecutive win elevates them to the sixth position in the league standings, providing a much-needed boost in confidence. Conversely, the Mumbai Indians, despite a strong batting performance, continue to struggle with their bowling execution, remaining in the eighth spot after six matches.
