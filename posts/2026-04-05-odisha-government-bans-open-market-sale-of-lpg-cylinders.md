Bhubaneswar, Odisha – The Odisha government has issued a directive prohibiting the sale of Liquefied Petroleum Gas (LPG) cylinders in the open market across the state. The move, implemented by the Food Supplies and Consumer Welfare Department, aims to curb the diversion of subsidized domestic LPG for unauthorized commercial purposes and to ensure equitable access for registered household consumers.

The new regulation stipulates that LPG cylinders can now only be sold through authorized distributors and agencies to registered consumers who possess valid connections. This measure directly targets the prevalent practice of small businesses, street vendors, and eateries procuring domestic-grade LPG cylinders from the open market, often at inflated prices or through unofficial channels. Such practices have historically contributed to artificial shortages for domestic users and created an unregulated market segment.

The decision follows consistent reports and concerns regarding the misuse of subsidized domestic LPG cylinders, which are intended solely for household consumption. Diversion for commercial activities not only circumvents government subsidies but also fuels black market operations and can pose significant safety risks due to potential illegal refilling or storage in unauthorized premises. The Food Supplies and Consumer Welfare Department highlighted that the ban is a crucial step towards streamlining the distribution system and cracking down on these malpractices.

Key details of the directive include:
*   **Restricted Sales:** LPG cylinders are now exclusively available through official, registered distributors and their designated outlets.
*   **Consumer Verification:** Sales are restricted to individuals with valid, registered domestic LPG connections.
*   **Targeted Misuse:** The ban specifically addresses the unauthorized use of domestic LPG cylinders for commercial establishments like restaurants, hotels, and roadside eateries.
*   **Enforcement:** State authorities are expected to intensify vigilance and conduct inspections to ensure compliance with the new regulations. Penalties for violations are likely to be enforced according to existing laws pertaining to essential commodities and gas distribution.

The immediate impact of this ban is expected to be felt by numerous small commercial establishments that previously relied on readily available domestic cylinders purchased from the open market. These businesses will now be required to transition to commercial LPG connections, which are typically priced higher and involve a formal registration process with gas companies. This shift is anticipated to increase operational costs for some commercial entities, potentially affecting their pricing structures.

Government officials stated that the long-term objective is to stabilize the supply chain for domestic consumers, eliminate price gouging in the black market, and enhance public safety by ensuring that all LPG transactions occur through regulated channels. The Food Supplies and Consumer Welfare Department will continue to monitor the implementation of the ban and assess its effectiveness in achieving these goals. Further public awareness campaigns are also anticipated to educate both consumers and commercial establishments about the new regulations and the proper procedures for LPG procurement.
