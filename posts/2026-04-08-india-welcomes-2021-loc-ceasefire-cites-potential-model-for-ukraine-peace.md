India has welcomed the February 2021 ceasefire agreement along the Line of Control (LoC) with Pakistan, noting its efficacy in de-escalating tensions and expressing hope that its success could serve as a model for achieving peace in the ongoing conflict in Ukraine. The statement reflects India’s consistent stance on advocating for dialogue and diplomacy in resolving international disputes.

The ceasefire, which India has acknowledged as being brokered by Pakistan, has largely held since its implementation more than three years ago. This agreement marked a significant reduction in hostilities and cross-border firing incidents, which had previously been a persistent feature along the de facto border in Jammu and Kashmir. The enduring nature of this ceasefire demonstrates that even between nations with a history of adversarial relations, a cessation of hostilities and a path to stability can be achieved through negotiation and commitment.

India's External Affairs Minister, S. Jaishankar, has publicly referred to the LoC ceasefire, highlighting its positive impact. He noted that the ceasefire agreement has allowed for a period of relative calm along the border, benefiting local populations and facilitating greater stability. The Minister's remarks underscore the practical outcomes of diplomatic engagement, even in challenging geopolitical contexts.

The Indian government has drawn a parallel between this bilateral de-escalation and the broader international efforts required to address the conflict in Ukraine. New Delhi has consistently called for a peaceful resolution to the Ukraine war through dialogue and diplomacy, avoiding military escalation. The success of the LoC ceasefire is presented as a tangible example of how structured discussions and agreements can lead to an enduring halt in conflict, even if underlying issues remain complex.

Key aspects of India's position include:
*   **Dialogue as a primary tool:** India maintains that direct negotiations between parties are crucial for conflict resolution.
*   **De-escalation:** The LoC ceasefire serves as evidence that de-escalation is achievable when there is a mutual commitment to it.
*   **Humanitarian impact:** India's statements often reference the positive impact of peace on civilian populations, a concern also frequently voiced regarding the Ukraine conflict.
*   **Consistency:** India's foreign policy consistently advocates for non-violent resolutions to global conflicts.

The continued observance of the 2021 LoC ceasefire remains a point of reference for India's diplomatic discourse. While the geopolitical complexities of the India-Pakistan relationship and the Russia-Ukraine conflict differ significantly, India's government continues to emphasize that the principles of dialogue, mutual agreement, and a commitment to peace are universally applicable in resolving armed confrontations. New Delhi's stance suggests a continued push for diplomatic pathways in various global flashpoints, leveraging its own experiences as illustrative examples.
