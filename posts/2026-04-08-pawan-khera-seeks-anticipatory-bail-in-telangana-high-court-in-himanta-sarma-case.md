Congress spokesperson Pawan Khera has recently filed an application seeking anticipatory bail before the Telangana High Court. The application is in connection with a criminal case initiated by Assam Chief Minister Himanta Biswa Sarma. This legal development marks another chapter in the ongoing judicial proceedings stemming from remarks Khera made earlier in 2023.

The bail plea in the Telangana High Court pertains specifically to a First Information Report (FIR) or complaint filed by Assam Chief Minister Sarma. This particular case is part of a broader series of legal challenges Khera has faced following statements he made about Prime Minister Narendra Modi's father during a press conference in February 2023. Khera's legal team is seeking protection from potential arrest through this anticipatory bail application.

The political leader's legal entanglement began in February 2023 when he was deplaned and arrested at Delhi Airport by Assam Police. The arrest was made in connection with multiple FIRs registered against him in various states, including Assam and Uttar Pradesh, for alleged offenses related to his statements.

Key developments in the case against Pawan Khera include:
*   **February 2023 Arrest:** Khera was arrested by Assam Police at Delhi Airport while en route to Raipur for a Congress party plenary session.
*   **Supreme Court Intervention:** Following his arrest, the Supreme Court of India promptly intervened, granting Khera interim bail and consolidating the multiple FIRs filed against him across different states into a single FIR, to be transferred to a court in Delhi. This consolidation was aimed at streamlining the legal process and preventing Khera from having to appear in multiple jurisdictions.
*   **Extension of Interim Bail:** The Supreme Court has, on multiple occasions, extended Khera's interim bail, providing him continued protection from arrest while the legal proceedings unfold.

The current application in the Telangana High Court signifies a strategic legal move by Khera's defense, likely addressing a specific aspect or remaining jurisdiction related to Chief Minister Sarma's complaint that may not be fully covered by the Supreme Court's earlier orders or requires specific relief. The move aims to secure a pre-arrest bail order, preventing his detention should any further action be taken in the case filed by the Assam Chief Minister.

The Telangana High Court is now expected to review Khera's application, considering the legal arguments presented by his counsel and any submissions made by the opposing parties. The outcome of this plea will determine Khera's immediate legal standing concerning the specific complaint by Himanta Biswa Sarma, contributing to the broader narrative of legal challenges often faced by prominent political figures in India. The decision from the High Court is currently awaited.
