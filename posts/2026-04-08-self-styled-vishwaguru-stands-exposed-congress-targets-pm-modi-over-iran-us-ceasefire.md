The Indian National Congress on Tuesday, May 7, 2024, publicly criticized Prime Minister Narendra Modi's government, asserting that its foreign policy efforts regarding the Iran-US situation had fallen short. The opposition party specifically invoked the phrase "'self-styled vishwaguru stands exposed'" while questioning India's influence in facilitating a de-escalation, particularly concerning the prospect of an Iran-US ceasefire.

Congress leaders contended that despite the Modi administration's consistent projection of India as a leading global voice and a "vishwaguru" (global teacher or leader), the nation's capacity to significantly impact or mediate complex international conflicts, such as the tensions between Iran and the United States, appeared limited. The criticism emerged in the context of ongoing geopolitical developments in the Middle East, which have seen heightened rhetoric and military posturing between various actors.

*   **Specific Allegations:** Congress spokespersons stated that the government's claims of global leadership were not substantiated by tangible results in critical international flashpoints.
*   **"Vishwaguru" Narrative:** The term "vishwaguru" has been frequently used by the ruling Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP) to describe India's growing stature under Prime Minister Modi's leadership, emphasizing its role in global diplomacy and problem-solving.
*   **Call for De-escalation:** India has consistently called for de-escalation of tensions in the Middle East and advocated for diplomatic resolutions to regional conflicts.

The opposition party's remarks suggest a perceived disconnect between the government's diplomatic assertions and the on-ground realities of international power dynamics. They implied that the absence of a clear resolution or a brokered ceasefire, despite India's purported global standing, indicated a deficiency in the nation's foreign policy strategy or its execution. This criticism places India's foreign policy approach, particularly its claims of neutrality and active mediation, under domestic scrutiny.

The Congress party's statement is anticipated to fuel further political debate within India regarding the effectiveness of the government's foreign policy and its impact on the nation's international image. The government has yet to issue an official response to these specific accusations, but such exchanges are common in the lead-up to and aftermath of significant geopolitical events, where opposition parties often challenge the ruling administration's handling of external affairs. The ongoing discussion is expected to continue focusing on India's role in global governance and its capacity to influence major international crises.
