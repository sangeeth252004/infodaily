The United States and Iran have reportedly agreed to a two-week ceasefire, marking a temporary pause in hostilities that could pave the way for further de-escalation in the region. This development comes as Tehran has put forward a comprehensive 10-point proposal outlining its conditions for a permanent resolution to the broader conflict.

The ceasefire, which is understood to have commenced recently, aims to create a window for diplomatic engagement and reduce immediate tensions. While the direct terms of the ceasefire have not been fully disclosed, its primary objective is to halt military actions and proxy clashes that have destabilized the Middle East. This pause allows for potential discussions on various contentious issues that have long strained relations between Washington and Tehran.

The announcement of the ceasefire coincides with details emerging from Tehran regarding its 10-point plan designed to bring an end to the prolonged conflict. This proposal primarily addresses long-standing Iranian grievances and demands, focusing heavily on economic relief and security assurances. International observers suggest the plan seeks to shift the dynamic of negotiations by presenting a clear set of requirements from Iran's perspective.

Key components of Tehran's 10-point proposal, as reported, include:
*   **Immediate and comprehensive lifting of all US sanctions:** This encompasses sanctions related to Iran's nuclear program, terrorism, and human rights.
*   **Guarantees against future sanctions:** Tehran seeks assurances that sanctions will not be reimposed unilaterally by future US administrations.
*   **Unfreezing of all Iranian assets held abroad:** Billions of dollars in Iranian funds currently frozen in international banks would be released.
*   **Cessation of US interference in Iran's internal affairs:** A call for Washington to refrain from actions perceived as destabilizing Iran's domestic political landscape.
*   **Security guarantees for Iran:** Assurances against military threats and regional destabilization efforts targeting Iranian interests.
*   **US withdrawal of military forces from the Persian Gulf region:** A demand for a significant reduction or complete removal of American military presence in the broader Middle East.
*   **Recognition of Iran's peaceful nuclear program:** Tehran insists on its right to enrich uranium for civilian purposes under international safeguards.
*   **Facilitation of Iranian oil exports:** Unrestricted access for Iran to global oil markets without punitive measures.
*   **Cooperation on regional security mechanisms:** A framework for regional dialogue and collective security involving all regional stakeholders.
*   **Compensation for past damages:** A demand for reparations for economic losses and humanitarian impacts attributed to US policies and sanctions.

The two-week ceasefire represents a critical test of goodwill from both sides. Its success could determine the feasibility of substantive negotiations on the broader 10-point proposal. While the United States has not formally responded to each point of Tehran's plan, the agreement to a ceasefire signals a willingness to engage in de-escalation. The next steps will likely involve intensive diplomatic efforts to monitor the ceasefire's adherence and explore common ground for a more lasting peace agreement, particularly concerning the future of the 2015 nuclear deal and regional stability.
