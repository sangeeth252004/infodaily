Ahmedabad, India – Vaibhav Sooryavanshi delivered a match-winning century that secured the Indian Premier League (IPL) 2026 title for Rajasthan Royals, marking their second championship victory in the tournament's history. His unbeaten 108 runs from just 45 balls propelled the Royals to a commanding total of 212/4 against Chennai Super Kings in the final match held on May 26, 2026, at the Narendra Modi Stadium. Sooryavanshi's performance was instrumental in the team's 37-run victory, etching his name into the IPL record books.

The decisive innings came after Rajasthan Royals were put into bat first. Sooryavanshi entered the crease with the Royals at 68/2 in the ninth over, facing a critical juncture in the match. His aggressive approach immediately shifted momentum, dismantling the Super Kings' bowling attack. The right-handed batsman's innings featured 10 sixes and 7 fours, reaching his century in only 42 deliveries – the fastest ever recorded in an IPL final. This explosive display allowed the Royals to accelerate significantly in the latter half of their innings, setting a formidable target that proved out of reach for their opponents.

Sooryavanshi's performance transcended individual brilliance, providing the crucial backbone for the Rajasthan Royals' batting effort. The team’s overall score of 212/4 stands as the highest ever in an IPL final, surpassing the previous record of 208/7 set by Sunrisers Hyderabad in 2016. His partnership with Captain Sanju Samson, who contributed a steady 38 runs, stabilized the innings before Sooryavanshi unleashed his full hitting power. The impact of his innings on the team's morale and final outcome was widely acknowledged by commentators and players alike.

Key details from the IPL 2026 Final:
*   **Match Result:** Rajasthan Royals (212/4) defeated Chennai Super Kings (175/9) by 37 runs.
*   **Player of the Match:** Vaibhav Sooryavanshi (Rajasthan Royals).

*   **Records Broken:** Fastest century in an IPL final (42 balls); Highest team total in an IPL final (212/4).
