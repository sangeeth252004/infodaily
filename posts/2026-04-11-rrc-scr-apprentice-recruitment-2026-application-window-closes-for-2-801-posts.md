The application period for the Railway Recruitment Cell (RRC), South Central Railway (SCR), Apprentice Recruitment 2026 concluded on May 30, 2024. This significant recruitment drive sought to fill 2,801 apprentice positions across various technical trades within the SCR zone, marking the end of the online submission phase for aspiring candidates.

The apprentice program, a crucial initiative under the Indian Railways, is designed to foster skill development and generate employment opportunities for young individuals across the nation. By providing hands-on training and practical experience, these programs prepare candidates for future roles not only within the extensive railway network but also in broader industrial sectors. The SCR recruitment aligns with the Indian Railways' ongoing commitment to workforce development and the enhancement of technical skills among the youth, contributing directly to the operational efficiency and maintenance capabilities of one of the world's largest railway systems.

Eligibility criteria for the 2,801 posts generally required candidates to have passed the 10th standard examination with a minimum of 50% aggregate marks. Additionally, applicants needed to possess a National Trade Certificate (ITI) in the relevant trade, issued by the National Council for Vocational Training (NCVT). Age requirements typically stipulated candidates to be between 15 and 24 years old as of a specified cut-off date, with provisions for age relaxation for Scheduled Caste (SC), Scheduled Tribe (ST), Other Backward Class (OBC), and Persons with Benchmark Disabilities (PwBD) categories, in adherence to government norms.

The application process was conducted exclusively online through the official RRC SCR portal. A non-refundable application fee, typically ₹100, was applicable for General and OBC candidates, while SC, ST, PwBD, and female candidates were generally exempted from this fee.

Selection for these apprentice positions is primarily merit-based, determined by the candidates' academic performance in both their 10th standard and ITI examinations. Unlike many recruitment drives, no written examination or interview is typically conducted for the initial selection of apprentices. The apprenticeship programs offer training across a diverse range of trades, including but not limited to Fitter, Welder, Machinist, Electrician, Mechanic Diesel, Carpenter, Painter, AC Mechanic, and Electronic Mechanic. These roles are fundamental to the regular maintenance, repair, and operational integrity of railway infrastructure and rolling stock. The duration of the apprenticeship typically ranges from one to two years, varying by trade, and is governed by the provisions of the Apprentices Act, 1961.

Key aspects of the RRC SCR Apprentice Recruitment 2026 include:
*   **Total Posts:** 2,801
*   **Application Deadline:** May 30, 2024
*   **Age Limit:** 15-24 years (with statutory relaxations)
*   **Minimum Qualification:** 10th Pass with 50% marks + ITI in relevant trade
*   **Selection Method:** Merit list based on academic performance

The program provides structured training within designated railway workshops and units, along with a monthly stipend to support apprentices during their training period. Successful completion culminates in the award of a National Apprenticeship Certificate, a credential that enhances future employment prospects.

Following the closure of the application window, RRC SCR will proceed with the scrutiny of all submitted applications and the preparation of a provisional merit list. Shortlisted candidates will subsequently be called for document verification and a medical examination. The successful completion of this apprenticeship program equips trainees with valuable, industry-specific skills and a recognized certification, significantly improving their eligibility for future employment within Indian Railways or other public and private sector entities requiring skilled technical personnel.
