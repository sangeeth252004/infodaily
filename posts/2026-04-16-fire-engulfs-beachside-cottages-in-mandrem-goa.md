A significant fire erupted in the early hours of Tuesday, May 28, 2024, at a cluster of beachside cottages in Mandrem, a popular coastal village in North Goa. The blaze, reported around 2:30 AM, caused extensive damage to multiple establishments before being brought under control by local fire services. Initial reports indicate no fatalities or critical injuries, though several individuals were treated for minor smoke inhalation.

The fire reportedly originated in one of the wooden and thatch-roofed structures and spread rapidly due to the highly flammable construction materials and strong coastal winds. Local residents and staff of nearby establishments alerted authorities, prompting an immediate response from the Goa Fire and Emergency Services. Multiple fire tenders from Pernem, Mapusa, and Panaji were deployed to the scene, battling the inferno for over four hours. Firefighters focused on containing the spread to adjacent properties, which were closely packed along the beach stretch.

The affected cottages are primarily part of seasonal resorts that cater to tourists, raising concerns about the immediate economic impact on local businesses and tourism. Property owners are currently assessing the full extent of the damage, which is estimated to be in the tens of millions of Indian Rupees. The Mandrem Police have registered a case and initiated an investigation into the exact cause of the fire. Forensic teams are expected to examine the site to determine the origin point and whether any electrical fault or other factor contributed to the incident.

Key details regarding the incident include:
*   **Affected Area:** The blaze impacted at least five commercial cottage establishments, including popular tourist accommodations like 'Dreamcatcher Beach Resort' and 'Shanti Retreat,' though specific official confirmation on all affected properties is pending.
*   **Emergency Response:** Over 40 firefighters were involved in the operation, utilizing foam and water to suppress the flames. Access to the beachside location presented logistical challenges, but crews managed to establish water lines effectively.
*   **Evacuation:** Approximately 70 guests and staff from the affected and nearby cottages were safely evacuated to secure locations. Local community members provided immediate assistance with temporary shelter and refreshments.
*   **Damage Assessment:** While structural damage is significant, a full financial assessment is ongoing. Many structures will require complete rebuilding.
*   **Injuries:** Three individuals, including two resort staff members and one guest, received first aid for minor smoke inhalation at a nearby private clinic and were subsequently discharged.

Superintendent of Police (North Goa), Akash Singh, stated, "Our teams are on the ground conducting preliminary inquiries. We are working closely with the Fire Department to ascertain the cause of this unfortunate incident. The priority now is to ensure the safety of the area and support those affected."

In the aftermath, local authorities, including the Goa Tourism Department, are expected to review safety protocols for temporary and semi-permanent beachside structures. This incident highlights the inherent risks associated with such constructions, particularly concerning fire safety measures and the rapid spread of flames in coastal environments. Further updates are anticipated as the investigation progresses and rehabilitation efforts begin for the affected properties and individuals.
