Intelligence agencies have confirmed that an alleged Lashkar-e-Taiba (LeT) operative, identified by the nickname 'Khargosh' and real name Muhammad Khurshid Parray, reportedly fled India in May 2023 using a fake passport. The escape, despite Parray being on the radar of security forces for his alleged involvement in radicalizing youth in Kashmir, involved a trail that included a nikah ceremony in Jaipur, Rajasthan, earlier this year and his departure from Chennai, Tamil Nadu, for Doha, Qatar.

The incident highlights a significant security lapse, raising questions about the efficacy of passport verification processes and intelligence gathering. Parray, a key figure alleged to have links with the LeT's proxy, The Resistance Front (TRF), was reportedly active in Jammu and Kashmir since 2018-19. His ability to obtain a fraudulent travel document and depart the country undetected underscores the persistent challenges faced by counter-terrorism agencies.

Key details surrounding the operative's alleged activities and escape include:
*   **Fake Passport Acquisition:** Parray allegedly acquired a fake passport in March 2023. This document reportedly allowed him to assume a false identity, facilitating his unhindered departure from India.
*   **Departure Route:** He reportedly departed from Chennai International Airport for Doha, Qatar, in May 2023.
*   **Jaipur Connection:** Despite being a wanted individual, Parray allegedly attended his nikah ceremony in Jaipur in January 2024. This event was reportedly attended by family members, further indicating a degree of operational freedom while under surveillance.
*   **Kashmir Activities:** Prior to his escape, Parray was reportedly instrumental in radicalizing local youth in Kashmir. He was also allegedly associated with Basit Dar, another LeT operative who was killed in an encounter in March of this year.
*   **Intelligence Tracking:** Parray had been on the radar of intelligence agencies. His movements and alleged activities were being monitored, yet he successfully managed to evade capture and flee the country.

The revelation of Parray's escape comes amid ongoing efforts by Indian security forces to dismantle terror networks and track individuals involved in radical activities within the country. The incident is expected to prompt a review of passport issuance procedures, particularly regarding identity verification, and an assessment of real-time intelligence sharing mechanisms between various security agencies. The alleged use of a fake passport by a high-value alleged operative signifies a serious breach and an area of concern for national security. Investigations are reportedly ongoing to determine the full extent of the network that facilitated Parray's escape and his current whereabouts.
