New Delhi – The Communist Party of India (Marxist) (CPM) and the Communist Party of India (CPI) have officially written to the Election Commission of India (ECI), lodging a complaint alleging that Prime Minister Narendra Modi’s recent address to the nation following the passage of the Women’s Reservation Bill constituted a violation of the Model Code of Conduct (MCC). The parties specifically questioned the use of state-owned broadcasters, Doordarshan and Akashvani, for the telecast of what they termed a "political speech" while the MCC is in effect.

The controversy centers on Prime Minister Modi's address, delivered on September 29, 2023, shortly after both houses of Parliament passed the 'Nari Shakti Vandan Adhiniyam' (Women's Reservation Bill), which reserves one-third of seats for women in the Lok Sabha and state assemblies. The communist parties contend that by addressing the nation on a policy matter with significant electoral implications, particularly through public broadcasters, the Prime Minister gained an unfair advantage ahead of upcoming state assembly elections.

The Model Code of Conduct, a set of guidelines issued by the Election Commission, comes into force immediately after the announcement of the election schedule and remains operative until the completion of the electoral process. Its primary purpose is to ensure a level playing field among political parties and candidates, preventing any abuse of state machinery or resources for electoral gain. Currently, the MCC is active in five states—Chhattisgarh, Madhya Pradesh, Rajasthan, Telangana, and Mizoram—where assembly elections are scheduled to be held in November.

*   **Key Allegations from CPM and CPI:**
    *   **Violation of MCC:** Both parties assert that the Prime Minister's address, a government communication, was broadcast at a time when the MCC restricts such public pronouncements that could influence voters.
    *   **Misuse of State Broadcasters:** They highlighted the extensive coverage of the address on Doordarshan (DD) and Akashvani (AIR), arguing that these public platforms should remain neutral during the election period.
    *   **Political Nature of Speech:** The parties characterized the address as a "political speech" designed to garner electoral benefits for the ruling party, rather than an urgent matter of national importance.
    *   **Lack of Emergency:** The letters questioned the necessity of a special address from the Prime Minister on the Women's Reservation Bill, suggesting no immediate emergency warranted such an intervention during the MCC period.

In separate letters, CPM General Secretary Sitaram Yechury and CPI General Secretary D. Raja urged the Election Commission to investigate the matter thoroughly. Mr. Yechury's letter reportedly stated, "The Prime Minister addressing the nation on a specific bill, in a political context and projected as a 'guarantee' for women, while the MCC is in force, is a clear violation." Mr. Raja’s communication echoed similar sentiments, emphasizing the need for the ECI to uphold the integrity of the electoral process.

The Election Commission of India has not yet issued a public statement regarding the complaints. The ECI is expected to review the submissions from the CPM and CPI and determine whether a breach of the Model Code of Conduct has occurred. Potential outcomes could include a formal warning, an advisory, or a dismissal of the complaints, depending on the Commission's assessment of the facts and precedents. This incident adds to ongoing discussions about the enforcement of the MCC and the use of public resources by political leaders during election cycles.
