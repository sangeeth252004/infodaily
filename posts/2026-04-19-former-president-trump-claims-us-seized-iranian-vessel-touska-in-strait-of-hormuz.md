Former U.S. President Donald Trump stated on April 2, 2024, that American forces seized an Iranian vessel named TOUSKA in the Strait of Hormuz. The assertion was made during a campaign rally in Green Bay, Wisconsin. Trump claimed that the vessel's engine room sustained damage during the alleged operation, detailing that US personnel "blew a hole in the engine room" before taking control of the ship.

The former President's remarks included the claim that "our great Navy and special forces" were responsible for the seizure. According to Trump, the Iranian vessel was transporting illegal weapons, which he suggested were destined for Yemen. He further criticized what he referred to as the "fake news" media for not reporting on the incident.

Crucially, there has been no immediate confirmation or denial of this specific incident from the Pentagon or any other official US government sources following Trump's public statements. The lack of official corroboration means the details surrounding the alleged seizure of the TOUSKA vessel remain unverified by current US defense or diplomatic channels.

Trump's claims emerged within the broader context of his criticisms regarding the current administration's foreign policy. During his rally speech, he drew comparisons between his own presidential actions and the foreign policy approach under President Joe Biden, aiming to portray his past decisions as more decisive in addressing regional security challenges.

The Strait of Hormuz, a narrow waterway connecting the Persian Gulf to the Arabian Sea, is globally recognized as a critical chokepoint for international oil shipments. It has been a flashpoint for regional tensions, with frequent naval patrols and occasional intercepts of vessels suspected of illegal activities.

Historically, the United States has reported multiple instances of intercepting weapons shipments in the region, attributing many to Iran and alleging they were destined for Houthi rebels in Yemen. In February 2024, for example, the US Central Command (CENTCOM) announced the seizure of advanced conventional weapons components from an unflagged vessel in the Arabian Sea. That operation, which resulted in the sinking of the vessel due to safety concerns, involved materials identified as having Iranian origin and intended for Houthi forces in Yemen.
