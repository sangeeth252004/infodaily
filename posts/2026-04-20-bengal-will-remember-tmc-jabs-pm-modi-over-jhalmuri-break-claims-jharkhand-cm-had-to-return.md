The Trinamool Congress (TMC) has launched strong criticism against Prime Minister Narendra Modi, alleging that he prioritized a "jhalmuri break" during his recent visit to West Bengal while Jharkhand Chief Minister Champai Soren was reportedly forced to return without meeting him. The incident, reported around mid-May 2024, has ignited a political debate, with the TMC stating that "Bengal will remember" the alleged slight.

According to statements from TMC leaders, Prime Minister Modi was on an election campaign tour in West Bengal when the purported incident occurred. The party specifically highlighted a moment when the Prime Minister was seen engaging with local street food, described as a "jhalmuri break." Concurrently, the TMC claimed that Jharkhand Chief Minister Champai Soren had travelled to West Bengal with the intention of meeting PM Modi, only to be denied an audience and consequently had to return without the meeting taking place.

The allegations have escalated political tensions between the TMC, which governs West Bengal, and the central government led by the Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP). The TMC framed the incident as a breach of protocol and a disregard for a democratically elected Chief Minister, particularly from an allied state. They asserted that such an action demonstrates a lack of respect for federal structure and inter-state relations. The timing of these accusations aligns with the ongoing Lok Sabha general elections, adding a layer of electoral rhetoric to the controversy in a politically crucial state.

*   **Primary Accusation:** The Trinamool Congress specifically accused Prime Minister Modi of taking a "jhalmuri break" during his campaign schedule in West Bengal.
*   **Allegation Regarding CM Soren:** Jharkhand Chief Minister Champai Soren reportedly traveled to meet the Prime Minister but was allegedly unable to secure an audience, leading to his return from the meeting venue or airport.
*   **TMC's Stance:** The TMC termed the alleged incident an "insult" to a Chief Minister and, by extension, to the people he represents, stating it would be remembered in Bengal's political landscape.
*   **Contextual Timing:** The controversy unfolded in mid-May 2024, a period marked by intense campaigning for the national elections, where West Bengal is a key battleground state.

The allegations are expected to remain a point of contention in the ongoing political discourse, particularly within West Bengal and Jharkhand. The incident underscores the often-strained dynamics between states governed by opposition parties and the central administration. Further clarifications or responses from the Prime Minister's Office or the BJP regarding the alleged protocol breach and the circumstances of Chief Minister Soren's visit are anticipated as the political fallout continues.
