The Power Grid Corporation of India Limited (PGCIL), a Maharatna Central Public Sector Undertaking under the Ministry of Power, Government of India, has officially commenced the recruitment process for 668 Diploma Trainee positions. Online registration for aspiring candidates began on Monday, May 20, 2024. This recruitment drive aims to strengthen PGCIL's workforce across various engineering disciplines for its extensive network of power transmission projects and operations, with selected candidates expected to join the 2026 intake.

This significant recruitment initiative by PGCIL targets qualified diploma holders in engineering, offering them career opportunities within India's crucial power transmission sector. PGCIL plays a pivotal role in the country's power infrastructure, owning and operating a vast network of transmission lines and substations across the nation. The induction of new Diploma Trainees is essential for maintaining operational efficiency, supporting ongoing expansion projects, and ensuring the reliability of the national grid. The posts are distributed across different regions and engineering streams, reflecting the diverse technical requirements of the corporation, and contribute to national employment generation.

Key details regarding the PGCIL Diploma Trainee recruitment are as follows:

*   **Total Vacancies:** A total of 668 Diploma Trainee positions are open for application. Specific breakdowns by discipline and region, including Electrical, Civil, Electronics, and IT, are detailed in the official notification.
*   **Eligibility Criteria:**
    *   **Educational Qualification:** Candidates must possess a full-time Diploma in the relevant engineering discipline (e.g., Electrical, Civil, Electronics, Information Technology) from a recognized university or institution. A minimum aggregate percentage is typically required, as specified in the official advertisement.
    *   **Age Limit:** The upper age limit for applicants is generally 27 years as of the closing date of application. Age relaxations are applicable for reserved categories (SC/ST/OBC/PwBD) as per government norms.
*   **Application Process:**
    *   Applications must be submitted online exclusively through PGCIL's official career portal, accessible via www.powergrid.in.
    *   The process involves online registration, filling out the application form with personal and educational details, uploading scanned copies of required documents (such as photograph, signature, and educational certificates), and payment of the non-refundable application fee.
*   **Important Dates:**
    *   Online Registration Commencement: May 20, 2024
    *   Online Registration End Date: June 18, 2024 (Applicants are advised to confirm exact dates from the official notification, as these may be subject to minor adjustments).
    *   Further dates for the Computer Based Test (CBT) and subsequent selection stages will be announced on the official website following the application period.

The selection process for the Diploma Trainee positions typically involves a Computer Based Test (CBT) to assess candidates' technical knowledge in their respective engineering disciplines and general aptitude. Shortlisted candidates will then proceed to document verification and a pre-employment medical examination. Successful candidates will undergo a mandatory period of training before being absorbed as Junior Engineers, contributing directly to PGCIL's operational and developmental initiatives.

Prospective applicants are strongly advised to thoroughly review the detailed official notification available on the PGCIL corporate website, www.powergrid.in, under the 'Career' section. The notification provides comprehensive information on eligibility, reservation policies, application fees, selection methodology, and regional allocations of posts, which are crucial for a successful application. This recruitment drive represents a significant opportunity for diploma holders to contribute to India's energy sector and build a career in a leading public sector enterprise.
