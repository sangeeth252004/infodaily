Seven individuals, including a mother and her six children, died following a house explosion and subsequent fire in Plum Borough, Pennsylvania, on Saturday, May 25, 2024. The incident occurred in the early morning hours, resulting in the complete destruction of one residence and significant damage to two neighboring homes.

The Allegheny County Medical Examiner's Office identified six of the deceased as Casey Clontz, 38; Keagan Clontz, 16; Everett Clontz, 13; Kressa Clontz, 10; Sami Clontz, 9; and Shawn Clontz, 5. A seventh child was also confirmed among the fatalities, with their name not yet publicly released. Tony Clontz, the father and husband, was reportedly at work at the time of the explosion. Three neighbors sustained injuries and were transported to local hospitals.

*   **Location:** Rustic Ridge Drive, Plum Borough, Pennsylvania
*   **Date and Time:** Early morning, Saturday, May 25, 2024
*   **Fatalities:** Seven (mother and six children)
*   **Injuries:** Three neighbors hospitalized
*   **Property Damage:** One home destroyed, two severely damaged

Emergency services, including multiple fire departments and law enforcement agencies, responded to the scene. The investigation into the cause of the explosion is ongoing, led by the Pennsylvania State Police Fire Marshal, the Allegheny County bomb squad, and Plum Borough police. Authorities have indicated that natural gas is a potential focus of the investigation, though a definitive cause has not yet been determined. Residents in the vicinity reported hearing a loud boom that some described as feeling like an earthquake, with the force of the blast shaking nearby homes.

Plum Borough has experienced multiple explosions over the past two decades, with some incidents linked to natural gas lines. In 2008, two people died in a house explosion within the borough. More recently, in August 2023, another explosion in Plum Borough resulted in the deaths of four individuals. These previous incidents highlight an existing concern regarding infrastructure in the area.

Community support efforts have commenced for the affected families and residents. The investigation into the May 25 explosion is expected to continue for an extended period as officials work to determine the precise origin and cause of the blast. Further updates will be provided by investigating authorities as information becomes available.
