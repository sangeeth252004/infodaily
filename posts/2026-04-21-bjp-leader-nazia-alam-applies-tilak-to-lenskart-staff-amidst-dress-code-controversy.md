BJP leader Nazia Alam recently visited a Lenskart store in Laxmi Nagar, Delhi, where she applied tilak to several staff members. The incident, which occurred on May 2, 2024, gained public attention amidst an ongoing controversy surrounding the eyewear retail chain's alleged dress code policy regarding religious symbols worn by its employees.

The event unfolded as reports circulated alleging that Lenskart had issued an internal directive asking staff to avoid wearing religious symbols such as tilak or bindi as part of their uniform. This alleged policy sparked public discussion on corporate guidelines and individual religious freedom in the workplace. Video footage of Ms. Alam's visit circulated on social media, showing her interacting with employees and applying the tilak on their foreheads.

Ms. Alam stated her actions were a response to the perceived restriction on religious expression. She reportedly challenged the company to take action against her for applying the tilak, emphasizing the importance of cultural and religious practices. Her visit underscored a broader debate about how multinational and national corporations balance their brand image and uniform policies with the diverse religious and cultural practices of their employees, particularly in a country like India.

Lenskart has since addressed the matter, clarifying its position on employee attire. The company stated that while it maintains a uniform policy for its staff, it also deeply respects religious sentiments and allows employees to wear traditional attire during festivals. This clarification seeks to address concerns raised by the public and by Ms. Alam's protest, indicating the company's intent to uphold religious expression within its operational framework.

The incident highlights a recurring point of contention in India: the intersection of corporate policy, cultural identity, and religious freedom. Public and political figures often intervene in such matters, reflecting the sensitivity surrounding religious practices. The controversy also underscores the role of social media in amplifying such debates, quickly bringing corporate policies under public scrutiny.
