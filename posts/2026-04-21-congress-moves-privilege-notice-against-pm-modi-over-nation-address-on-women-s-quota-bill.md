The Indian National Congress party recently moved a privilege notice against Prime Minister Narendra Modi, alleging a "blatant abuse of power" following his address to the nation concerning the Women's Reservation Bill, officially known as the Nari Shakti Vandan Adhiniyam. The notice was filed by Congress members, raising concerns over the Prime Minister's conduct in relation to parliamentary proceedings.

The privilege notice centers on the premise that the Prime Minister's address to the nation, prior to or during the legislative process surrounding the bill, constituted a potential breach of parliamentary privilege. Members of Parliament are protected by certain privileges to ensure they can perform their duties without obstruction or improper influence. A breach of privilege occurs when an individual or entity obstructs, impedes, or unduly influences the functioning of Parliament or its members.

Congress leaders contended that the Prime Minister's national address about the Women's Reservation Bill pre-empted parliamentary debate and deliberation, thereby potentially undermining the legislative process. They argued that such an address could be perceived as influencing parliamentary proceedings outside the established rules of conduct. The move marks a significant procedural development in the context of the recent special session of Parliament.

Key details surrounding the privilege notice include:
*   **Allegation of Pre-emption:** Congress asserted that the Prime Minister's address on the Nari Shakti Vandan Adhiniyam effectively announced government policy and details of the bill outside parliamentary chambers before it had been fully debated and passed by both Houses.
*   **"Blatant Abuse of Power":** This specific phrase was used by Congress members to characterize the Prime Minister's action, suggesting a misuse of his office to circumvent or overshadow parliamentary procedures.
*   **Parliamentary Rules:** The notice invokes the rules governing parliamentary conduct and privileges, which prohibit actions that disrespect or undermine the authority and dignity of the legislature.

The Women's Reservation Bill, seeking to reserve one-third of seats for women in the Lok Sabha and state assemblies, was a central focus of the recent special parliamentary session. The bill subsequently passed both the Lok Sabha and the Rajya Sabha, receiving widespread political attention. The privilege notice, however, targets the procedural aspect of the Prime Minister's communication regarding the bill rather than the merits of the legislation itself.

Upon the submission of a privilege notice, the matter is typically referred to the Speaker of the Lok Sabha or the Chairman of the Rajya Sabha, depending on which House the notice pertains to. The presiding officer then has the discretion to refer the matter to the respective House's Committee of Privileges for examination. The Committee investigates the alleged breach, gathers evidence, and subsequently submits a report to the House. Based on the Committee's recommendations, the House may decide on further action. This process can range from no action being taken to a reprimand or other penalties, depending on the severity of the alleged breach. The filing of this privilege notice indicates a new phase in the political discourse surrounding the recent parliamentary session.
