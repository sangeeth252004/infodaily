Congress leader Rahul Gandhi on Saturday, May 25, 2024, paid tribute to the victims of the 2000 Pahalgam terror attack in Jammu and Kashmir. Speaking near the site of the tragic incident in Pahalgam, Mr. Gandhi expressed solidarity with the victims, stating, "Memory still shakes our heart," reiterating the need for collective remembrance and peace in the region.

The tribute occurred during Mr. Gandhi's election campaign activities for the Anantnag-Rajouri Lok Sabha constituency. His visit to Pahalgam underscored a moment of somber reflection on past violence, acknowledging the enduring impact of the August 2000 attack on the local community and the nation. The incident remains a significant event in the history of militancy in Jammu and Kashmir, having claimed the lives of dozens.

The 2000 Pahalgam terror attack involved militants opening fire on a camp of Amarnath pilgrims and security forces. This coordinated act of violence resulted in numerous casualties, leaving a lasting scar on the region's socio-political landscape. The attack highlighted vulnerabilities in pilgrimage security and intensified counter-terrorism efforts.

Key details of the 2000 Pahalgam terror attack:
*   **Date:** August 1, 2000
*   **Location:** Nunwan base camp, Pahalgam, Jammu and Kashmir
*   **Victims:** 32 Amarnath pilgrims and three security personnel were killed.
*   **Perpetrators:** Militants opened fire on the encampment during the annual Amarnath Yatra pilgrimage.

Mr. Gandhi’s tribute serves as a reminder of the human cost of conflict and the importance of fostering an environment of peace. While engaging with local residents and party workers as part of his political outreach, he utilized the platform to reflect on historical events that have shaped the region. The Congress leader's presence in Pahalgam and his message of remembrance aim to connect with the sentiments of the people, emphasizing unity and healing following periods of turmoil.

The commemoration comes amidst ongoing general elections, where national security and regional stability are frequently discussed topics. Rahul Gandhi's act of remembrance during his campaign itinerary underscores a broader political and humanitarian message, focusing on the need to address past grievances and work towards a more peaceful future for Jammu and Kashmir. His visit concludes with continued engagements in the constituency ahead of the polling date.
