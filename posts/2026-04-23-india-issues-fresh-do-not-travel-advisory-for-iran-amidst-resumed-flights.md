New Delhi, India – The Indian Ministry of External Affairs (MEA) has issued a fresh travel advisory, strongly cautioning its citizens against all non-essential travel to Iran. The advisory, which explicitly states "Do not travel to Iran," has been issued amidst reports of some limited flight operations to the country having resumed. This directive underscores the Indian government's continued assessment of prevailing security conditions in the region.

The updated guidance reflects the government's commitment to prioritizing the safety and well-being of Indian nationals. Such advisories are typically issued following comprehensive assessments of geopolitical tensions, internal security situations, and other factors that could pose risks to travelers. While the specific triggers for this particular "fresh" advisory were not detailed in the public announcement, it aligns with broader concerns regarding regional stability.

For Indian nationals currently residing in Iran, the advisory typically includes recommendations to exercise extreme caution, avoid unnecessary movement, and stay in continuous contact with the Indian Embassy in Tehran. It also advises them to register with the Embassy if they have not already done so, to ensure prompt assistance in case of emergencies.

The explicit instruction "Do not travel to Iran" represents a heightened level of caution, indicating that the government perceives significant safety and security risks that outweigh any potential benefits of travel. This level of advisory is generally reserved for situations where the risks are deemed substantial, potentially including threats of terrorism, civil unrest, or other unpredictable dangers. The context of limited flights resuming highlights a nuanced situation: while physical travel might be logistically possible, the Indian government's strong recommendation is to avoid it due to underlying dangers.

Typical recommendations associated with such high-level travel advisories often include:
*   **Avoid all non-essential travel:** This means refraining from tourism, business trips not critical to national interests, and any other travel that can be postponed or cancelled.
*   **Monitor local media:** Staying informed about developments in the region and following instructions from local authorities.
*   **Review personal security plans:** For those already in the country, assessing and updating their safety protocols.
*   **Maintain contact with family:** Ensuring family members in India are aware of their whereabouts and status.

This advisory directly impacts Indian citizens planning travel to Iran for tourism, business, or family visits. It also serves as a formal communication of the government's stance on the prevailing conditions, potentially influencing travel insurance policies and corporate travel guidelines for Indian companies with operations or interests in the region. The Ministry of External Affairs will likely continue to monitor the security landscape in Iran and the broader region. Travel advisories are subject to periodic review and updates based on evolving ground realities, with the primary objective remaining the safety and well-being of Indian nationals abroad.
