New Delhi, India – Mallikarjun Kharge, President of the Indian National Congress, has publicly challenged Prime Minister Narendra Modi regarding his perceived silence on an alleged "hellhole" remark attributed to former US President Donald Trump concerning India. Kharge's direct question, "Modi ji what are you scared of?", was delivered during a recent public address, marking a pointed criticism of the government's stance on international perceptions of the nation.

Kharge's criticism centers on the absence of an official response from the Indian government, specifically from Prime Minister Modi, to the alleged comments. The Congress leader highlighted that such remarks, if left unaddressed, could impact India's global standing and national prestige. He articulated his concern that despite India's growing international influence, a lack of assertive diplomacy on such matters could be detrimental.

The "hellhole" controversy dates back to January 2018, when then-US President Donald Trump used the term "shithole countries" to describe certain nations in Africa and Haiti during a White House meeting on immigration. While Trump's original remarks did not directly name India, Kharge's recent statements suggest a concern that similar derogatory sentiments may have been expressed or inferred regarding India, or that the general implication casts a shadow, necessitating a strong rebuttal from the Indian leadership.

Key points of Kharge's challenge include:
*   **Direct Question to PM Modi:** Kharge directly queried the Prime Minister's motivations for silence, asking, "Modi ji what are you scared of? Why is there no response to what Trump has said about India?"
*   **Impact on India's Image:** The Congress President underscored that an alleged denigrating remark from a former head of state, particularly of a key strategic partner like the United States, requires a robust and immediate diplomatic response to safeguard India's image on the world stage.
*   **Government's Perceived Inaction:** Kharge's statements imply a failure on the part of the current administration to defend India's honor effectively against perceived insults from international figures.
*   **Call for Accountability:** The remarks serve as a call for greater transparency and accountability from the government regarding its foreign policy and its approach to defending national interests.

The Congress party has frequently criticized the Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP)-led government on various aspects of its foreign policy and domestic governance. This latest intervention by Kharge reignites debate on how India addresses international critiques and allegations, particularly those from prominent global leaders. The opposition's stance suggests a belief that the government's perceived inaction could be interpreted as weakness or indifference, potentially undermining India's diplomatic position.

As of the time of reporting, the Prime Minister's Office or the Ministry of External Affairs has not issued an official response to Kharge's specific challenge or the underlying allegations regarding Trump's comments. The political pressure from the opposition is likely to continue, prompting further scrutiny of the government's communication strategy on international incidents and its approach to defending the nation's reputation globally. The unfolding political discourse will indicate how the ruling party chooses to address these allegations and whether it provides a formal clarification on the matter.
