New Delhi, India – The Delhi Capitals (DC) franchise has officially announced the inclusion of Vipraj Nigam as a concussion substitute for their injured fast bowler Lungi Ngidi for the ongoing Indian Premier League (IPL) 2026 season. The decision comes after Ngidi sustained a concussion during a recent match, necessitating his withdrawal from active play and prompting the team to activate the league's concussion substitute protocols.

The selection of Nigam, a domestic player, over another available specialist fast bowler like Dushmantha Chameera, has drawn attention, underscoring the strict application of the IPL's concussion substitute rule. This regulation, aligned with International Cricket Council (ICC) guidelines, mandates that any replacement player must be a "similar type of player" to the individual they are replacing, with the final assessment resting with the match referee.

Under these specific rules, the match referee evaluates various factors, including a player's primary skills in batting, bowling (type and pace), and wicket-keeping. The objective is to ensure that the integrity of the match is maintained and that teams cannot gain an undue advantage through a substitution. In Ngidi's case, despite his primary role as a pace bowler, the assessment by the match referee deemed Vipraj Nigam's overall skill set as a more appropriate "like-for-like" replacement under the existing criteria, compared to other available squad members. This implies that Ngidi's contributions, or Nigam's specific attributes, were seen as a closer match in the context of the playing conditions and team balance.

Ngidi's absence is a significant setback for the Delhi Capitals, as he has been a key component of their pace attack. The introduction of Nigam will require the team to adapt its strategies for upcoming fixtures. While Nigam's specific role within the squad is yet to be fully defined, his inclusion provides an additional option as DC navigate the remainder of the tournament.

Key details regarding the substitution include:

*   **Injured Player:** Lungi Ngidi, a prominent international fast bowler for the Delhi Capitals.
*   **Injury Type:** Concussion, sustained during an IPL 2026 match.
*   **Concussion Substitute:** Vipraj Nigam, a domestic player.
*   **Rule Application:** The selection strictly adheres to the IPL's "like-for-like" concussion substitute rule, as determined by the match referee. This rule prioritizes the functional replacement of a player's overall skill set rather than simply their primary discipline.
*   **Timing:** The substitution was activated mid-season during the IPL 2026 campaign.

The Delhi Capitals will now integrate Vipraj Nigam into their training and strategic plans, aiming to maintain their competitive edge despite the loss of Ngidi. Ngidi's recovery will be monitored, and further updates on his return to play will be subject to medical clearance and league protocols. The team's immediate focus will be on optimizing its playing XI to cover the void left by Ngidi's absence and leveraging Nigam's capabilities.
