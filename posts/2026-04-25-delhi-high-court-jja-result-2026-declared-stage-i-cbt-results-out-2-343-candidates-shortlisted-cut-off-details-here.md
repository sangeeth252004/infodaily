The Delhi High Court has officially announced the results for the Stage-I Computer-Based Test (CBT) for the Junior Judicial Assistant (JJA) recruitment examination for the 2026 cycle. The declaration, made public recently, marks a significant step in the recruitment process designed to fill crucial administrative roles within the Delhi judiciary. A total of 2,343 candidates have been provisionally shortlisted based on their performance in the initial screening examination.

The Stage-I CBT served as the preliminary assessment for aspiring Junior Judicial Assistants, testing candidates on various aptitudes relevant to the demanding administrative environment of the High Court. The successful candidates will now proceed to subsequent stages of the selection process, which typically include a descriptive test, a typing/skill test, and an interview, as outlined in the official recruitment notification. These further stages are designed to evaluate the candidates' writing abilities, typing proficiency, and overall suitability for the JJA position.

The role of a Junior Judicial Assistant is integral to the smooth functioning of the judicial system, providing essential support to judges and court staff. Responsibilities often include managing court records, assisting in administrative tasks, and ensuring efficient workflow within the court premises. The rigorous selection process aims to identify individuals with the necessary skills, attention to detail, and commitment to public service required for such a pivotal role.

Key details regarding the Stage-I CBT results:

*   **Post Name:** Junior Judicial Assistant (JJA)
*   **Recruitment Cycle:** 2026
*   **Exam Stage:** Stage-I Computer-Based Test (CBT)
*   **Declaring Authority:** Delhi High Court
*   **Number of Shortlisted Candidates:** 2,343
*   **Result Availability:** Candidates can access their individual scores and the category-wise cut-off marks on the official website of the Delhi High Court.

Candidates who appeared for the Stage-I CBT are advised to visit the official Delhi High Court website, delhihighcourt.nic.in, to check their results and review the published cut-off scores. The website provides a direct link to the result notification and a portal for candidates to log in and view their detailed scorecards. It is crucial for all shortlisted candidates to regularly monitor the official portal for upcoming announcements concerning the schedule and specific requirements for Stage-II of the recruitment process. The precise dates and modalities for the subsequent descriptive test, skill test, and interviews are expected to be released in due course.
