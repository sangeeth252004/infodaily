Punjab Chief Minister Bhagwant Mann has formally sought the intervention of President Droupadi Murmu regarding seven Aam Aadmi Party (AAP) Members of Parliament (MPs) in the Rajya Sabha. The Chief Minister's appeal, made recently, stems from allegations that these MPs, elected on AAP tickets, have subsequently aligned themselves with or joined the Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP). This move highlights a significant political dispute concerning party allegiance and parliamentary ethics within India's Upper House.

In a formal letter dispatched to the President, Mr. Mann outlined his concerns over the alleged defection of these seven Rajya Sabha members. He contended that these individuals secured their positions representing AAP, thereby obtaining a mandate from the party's supporters. The Chief Minister stated that their current actions and perceived affiliation with the BJP constitute a betrayal of that electoral trust and a violation of the principles of the anti-defection law.

The anti-defection law, enshrined in the Tenth Schedule of the Indian Constitution, aims to prevent political defections that can undermine the stability of governments and the electoral process. It specifies grounds for disqualification of an MP or MLA if they voluntarily give up membership of their political party, or if they vote or abstain from voting in the House contrary to any direction issued by their political party, without prior permission. The law designates the presiding officer of the House (in this case, the Chairman of the Rajya Sabha) as the authority to decide on disqualification, often after consulting the Election Commission of India. Mr. Mann's direct appeal to the President seeks to initiate this process at the highest executive level.

While the Chief Minister's letter refers to seven MPs, prominent among the figures discussed in related political discourse is Rajya Sabha MP Raghav Chadha, although the specific grounds for each of the seven MPs' alleged defection would require individual examination. AAP leadership has consistently maintained that elected representatives must adhere to the party's principles and mandate. This current development underscores the party's efforts to address perceived breaches of trust and maintain party discipline within its parliamentary ranks. The party argues that such actions not only violate internal party rules but also challenge the integrity of the democratic process.

Key details surrounding the appeal include:
*   **Constitutional Basis:** The demand for action is rooted in the Tenth Schedule of the Indian Constitution, commonly known as the anti-defection law.
*   **Number of MPs:** The complaint specifically targets seven Rajya Sabha Members of Parliament.
*   **President's Role:** The President's intervention is sought to initiate the disqualification process, which typically involves a reference to the Election Commission for advice before a final decision by the presiding officer.
*   **Allegation:** MPs are accused of aligning with or joining the Bharatiya Janata Party despite being elected on Aam Aadmi Party tickets.

Following receipt of Chief Minister Mann's letter, President Droupadi Murmu's office will review the submission. Typically, such matters involving the disqualification of Members of Parliament are referred to the Chairman of the Rajya Sabha, who then consults with the Election Commission of India before making a final determination. The outcome of this process could potentially lead to the disqualification of the concerned MPs, setting a precedent for similar cases of alleged party defection in the future. Political observers will closely monitor the President's response and the subsequent actions of the Rajya Sabha Secretariat and the Election Commission.
