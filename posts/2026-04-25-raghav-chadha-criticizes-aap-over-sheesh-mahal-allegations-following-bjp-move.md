Raghav Chadha, a former prominent leader of the Aam Aadmi Party (AAP) who recently joined the Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP), has publicly criticized his erstwhile party over the ongoing "Sheesh Mahal 2.0" controversy. Chadha stated his decision to leave AAP was driven by "disgust, not fear," directly linking his departure to the alleged financial irregularities and deviation from the party's founding principles. The comments mark a significant escalation in the political discourse between the two parties and from a former insider.

Chadha's remarks came during a recent public statement, where he leveled accusations against the AAP leadership regarding alleged lavish expenditures. The "Sheesh Mahal 2.0" reference pertains to allegations surrounding the renovation and furnishing costs associated with the official residence of Delhi Chief Minister Arvind Kejriwal. This issue has been a point of contention and debate in Delhi's political landscape for several months, with the BJP consistently highlighting the reported expenses as a betrayal of AAP's original promises of austerity and good governance.

The former AAP Member of Parliament accused his former party of abandoning its core values, which were initially centered on anti-corruption and representing the common man. Chadha's shift to the BJP, and his subsequent vocal criticism, provides a new dimension to the allegations, offering an insider's perspective on the party's internal workings and perceived changes. His statement about leaving out of "disgust" directly challenges AAP's narrative and leadership.

Key details surrounding the controversy and Chadha's statements include:
*   **Alleged Expenditure:** The "Sheesh Mahal 2.0" controversy refers to accusations of exorbitant spending on the refurbishment of the official bungalow of Delhi Chief Minister Arvind Kejriwal. Critics have cited specific figures, alleging that a substantial amount of public money was utilized for this purpose.
*   **Political Context:** The Aam Aadmi Party, founded on an anti-corruption platform following the India Against Corruption movement, faces scrutiny over these expenditure claims, which its opponents argue contradict its initial ideological stance.
*   **Raghav Chadha's Background:** Chadha was a high-profile figure within AAP, serving as a spokesperson and Rajya Sabha MP before his defection to the BJP. His insights are viewed by the BJP as credible testimony against his former party.
*   **Motives for Departure:** Chadha's assertion of "disgust" as the reason for leaving directly contradicts any suggestions that his move was driven by political expediency or pressure, instead framing it as a moral stand against perceived corruption within AAP.

The intensified criticism from a former senior party member is expected to fuel further political debate and could influence public perception ahead of upcoming elections. The AAP has consistently defended its actions regarding the Chief Minister's residence, asserting that all expenditures were within official guidelines and necessary for security and functional requirements. However, Chadha's direct assault from within the BJP camp adds another layer of complexity to the ongoing political battle in the national capital. This issue is likely to remain a significant point of contention as both parties prepare for future electoral contests.
