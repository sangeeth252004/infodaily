Swati Maliwal, former chairperson of the Delhi Commission for Women (DCW), recounted an alleged assault she experienced in May 2024 at Delhi Chief Minister Arvind Kejriwal's official residence. Her statements were made on May 29, 2024, shortly after she officially joined the Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP). Maliwal described being "beaten up, threatened and dragged" during the incident, which led to a First Information Report (FIR) being filed and the arrest of Bibhav Kumar, Chief Minister Kejriwal's former aide.

The incident gained significant public attention in May 2024, prompting a police investigation and legal proceedings. Maliwal stated that her decision to join the BJP was driven by a commitment to continue her work for women's safety and to fight against what she termed "tyranny." She also claimed to have faced a "systematic smear campaign" from her former party colleagues following the alleged assault.

Key details surrounding the incident and its aftermath include:
*   **Alleged Assault:** Maliwal stated the incident occurred at the Chief Minister's residence in mid-May 2024.
*   **Accused Individual:** Bibhav Kumar, who served as Chief Minister Kejriwal's personal secretary, was identified as the individual involved in the alleged assault.
*   **Legal Action:** Maliwal filed an FIR against Kumar. He was subsequently arrested by the Delhi Police on May 18, 2024.
*   **Bail Granted:** A Delhi court granted Bibhav Kumar bail on May 27, 2024, just days before Maliwal's public statements after joining the BJP.
*   **Political Context:** Maliwal, a long-time member of the Aam Aadmi Party (AAP) and a prominent figure in women's rights advocacy as the DCW chief for nine years, transitioned to the BJP, a primary political rival of the AAP.

Maliwal emphasized that despite her extensive work for women's safety and her association with her former party, she felt isolated and unsupported in the aftermath of the alleged incident. She articulated her decision to align with the BJP as a continuation of her personal mission to address issues of injustice and ensure safety for women.

The legal proceedings related to the alleged assault remain ongoing. Delhi Police investigations are continuing, and further court hearings are anticipated. The incident and Maliwal's subsequent political move have generated significant discussion within political circles and among the public, highlighting issues of personal safety, political conduct, and legal accountability.
