A thrilling encounter in the Indian Premier League (IPL) 2026 between the Lucknow Super Giants (LSG) and the Kolkata Knight Riders (KKR) culminated in a Super Over, following a dramatic final over that saw bowler Mayank Tyagi deliver two no-ball beamers and batsman Mohammad Shami hit a crucial last-ball six. The high-stakes match, held as part of the ongoing IPL season, concluded with scores tied, forcing the tie-breaker to determine a winner.

The match had reached its climax with the final over of the second innings, where the target required by the batting side was within reach but presented a significant challenge. LSG's Mayank Tyagi, tasked with bowling the decisive over, found himself under immense pressure. The situation escalated rapidly when Tyagi bowled his first delivery of the over as a high full toss, deemed a no-ball beamer by the on-field umpire. This resulted in an immediate penalty of one run to the batting side and a free hit on the subsequent delivery, a standard cricket rule for such an infraction.

The intensity further heightened when Tyagi delivered a second high full toss in the same over, again called a no-ball beamer. Under IPL regulations and the Laws of Cricket, a bowler who delivers two such dangerous full tosses in an innings is immediately removed from the attack and is not permitted to bowl further in that innings. This rule necessitated a change in bowling, with another KKR player stepping in to complete the remaining deliveries of the over. The two beamers, in addition to the penalty runs, provided the batting team with two free hit opportunities, significantly altering the required run equation.

As the final delivery of the stipulated twenty overs approached, the scores were precariously balanced, with the batting team requiring a specific number of runs to either win or tie the match. Facing the new bowler on the last ball, Mohammad Shami of the Lucknow Super Giants showcased composure under pressure. Shami successfully dispatched the final delivery for a six, a monumental hit that brought the scores level. This decisive stroke nullified the efforts of the fielding side, resulting in a rare tie in the competitive league.

The rules surrounding beamers are stringent, designed to protect batsmen from potentially dangerous deliveries. A high full toss, often referred to as a "beamer," is deemed a no-ball if it passes the batsman above waist height on the full. Two such deliveries from the same bowler in an innings lead to their immediate removal from bowling duties. The subsequent free hits and penalty runs proved pivotal in LSG's chase, ultimately enabling Shami to achieve the match-tying six.

Following the tied scores, the match proceeded to a Super Over, a specialized tie-breaking format in T20 cricket. In a Super Over, each team bats for one over, with a maximum of three batsmen allowed. The team scoring the most runs in their Super Over innings is declared the winner of the match, ensuring a definitive outcome in such close contests within the IPL 2026 tournament. The outcome of the Super Over was awaited to conclude what had become an extended and dramatic sporting event.
