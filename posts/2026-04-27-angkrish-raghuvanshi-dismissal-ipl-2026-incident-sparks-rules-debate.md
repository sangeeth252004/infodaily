In a notable incident during an Indian Premier League (IPL) 2026 fixture between the Delhi Capitals and Kolkata Knight Riders on April 15 at the Arun Jaitley Stadium, Kolkata Knight Riders batsman Angkrish Raghuvanshi was dismissed for "obstructing the field." The rare form of dismissal, which occurred during the 15th over of KKR's innings, followed an appeal from Delhi Capitals captain and wicketkeeper Rishabh Pant, prompting immediate discussion among players, officials, and cricket analysts regarding the nuances of the game's laws and the "spirit of cricket."

The incident unfolded when Raghuvanshi, batting on 38 runs, played a shot towards the leg side. Following a quick throw from a Delhi Capitals fielder, intended to run out the non-striker, Raghuvanshi, while out of his crease and attempting to regain it, intercepted the ball with his hand. This action, interpreted as intentionally preventing a potential run-out, led to the Delhi Capitals appealing to the on-field umpires. After a review by the third umpire, the decision to declare Raghuvanshi out for obstructing the field was upheld, making it one of the few instances of such a dismissal in IPL history.

The dismissal is governed by Law 37 of the Laws of Cricket, which states that a batsman is out obstructing the field if he "wilfully obstructs or distracts an opponent by word or action." The law further clarifies that "it shall be for the umpire to decide whether such obstruction or distraction is wilful." In this specific instance, the crucial element was whether Raghuvanshi’s hand deflection was a deliberate act to prevent a run-out or an involuntary reaction. The third umpire's review concluded it was intentional, leading to the decision.
