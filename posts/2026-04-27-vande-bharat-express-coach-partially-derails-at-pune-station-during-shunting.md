A coach of the Pune-Secunderabad Vande Bharat Express experienced a partial derailment at Pune railway station on the morning of Tuesday, May 28, 2024. The incident occurred while the train was undergoing a shunting operation, moving from the maintenance depot towards the platform for its scheduled departure. Official reports confirmed that no injuries were sustained by any personnel or passengers during the event.

The incident involved one trolley of a Vande Bharat coach, which slipped off the tracks. Railway officials swiftly responded to the scene following the occurrence. Preliminary information indicated the derailment was minor and took place within the railway yard premises, away from public platforms during an operational maneuver.

This event underscores the operational complexities inherent in large-scale railway networks. The Vande Bharat Express trains represent a significant modernization effort by Indian Railways, designed for speed and enhanced passenger experience. Incidents such as this, even minor and without injury, prompt immediate procedural review and safety assessments to prevent recurrence and maintain operational integrity.

Key details surrounding the incident include:
*   **Date and Time:** Tuesday, May 28, 2024, morning hours.
*   **Location:** Pune railway station, within the operational yard area.
*   **Train Involved:** Pune-Secunderabad Vande Bharat Express.
*   **Nature of Incident:** Partial derailment involving one trolley of a single coach.
*   **Cause:** Occurred during a shunting operation, moving the train from the depot to the platform.
*   **Casualties:** No injuries reported.
*   **Service Impact:** The scheduled departure of the Pune-Secunderabad Vande Bharat Express was not disrupted. Indian Railways promptly arranged a spare rake to ensure the train departed on time, minimizing inconvenience to passengers.

Railway personnel initiated re-railing operations immediately after the incident. These technical operations involve specialized equipment and trained staff to safely place the derailed coach back onto the tracks. The prompt deployment of a spare rake highlights Indian Railways' contingency planning to maintain service continuity for its premium offerings.
