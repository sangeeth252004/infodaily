Srinagar, Jammu and Kashmir – Iltija Mufti, a prominent political figure in Jammu and Kashmir, has voiced strong objections to recent amendments in job recruitment eligibility rules within the Union Territory. Her protest specifically targets the perceived reduction or removal of Urdu language proficiency as a key criterion for government employment, a move she claims undermines the region's linguistic and cultural heritage. Mufti has publicly criticized the National Conference (NC) political party, attributing responsibility to them for these policy shifts.

The changes in question pertain to the qualifications required for various government positions in Jammu and Kashmir. Historically, Urdu has held significant status as one of the official languages of the region, and proficiency in it was often a mandatory or highly weighted factor in public sector hiring. Critics argue that diluting or eliminating this requirement could marginalize a significant portion of the population for whom Urdu is a primary language of education and administration, potentially disadvantaging them in the job market.

Iltija Mufti, daughter of former Chief Minister Mehbooba Mufti, articulated her concerns through public statements. She reportedly emphasized the deep-rooted connection of Urdu to Kashmiri identity and history, stating that the perceived removal of Urdu from eligibility criteria is an attempt to erode the linguistic and cultural fabric of Jammu and Kashmir. Her criticism of the National Conference indicates a political accusation, suggesting the party's alleged role or complicity in formulating or supporting these changes.

The protest by Iltija Mufti underscores a broader debate about language policy and its implications for public life and employment opportunities in the Union Territory. While specific details of the amended rules and the exact timing of their implementation are subjects of public discussion, the core of the controversy centers on the role of Urdu in the region's future administrative and educational landscape.

Key aspects highlighted by the protest include:
*   **Cultural Significance:** Urdu has historically served as a language of administration, literature, and daily communication for many in Jammu and Kashmir, reflecting a distinct cultural identity.
*   **Educational Impact:** Critics suggest its diminished role in eligibility criteria could de-incentivize its study and usage among younger generations, potentially affecting educational institutions focused on Urdu.
*   **Job Market Disadvantage:** Concerns exist that job aspirants proficient primarily in Urdu might face increased barriers to entry into government service if the language is no longer recognized as a vital skill.
*   **Political Context:** The protest takes place amidst ongoing political discourse in Jammu and Kashmir regarding identity, autonomy, and administrative policies.

As the political discourse continues, observers anticipate further reactions from various stakeholders, including linguistic groups, educational bodies, and other political parties. The government or relevant administrative authorities have not yet issued a comprehensive public statement specifically addressing Mufti's allegations and the broader concerns regarding Urdu's status in recruitment processes. The situation highlights the sensitive interplay between language, culture, and governance in Jammu and Kashmir, indicating that discussions surrounding these policy changes are likely to persist.
