The Federation of Indian Airlines (FIA), representing a significant portion of the country's domestic airline industry, has formally communicated to the Central Government that carriers are "on the verge of stopping operations" due to sustained and escalating Aviation Turbine Fuel (ATF) prices. The urgent communication, sent in early May 2024, highlights a critical financial strain on airlines and calls for immediate government intervention to prevent a potential industry-wide crisis.

The letter, addressed to key ministries including the Ministry of Civil Aviation and the Ministry of Finance, underscores the unprecedented financial pressure faced by carriers. Airlines report that the continuous rise in ATF costs, a major operational expenditure, is rendering current business models unsustainable. This situation, according to the FIA, threatens to severely disrupt air travel connectivity across India and impact the broader economy.

Aviation Turbine Fuel typically constitutes over 40% of an airline's total operating expenses in India. Recent months have seen multiple consecutive hikes in ATF prices, exacerbating financial challenges for carriers already navigating a competitive market. The industry has long advocated for a rationalization of taxes on jet fuel, which are significantly higher in India compared to many other global markets.

Key concerns and demands outlined by the FIA include:

*   **Tax Rationalization:** A primary demand is for a reduction in the various central and state-level taxes imposed on ATF. Currently, ATF is subject to central excise duties and state-level Value Added Tax (VAT), leading to higher domestic prices.
*   **Inclusion in Goods and Services Tax (GST):** The FIA has reiterated its long-standing plea for ATF to be brought under the ambit of the Goods and Services Tax (GST) regime. This move, according to the industry, would allow airlines to avail input tax credits, significantly reducing their tax burden and operating costs.
*   **Industry Viability:** The letter emphasizes that without immediate and substantial government support or policy changes, the financial viability of several airlines is at risk, potentially leading to job losses and reduced capacity.

The potential cessation of operations by Indian airlines could have widespread ramifications beyond the aviation sector. It would impact critical sectors such as tourism, business travel, and cargo logistics, which rely heavily on efficient air connectivity. Passengers could face reduced flight options and potentially higher ticket prices if airlines are forced to scale back services or cease operations.

The Central Government is expected to review the representation from the FIA. Discussions regarding the tax structure of ATF have been ongoing for several years, with the Ministry of Civil Aviation often advocating for measures to ease the financial burden on airlines. The coming weeks will likely determine the government's response to the industry's latest and most stark warning regarding its operational future.
