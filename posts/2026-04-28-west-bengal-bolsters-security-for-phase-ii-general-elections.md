Kolkata, West Bengal – Ahead of the second phase of the ongoing general elections, West Bengal has seen a substantial deployment of security personnel, transforming the state into a fortified zone for polling day. Approximately 2.3 lakh (230,000) personnel from the Central Armed Police Force (CAPF) have been stationed across various constituencies, complemented by 38,297 officers from the West Bengal Police. This extensive mobilization underscores the Election Commission of India's (ECI) commitment to ensuring free, fair, and peaceful elections.

The comprehensive security arrangements are designed to address potential challenges and instill confidence among voters. The CAPF, comprising various paramilitary forces, is primarily responsible for securing polling stations, conducting area domination patrols, and maintaining law and order in sensitive zones. Their presence aims to deter any attempts at voter intimidation or electoral malpractice. The deployment strategy focuses on covering critical areas and ensuring quick response capabilities across all polling booths in the constituencies voting in this phase.

The 38,297 personnel from the West Bengal Police are working in coordination with the CAPF. Their roles include local law enforcement, crowd management, regulating traffic, and assisting in general security duties around polling premises. This collaborative effort between central and state forces is a standard protocol adopted by the ECI to manage the logistical and security complexities of large-scale elections. The sheer number of personnel deployed reflects the scale of the electoral exercise and the emphasis placed on ensuring voter safety and a conducive environment for democratic participation.

*   **Central Armed Police Force (CAPF):** Approximately 2.3 lakh personnel
*   **West Bengal Police:** 38,297 officers
*   **Objective:** To ensure free, fair, and peaceful conduct of Phase II general elections.
*   **Roles:** CAPF for booth security, area domination; state police for local law enforcement and support.

The heightened security measures follow past election experiences in the state, where localized incidents have occasionally marred polling processes. By deploying such a significant force, election authorities aim to prevent any disruptions and facilitate a smooth electoral process. Voters are encouraged to exercise their franchise without apprehension, knowing that robust security mechanisms are in place to safeguard their rights and the integrity of the election.

As the state prepares for the upcoming polling day, the focus remains on the effective implementation of these security protocols. The ECI continues to monitor the ground situation closely, making adjustments as necessary to ensure that all polling activities proceed without incident. This substantial security presence is expected to remain in place throughout the polling period for Phase II, with similar arrangements anticipated for subsequent phases of the general elections in West Bengal.
