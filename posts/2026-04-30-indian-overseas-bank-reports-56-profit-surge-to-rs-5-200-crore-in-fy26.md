Chennai-headquartered Indian Overseas Bank (IOB) has reported a substantial 56% increase in its net profit, reaching Rs 5,200 crore for the fiscal year ending March 31, 2026. This performance marks a significant financial strengthening for the public sector bank, indicating enhanced operational efficiency and asset quality.

The recorded profit for FY26 represents a notable rise from Rs 3,333 crore in the preceding fiscal year, FY25. This growth reflects the bank's strategic initiatives focused on balance sheet management and robust credit expansion across various segments. Improved asset quality has been identified as a primary contributor to this profit surge, alongside healthy growth in net interest income.

The bank's financial statements indicate a significant reduction in its non-performing assets (NPAs). The Gross Non-Performing Assets (GNPA) ratio saw a substantial decline, improving from approximately 6.50% in FY25 to an estimated 4.15% by the end of FY26. Concurrently, the Net Non-Performing Assets (NNPA) ratio also experienced a considerable reduction, falling from around 1.60% to an estimated 0.95% during the same period. Such improvements directly contribute to profitability by reducing the need for provisioning against bad loans.

Key financial highlights for Indian Overseas Bank in FY26 include:
*   **Net Profit:** Rs 5,200 crore
*   **Year-on-Year Growth:** 56%
*   **Gross NPA Ratio:** Decreased to approximately 4.15%
*   **Net NPA Ratio:** Decreased to approximately 0.95%
*   **Total Advances Growth:** Estimated at 14.5%, driven by retail, agriculture, and MSME sectors
*   **Net Interest Income (NII) Growth:** Approximately 18.2%
*   **Capital Adequacy Ratio (CRAR):** Maintained at a robust level, estimated at 15.80%, well above regulatory requirements.

The robust credit growth observed in FY26, particularly across priority sectors and retail lending, has bolstered the bank's interest income. Furthermore, the bank's efforts in recoveries and upgrades of existing non-performing accounts have contributed to the overall improvement in its financial health. The sustained capital adequacy ratio above regulatory minimums ensures the bank's stability and capacity for future growth.

Looking ahead, Indian Overseas Bank's management is expected to maintain its focus on further strengthening the balance sheet, leveraging digital transformation initiatives, and expanding its credit portfolio while adhering to prudent risk management practices. The bank's improved financial standing is seen as a positive development within the broader Indian public sector banking landscape.
