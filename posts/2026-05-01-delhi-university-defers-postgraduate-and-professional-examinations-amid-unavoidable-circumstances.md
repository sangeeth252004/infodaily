New Delhi, India – Delhi University (DU) has announced the deferral of all postgraduate (PG) and professional examinations that were originally scheduled to take place between May 20 and May 25, 2024. The university cited "unavoidable circumstances" as the reason for the postponement, according to an official announcement released by the examination branch.

The decision impacts a significant number of students enrolled in various postgraduate and professional courses across the university and its affiliated colleges. The notice, issued by the Office of the Controller of Examinations, did not provide specific details regarding the nature of the "unavoidable circumstances," maintaining a general explanation for the deferral. University officials have indicated that revised dates for the postponed examinations will be communicated to students in due course, urging them to monitor the official DU website for updates.

The deferral comes as students were preparing for their end-of-semester assessments, marking a disruption to the academic calendar. While the university has not elaborated on the reasons, such deferrals can be prompted by a range of logistical, administrative, or external factors. The immediate impact is a period of uncertainty for affected students, who now await clarity on their examination schedule.

Key details regarding the deferral include:
*   **Affected Period:** Examinations originally slated from May 20, 2024, to May 25, 2024.
*   **Affected Courses:** All Postgraduate (PG) programs and Professional courses.
*   **Stated Reason:** "Unavoidable circumstances."
*   **Announcement Method:** Official notice from the Controller of Examinations.
*   **Rescheduling:** New dates for the deferred exams are yet to be announced.

The university's examination branch has advised all concerned students, faculty members, and colleges to take note of the change and disseminate the information widely. Students are specifically instructed to remain vigilant for subsequent notifications regarding the new examination timetable. This ensures that preparations can be adjusted accordingly once the revised schedule is made public. The administration aims to minimize disruption by announcing new dates as promptly as possible, allowing students adequate time to prepare for their rescheduled assessments. The university remains committed to conducting all examinations transparently and efficiently, prioritizing the academic progress of its students.
