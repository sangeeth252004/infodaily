**Jammu and Kashmir: Chief Minister Omar Abdullah Denies Plans to Drop Urdu in Revenue Jobs, Slams PDP**

SRINAGAR, J&K – Chief Minister Omar Abdullah of Jammu and Kashmir has unequivocally denied any government plans to remove Urdu language proficiency as a requirement for positions within the crucial revenue department. Responding forcefully to allegations, Abdullah publicly accused the opposition Peoples Democratic Party (PDP) of propagating "lies" regarding the administration's policy on official languages in public sector employment.

The controversy emerged following claims, reportedly circulated by the PDP, suggesting that Abdullah's government intended to diminish the role of Urdu in administrative jobs, specifically those within the revenue sector. Such a policy shift would mark a significant departure from long-standing practice, given Urdu’s status as an official language in Jammu and Kashmir and its extensive use in government documentation, land records, and administrative functions. Chief Minister Abdullah’s direct and sharp rebuttal underscores the political sensitivity of language policy and its intersection with government employment in the region.

Abdullah's statements, delivered in a firm tone, directly addressed the accusations, asserting that his government held no intention of altering the established language requirements for revenue posts. He emphasized that any claims to the contrary were unfounded and served a clear political agenda. The Chief Minister characterized these allegations as a deliberate attempt by the PDP to mislead the public, create unnecessary public anxiety, and generate political discord over an issue with deep cultural, historical, and practical significance in Jammu and Kashmir.

The debate surrounding the role of Urdu in government jobs carries substantial weight due to several critical factors:

*   **Official Language Status:** Urdu has long held the status of an official language in Jammu and Kashmir, deeply embedded within the administrative, judicial, and educational systems. Its historical prominence means that any perceived threat to its official use in government, particularly concerning employment, often triggers significant public and political discussion.
*   **Employment Implications:** For thousands of aspirants seeking government jobs, especially in departments like revenue where land records, official correspondence, and public interactions are traditionally conducted in Urdu, proficiency in the language remains a fundamental skill. Any alteration to this requirement would directly impact job seekers and potentially the efficiency of public service delivery.
*   **Political Dynamics:** This public exchange highlights the intense political rivalry between the National Conference, led by Chief Minister Abdullah, and the Peoples Democratic Party. In Jammu and Kashmir’s political landscape, language, regional identity, and employment policies frequently become central points of contention and campaigning.
*   **Cultural and Historical Context:** Language is intricately linked to cultural identity and heritage. Debates over its official recognition and utility, particularly in government administration, resonate deeply within communities and often become potent symbols in broader political narratives.

As political discourse continues in Jammu and Kashmir, Chief Minister Abdullah's definitive statements aim to clarify his administration's official policy regarding the use of languages in public service. The incident reflects the enduring importance of language and employment as key issues within the region's political and social fabric, and the ongoing scrutiny applied to government decisions and public statements affecting these vital areas. The focus remains on ensuring transparent governance and addressing public concerns without succumbing to misinformation.
