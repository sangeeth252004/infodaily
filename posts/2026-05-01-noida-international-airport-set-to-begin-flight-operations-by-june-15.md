Noida International Airport (NIA) in Jewar, Uttar Pradesh, is scheduled to commence flight operations by June 15, marking a significant milestone in India's aviation infrastructure development. The announcement indicates the readiness of the first phase of the greenfield airport project to begin serving passengers and cargo, positioned to alleviate air traffic congestion in the bustling Delhi-National Capital Region (NCR).

Developed by Yamuna International Airport Private Limited (YIAPL), a wholly-owned subsidiary of Switzerland's Zurich Airport International AG, NIA is envisioned as the second international airport serving the Delhi-NCR, complementing the existing Indira Gandhi International Airport (IGIA). The project aims to cater to the growing air travel demand in the region and boost economic activity across western Uttar Pradesh.

The initial phase of the Noida International Airport project focuses on establishing core operational capabilities. This includes the construction of a single runway, a passenger terminal building, and associated air traffic control (ATC) infrastructure. This phase is designed to handle an annual passenger capacity of approximately 12 million once fully operational. Financial outlays for the first phase are estimated at INR 5,730 crore (approximately USD 685 million). Construction has progressed steadily, with significant portions of the terminal building, runway, and airside infrastructure nearing completion or already completed.

Key details regarding the Noida International Airport project include:
*   **Location:** Situated in Jewar, within the Gautam Buddh Nagar district of Uttar Pradesh.
*   **Project Scale:** Upon full completion across multiple phases, NIA is projected to become India's largest airport, ultimately featuring up to five runways and an annual passenger capacity exceeding 70 million.
*   **Developer and Operator:** Yamuna International Airport Private Limited (YIAPL), a subsidiary of Zurich Airport International AG, holds the concession to develop and operate NIA.
*   **Connectivity:** The airport is planned as a multi-modal transport hub, integrating seamlessly with road networks, an upcoming metro extension, and dedicated rapid rail links to improve accessibility from Delhi and other parts of the NCR.
*   **Economic Impact:** The development is anticipated to generate substantial employment opportunities and attract significant investment in aviation-related industries, logistics, and hospitality sectors in the surrounding region.
*   **Sustainability Focus:** NIA is being developed with an emphasis on sustainable practices, aiming for net-zero emissions and incorporating green building principles and renewable energy solutions.
*   **Cargo Operations:** Alongside passenger services, the airport will feature dedicated cargo facilities, positioning it as a key logistics hub for northern India.

The initiation of flight operations by mid-June represents a critical step towards realizing the full potential of Noida International Airport as a major gateway for both domestic and international travel. The airport is expected to play a crucial role in enhancing regional connectivity and supporting the economic growth trajectory of Uttar Pradesh, cementing its position as a vital piece of India's expanding aviation landscape. Further phases of development are planned to progressively expand capacity and infrastructure in line with projected demand.
