The Supreme Court of India convened a special vacation bench on May 30, 2024, to hear a plea filed by the Trinamool Congress (TMC). The petition challenged a Calcutta High Court order mandating the exclusive deployment of central government employees for the counting of votes in the ongoing Lok Sabha elections 2024, scheduled for June 4. The hearing addressed concerns regarding the operational protocols for the meticulous tabulation of votes just days before the national results were due.

The Calcutta High Court, in an order issued on May 29, 2024, had directed the Election Commission of India (ECI) to ensure that only personnel from the central government were assigned duties for the critical vote counting process. This directive marked a significant procedural instruction, as state government employees have historically been part of the electoral counting teams. The High Court's ruling aimed to streamline and potentially enhance the integrity of the counting process, according to its pronouncement.

The Trinamool Congress, through its plea to the apex court, argued that the Calcutta High Court's directive could "derail the entire counting process" due to the sudden exclusion of experienced state government staff. The party contended that the reliance solely on central government employees represented a significant departure from established electoral practices. It raised concerns about potential logistical challenges and the disruption this change could introduce during the vote tabulation, particularly given the large scale of the Lok Sabha elections across numerous constituencies.

The special vacation bench of the Supreme Court, comprising Justices S.V.N. Bhatti and Dipankar Datta, was constituted to address the urgent nature of the plea. The hearing on May 30 examined the legal basis of the Calcutta High Court's order and the ramifications of changing personnel deployment guidelines so close to the counting day. Legal representatives for the TMC presented arguments highlighting the operational complexities and the precedent of involving state government employees in previous elections. The Election Commission of India, as the primary body responsible for election conduct, was expected to consider the Supreme Court's deliberations in its final preparations for June 4.

Key details surrounding the hearing include:
*   **Petitioner:** Trinamool Congress (TMC).
*   **Challenged Order:** Calcutta High Court's order dated May 29, 2024.
*   **Core Issue:** The exclusive deployment of central government employees for Lok Sabha vote counting on June 4, 2024, contrasting with the previous inclusion of state government staff.
*   **Supreme Court Bench:** Vacation Bench of Justices S.V.N. Bhatti and Dipankar Datta.
*   **Context:** Final preparations for Lok Sabha Elections 2024 vote counting, which determines the composition of the next Indian Parliament.

The Supreme Court's examination of the TMC's plea underscored the procedural complexities inherent in India's electoral process and the role of judicial review in ensuring fairness and efficiency. The outcome of the hearing, whether an affirmation or reversal of the High Court's order, was anticipated to have immediate implications for the ECI's operational plans for the nationwide vote counting on June 4. Election authorities continued to finalize arrangements, awaiting any further directives or clarifications from the apex court.
