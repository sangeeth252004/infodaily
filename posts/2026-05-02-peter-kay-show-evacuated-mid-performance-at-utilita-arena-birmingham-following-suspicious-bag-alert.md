A sold-out comedy performance by British comedian Peter Kay at Utilita Arena Birmingham was evacuated mid-show on Saturday, April 27, 2024, following the discovery of a suspicious bag. The incident, which occurred at approximately 9:30 PM, led to the temporary disruption of the 'Better Late Than Never' tour date, affecting thousands of attendees.

Arena management initiated the full evacuation of the venue as a precautionary measure after the item was identified. Attendees were instructed to exit the building calmly, a process managed by arena staff. Eyewitness accounts indicated an orderly departure from the venue. West Midlands Police confirmed they were made aware of the incident, which was primarily handled by the arena's internal security protocols.

The decision to evacuate prioritised public safety, adhering to established security procedures for such alerts. Operations were paused while security personnel investigated the unattended item. The disruption lasted for approximately 40 minutes as the assessment took place.

Key details of the incident include:
*   **Date and Time:** Saturday, April 27, 2024, around 9:30 PM.
*   **Location:** Utilita Arena Birmingham.
*   **Event:** Peter Kay's 'Better Late Than Never' comedy tour.
*   **Cause:** Discovery of a suspicious, unattended bag.
*   **Response:** Full evacuation initiated by arena management.
*   **Duration of Disruption:** Approximately 40 minutes.
*   **Resolution:** The bag was deemed non-suspicious following a security review.

Following a thorough investigation, the suspicious bag was determined to pose no threat, allowing the arena to be re-opened. Peter Kay subsequently returned to the stage, resuming his performance to an audience that largely re-entered the venue. The comedian acknowledged the interruption with humor, reassuring the crowd and continuing his show.

The incident highlights the stringent security measures in place at large public venues to ensure attendee safety. Despite the temporary halt, the efficient response by arena staff and security protocols enabled a swift resolution and the continuation of the scheduled event, minimising the overall impact on the performance and the audience experience. No arrests were made in connection with the incident, and normal operations at Utilita Arena Birmingham have since resumed.
