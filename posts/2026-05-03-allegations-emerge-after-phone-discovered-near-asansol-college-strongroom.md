ASANSOL, West Bengal – Tensions escalated near an election strongroom at Asansol College following the discovery of a mobile phone in an envelope purportedly marked "All officers of TMC." The incident, which occurred on Wednesday, May 29, 2024, prompted immediate allegations of electoral malpractice from the Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP), drawing a crowd of political workers and increasing scrutiny on the security of Electronic Voting Machines (EVMs).

The discovery was made during the period when EVMs are stored under continuous surveillance following the conclusion of polling and prior to the official counting of votes. Strongrooms are designated high-security zones, typically guarded by central armed police forces and monitored via CCTV cameras, to ensure the integrity of the electoral process. The presence of an unauthorized item, particularly one associated with a political party, within the vicinity of such a sensitive area, has raised concerns regarding adherence to Election Commission protocols.

Key details surrounding the incident include:
*   **Discovery Location:** The mobile phone was reportedly found in an envelope near the strongroom at Asansol College.
*   **Envelope Marking:** The envelope bore the inscription "All officers of TMC," according to reports.
*   **Allegations:** BJP spokesperson and Asansol Lok Sabha candidate, Locket Chatterjee, publicly stated concerns, alleging potential irregularities and questioning the strongroom's security.
*   **Local Response:** Following the allegations, a significant number of political workers from various parties gathered outside the college, leading to heightened security presence by police and election officials to manage the crowd and maintain order.
*   **EVM Security:** The incident underscores the critical importance of maintaining stringent security measures around strongrooms, which are central to safeguarding EVMs and VVPATs (Voter Verifiable Paper Audit Trail) units until counting day.

The BJP has formally called upon the Election Commission of India (ECI) to launch a thorough investigation into the matter. Party representatives emphasized the need for transparency and accountability, particularly given the sensitive nature of election strongrooms, which are designed to be impenetrable to unauthorized access or influence. The discovery has fueled broader discussions on the vigilance required during the post-polling, pre-counting phase of elections.

Election officials present at the scene initiated an inquiry into how the mobile phone came to be in the restricted area and its potential implications. Authorities have reiterated their commitment to upholding electoral integrity and ensuring that all protocols for strongroom security are strictly followed. As of now, no official statement regarding the ownership or purpose of the phone has been released by the Election Commission or local administration.

The incident is expected to maintain focus on the security arrangements at strongrooms across constituencies ahead of the vote counting scheduled for June 4, 2024. Further updates are anticipated as the Election Commission's investigation progresses and official findings are released.
