Financial analysts and investment firms have released their projections for global gold prices for the trading week commencing May 4, 2026. These outlooks detail anticipated price movements, key support and resistance levels, and the primary factors expected to influence the precious metal's performance across major exchanges such as the Multi Commodity Exchange (MCX) and the COMEX market. The analyses aim to provide guidance for market participants amidst ongoing economic and geopolitical considerations.

The current assessments highlight a period of potential volatility for gold, driven by a combination of macroeconomic indicators and international developments. Experts are closely monitoring inflation data, central bank monetary policy signals, and global currency fluctuations, particularly the strength of the US dollar, which often bears an inverse relationship with gold prices. Geopolitical tensions, notably those emanating from the Middle East, are also cited as significant factors with the potential to impact safe-haven demand for gold.

Key details from the analyst reports include specific price thresholds that traders and investors are advised to watch:

*   **Resistance Levels:** Analysts have identified significant resistance for COMEX gold in the range of $2,380 to $2,410 per ounce. For MCX gold, resistance is projected between ₹72,500 and ₹73,200 per 10 grams. Breaching these levels could indicate a strong upward momentum.
*   **Support Levels:** Conversely, critical support for COMEX gold is estimated to lie between $2,300 and $2,270 per ounce. On the MCX, support levels are noted in the range of ₹70,800 to ₹70,100 per 10 grams. A fall below these points might signal downward pressure.
*   **Market Drivers:**
    *   **Geopolitical Stability:** Persistent or escalating tensions in the Middle East region are consistently cited as a primary catalyst for increased safe-haven buying, potentially pushing gold prices higher. Conversely, any de-escalation could reduce this demand.
    *   **Inflation Expectations:** Stronger-than-expected inflation reports could bolster gold's appeal as a hedge against rising prices, while cooling inflation might lessen this impetus.
    *   **Interest Rate Policy:** The future trajectory of interest rates by major central banks, particularly the U.S. Federal Reserve, remains a key determinant. Higher real interest rates typically increase the opportunity cost of holding non-yielding assets like gold.
    *   **Currency Movements:** The performance of the US dollar against a basket of major currencies is also a crucial factor, as a stronger dollar typically makes gold more expensive for international buyers, potentially dampening demand.

The collective outlook for the week of May 4, 2026, suggests that gold prices will likely remain sensitive to breaking news and economic data releases. Market participants are expected to adjust their strategies based on the materialization of these forecasted influences. Monitoring central bank communications, geopolitical shifts, and incoming economic indicators will be essential for understanding gold's trajectory in the immediate future.
