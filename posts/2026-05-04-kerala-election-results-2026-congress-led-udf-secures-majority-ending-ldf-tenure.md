Thiruvananthapuram, Kerala – The Congress-led United Democratic Front (UDF) has emerged as the clear winner in the 2026 Kerala Assembly elections, securing a decisive majority and signaling a significant political transition in the state. Official results announced today confirm the UDF's comeback, bringing an end to the two consecutive terms of the Left Democratic Front (LDF) government led by Chief Minister Pinarayi Vijayan. The outcome reflects a notable shift in voter sentiment across Kerala's 140 constituencies.

The UDF’s victory ensures it will form the next state government, having surpassed the required majority mark of 71 seats. This electoral outcome represents a reversal from the previous two assembly elections, where the LDF had maintained a strong mandate. The election, held on various dates culminating in a unified vote count, saw high voter turnout across all 14 districts, indicating broad public engagement with the democratic process.

Key details from the election results include:
*   The United Democratic Front (UDF), comprising the Indian National Congress and its allies, secured approximately 85 seats, comfortably crossing the majority threshold.
*   The Left Democratic Front (LDF), led by the Communist Party of India (Marxist), is projected to win around 50 seats, marking a substantial reduction from its previous tally.
*   Other parties and independent candidates secured the remaining seats.
*   Several high-profile LDF ministers and prominent figures reportedly faced defeat in their respective constituencies, contributing to the front's overall diminished performance.
*   Voter turnout was recorded at approximately 75%, reflecting strong democratic participation across the state.

The mandate signals a fresh direction for Kerala's governance. Analysts suggest that issues such as economic development, employment, and state-specific policy decisions played crucial roles in influencing voter choices. The UDF's campaign focused on promises of administrative reforms and welfare initiatives, resonating with a significant portion of the electorate seeking change.

Following the declaration of results, the UDF leadership is expected to convene shortly to elect its parliamentary party leader, who will then be designated as the Chief Minister. The new government will face immediate challenges and priorities, including managing the state's economic landscape and addressing socio-political issues. The LDF, now in opposition, is anticipated to undertake a review of its electoral performance and strategize for its role in the new assembly. The transition of power is expected to proceed in an orderly manner, with the swearing-in ceremony of the new Chief Minister and cabinet likely to take place within the coming weeks.
