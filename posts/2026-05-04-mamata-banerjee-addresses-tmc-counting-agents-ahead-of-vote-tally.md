West Bengal Chief Minister and Trinamool Congress (TMC) chairperson Mamata Banerjee addressed party counting agents virtually on Monday, May 27, ahead of the Lok Sabha election vote counting scheduled for June 4. During her address, Banerjee urged agents to remain vigilant and fearless, stating, "No need to be afraid... victory will be ours after sunset."

The virtual meeting served as a final briefing for TMC agents tasked with overseeing the vote tabulation process across various counting centers in West Bengal. With the Lok Sabha election results nearing, the Trinamool Congress leadership emphasized the critical role of its representatives in ensuring transparency and preventing irregularities. Banerjee instructed agents to diligently monitor every stage of the counting process, from the opening of Electronic Voting Machines (EVMs) to the final declaration of results.

A key focus of the chief minister's address was the importance of thorough verification procedures. Agents were specifically advised to meticulously check seals on EVMs and to compare the numbers on the control unit with those recorded in Form 17C, a document that records the total votes polled at a particular polling station. This instruction follows past concerns raised by various political parties regarding the integrity of the voting and counting process. Banerjee reportedly highlighted previous instances where attempts were allegedly made to influence election outcomes, reinforcing the need for unwavering vigilance.

Furthermore, Banerjee urged agents not to leave the counting centers under any circumstances until the final results for their respective constituencies are officially declared and certified. She stressed the necessity of obtaining the duly signed Form 17C from the Returning Officer (RO) after the completion of counting, asserting its importance as proof of the official tally. The chief minister's message aimed to bolster the morale of the agents and equip them with specific guidelines to navigate the often-tense environment of election counting.

*   **Key Instructions for Counting Agents:**
    *   Remain present at counting centers until final results are officially announced.
    *   Verify seals on all Electronic Voting Machines (EVMs) before counting begins.
    *   Match control unit numbers with records documented on Form 17C.
    *   Obtain a signed copy of Form 17C from the Returning Officer.
    *   Report any anomalies or suspicious activities to party superiors immediately.

The TMC chairperson also reportedly advised agents against being intimidated by any officials or opposition representatives during the counting process. Her remarks underscored the party's determination to safeguard its electoral interests and ensure a fair and accurate reflection of the public mandate. The virtual session concluded with a message of optimism from Banerjee, who reiterated her belief in the party's strong performance and anticipated a favorable outcome once all votes are tallied.

As the nation prepares for the Lok Sabha election results on June 4, the instructions issued by Mamata Banerjee to her party's counting agents highlight the meticulous preparations political parties undertake to protect their votes. The role of these agents will be crucial in the coming days as millions of votes are counted, with the ultimate results shaping the political landscape for the next five years.
