Chandranath Rath, identified as a close aide to West Bengal's Leader of the Opposition and prominent Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP) leader Suvendu Adhikari, was found shot dead in the Madhyamgram area of North 24 Parganas district early Monday morning. Local police have initiated a murder investigation following the discovery of Rath's body, which bore multiple gunshot wounds.

Rath's body was discovered by residents near his residence in the Kajipara area of Madhyamgram late Sunday night or early Monday morning. Authorities were immediately alerted, and the body was subsequently sent for post-mortem examination to ascertain the exact cause and time of death. The incident has led to increased tension in the locality.

The killing has quickly drawn significant political attention due to Rath's close association with Suvendu Adhikari. Adhikari, confirming Rath's identity as a key associate, publicly alleged that the incident was a targeted murder. He asserted that Rath was targeted specifically due to his political affiliations with the BJP and his proximity to Adhikari himself. Adhikari, currently campaigning for the ongoing Lok Sabha elections in Bankura, demanded an impartial and thorough investigation into the matter.

Local law enforcement has registered a First Information Report (FIR) in connection with the killing, targeting unidentified assailants. As of the latest reports, no arrests have been made, and the police investigation is actively underway to identify the perpetrators and establish the motive behind the shooting. Forensic teams are reportedly collecting evidence from the scene.

The incident unfolds amidst a period of heightened political activity and contention in West Bengal, particularly with the ongoing phases of the Lok Sabha elections. Political killings and violence have historically been a sensitive issue in the state, often leading to accusations and counter-accusations among political parties. Following Rath's death, Suvendu Adhikari has accused the state's ruling Trinamool Congress (TMC) of orchestrating the killing, a charge the TMC has vehemently denied, dismissing it as politically motivated rhetoric.

The investigation is expected to continue with intensified efforts to gather evidence, question potential witnesses, and trace leads. Further political reactions and developments are anticipated as the police probe progresses and as the political discourse surrounding law and order in the state continues, particularly in the context of the general elections.
