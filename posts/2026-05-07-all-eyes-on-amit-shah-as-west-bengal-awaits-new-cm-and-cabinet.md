KOLKATA, WEST BENGAL – Union Home Minister Amit Shah arrived in Kolkata on Tuesday, initiating a series of crucial meetings with Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP) leaders and newly elected Members of the Legislative Assembly (MLAs) as West Bengal stands on the cusp of forming a new state government. His visit underscores the central leadership's direct involvement in the selection of the state's next Chief Minister and cabinet, following the recent assembly elections held on May 15, 2024. The state anticipates the formal announcement of its new political leadership within the coming days.

Mr. Shah's itinerary includes consultations with BJP's central observers, state unit president Sukanta Majumdar, and other senior leaders to finalize the party's choice for Chief Minister. The BJP emerged as the single-largest party in the 2024 West Bengal Assembly Elections, securing 152 seats in the 294-member house, surpassing the simple majority mark of 148. This electoral outcome marks a significant shift in the state's political landscape, which has historically been dominated by other parties.

The primary task for Mr. Shah and the central leadership is to facilitate a consensus choice for the legislative party leader, who will subsequently be sworn in as the Chief Minister. Sources within the party indicate that deliberations are focused on balancing experience with a fresh approach, while also considering regional representation and administrative capabilities. The selection process is critical for establishing a stable and effective government, capable of delivering on the party's electoral promises.

*   **Election Outcome:** BJP secured 152 seats out of 294 in the recently concluded West Bengal Assembly Elections, forming a clear majority.
*   **Central Observers:** Senior party leaders Saroj Pandey and Vinod Tawde have been dispatched by the BJP high command to oversee the legislative party meeting and ensure a smooth selection process.
*   **Key Meetings:** Mr. Shah is scheduled to meet individually with prominent state leaders and potential Chief Ministerial candidates before a collective decision is made.
*   **Potential Candidates:** Names such as Suvendu Adhikari, Leader of Opposition in the previous assembly, and Dr. Subhas Sarkar, a Union Minister from the state, are among those reportedly under consideration by the party high command.
*   **Oath-Taking Ceremony:** The newly elected MLAs are expected to take their oath of office by the end of the week, with the Chief Minister's swearing-in ceremony anticipated shortly thereafter at Raj Bhavan.

The political attention remains firmly fixed on Kolkata as the BJP leadership works towards forming its first government in West Bengal. The outcome of these high-level consultations is expected to shape the state's political trajectory for the next five years. An announcement regarding the Chief Minister-designate and the initial cabinet composition is widely anticipated before the weekend, signaling the formal commencement of the new administration.
