Delhi Capitals (DC) all-rounder Axar Patel has indicated a pragmatic outlook on the team's prospects for the Indian Premier League (IPL) 2024 playoffs, acknowledging that their chances are significantly diminished following a recent defeat. Patel's comments, made in the aftermath of DC's loss to Kolkata Knight Riders (KKR) on April 29, 2024, signaled a shift in focus towards future seasons, with the player reportedly stating, "Now, it's about 'next year'."

The Delhi Capitals faced a seven-wicket loss against KKR at the Eden Gardens in Kolkata. This defeat marked a critical setback for DC, further complicating their path to the playoff stage of the tournament. The team, led by Rishabh Pant, has struggled with inconsistency throughout the season, securing victories intermittently but failing to build sustained winning momentum.

Patel's statement highlights a growing realization within the DC camp regarding their precarious position on the IPL 2024 points table. As of April 30, 2024, the Delhi Capitals had played 11 matches, winning 5 and losing 6, accumulating 10 points. Their Net Run Rate (NRR) also remained a concern, currently at -0.442, which adds an additional challenge in a tightly contested league where NRR can be a decisive factor for teams tied on points.

The all-rounder elaborated on the team's current mindset, reportedly stating that while they would continue to play with full effort in their remaining matches, the mathematical realities have become increasingly challenging. For DC to qualify, they would likely need to win all their remaining fixtures and depend heavily on the results of other matches involving teams vying for the top four spots. This scenario is widely considered difficult given the competitive nature of the IPL.

The loss to KKR saw DC post a total of 153 runs, which KKR successfully chased down with 21 balls to spare, underlining the challenges DC faced in both their batting and bowling departments during the crucial encounter. Despite individual performances, including Patel's own contributions with both bat and ball in various matches, the team has not consistently performed as a cohesive unit capable of dominating opponents.
