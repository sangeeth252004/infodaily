Indian cricketers Yashasvi Jaiswal and Shafali Verma have received official notices from the National Anti-Doping Agency (NADA) concerning missed dope tests. The notices, issued in accordance with global anti-doping regulations, pertain to administrative non-compliance with the "whereabouts clause" rather than a failed drug test. This development initiates a formal review process by the anti-doping body.

The notices issued to Jaiswal, a prominent batter for the Indian men's team, and Verma, a key player for the Indian women's team, stem from their obligations under the World Anti-Doping Agency (WADA) Code, which NADA enforces domestically. Under these rules, athletes registered in a testing pool are required to provide their accurate whereabouts information to enable out-of-competition drug testing. This includes specifying a 60-minute window each day when they will be available at a particular location for testing.

Failure to provide accurate or timely whereabouts information, or being unavailable for a test during the specified window, can constitute a "missed test" or a "filing failure." Accumulating three such infractions—whether missed tests, filing failures, or a combination—within a 12-month period can lead to an Anti-Doping Rule Violation (ADRV) and subsequent sanctions, including potential bans from competition.

Sources close to the development indicate that these notices serve as a procedural alert, allowing the players an opportunity to explain the circumstances surrounding the alleged missed tests. It is a standard component of the anti-doping framework designed to ensure accountability and adherence to clean sport protocols. Both Jaiswal and Verma are high-profile athletes who regularly feature in national and international cricket, placing them under strict anti-doping surveillance.

NADA operates under the aegis of the Ministry of Youth Affairs and Sports, Government of India, and is responsible for promoting, coordinating, and monitoring the doping control programme in sports in India. Its mandate aligns with the principles and rules laid down by WADA, which aim to protect the integrity of sport and the health of athletes worldwide. The Board of Control for Cricket in India (BCCI) has a long-standing partnership with NADA to ensure compliance with these regulations within Indian cricket.

At this stage, the receipt of a notice does not imply guilt or an immediate sanction. It signifies the commencement of a formal process where the athletes, through their representatives, can submit explanations or supporting documentation to NADA. The agency will then review these submissions to determine if a violation occurred and what, if any, further action is warranted. This mechanism ensures due process for athletes under the anti-doping code.

The situation underscores the stringent requirements placed on professional athletes regarding anti-doping compliance, even when not actively competing. The next steps will involve the players' responses to NADA, followed by a review by the agency to conclude the matter in accordance with established anti-doping protocols.
