CHENNAI, Tamil Nadu – The anticipated swearing-in of TVK party leader Vijay as Chief Minister of Tamil Nadu was delayed by five days following a critical period of intense political negotiations. The delay stemmed from the initial inability of the TVK and its allies to secure the requisite legislative majority, necessitating a concerted effort to increase their seat count from 107 to the crucial 120 mark within the Tamil Nadu Legislative Assembly.

The 5-day period was marked by significant political activity aimed at consolidating support to form a stable government. In the Tamil Nadu Legislative Assembly, a simple majority typically requires support from 118 members out of a total of 234 elected seats. The target of 120 indicated a strategic aim to achieve a comfortable working majority, allowing for stable governance and the effective passage of legislation. The uncertainty surrounding government formation created a period of political apprehension within the state.

Following the initial election results, TVK and its pre-election allies collectively held 107 seats, placing them short of the majority threshold. This necessitated a series of cross-party dialogues and engagements with independent legislators to bridge the gap. The process involved extensive negotiations concerning policy alignments, ministerial portfolios, and assurances of support from smaller parties and unaligned Members of Legislative Assembly (MLAs).

Key developments during the five-day period included:
*   **Initial Count:** TVK and its alliance secured 107 seats, falling 13 seats short of the 120-seat target.
*   **Negotiation Period:** Five consecutive days of intensive discussions were conducted with various political entities and independent representatives.
*   **Target Achieved:** The 120-seat threshold was reportedly met through securing commitments from a combination of smaller regional parties and independent MLAs.
*   **Government Formation:** The successful acquisition of additional support enabled the formation of a government with a stable majority.

The culmination of these efforts saw the alliance successfully reaching the 120-seat target, paving the way for Vijay to officially assume the Chief Minister's office. This resolution brought an end to the period of political uncertainty and allowed for the formal establishment of the new state government. The process underscored the intricate nature of coalition politics and the importance of post-election negotiations in multi-party systems to achieve governance.

With the majority secured, the focus has now shifted to the formal swearing-in ceremony of Chief Minister Vijay and his cabinet. The incoming administration is expected to prioritize immediate policy implementation and address the electoral promises made during the campaign. The stability achieved through the formation of a clear majority is anticipated to provide a foundation for legislative action and state governance in the coming term.
