Nottinghamshire Police have arrested a 40-year-old man on suspicion of attempted murder following a car collision that injured five men in Arnold. The incident occurred on Front Street in the early hours of Friday, May 17, with emergency services responding to reports around 12:45 AM. The arrested suspect remains in police custody as investigations continue into the circumstances.

The collision involved a vehicle striking five male pedestrians. Three of the men are reported to have sustained non-life-threatening injuries and received hospital treatment. The other two men were treated for minor injuries at the scene and subsequently released. Immediately following the incident, Front Street was closed for several hours to allow officers to conduct their initial inquiries and thoroughly examine the scene.

Detectives from Nottinghamshire Police's Major Incident Team are leading the probe. Superintendent Paul Kirkby appealed to the public for assistance, stating, "This was a very serious incident that has led to a significant police response. Our team of detectives is working hard to establish exactly what happened, and we are keen to speak to anyone who may have witnessed the incident or has any information that could assist our investigation."

Key details regarding the ongoing investigation include:
*   **Incident Location:** Front Street, Arnold, Nottinghamshire.
*   **Time of Incident:** Approximately 12:45 AM, Friday, May 17, 2024.
*   **Suspect Status:** A 40-year-old man arrested on suspicion of attempted murder.
*   **Victim Count:** Five men injured.
*   **Injury Status:** Three men sustained non-life-threatening injuries requiring hospital treatment; two men received minor injuries and were released at the scene.
*   **Road Impact:** Front Street was closed for several hours to facilitate police investigation.
*   **Police Appeal:** Witnesses, individuals with CCTV footage, or dashcam recordings from the vicinity of Front Street around the time of the incident are urged to contact Nottinghamshire Police via 101, quoting incident number 0029 of May 17, 2024.

The police investigation is actively progressing, with officers working to piece together the sequence of events. Authorities have confirmed that additional patrols will be deployed in the area to reassure the community as the inquiry develops. Further updates are expected as new information emerges.
