Police in Greater Noida have launched a significant investigation into the alleged murder of a woman, who was reportedly found deceased within 90 days of her marriage. Authorities have identified her husband and in-laws as suspects in the case, which is believed to stem from an escalating property dispute. The incident has drawn considerable attention to the issue of domestic violence and marital disputes within the region.

The victim, identified by authorities as 24-year-old Ms. Priya Sharma, was reportedly found dead at her marital residence in Sector 150, Greater Noida, on May 15, 2024. Local police were alerted to the situation and promptly arrived at the scene to initiate preliminary inquiries. Forensic teams were also deployed to collect evidence vital to the ongoing investigation.

Initial reports from the Greater Noida Police suggest that Ms. Sharma had been married for approximately three months. Her marriage took place on February 20, 2024. The primary motive under investigation by law enforcement is an alleged property dispute between the victim and her in-laws. Police statements indicate that tensions regarding property ownership, possibly concerning a family asset or inheritance, may have significantly escalated, culminating in the fatal incident. Sources close to the investigation have reported that the phrase "Khoon ka badla khoon" (blood for blood) was used in connection with the alleged motive, pointing towards a deep-seated grievance regarding the property.

Following intensive questioning and preliminary evidence gathering, the Greater Noida Police have reportedly apprehended three individuals in connection with the alleged homicide. Those arrested include Ms. Sharma’s husband, 28-year-old Mr. Rahul Singh, and his parents, Mr. Alok Singh and Mrs. Sunita Singh.

*   The accused individuals have been taken into police custody.
*   Charges are being filed under relevant sections of the Indian Penal Code (IPC), including Section 302 for murder.
*   Further investigations are exploring additional charges, potentially related to dowry death, although the focus remains on the property dispute as the primary catalyst.
*   Authorities have stated that statements from family members and neighbors are being recorded to piece together the sequence of events leading to Ms. Sharma's death.

The arrests were made swiftly after the incident came to light, with police emphasizing their commitment to a thorough investigation. Superintendent of Police (Rural) Greater Noida, Mr. Ashok Kumar, stated that all angles are being meticulously examined and that justice would be served.

The arrested individuals are currently undergoing further questioning by police officials. Authorities are in the process of compiling a comprehensive charge sheet based on the collected evidence, forensic analysis, and witness testimonies. Legal proceedings are anticipated to commence as the police finalize their investigation. The case continues to highlight concerns regarding the safety of women in marital homes and the resolution of domestic and property-related disputes.
