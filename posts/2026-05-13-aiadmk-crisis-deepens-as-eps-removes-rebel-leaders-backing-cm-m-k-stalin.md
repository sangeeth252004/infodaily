Chennai, Tamil Nadu – The All India Anna Dravida Munnetra Kazhagam (AIADMK) has witnessed a further escalation of its internal power struggle, as party General Secretary Edappadi K. Palaniswami (EPS) announced the removal of prominent leaders, including former Coordinator O. Panneerselvam (OPS), from all party positions. The decision, made public on June 12, 2024, follows accusations that these individuals supported Tamil Nadu Chief Minister M.K. Stalin and the ruling Dravida Munnetra Kazhagam (DMK) government during a recent floor test.

This development underscores the deepening factionalism within the AIADMK, which has been grappling with leadership challenges since the demise of its former leader, J. Jayalalithaa, in 2016. The move by EPS is viewed as an assertive step to consolidate his authority and address perceived dissent within the party ranks, particularly those seen aligning with rival political forces.

Key details of the recent action include:
*   **Individuals Removed:** O. Panneerselvam, along with R. Vaithilingam, P.H. Manoj Pandian, and J.C.D. Prabhakar, have been stripped of their party posts and primary membership.
*   **Reason Cited:** The official announcement stated that these leaders were acting against the party's interests and had extended support to Chief Minister M.K. Stalin during a crucial floor test in the Tamil Nadu Legislative Assembly. This alignment with the DMK, AIADMK's primary political rival, was deemed an act of defiance by the party leadership.
*   **Background of Conflict:** The feud between EPS and OPS has been protracted, marked by legal battles and power struggles over the AIADMK's leadership. EPS's elevation to General Secretary was affirmed by the Supreme Court, solidifying his position and diminishing OPS's influence within the party structure.

The removal of OPS and his associates signals EPS's resolve to eliminate dissenting voices and enforce party discipline. O. Panneerselvam, a veteran leader and former Chief Minister, has consistently challenged EPS's leadership, advocating for a dual leadership structure that was in place for some time after Jayalalithaa's death. This latest action by EPS effectively severs OPS's remaining ties to the official party apparatus.

The implications of these expulsions are significant for Tamil Nadu politics. The AIADMK, currently the principal opposition party, faces the challenge of presenting a united front ahead of future elections. Further fragmentation could weaken its electoral prospects and create opportunities for other parties. It also raises questions about the long-term stability and leadership trajectory of one of Tamil Nadu's most influential political outfits. While EPS aims to project strength and unity under his leadership, the ongoing internal strife continues to be a defining characteristic of the AIADMK's current political landscape.

Observers anticipate that the removed leaders may explore legal avenues or seek to form new political alliances, potentially leading to further realignments within the state's political spectrum. The AIADMK's ability to navigate these internal divisions will be crucial for its relevance in Tamil Nadu's evolving political environment.
