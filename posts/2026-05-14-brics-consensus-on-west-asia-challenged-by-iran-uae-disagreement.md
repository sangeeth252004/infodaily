A recent meeting of the BRICS bloc saw a significant disagreement between new member states Iran and the United Arab Emirates (UAE) over issues pertaining to West Asia, reportedly casting uncertainty on the group's ability to forge a unified consensus on the region. The incident, which occurred during discussions aimed at developing a collective BRICS position, underscored the internal complexities facing the expanded intergovernmental organization.

The disagreement emerged as BRICS, originally comprising Brazil, Russia, India, China, and South Africa, embarked on its first year with a significantly expanded membership. On January 1, 2024, Iran and the UAE, alongside Egypt, Ethiopia, and Saudi Arabia, formally joined the bloc. This expansion was widely seen as a strategic move to amplify the group's global influence and provide a stronger voice for the Global South in international affairs, particularly in challenging established geopolitical norms and advocating for a multipolar world order.

However, the reported friction between two key West Asian members highlights potential hurdles in achieving cohesive foreign policy positions. While specific details of the "sparring" were not immediately made public, the broader relationship between Iran and the UAE has historically involved points of contention. These include:

*   **Territorial Disputes:** Long-standing claims over the three Persian Gulf islands of Greater Tunb, Lesser Tunb, and Abu Musa, which Iran controls but the UAE asserts sovereignty over.
*   **Regional Security Postures:** Differing perspectives on regional security architectures and alliances, often influenced by their respective relationships with global powers and other regional actors.
*   **Broader Geopolitical Dynamics:** Divergent approaches to various regional conflicts and geopolitical shifts across the Middle East.

The BRICS platform aims to foster cooperation across economic, political, and cultural domains. For the bloc to effectively project a united front on complex international issues, especially those concerning stability and development in regions where its members are directly involved, achieving internal consensus is paramount. The incident suggests that despite shared objectives of economic cooperation and a desire for a multipolar world, deep-seated regional rivalries and distinct national interests continue to present challenges within the enlarged BRICS framework.

Observers suggest that the ability of BRICS to manage and reconcile these internal differences will be crucial for its aspirations of becoming a more influential geopolitical actor. The absence of immediate resolution to the reported disagreement indicates that diplomatic efforts may be ongoing to bridge the gap between Tehran and Abu Dhabi within the BRICS context. Future meetings and high-level engagements within the bloc will likely focus on navigating these internal dynamics, as BRICS endeavors to formulate coherent strategies on pressing global and regional matters, including the volatile landscape of West Asia. The episode serves as an early indicator of the complex diplomatic work required to solidify the expanded bloc's collective voice and influence.
