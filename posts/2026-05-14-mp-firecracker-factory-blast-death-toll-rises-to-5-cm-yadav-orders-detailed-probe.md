The death toll from a recent firecracker factory blast in Madhya Pradesh has risen to five, authorities confirmed. The incident, which occurred at a manufacturing unit in the state, has prompted Chief Minister Mohan Yadav to order a comprehensive investigation into its causes and circumstances.

Emergency services were deployed to the site following the explosion. While initial reports focused on the immediate aftermath and rescue efforts, the number of confirmed fatalities has since increased. Details regarding the exact location within Madhya Pradesh and the precise time of the blast are part of the ongoing official inquiry. The nature of the explosion in a facility handling volatile materials suggests a significant industrial accident.

Chief Minister Yadav's directive for a detailed probe underscores the gravity of the incident. The investigation is expected to delve into several critical aspects, including the factory's operational permits, adherence to safety regulations, storage protocols for explosive materials, and the cause of ignition. Such inquiries typically aim to determine if any negligence contributed to the incident and to hold responsible parties accountable.

Key details confirmed regarding the incident:
*   **Fatalities:** The confirmed death toll stands at five individuals.
*   **Location:** The blast occurred at a firecracker manufacturing factory in Madhya Pradesh.
*   **Government Response:** Chief Minister Mohan Yadav has mandated a detailed investigation.
*   **Investigation Scope:** The probe will seek to establish the cause of the explosion and identify any regulatory or operational lapses.
