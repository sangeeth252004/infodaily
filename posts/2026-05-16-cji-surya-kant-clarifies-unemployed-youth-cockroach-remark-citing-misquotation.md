Chief Justice of India (CJI) Surya Kant has issued a public clarification regarding recent media reports attributing an "unemployed youth cockroach" remark to him. The CJI stated that his oral observations were "misquoted" and taken out of context, emphasizing that he never used the word "cockroach" to describe unemployed youth directly.

The clarification comes after widespread media coverage and public discussion surrounding remarks allegedly made during a recent court proceeding. Reports had suggested the Chief Justice used the analogy in a disparaging manner towards unemployed individuals, sparking considerable debate.

During his clarification, CJI Surya Kant underscored his deep concern for the nation's youth, particularly those facing unemployment challenges. He explained that his intention was to express apprehension about how unemployed young people might be exploited or misled by certain elements, rather than to disparage the youth themselves. The analogy, he clarified, was aimed at the manipulative forces or individuals who might take advantage of the vulnerable situation of the unemployed, not at the youth seeking employment.

*   **Correction of Narrative:** The Chief Justice firmly denied using the term "cockroach" in reference to unemployed individuals, asserting that such an interpretation misrepresents his judicial observations.
*   **Contextual Misinterpretation:** He indicated that specific phrases were isolated from the broader discussion, leading to an inaccurate portrayal of his sentiments.
*   **Concern for Youth:** CJI Surya Kant reiterated his compassion and understanding for the plight of unemployed youth, highlighting his commitment to ensuring justice and opportunity for all citizens.

The clarification seeks to rectify the public perception generated by the initial reports, aiming to restore the true context and intent behind his original statements. Senior legal correspondents noted that judicial pronouncements, particularly oral observations, are often subject to varied interpretations and reporting, underscoring the importance of accurate transcription and contextual understanding.

This incident highlights the critical relationship between judicial proceedings, media reporting, and public discourse, particularly concerning matters of significant social and economic relevance. The Chief Justice's proactive clarification underscores a commitment to transparent communication and accountability in public office. Further details on the specific case or proceedings during which the original remarks were made have not been provided alongside the clarification.
