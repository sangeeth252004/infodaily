Indian Army Chief General Manoj Pande issued a stern warning to Pakistan on Monday, June 10, 2024, stating that India reserves the right to respond decisively if its western neighbor continues to harbor terrorists and facilitate cross-border infiltration. The Chief's remarks underscore India's firm stance against state-sponsored terrorism and its commitment to national security, particularly along the Line of Control (LoC) in Jammu and Kashmir.

General Pande, speaking at a public event, articulated India's consistent position that the onus is on Pakistan to cease its support for terrorist groups operating from its soil. He highlighted the ongoing challenges posed by such activities, including attempts to destabilize the region and impede peace efforts. The warning comes amid continued efforts by the Indian armed forces to counter infiltration bids and maintain vigil on the country's borders.

The Army Chief’s statement serves as a reiteration of India's zero-tolerance policy towards terrorism emanating from across the border. India has consistently provided evidence of Pakistan’s alleged involvement in nurturing militant outfits and using them as proxies against India. These activities, General Pande noted, not only threaten India’s sovereignty but also undermine regional stability and international counter-terrorism efforts.

Key aspects highlighted in the Army Chief’s address included:

*   **Persistent Threat**: The continued presence and operation of terrorist launch pads and training camps within Pakistan, especially close to the Line of Control.
*   **Infiltration Attempts**: Regular attempts by armed terrorists to infiltrate Indian territory, often supported by cover fire from Pakistani forces.
*   **Technological Shift**: The increasing use of drones and other unmanned aerial vehicles for smuggling weapons, narcotics, and surveillance across the border.
*   **India's Resolve**: An unwavering commitment from the Indian Armed Forces to thwart all such attempts and respond with necessary measures to protect national interests.

General Pande emphasized that India has demonstrated its capability and resolve to respond effectively to acts of terrorism. He referred to past actions, asserting that India has proven its capacity to undertake operations against terrorist infrastructure when deemed necessary for national security. Such statements reinforce the potential for robust responses should the situation warrant it.

The Indian government has repeatedly called upon Pakistan to take demonstrable action against terrorist groups like Lashkar-e-Taiba (LeT), Jaish-e-Mohammed (JeM), and Hizbul Mujahideen (HM), which are proscribed internationally. The Army Chief's latest warning signals that despite diplomatic efforts, the security situation along the LoC remains a critical concern, necessitating continuous vigilance and a prepared response from the Indian military.

Looking ahead, the warning is expected to reinforce India's defensive and counter-terrorism postures. The Indian Army is maintaining a robust deployment along the LoC, employing advanced surveillance technologies and tactical responses to counter evolving threats. The statement also underscores the ongoing need for international pressure on Pakistan to dismantle terrorist networks operating within its borders, emphasizing that regional peace is contingent upon an end to such hostilities.
