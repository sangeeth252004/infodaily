Kolkata, India – Gujarat Titans (GT) batsman Sai Sudharsan was forced to retire hurt during his team's Indian Premier League (IPL) 2026 fixture against Kolkata Knight Riders (KKR) at Eden Gardens on April 25, 2026. The left-handed opener sustained a blow to his right elbow from a bouncer, an incident that prompted immediate medical assessment and concern within the GT camp.

The injury occurred in the 13th over of Gujarat Titans' innings. Sudharsan, batting on 34 runs off 28 deliveries, attempted to pull a short-pitched delivery from KKR fast bowler Harshit Rana. The ball rose sharply, striking him directly on the elbow. Following the impact, Sudharsan appeared to be in considerable discomfort, immediately dropping his bat and clutching his arm. The GT medical team, including head physio Rohit Soni, rushed onto the field to assess the situation. After a brief consultation, Sudharsan was escorted off the field, unable to continue his innings, with the score at 98 for 2 wickets.

Sudharsan's retirement significantly impacted the Gujarat Titans' batting momentum. Prior to the injury, he had established a stable partnership with captain Shubman Gill, contributing to a crucial 62-run stand for the second wicket. His departure necessitated a change in batting order, bringing in David Miller earlier than anticipated. While GT managed to post a competitive total of 165 for 7, the loss of Sudharsan's presence at a critical juncture was notable. KKR went on to win the match by 5 wickets in the final over.

Following the match, a preliminary assessment by the GT medical staff confirmed significant bruising and swelling to the elbow area. Sai Sudharsan was transported to a local medical facility for precautionary scans, including X-rays, to rule out any bone fractures. As of the latest update, no official statement has been released regarding the extent of the injury or a timeline for his return.

The immediate concern for Gujarat Titans is Sudharsan's availability for their upcoming fixtures. He has been a consistent performer for the franchise since his debut, scoring 362 runs in 13 matches in the IPL 2025 season at an average of 32.90. His reliable starts at the top of the order are crucial to GT's batting strategy. The team management is expected to provide an update once the scan results are thoroughly evaluated. Gujarat Titans' next match is scheduled for April 29 against Royal Challengers Bangalore. The team will closely monitor Sudharsan's condition, as his absence could necessitate tactical adjustments to their playing XI.
