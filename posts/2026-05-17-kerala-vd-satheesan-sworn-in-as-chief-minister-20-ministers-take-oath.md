VD Satheesan was formally sworn in as the Chief Minister of Kerala today, marking the official commencement of the state's new government. The ceremony, held at the Raj Bhavan in Thiruvananthapuram, also saw 20 other ministers take their oath of office and secrecy, thereby constituting the new state cabinet. This event formally establishes the administrative leadership for Kerala for the upcoming term.

The oath-taking ceremony was administered by the Governor of Kerala. Chief Minister Satheesan, a prominent political figure, took his oath pledging allegiance to the Constitution and committing to dedicated service for the people of Kerala. Following his oath, the 20 designated ministers were administered their oaths individually, each committing to their respective roles in the state's governance. The event was attended by various dignitaries, including senior political leaders, high-ranking state officials, and invited guests.

The formation of this 21-member cabinet, including the Chief Minister, signifies a crucial phase in the state's political landscape. Each minister will be responsible for overseeing specific government departments, ranging from public finance and infrastructure to social welfare and public safety. The selection of these ministers follows extensive deliberations, aimed at forming a cohesive and effective administrative body.

Key details from the ceremony include:
*   **Chief Minister:** VD Satheesan
*   **Cabinet Strength:** 20 additional ministers sworn in
*   **Event Location:** Raj Bhavan, Thiruvananthapuram
*   **Administering Authority:** Governor of Kerala

Following the conclusion of the oath-taking ceremony, discussions concerning the allocation of portfolios among the newly appointed ministers are actively underway. These critical talks will determine which specific government departments each minister will lead. Portfolio assignments are a fundamental step in operationalizing the new government, aligning ministerial responsibilities with strategic priorities and individual competencies.

Sources indicate that these discussions are being conducted with urgency, with an announcement regarding portfolio distributions expected shortly. Once these allocations are finalized, the newly constituted cabinet is anticipated to convene its inaugural meeting. This first meeting will likely focus on outlining the immediate governmental agenda, addressing pressing state issues, and setting the strategic direction for the administration. The smooth transition of power and the swift establishment of ministerial roles are essential for ensuring continuity in state governance and the timely implementation of public policies. The government's immediate focus will now shift to its operational phase, addressing the administrative challenges and aspirations of Kerala's populace.
