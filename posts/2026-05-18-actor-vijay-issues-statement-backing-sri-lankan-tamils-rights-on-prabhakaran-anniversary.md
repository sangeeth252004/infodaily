CHENNAI – Actor Vijay, leader of the recently formed political party Tamilaga Vettri Kazhagam, issued a statement on May 18, advocating for the rights of Sri Lankan Tamils. The statement coincided with the 15th death anniversary of Liberation Tigers of Tamil Eelam (LTTE) leader V. Prabhakaran, a date that also marks the official end of the Sri Lankan civil war in 2009. The official announcement from his party underscored a commitment to the "long struggle for equality and self-determination" of the Tamil community in Sri Lanka.

The statement, released through official channels, highlighted the ongoing suffering of Sri Lankan Tamils and called for them to be granted a "dignified life." Vijay's communication emphasized that the pursuit of justice and equality for the community is a historical struggle that requires continued attention and support. The actor-turned-politician's stance resonates with long-standing political sentiments in Tamil Nadu, where support for the Sri Lankan Tamil cause has been a significant electoral issue across various political parties for decades.

This public declaration by Vijay carries particular weight given his recent entry into electoral politics. In February 2024, Vijay announced the launch of his political party, the Tamilaga Vettri Kazhagam, with intentions to contest the 2026 Tamil Nadu Assembly elections. His statement on such a sensitive and historically charged anniversary signals his party's alignment with a core issue that has historically mobilised voters in the state.

Key details surrounding the statement and its context include:

*   **Date of Statement:** May 18, 2024.
*   **Anniversary:** The 15th death anniversary of LTTE leader V. Prabhakaran, and the official conclusion of the Sri Lankan civil war.
*   **Core Message:** Advocacy for the rights, equality, and self-determination of Sri Lankan Tamils, emphasizing the need for a dignified life.
*   **Political Context:** Issued by actor Vijay, leader of the Tamilaga Vettri Kazhagam, a political party established earlier this year.
*   **Historical Significance of May 18:** Marks the conclusion of the nearly three-decade-long conflict in Sri Lanka, which resulted in significant loss of life and displacement, particularly within the Tamil community.

Historically, political parties in Tamil Nadu, including the Dravida Munnetra Kazhagam (DMK) and the All India Anna Dravida Munnetra Kazhagam (AIADMK), have frequently expressed solidarity with Sri Lankan Tamils. These expressions often occur on significant dates related to the conflict or human rights issues affecting the community. Vijay's statement positions his new party within this established political tradition, potentially seeking to consolidate support among voters who are deeply connected to the issue.

While the statement does not outline specific policy proposals, it signals the Tamilaga Vettri Kazhagam's commitment to addressing humanitarian concerns and advocating for the rights of the Tamil diaspora. The political implications of such statements are expected to be further observed as the party prepares for its inaugural electoral campaign, with the Sri Lankan Tamil issue likely remaining a recurring theme in political discourse leading up to the 2026 state assembly elections.
