The Central Bureau of Investigation (CBI) has intensified its investigation into the alleged irregularities surrounding the National Eligibility cum Entrance Test (NEET-UG) 2024, questioning a doctor, a teacher, and a parlour owner in connection with the suspected paper leak. This development underscores the widening scope of the federal agency's probe into the examination, which has been marred by allegations of malpractices and a loss of public trust.

The individuals were reportedly questioned over several days as part of the CBI's effort to unravel the network responsible for the purported leak. Their involvement suggests a potential nexus spanning various professional backgrounds, indicating a sophisticated operation behind the alleged compromise of the examination process. The NEET-UG, conducted annually for admission to undergraduate medical courses across India, is taken by over two million candidates, making any compromise a matter of significant national concern.

The CBI took over the investigation following a notification from the Ministry of Education on June 22, 2024, which cited reports of irregularities, cheating, impersonation, and malpractices in the conduct of the exam held on May 5, 2024. Prior to the CBI's intervention, multiple state police departments, including the Economic Offences Unit (EOU) of Bihar Police, had initiated their own investigations and made arrests. The EOU had previously arrested individuals in Bihar allegedly involved in leaking the paper and providing solutions to candidates before the exam.

The alleged paper leak and subsequent irregularities have triggered widespread protests from students and parents, demanding accountability and a re-examination. The Supreme Court of India is also hearing several petitions related to the NEET-UG 2024 results, including pleas for a retest and cancellation of the examination. The National Testing Agency (NTA), responsible for conducting NEET-UG, has faced intense scrutiny regarding its protocols and the integrity of its examination system.

Key aspects of the ongoing investigation include:
*   **Expanded Scope:** The questioning of individuals from diverse professions like a doctor, teacher, and parlour owner suggests investigators are looking into a broader network beyond initial arrests of coaching centre owners or students.
*   **Federal Oversight:** The CBI's involvement elevates the investigation to a federal level, aiming to identify and dismantle inter-state or national networks involved in the alleged fraud.
*   **Focus on Evidence:** The agency is reportedly collecting electronic evidence, financial transaction records, and call data to establish links between suspects and the alleged leak.

As the CBI's probe continues, further arrests and disclosures are anticipated. The outcome of this investigation is expected to have significant implications for the future conduct of national-level examinations in India and may lead to comprehensive reforms in the testing and evaluation mechanisms to restore faith in the system. The Ministry of Education has also formed a high-level committee to review the functioning of the NTA and recommend improvements.
