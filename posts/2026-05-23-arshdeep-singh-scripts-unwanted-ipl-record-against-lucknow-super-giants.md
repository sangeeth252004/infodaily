Lucknow, Uttar Pradesh – May 10, 2026 – Punjab Kings fast bowler Arshdeep Singh recorded the joint-most expensive spell by a bowler representing the franchise in the Indian Premier League (IPL) during a match against Lucknow Super Giants (LSG) at the Bharat Ratna Shri Atal Bihari Vajpayee Ekana Cricket Stadium. Singh concluded his four overs with figures of 0 wickets for 66 runs, contributing to LSG's commanding total of 257 for 5.

The left-arm pacer, typically a key component of Punjab Kings' death bowling strategy, struggled to contain the opposition batsmen throughout his quota of overs. His spell proved costly, significantly aiding LSG in setting a formidable target. The performance marks a notable setback for Singh, who is also an established international bowler for India.

Singh's expensive outing occurred as LSG batsmen aggressively targeted his overs. Marcus Stoinis and Nicholas Pooran, among others, capitalized on his bowling, striking multiple boundaries and sixes. The substantial runs conceded by Singh played a direct role in the large final score posted by the Lucknow-based franchise, making the run chase exceptionally challenging for Punjab Kings.

The 0/66 figures now place Arshdeep Singh alongside former Punjab Kings spinner Mujeeb Ur Rahman, who previously held the record for the franchise's most expensive spell. Rahman conceded an identical 66 runs from his four overs against Sunrisers Hyderabad during the 2019 IPL season. This statistic underscores the challenging nature of Twenty20 cricket, where even experienced bowlers can endure difficult days.
