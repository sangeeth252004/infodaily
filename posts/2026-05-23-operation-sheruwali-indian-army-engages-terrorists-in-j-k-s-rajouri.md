**Operation Sheruwali: Indian Army Engages Terrorists in J&K's Rajouri**

The Indian Army has initiated a significant counter-terrorism operation, codenamed "Operation Sheruwali," in the Rajouri district of Jammu and Kashmir. Reports indicate that security forces established contact with a group of terrorists, leading to an ongoing encounter in the region. The operation commenced recently, with troops engaging the militants in what is described as a challenging topographical area.

Details emerging from official sources confirm that the operation is intelligence-led, targeting specific inputs regarding the presence of terrorists. The activation of "Operation Sheruwali" underscores the sustained efforts by security forces to neutralize militant threats and maintain peace and stability across the Union Territory. The Rajouri district, situated in the Pir Panjal range, has historically been a challenging area due to its dense forests and rugged terrain, which militants often exploit for cover and movement.

This recent engagement in Rajouri highlights the persistent security challenges in the Poonch-Rajouri sector. Security agencies have intensified their surveillance and cordon-and-search operations following several incidents reported in recent months. The objective of "Operation Sheruwali" is to effectively track, corner, and eliminate the identified terrorist elements, thereby disrupting their operational capabilities and networks in the region.

Key aspects of the ongoing operation include:
*   **Intelligence-Driven:** The operation is based on actionable intelligence regarding terrorist movement and hideouts.
*   **Challenging Terrain:** Troops are navigating difficult geographical conditions, including dense forests and mountainous areas, typical of the Pir Panjal range.
*   **Force Coordination:** Multiple units of the Indian Army are involved, potentially supported by local police and paramilitary forces, ensuring comprehensive area domination.
*   **Area Sanitization:** The operation aims not only to neutralize the immediate threat but also to sanitize the area for any lingering militant presence or logistical support.

The Indian Army, alongside other security agencies, remains committed to preventing infiltration attempts and eliminating residual terrorist modules within Jammu and Kashmir. The ongoing encounter in Rajouri is a testament to the continuous vigilance and proactive measures undertaken by the forces to safeguard national security interests. Further updates from the ground are expected as the operation progresses. The area has been cordoned off, and additional troops have been deployed to reinforce the perimeter and manage any potential contingencies. Residents in nearby localities have been advised to remain cautious, and all necessary precautions are being taken to ensure civilian safety during the active engagement.
