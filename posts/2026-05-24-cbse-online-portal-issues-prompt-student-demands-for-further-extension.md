New Delhi, India – The Central Board of Secondary Education (CBSE) is confronting mounting pressure from students and parents to grant another extension for its online answer sheet verification and re-evaluation portal. This comes amid widespread reports of technical difficulties, including slow loading times, payment processing failures, and "page not found" errors, which have hampered efforts by thousands of students to access crucial post-result services.

The current deadline for applying for re-evaluation, verification of marks, and obtaining photocopies of answer sheets for the recently declared CBSE Class 10 and 12 board exam results was Friday, May 17, 2024. This date was itself an extension from an initial deadline of May 10, 2024, granted following similar complaints from students regarding portal functionality. Despite the earlier extension, a significant number of applicants continue to report unresolved technical issues, preventing them from completing their applications.

The services, which are critical for students seeking clarity or corrections in their exam results, are primarily accessed online. Students requiring re-evaluation or verification must navigate the official CBSE portal, pay the requisite fees, and submit their requests within strict timelines. The reported glitches have created significant anxiety, particularly for those whose higher education admissions or future academic plans depend on these revised outcomes.

Key issues reported by students and parents include:
*   **Persistent Technical Errors:** Users frequently encounter error messages, page crashes, or unresponsive interfaces when attempting to log in or proceed with applications.
*   **Payment Gateway Failures:** Numerous complaints detail unsuccessful payment attempts, with transactions either failing to complete or payments being debited without the application being registered on the portal.
*   **Slow Server Response:** The website reportedly experiences prolonged loading times, especially during peak hours, making it difficult to complete the multi-step application process efficiently.
*   **Incomplete Application Submissions:** Some students have reported that despite completing initial steps, their applications are not fully submitted or registered due to portal errors.

Students assert that these technical impediments have made it impossible for them to meet the stipulated deadlines, regardless of their timely efforts. Representatives from various student bodies and parent associations have appealed to the CBSE to acknowledge these systemic issues and provide a fair opportunity for all eligible students. Their primary demand is for an immediate further extension of the application window, alongside a commitment from CBSE to rectify the underlying technical problems.

The ongoing situation has placed additional stress on students, many of whom are concurrently navigating university admissions processes where final mark sheets or re-evaluated results are prerequisite. A failure to secure these services could potentially jeopardize their academic pathways. As of the latest reports, the CBSE has yet to issue an official statement regarding a further extension or a comprehensive resolution plan for the reported technical difficulties. Students and educational stakeholders continue to monitor the situation closely, awaiting a response from the examination board.
