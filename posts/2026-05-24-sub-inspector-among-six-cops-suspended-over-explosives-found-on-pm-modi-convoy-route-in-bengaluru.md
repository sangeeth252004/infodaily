Six police officers, including a sub-inspector, have been suspended in Bengaluru, Karnataka, following the discovery of explosive materials along a route designated for Prime Minister Narendra Modi's convoy. The suspensions occurred after several gelatin sticks were found under a bridge near a railway track on a specific route that the Prime Minister was scheduled to use during a recent visit to the city. This incident has led to an immediate internal inquiry into potential security protocol failures.

The decision to suspend the officers was issued by senior police authorities, citing lapses in security protocol and perceived dereliction of duty. The discovery of explosives on a high-security route has raised significant concerns regarding VIP security arrangements within the state. Initial reports indicate that the gelatin sticks were found during a routine security sweep conducted ahead of the Prime Minister's arrival.

Prime Minister Modi was in Bengaluru for a series of official engagements, and his security detail mandates comprehensive checks of all potential routes. The presence of explosive materials on such a critical path constitutes a serious breach of established security procedures. The Karnataka Director General of Police (DGP) and the Bengaluru City Police Commissioner have initiated a high-level inquiry to ascertain the full extent of the security lapse and identify all individuals responsible for the oversight.

Key details surrounding the incident include:
*   The suspended personnel comprise one Sub-inspector and five other police officers. Their names have not been publicly disclosed.
*   The explosives were identified as gelatin sticks, commonly used in quarrying and construction.
*   The specific location of the discovery was under a bridge near a railway track, part of the pre-planned convoy route.
*   The suspension order emphasizes the failure to adequately secure a critical transportation path intended for a high-profile dignitary.

The ongoing investigation aims to determine how the explosives came to be at the location, whether there was any malicious intent, or if the incident was a result of extreme negligence. Authorities are examining all angles, including potential sabotage or accidental placement, though the immediate focus remains on the failure to detect the materials during preliminary security checks. The suspended officers will remain off duty pending the outcome of the internal inquiry and any subsequent disciplinary actions. This incident underscores the continuous challenge of maintaining stringent security measures for high-profile political figures and the imperative for constant vigilance.
