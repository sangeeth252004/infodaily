Spain's national football team coach, Luis de la Fuente, has officially announced the roster for the upcoming FIFA World Cup, a selection notable for the absence of any players from Real Madrid. The squad unveiled by the Spanish Football Federation also confirms the inclusion of promising young talents, Barcelona forward Lamine Yamal and Athletic Bilbao winger Nico Williams.

The decision to exclude all Real Madrid players marks a significant departure from previous major tournament squads. Historically, players from the Madrid-based club have often formed a core component of the Spanish national team. This omission reflects Coach de la Fuente's current tactical preferences and the form of players from other clubs across La Liga and European leagues. The selection prioritizes a diverse pool of talent as Spain prepares for the global competition.

The inclusion of Lamine Yamal is another key highlight of the squad announcement. At 17 years old, Yamal’s presence in the World Cup squad establishes him as one of the youngest players ever to represent Spain at a major international tournament. His rapid ascent through Barcelona’s ranks and consistent performances in both domestic and European competitions have earned him a spot, underscoring the coaching staff's confidence in his abilities and potential impact on the international stage.

Nico Williams of Athletic Bilbao has also secured a place in the squad. His selection comes despite recent reports of injury concerns, indicating that medical assessments have cleared him for participation and that Coach de la Fuente values his offensive capabilities. The broader squad features a blend of seasoned international players and emerging talents, designed to provide both experience and dynamism throughout the tournament.

The complete squad list, as released by the Spanish Football Federation, includes players from various Spanish clubs, signaling a focus on current form and specific tactical roles. The composition suggests an emphasis on player versatility and team cohesion as key attributes for the tournament ahead.
