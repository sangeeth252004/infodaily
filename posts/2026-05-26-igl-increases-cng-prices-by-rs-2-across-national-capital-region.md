Indraprastha Gas Limited (IGL) has announced an increase of Rs 2 per kilogram in the price of Compressed Natural Gas (CNG) across the National Capital Region (NCR), effective immediately. This adjustment marks the fourth price revision for CNG by IGL in recent months, impacting millions of commuters, public transport operators, and commercial vehicle owners in Delhi, Noida, Greater Noida, Ghaziabad, and Gurugram.

Following the latest revision, the price of CNG in Delhi now stands at Rs 81.56 per kilogram. For consumers in Noida, Greater Noida, and Ghaziabad, the new rate is Rs 84.12 per kilogram. In Gurugram, the price has been adjusted to Rs 84.94 per kilogram. These changes reflect an immediate increase in operating costs for vehicles utilizing CNG as fuel.

The consistent upward trend in CNG prices has drawn attention from transport sector stakeholders. The previous three hikes, occurring within a relatively short timeframe, have contributed to increased operational expenditures for auto-rickshaws, taxis, buses, and commercial logistics fleets, many of which rely on CNG due to its perceived cost-effectiveness and environmental benefits compared to petrol and diesel. This current hike further tightens margins for these service providers, potentially leading to increased fares or higher costs for goods transport.

IGL, one of India's largest city gas distribution companies, typically adjusts prices in response to fluctuations in input gas costs. These costs can be influenced by various factors, including international natural gas prices, exchange rates, and changes in domestic gas allocation policies or pricing mechanisms. While a specific reason for the current hike was not explicitly detailed in the immediate announcement, such revisions are generally attributed to the need to align retail prices with the procurement expenses of natural gas. The company's pricing strategy aims to balance market dynamics with consumer affordability, while ensuring the viability of its distribution network.

Industry analysts and consumers will continue to monitor the trajectory of natural gas prices and any subsequent adjustments by city gas distributors in the coming period. Future price reviews by IGL will remain subject to the evolving landscape of global and domestic energy markets.
