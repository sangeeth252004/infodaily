NEW DELHI – India's Finance Ministry is scheduled to conduct a comprehensive review of public sector banks' (PSU banks) lending performance to the agriculture and Micro, Small, and Medium Enterprises (MSME) sectors on May 29. The meeting aims to assess the current state of credit disbursement, identify potential bottlenecks, and strategize measures to enhance financial support for these two critical segments of the Indian economy.

The review underscores the government's continued focus on strengthening credit flow to sectors vital for inclusive growth and job creation. Both agriculture and MSMEs are significant contributors to India's Gross Domestic Product (GDP) and employment, making their financial health a key economic indicator. The meeting will likely involve senior officials from the Finance Ministry and representatives from various PSU banks.

The agricultural sector, which employs a large portion of the Indian workforce, relies heavily on institutional credit for operational expenses, investments in machinery, and crop insurance. MSMEs, often referred to as the backbone of the economy, face unique challenges in accessing timely and affordable credit, which can impede their growth and capacity to generate employment. Government initiatives, such as the Kisan Credit Card (KCC) scheme for farmers and the Pradhan Mantri MUDRA Yojana for small businesses, have aimed to bridge these credit gaps.

Key areas of discussion during the May 29 review are expected to include:

*   **Credit Growth Assessment:** Evaluation of the year-on-year growth in lending to both agriculture and MSME sectors by PSU banks.
*   **Scheme Implementation:** Review of the progress and effectiveness of government-backed credit schemes tailored for these sectors, including KCC and MUDRA Yojana, and identifying areas for improvement.
*   **Non-Performing Assets (NPAs):** Examination of the health of loan portfolios in agriculture and MSMEs, including trends in non-performing assets and strategies for resolution.
*   **Operational Efficiency:** Discussion on streamlining loan application processes, reducing turnaround times, and enhancing the adoption of digital lending solutions to improve credit accessibility.
*   **Target Achievement:** Assessment of banks' performance against targets set for priority sector lending, particularly for agriculture and MSMEs.
*   **Financial Inclusion:** Measures to extend banking services and credit to unserved and underserved segments within these sectors, including rural and remote areas.

The Finance Ministry's proactive engagement with PSU banks reflects its commitment to ensure that financial institutions align with national economic priorities. Outcomes from such reviews often inform future policy directives, lending targets, and support mechanisms aimed at boosting credit availability and fostering sustainable growth in agriculture and MSMEs. The meeting's findings are anticipated to guide subsequent strategies for PSU banks in supporting these foundational economic sectors.
