On June 12, 2024, Karnataka Deputy Chief Minister D.K. Shivakumar publicly addressed persistent speculation regarding a potential change in the state's Chief Ministerial position. Speaking in Bengaluru, Mr. Shivakumar dismissed rumours of an imminent leadership transition, stating, "I am the Deputy Chief Minister. The Chief Minister is Siddaramaiah. The nameplate cannot be changed. This is a Congress government."

Mr. Shivakumar's remarks come amidst intermittent reports and discussions within political circles concerning the stability and future leadership of the ruling Congress government in Karnataka. The speculation has periodically surfaced since the party's decisive victory in the May 2023 assembly elections.

Following the Congress party's electoral triumph, which saw it secure 135 out of 224 seats, intense negotiations took place within the party's high command in Delhi to select the Chief Minister. These deliberations ultimately led to the appointment of veteran leader Siddaramaiah as Chief Minister, with D.K. Shivakumar assuming the role of Deputy Chief Minister. Despite this resolution, unconfirmed reports suggesting a potential power-sharing arrangement for the Chief Ministerial post, possibly after a two-and-a-half-year period, have continued to circulate.

In his recent statement, Mr. Shivakumar directly confronted these conjectures, asserting that such notions were merely "rumours" propagated by "some people." He underscored the unity within the Congress government and its focus on fulfilling its mandate to the people of Karnataka.

Key details surrounding the current political landscape and Mr. Shivakumar's statements include:

*   **Current Leadership Structure:** Siddaramaiah holds the position of Chief Minister of Karnataka, with D.K. Shivakumar serving as the Deputy Chief Minister. This arrangement was finalised after extensive consultations following the 2023 assembly elections.
*   **Context of Speculation:** Since the formation of the government in May 2023, reports have emerged suggesting a rotational Chief Ministership, though the Congress party has not officially confirmed any such agreement. The high command had made the final decision on the leadership roles.
*   **Party's Official Stance:** Senior Congress leaders have consistently denied any formal power-sharing deal for the top post, reiterating that the party functions under a unified leadership.
*   **Government's Priority:** Mr. Shivakumar reiterated the administration's commitment to developmental activities and the welfare of the state, aiming to project an image of stability and progress.

Mr. Shivakumar's public declaration aims to quell ongoing speculation and reinforce the existing leadership structure within the state. The Congress party, having received a strong mandate from the electorate, continues to project a united front, with its leaders emphasising governance and stability as their primary objectives for the state. This stance is seen as crucial for maintaining focus on administrative priorities and preparing for future electoral challenges.
