Domestic demand for gold has reportedly experienced a substantial 70% reduction, a development linked directly to a recent increase in the import duty on the precious metal. The duty rate has been raised from 6% to 15%, causing a notable shift in buyer behavior and market dynamics.

This sharp decline in demand, as indicated by an official company announcement, suggests an immediate impact on consumers and businesses operating within the gold sector. The significant hike in import tariffs makes gold considerably more expensive for domestic purchasers, directly influencing affordability and purchasing decisions. Such a rapid adjustment in demand highlights the price sensitivity of the gold market.

The revision of the import duty from 6% to 15% represents a 150% increase in the tariff applied to imported gold. This policy adjustment aims to achieve specific economic objectives, which typically include managing trade deficits, bolstering domestic production, or curbing non-essential imports. However, the immediate consequence observed is a contraction in market activity.

Key details regarding the impact include:
*   **Demand Reduction:** A reported 70% decrease in overall gold demand.
*   **Import Duty Increase:** The tariff on imported gold rose from 6% to 15%.
*   **Buyer Impact:** Consumers and retail buyers face higher acquisition costs for gold products.
*   **Market Adjustments:** Gold retailers and jewelers are likely adapting to reduced sales volumes and potential inventory recalibrations.

The substantial duty increase alters the cost structure for jewelers and retailers who largely depend on imported gold. These increased costs are typically passed on to the end consumer, leading to higher retail prices. The 70% drop in demand indicates that a significant portion of the market is unwilling or unable to absorb these increased costs at current price levels.

Industry observers anticipate continued market adjustments in response to these revised duties. The extent of the long-term impact on the domestic gold industry, consumer purchasing patterns, and potential shifts towards alternative investment avenues will be a subject of ongoing monitoring by market analysts and stakeholders. The immediate future will likely see retailers navigating a period of reduced sales and strategizing to mitigate the effects of higher import costs.
