The Securities and Exchange Board of India (SEBI) has imposed a six-month ban on Rajesh Mehta, Chairman and Managing Director (CMD) of Rajesh Exports Ltd (REL), and the company itself from accessing the securities market. The market regulator's order, issued recently, stems from findings of share price manipulation and significant misrepresentation of the company's financial statements between 2011-12 and 2014-15.

This regulatory action underscores SEBI's commitment to upholding market integrity and protecting investor interests against corporate malfeasance. The ban prohibits Mr. Mehta from buying, selling, or dealing in securities, and from holding any position as a director or key managerial personnel in any listed public company during the six-month period. Rajesh Exports Ltd is also barred from the securities market for the same duration.

SEBI's investigation revealed a scheme where Rajesh Exports allegedly manipulated its sales figures, reporting consistently growing turnover to project a favorable financial image. This misrepresentation, according to the regulator, aimed at artificially inflating the company's share price and misleading investors regarding its true financial health. Rajesh Exports, a prominent gold refiner and manufacturer, is a significant player in the precious metals sector.

Key findings from SEBI's probe include:
*   **Misrepresentation of Financials:** The company was found to have significantly inflated its sales turnover in its financial statements during the specified period.
*   **Share Price Manipulation:** The misleading financial reporting was deemed to have manipulated the company's share price, benefiting certain individuals and entities connected to Mr. Mehta.
*   **Lack of Due Diligence:** The investigation highlighted a failure in corporate governance and due diligence, particularly concerning the accuracy and transparency of financial disclosures.

In addition to the ban, SEBI has directed Mr. Mehta and other related entities to disgorge unlawful gains amounting to Rs 15.65 crore, along with an annual interest of 12% from March 31, 2015, until the date of actual payment. This disgorgement order aims to recover the benefits accrued through the alleged fraudulent activities. The entities identified for disgorgement include Rajesh Mehta, Rajesh Exports Ltd, and several other individuals and firms.

The regulatory body emphasised that such actions by a listed entity's top management not only violate securities laws but also erode investor confidence and destabilize the market. This enforcement action sends a clear signal to corporate entities about the severe consequences of non-compliance with disclosure norms and fair market practices.

Rajesh Exports Ltd has yet to issue a detailed statement regarding the SEBI order. The company is expected to comply with the directives, which could have implications for its operational financing and investor relations in the short term. The wider market will monitor the company's response and any subsequent steps to address the regulatory findings and restore stakeholder trust.
