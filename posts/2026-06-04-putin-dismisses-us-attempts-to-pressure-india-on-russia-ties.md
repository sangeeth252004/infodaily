Russian President Vladimir Putin recently affirmed that attempts by the United States to pressure Indian Prime Minister Narendra Modi into altering India’s relationship with Russia are unproductive. Speaking in an interview with the 'Pravda' newspaper ahead of the St. Petersburg International Economic Forum, President Putin underscored India's independent foreign policy and its robust leadership under Prime Minister Modi.

President Putin stated that India is "an independent state, headed by a strong leader in the international arena, Prime Minister Narendra Modi, who is able to make independent decisions." He further emphasized, "It is useless to try to pressure anyone there, especially such a giant country as India." This statement reflects Moscow's perspective on Washington's diplomatic efforts, particularly in the wake of geopolitical shifts following the conflict in Ukraine.

India and Russia have maintained a long-standing strategic partnership characterized by extensive cooperation in defense, energy, and technology sectors. Despite increasing engagement between India and Western nations, including the United States, New Delhi has consistently reiterated its policy of strategic autonomy, pursuing its national interests through a multi-aligned foreign policy approach.

Key aspects of the India-Russia relationship cited by President Putin include:
*   **Strong Leadership:** Recognition of Prime Minister Modi's capacity for independent decision-making on the global stage.
*   **Growing Trade:** A significant increase in bilateral trade turnover, particularly over the last 12 months, driven largely by India's increased imports of Russian oil and other commodities.
*   **Diverse Cooperation:** Continued collaboration in areas such as economic development, defense procurement, high-technology sectors, and nuclear energy.
*   **Defense Ties:** Russia remains a primary supplier of military equipment and technology to India, with a historical partnership that has seen joint development and production initiatives.

The United States has engaged in diplomatic efforts to encourage India to diversify its defense suppliers and reduce its reliance on Russian energy, especially since the full-scale invasion of Ukraine in February 2022. These efforts align with broader US foreign policy goals to isolate Russia and strengthen alliances with democratic partners. However, India has continued to purchase discounted Russian crude oil, citing its energy security needs, and has largely maintained its abstention on UN resolutions critical of Russia.

President Putin's remarks highlight the perceived resilience of India-Russia ties against external influence. As India continues to navigate complex geopolitical landscapes, its ability to maintain relations with multiple major powers, including the US and Russia, remains a defining feature of its foreign policy. The coming months will likely see continued diplomatic activity from all parties as global alignments evolve.
