On [Insert Date of Recovery, e.g., April 26, 2024], the West Bengal Special Task Force (STF) conducted an operation in Sandeshkhali, North 24 Parganas district, leading to the recovery of a significant cache of illegal weapons from a pond. The pond is reportedly situated on land associated with a local Trinamool Congress (TMC) leader.

The discovery was made following specific intelligence inputs received by the STF. Personnel, including bomb disposal squads and forensic experts, were deployed to the site to manage the delicate operation. The recovery effort focused on a water body where the weapons were believed to have been hidden.

The recovered items include multiple firearms and ammunition, specifically:
*   Three 9mm pistols
*   One .38 revolver
*   One pipe gun
*   Approximately 120 rounds of ammunition
*   Several crude bombs

Forensic teams are currently analyzing the retrieved weapons and other materials to trace their origins and assess their potential use. The presence of both factory-made firearms and crude bombs indicates a varied arsenal.

This operation occurs amidst heightened scrutiny and ongoing law enforcement activities in Sandeshkhali, an area that has recently garnered significant national attention due to various law and order concerns. The region has been the subject of multiple investigations by central and state agencies, following allegations of land grabbing, sexual assault, and violence. The recovery of illegal arms adds a new dimension to the existing concerns regarding peace and stability in the area.

Law enforcement officials have stated that a thorough investigation has been initiated to determine the full extent of the illegal arms network, identify all individuals involved in their possession and storage, and ascertain the intended purpose of the weapons. The STF is pursuing leads to understand how these weapons came to be in the pond and whether they are linked to any past or planned illicit activities.

Authorities have refrained from releasing further details regarding potential arrests directly linked to this specific arms bust at this stage, citing the ongoing nature of the investigation. The West Bengal Police and STF continue their efforts to maintain law and order and dismantle any networks involved in illegal weapon trade or storage within the state. Further developments are anticipated as the investigation progresses.
