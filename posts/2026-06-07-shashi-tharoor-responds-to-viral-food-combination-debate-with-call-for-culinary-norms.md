NEW DELHI – Indian Member of Parliament Shashi Tharoor recently participated in a trending online debate regarding unconventional food pairings, offering a succinct and widely shared response. On June 17, 2024, Tharoor used social media to comment on the ongoing discussion, stating, "Keep chai in cup, idli on plate," a remark that resonated across various platforms and garnered significant public engagement.

The comment by the MP for Thiruvananthapuram addressed a persistent trend on social media where users share and debate unusual food combinations, often challenging traditional culinary norms. These viral posts frequently feature dishes like idli (a South Indian savory cake) being dipped in chai (tea), or other perceived culinary mismatches, sparking strong reactions among food enthusiasts and cultural commentators. The debate often centers on preferences for culinary purity versus experimental eating.

Tharoor's intervention was interpreted as a lighthearted endorsement of traditional food consumption methods. Known for his wit and active presence on social media, his brief yet impactful statement served as a memorable contribution to the discourse. The phrase quickly gained traction, with many users expressing agreement and amusement, while others continued to champion the freedom to experiment with food.

The genesis of the wider debate can be traced to numerous user-generated posts showcasing unconventional food practices. Examples that have frequently surfaced include:
*   Idli submerged in a cup of hot tea.
*   The combination of pizza with various Indian chutneys or pickles.
*   Ice cream paired with unexpected savory condiments.

Such content often elicits polarized responses, with some users expressing disgust and defending culinary traditions, while others celebrate creativity and personal taste. Tharoor's comment skillfully navigated this divide by advocating for classic pairings without explicitly condemning individual choices. His contribution highlighted the cultural significance attached to food preparation and consumption in India.

The ongoing conversation underscores the role of social media as a platform for public discourse, even on topics as seemingly trivial as food combinations. Tharoor's participation demonstrates how public figures can engage with trending cultural phenomena, often adding a layer of humor and perspective. While the debate over experimental food pairings is likely to continue, Tharoor's phrase has cemented itself as a notable contribution to the discussion, serving as a memorable reminder of culinary traditions.
