The Supreme Court of India is scheduled to hear a petition filed by Natarajan today, challenging the rejection of his nomination for a seat in the Rajya Sabha. The urgent listing comes after an appeal against the returning officer's decision to invalidate his candidature was moved before the apex court. This development sets the stage for a significant legal review concerning electoral processes and candidate eligibility for the upper house of India's Parliament.

The plea specifically contests the grounds cited for the initial rejection of Natarajan's nomination papers. While the precise reasons for the rejection by the returning officer have not been fully detailed in public records leading up to this hearing, such rejections typically stem from discrepancies in affidavit details, non-compliance with electoral rules, or issues with proposer signatures. Natarajan's legal team is expected to argue against the validity of these reasons, asserting that his nomination met all necessary criteria as per the Representation of the People Act, 1951.

The rejection of a Rajya Sabha nomination is a critical event, as it directly impacts an individual's potential entry into national legislative politics. Rajya Sabha elections are indirect, with members elected by the elected members of the state legislative assemblies. A successful challenge in the Supreme Court could potentially reinstate Natarajan's candidature, allowing him to participate in the ongoing or upcoming electoral process for the Rajya Sabha seat. This case underscores the judiciary's role in adjudicating disputes arising from electoral decisions made by statutory authorities.

*   **Key Parties Involved:** Natarajan (petitioner), Election Commission of India (represented by returning officer's decision), Supreme Court of India.
*   **Previous Steps:** Prior to approaching the Supreme Court, Natarajan's legal recourse may have included representations to the Election Commission or appeals to High Courts, depending on the specific procedures followed in such cases. The direct appeal to the Supreme Court highlights the perceived urgency and significance of the matter.
*   **Legal Precedent:** The Court's ruling could establish or reinforce precedents regarding the powers of returning officers in scrutinizing nomination papers and the scope of judicial review in electoral matters.

Today's hearing will focus on the arguments presented by Natarajan's counsel regarding the alleged arbitrary or incorrect nature of the rejection. The Supreme Court will assess whether the returning officer's decision was legally sound and in accordance with established electoral laws and constitutional provisions. The outcome of this hearing is highly anticipated, as it will determine the immediate political trajectory for Natarajan and could have broader implications for the integrity and procedural fairness of the nomination process for parliamentary elections.
