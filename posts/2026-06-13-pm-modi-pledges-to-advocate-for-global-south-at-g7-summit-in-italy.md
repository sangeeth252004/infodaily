Prime Minister Narendra Modi departed for Apulia, Italy, on June 13, 2024, to participate in the 50th G7 Summit. Prior to his departure, PM Modi issued a statement affirming his commitment to representing the aspirations and concerns of the Global South during the gathering of leading industrialized nations. This visit marks his first foreign engagement since his re-election for a third term and his seventh participation in the G7 summit as an outreach partner.

The Indian Prime Minister's attendance comes at the invitation of Italian Prime Minister Giorgia Meloni, underscoring India's growing diplomatic significance on the international stage. India, while not a member of the G7, is regularly invited to participate in outreach sessions, serving as a key bridge between the G7 and developing economies. PM Modi stated his intention to focus on collective solutions for global challenges, advocating for a human-centric approach across various issues.

India has consistently positioned itself as a leading voice for the Global South, a term referring to developing and emerging economies primarily located in Asia, Africa, and Latin America. This role gained particular prominence during India's presidency of the G20 in 2023, where it successfully championed the permanent inclusion of the African Union as a G20 member. PM Modi's statement indicates a continuation of this diplomatic priority, aiming to ensure that the perspectives and needs of a significant portion of the world's population are heard by the G7 nations.

Discussions at the G7 Summit are expected to cover a range of critical global issues, with PM Modi highlighting specific areas of focus during his intervention. These include:
*   **Artificial Intelligence (AI):** Addressing the ethical governance, equitable access, and potential benefits of rapidly advancing AI technologies.
*   **Energy:** Discussing pathways for sustainable energy transitions, energy security, and access for developing nations.
*   **Africa:** Focusing on development initiatives, investment, and addressing the continent's specific challenges and opportunities.
*   **Mediterranean:** Exploring cooperation and stability in the Mediterranean region.

In addition to these themes, the summit agenda traditionally encompasses topics such as climate change, food security, international conflicts, and supply chain resilience. PM Modi's presence is anticipated to inject the Global South's perspective into deliberations on these subjects, particularly regarding equitable climate finance, technology transfer, and sustainable development goals. His engagements will also include bilateral meetings with several world leaders on the sidelines of the summit, aiming to strengthen diplomatic ties and foster cooperation on shared priorities.

The G7 Summit is scheduled to conclude on June 15, 2024. India's consistent engagement with the G7 as an outreach country signifies the growing recognition of its economic and strategic influence, as well as its self-assumed role as an advocate for the broader developing world. The outcomes of the summit regarding these crucial global issues will be closely watched by nations across the Global South.
