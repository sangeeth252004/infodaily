NEW DELHI – The National Testing Agency (NTA) has officially released the admit cards for the Study Webs of Active–Learning for Young Aspiring Minds (SWAYAM) examinations scheduled for the January 2026 session. Candidates registered for these examinations can now access and download their admit cards directly from the designated NTA SWAYAM website, exams.nta.nic.in. This release is a critical step for thousands of students preparing to appear for their respective courses offered under the SWAYAM initiative.

SWAYAM, an initiative by the Ministry of Education, Government of India, functions as an online learning platform providing access to free courses across various disciplines. These courses often include optional proctored examinations, success in which can lead to academic certification. The NTA is the designated body responsible for conducting these examinations, ensuring standardized procedures and equitable access for all registered participants. The timely availability of admit cards is essential for candidates to finalize their preparation and logistical arrangements for the examination day.

To download the admit card, candidates are required to input their unique application number and date of birth into the portal. The admit card serves as a mandatory document for entry into the examination hall and contains crucial information specific to each candidate and their individual examination schedule. Candidates are strongly advised to download, print, and thoroughly review all details presented on their admit card immediately upon retrieval.

Key details typically included on the SWAYAM January 2026 admit card encompass:

*   Candidate’s Name, Photograph, and Signature
*   Application Number and Assigned Roll Number
*   Specific Course Name and Code
*   Examination Date and Scheduled Time Slot
*   Mandatory Reporting Time at the Examination Centre
*   Complete Address of the Allotted Examination Centre
*   General and Specific Instructions for Examination Day

Candidates must verify all personal information, the designated examination course, and the allotted centre details. In the event of any discrepancies or errors identified on the admit card, candidates are instructed to contact the NTA Helpdesk promptly for resolution. Support contact details are typically made available on the NTA website or directly on the admit card document itself.

The printed admit card, accompanied by a valid government-issued photo identification proof, is compulsory for gaining entry to the examination centre. Digital copies or unauthenticated photocopies of the admit card are generally not accepted. The release of these admit cards is expected to intensify preparations among registered candidates, with logistical planning such as travel and accommodation becoming a priority for those appearing at examination centres located away from their current residences. The NTA has reiterated its commitment to upholding a fair and transparent examination process for the SWAYAM platform. Further official announcements regarding examination guidelines or any subsequent updates will be communicated through the official NTA and SWAYAM web portals.
