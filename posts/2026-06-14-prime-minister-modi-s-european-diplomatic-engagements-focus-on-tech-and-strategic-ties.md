Prime Minister Narendra Modi commenced a multi-country diplomatic tour to Europe, arriving in Apulia, Italy, on June 13, 2024, to participate in the G7 Leaders' Summit. His agenda includes addressing key global issues and strengthening bilateral ties, notably with French President Emmanuel Macron in Paris and a potential meeting with former U.S. President Donald Trump on the sidelines of the G7 gathering. The engagements are set to underscore India's growing role in global affairs and its focus on advanced technological partnerships.

India's participation at the G7 Summit, hosted by Italy, marks its eleventh invitation to the gathering of the world's leading industrialized nations. Prime Minister Modi is scheduled to engage in discussions covering artificial intelligence, energy, Africa, and the Indo-Pacific region. The summit, running until June 15, aims to foster collaboration on pressing global challenges and reinforce multilateral approaches in areas such as climate change, development, and supply chain resilience.

Following the G7 Summit, Prime Minister Modi is slated for a working visit to Paris for bilateral discussions with French President Emmanuel Macron. These talks are expected to build upon the 'Horizon 2047' roadmap, a framework established during President Macron's visit to India earlier in the year. Key areas of focus include further enhancing the strategic partnership, with particular emphasis on defense cooperation, space technology, clean energy initiatives, and critical and emerging technologies such as semiconductors.

Reports indicate a possible meeting between Prime Minister Modi and former U.S. President Donald Trump during the G7 Summit. While not officially confirmed by all parties, such an interaction would occur amidst the ongoing U.S. presidential election campaign, potentially highlighting continued diplomatic engagement across political transitions and a shared history of bilateral interactions.

The overarching theme of Prime Minister Modi's European engagements centers on advancing India's economic and technological partnerships. Discussions on critical technologies, supply chain resilience, and digital public infrastructure are expected to feature prominently, reflecting India's growing role in the global technology landscape and its ambition to become a manufacturing and innovation hub.

Key discussion points during the European tour include:

*   **At the G7 Summit:**
    *   Artificial Intelligence: Governance, ethical development, and responsible deployment.
    *   Global Energy Transitions: Promoting renewable energy and climate financing.
    *   Development for Africa: Strategies for economic growth and humanitarian aid.
    *   Indo-Pacific Security: Ensuring stability and freedom of navigation in the region.

*   **Bilateral with France:**
    *   Defense: Deepening manufacturing collaboration and technology transfer.
    *   Space: Joint ventures in satellite technology and space exploration.
    *   Critical Technologies: Expansion of cooperation in semiconductors, quantum computing, and cybersecurity.
    *   Clean Energy: Partnerships in civil nuclear power and green hydrogen projects.

These high-level interactions are anticipated to solidify existing strategic alliances and explore new avenues for cooperation across various sectors. The outcomes of these discussions are expected to shape India's diplomatic and economic trajectories, reinforcing its position as a key global partner in addressing both regional and international challenges. Further announcements on specific agreements and future initiatives are anticipated following the conclusion of the visits.
