Jammu and Kashmir Police have significantly disrupted a major narco-terror module, arresting 20 individuals in a multi-pronged operation spanning various districts of Jammu & Kashmir and multiple locations in Punjab. The arrests, made on Saturday, June 29, 2024, targeted a sophisticated network involved in cross-border drug smuggling and financing terrorist activities, with alleged operational links to handlers in Pakistan.

The operation led to the seizure of substantial quantities of narcotics, including heroin and charas, alongside Rs 19,10,500 in cash. Additional Director General of Police (ADGP) Mukesh Singh confirmed the coordinated action, highlighting the intricate nature of the module, which was reportedly being run from Pakistan. The apprehended individuals include alleged financiers, kingpins, and couriers, some of whom were actively involved in supplying drugs to militants and channeling funds for terror operations within the region.

This bust represents a critical blow to the illicit drug trade that often serves as a funding mechanism for terror outfits. Law enforcement officials have been actively tracking these networks, which exploit the vulnerability of border regions to facilitate the flow of contraband and illicit finances. The collaboration between J&K police units and intelligence inputs enabled the identification and apprehension of key members across the widespread network.

Key details of the operation include:
*   **Arrests:** A total of 20 individuals were apprehended. Among them, six were identified by authorities as hardcore criminals with direct involvement in cross-border drug smuggling and terror financing.
*   **Seizures:** Recovered items include heroin, charas, and a significant sum of cash totaling Rs 19,10,500.
*   **Operational Areas:** Raids and arrests were conducted across Ramban, Kathua, Samba, Jammu, and Udhampur districts in Jammu & Kashmir, as well as several locations in Punjab.
*   **Modus Operandi:** Investigations revealed that drug consignments were allegedly smuggled from Pakistan across the international border into India, subsequently transported to various parts of J&K and Punjab for distribution and sale. The proceeds were then used to fund terror activities.

The dismantling of this module underscores the persistent efforts of security agencies to combat the dual threat of narcotics and terrorism in the region. The nexus between drug trafficking and terror financing poses a significant challenge to national security, often providing financial lifelines to extremist groups. By targeting such networks, authorities aim to sever a crucial revenue stream for militant organizations and enhance stability in Jammu & Kashmir.

Further investigations are actively underway. Police officials have indicated that the ongoing inquiry aims to unearth more links, identify additional individuals involved in the network, and dismantle the entire infrastructure supporting these illegal operations. Authorities remain committed to pursuing all leads to prevent such modules from re-establishing their activities and to safeguard the region from narco-terror threats.
