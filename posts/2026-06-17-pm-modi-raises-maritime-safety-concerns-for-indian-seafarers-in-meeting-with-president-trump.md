During a high-level bilateral meeting, Prime Minister Narendra Modi raised the critical issue of maritime safety and the welfare of Indian seafarers with then-United States President Donald Trump. The discussions underscored India's significant contribution to the global maritime workforce, with the Prime Minister noting that "lakhs of Indians" are employed in the seafaring profession worldwide. This diplomatic engagement highlighted the shared interest of both nations in ensuring secure and safe working conditions across international waters.

The dialogue, which took place during a previous summit, focused on the vulnerabilities faced by seafarers and the broader implications for global trade and supply chains. India is recognized as one of the largest providers of officers and ratings to the international shipping industry, making the safety of its maritime personnel a key national and international concern. The discussions between the two leaders aimed to foster greater cooperation on maritime security and seafarer protection, reflecting the strategic partnership between India and the United States.

The welfare of Indian seafarers is a multi-faceted issue encompassing protection against piracy, fair labor practices, repatriation procedures, and access to medical care, particularly in challenging geopolitical zones or during global health crises. With hundreds of thousands of Indians working on merchant ships that traverse the world's oceans, their safety directly impacts numerous families in India and contributes substantially to the national economy through remittances. The Indian government, through agencies like the Directorate General of Shipping, actively works to protect its seafarers' rights and safety.

Key aspects of maritime safety and seafarer welfare often include:
*   **Anti-Piracy Measures:** Protecting vessels and crew from pirate attacks in high-risk areas, particularly off the coast of Somalia and in the Gulf of Guinea.
*   **Search and Rescue (SAR) Operations:** Ensuring effective international cooperation for SAR in case of maritime accidents or emergencies.
*   **Labor Standards:** Adherence to international conventions like the Maritime Labour Convention, 2006, which sets out seafarers' rights to decent working and living conditions.
*   **Geopolitical Security:** Navigating heightened tensions in critical shipping lanes and ensuring safe passage for commercial vessels and their crews.
*   **Health and Medical Support:** Providing adequate healthcare facilities on board and ensuring timely medical evacuations when necessary.

The discussion between Prime Minister Modi and President Trump aimed to strengthen bilateral and multilateral efforts to address these challenges. It underscored the need for enhanced intelligence sharing, coordinated patrols, and diplomatic initiatives to create a more secure maritime environment. Such high-level engagements are crucial for mobilizing international support and resources to safeguard the interests of seafarers, who are vital to the functioning of global commerce.

While specific outcomes of the discussion were not immediately detailed, the exchange signaled a joint commitment to prioritizing maritime safety and the welfare of a critical segment of the global workforce. Future cooperation between India and the United States in maritime domain awareness, capacity building, and policy formulation is expected to continue evolving, aiming to enhance the security and resilience of international shipping routes and protect the livelihoods of seafarers from nations like India.
