KOLKATA – West Bengal Assembly Speaker Biman Banerjee is scheduled to hear Trinamool Congress (TMC) leader Abhishek Banerjee on an upcoming date concerning allegations of an internal split within the party, according to recent official announcements. The hearing addresses petitions filed by the TMC seeking the disqualification of certain Members of Legislative Assembly (MLAs) under the anti-defection law, marking a significant development in the state's political landscape.

The proceedings initiated by the Speaker stem from formal complaints lodged by the Trinamool Congress. These petitions assert that several of its elected representatives have acted contrary to party directives or defected, thereby inviting provisions of the Tenth Schedule of the Indian Constitution, commonly known as the anti-defection law. Abhishek Banerjee, a prominent figure within the TMC and a Member of Parliament, is expected to present the party's arguments and evidence during the session.

The anti-defection law was enacted to prevent political defections that could lead to instability in governments. It specifies that an MLA can be disqualified if they voluntarily give up membership of their political party or vote/abstain from voting in the House contrary to any direction issued by their political party, without obtaining prior permission. The ultimate authority for deciding on disqualification petitions rests with the Speaker of the respective legislative assembly.

*   **Legal Framework:** The Tenth Schedule of the Constitution of India governs the disqualification of elected members on grounds of defection.
*   **Party Position:** The TMC has formally petitioned the Speaker, alleging violations of party discipline and loyalty by specific MLAs.
*   **Key Figure:** Abhishek Banerjee is a central figure in the Trinamool Congress and is involved in representing the party's stance on this issue before the Speaker.
*   **Speaker's Role:** Speaker Biman Banerjee holds the constitutional authority to adjudicate on these disqualification petitions after hearing all involved parties.

This hearing carries substantial political implications for West Bengal. A decision to disqualify MLAs could alter the legislative composition of the Assembly, potentially impacting the ruling party's strength and future policy initiatives. It also highlights ongoing internal dynamics and challenges within the Trinamool Congress as it navigates its third consecutive term in power. The outcome will be closely watched by political observers and could set precedents for future intra-party disputes in the state.

Following the hearing, Speaker Biman Banerjee is expected to deliberate on the evidence and arguments presented before delivering a ruling. This decision could be subject to further legal challenges in higher courts, depending on the outcome and satisfaction of the parties involved. The proceedings underscore the robust legal and political mechanisms in place to address disputes over party loyalty and legislative representation in India.
