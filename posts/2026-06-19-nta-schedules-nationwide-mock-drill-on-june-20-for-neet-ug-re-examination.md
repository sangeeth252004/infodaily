The National Testing Agency (NTA) is set to conduct a comprehensive nationwide mock drill on Thursday, June 20, across all designated examination centers. This exercise is a crucial preparatory step for the upcoming National Eligibility cum Entrance Test (Undergraduate) (NEET-UG) re-examination, scheduled for June 23, involving 1,563 candidates. The drill's primary objective is to meticulously test and validate all logistical, technical, and security arrangements to ensure a seamless and fair re-examination process.

The mock drill will encompass a full simulation of the examination environment. This includes testing the secure distribution and collection of question papers, verification of candidate identities through biometric attendance, management of electronic devices, and ensuring robust communication channels between centers and NTA headquarters. Officials, invigilators, and technical support staff will participate to practice their roles and identify any potential operational bottlenecks or technical glitches that could arise during the actual re-examination. The NTA aims to address and resolve any identified issues promptly, enhancing preparedness and operational efficiency.

This preparatory measure follows a recent directive from the Supreme Court. The court upheld the NTA's decision to cancel the compensatory grace marks previously awarded to 1,563 candidates. These grace marks were initially provided due to reported loss of examination time during the main NEET-UG examination held on May 5. The Supreme Court's ruling stipulated that these affected candidates would be given the option to either accept their original scores without the grace marks or appear for a fresh re-examination.

The NEET-UG examination is a critical national-level entrance test for admissions to undergraduate medical, dental, AYUSH, and veterinary courses across India. Millions of aspirants participate annually, underscoring the importance of maintaining the integrity and fairness of the examination process. The decision to conduct a mock drill highlights the NTA's commitment to ensuring transparency and preventing any discrepancies, particularly in light of recent public and legal scrutiny surrounding the examination's conduct and evaluation.

*   **Mock Drill Date:** Thursday, June 20, 2024
*   **Re-examination Date:** Sunday, June 23, 2024
*   **Affected Candidates:** 1,563
*   **Context:** Cancellation of grace marks by NTA, affirmed by Supreme Court
*   **Purpose:** To validate logistical, technical, and security protocols for re-examination

Following the successful completion of the nationwide mock drill on June 20, the NTA will proceed with the re-examination for the eligible candidates on June 23. The results for these candidates are anticipated to be declared by June 30, ahead of the commencement of the NEET-UG counseling process. The agency's overarching goal is to facilitate an equitable and high-integrity examination experience, reinforcing public trust in India's medical entrance system.
