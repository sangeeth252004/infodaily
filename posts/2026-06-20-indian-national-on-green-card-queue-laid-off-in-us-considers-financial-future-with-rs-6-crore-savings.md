An Indian national residing in the United States, who has been navigating the lengthy employment-based Green Card queue, was recently laid off from his position. The individual's situation has drawn attention after he publicly sought guidance on his financial viability, specifically asking if he could sustain himself in India without employment, possessing savings of Rs 6 crore (approximately $720,000 USD). This development underscores the challenges faced by many professionals on temporary work visas amidst a fluctuating job market.

The layoff places the individual in a precarious position concerning his immigration status in the US. Professionals on H-1B visas, which are commonly held by individuals awaiting Green Card approval, typically have a limited grace period following job termination to secure new employment or change their visa status before being required to depart the country. The lengthy Green Card backlog for Indian nationals often means individuals remain on H-1B visas for many years, tying their residency status directly to their employment.

This incident highlights several critical aspects for Indian professionals in the US:

*   **Vulnerability of Visa Status:** Employment-based visas like the H-1B are non-immigrant visas, meaning continued legal presence in the US is contingent upon specific employment. Layoffs directly impact this status, often providing a 60-day grace period for visa holders to find a new sponsoring employer.
*   **Green Card Backlog:** Indian nationals face one of the longest waits for employment-based Green Cards due to per-country caps, stretching for decades in some categories. This protracted wait means many skilled workers remain on temporary visas, susceptible to job market downturns.
*   **Financial Planning:** The question regarding Rs 6 crore in savings in India reflects the significant financial decisions individuals must make when facing unexpected job loss abroad. This sum, while substantial in India, requires careful consideration regarding cost of living, investment, and long-term sustainability without active income.
*   **Repatriation Considerations:** For many, returning to India after a period in the US involves weighing family ties, career opportunities, and adapting to a different economic and social landscape.

The individual's public query sheds light on the dilemmas faced by a segment of the Indian diaspora. The decision to remain in the US, pursue new employment, or return to India with accumulated savings involves complex calculations encompassing immigration rules, personal financial goals, and long-term career aspirations.

As the global job market, particularly in the technology sector, continues to experience shifts, the experiences of individuals like the Indian national laid off in the US serve as a reminder of the intricate interplay between global employment trends, immigration policies, and personal financial resilience. The outcome for this individual remains to be seen as he navigates his options, which include seeking alternative employment in the US within the grace period, changing to a different visa category if eligible, or making preparations for a potential return to India.
