A recent comprehensive report, titled SRS 2024, has shed light on contemporary marital status distributions within the surveyed population. The official announcement of the findings indicates that 3.5% of individuals surveyed were identified as widowed, divorced, or separated. A significant aspect of the data highlights a clear gender disparity, with 5.5% of women recorded in these categories compared to 1.6% of men.

The SRS 2024 report, released through an official company announcement, provides a current snapshot of demographic patterns relevant to family structures and social indicators. This data offers foundational insights for researchers and policymakers examining societal changes and their impact on different population segments. The aggregated category of "widowed, divorced, or separated" provides a broad view of individuals no longer in a current marital union, reflecting diverse life circumstances.

The distinct difference in percentages between genders is a key finding from the SRS 2024 study. The 5.5% figure for women in these categories is more than triple the 1.6% reported for men. This discrepancy is consistent with historical demographic trends observed in various societies, often attributed to factors such as differing life expectancies, remarriage rates, and social support systems for men and women. The data does not elaborate on the specific reasons behind these differences, strictly presenting the statistical outcome.

Key findings from the SRS 2024 report regarding marital status include:
*   **Overall Population:** 3.5% of the total surveyed population was identified as widowed, divorced, or separated.
*   **Women:** 5.5% of women fell into the combined category of widowed, divorced, or separated.
*   **Men:** 1.6% of men were categorized as widowed, divorced, or separated.
*   **Categories:** The data specifically groups individuals who are widowed, divorced, or separated, without providing further breakdown of each sub-category within this report.
