The Tamil Nadu Electricity Board (TNEB) has announced a significant recruitment drive planned for 2026, targeting the filling of 20,449 vacant positions across various departments within the state's power sector. This initiative, communicated through an official company announcement, represents one of the largest recruitment exercises by the public utility in recent years, aimed at bolstering its workforce and operational capabilities.

The extensive recruitment program is designed to address critical staffing requirements across TNEB's operations, including generation, transmission, and distribution segments. The influx of new personnel is anticipated to enhance efficiency, support ongoing infrastructure projects, and ensure the consistent delivery of power services across Tamil Nadu. This move also presents substantial employment opportunities for a wide range of qualified candidates within the state.

Details indicate that the 20,449 vacancies will encompass diverse roles, catering to various educational and technical backgrounds. These positions are crucial for maintaining the operational integrity and future development of the state's electricity infrastructure. Prospective candidates are expected to include engineering graduates, diploma holders, ITI certificate holders, and those with administrative qualifications.

Key positions identified within this recruitment drive include:

*   **Assistant Engineer (AE):** Approximately 500 vacancies
*   **Junior Assistant (JA):** Approximately 1,500 vacancies
*   **Field Assistant (Trainee):** Approximately 5,000 vacancies
*   **Technical Assistant:** Approximately 1,000 vacancies
*   **Typist:** Approximately 1,800 vacancies
*   **Steno-Typist:** Approximately 2,000 vacancies
*   **Accounts Officer (AO):** Approximately 60 vacancies
*   **Junior Auditor (JA):** Approximately 120 vacancies
*   **Draughtsman:** Approximately 500 vacancies

The remaining vacancies will be allocated to various other technical, administrative, and support roles essential for the functioning of the power board. The recruitment drive is expected to be conducted through a structured selection process, including competitive examinations and interviews, though specific details regarding the methodology are yet to be formally released.

Candidates interested in these opportunities are advised to monitor the official TNEB website for detailed notifications. The board is expected to publish comprehensive information regarding eligibility criteria, application procedures, examination syllabi, and important dates closer to the commencement of the application window, which is anticipated in late 2025 or early 2026. This recruitment drive underscores TNEB's commitment to strengthening its human resources in line with the evolving demands of the power sector in Tamil Nadu.
