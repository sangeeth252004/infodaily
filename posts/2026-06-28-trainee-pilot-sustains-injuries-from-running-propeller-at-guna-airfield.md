GUNA, MADHYA PRADESH – A trainee pilot sustained head injuries after being struck by a running propeller while exiting a two-seater Diamond DA40 aircraft at Mach Aviation's Guna airfield on Monday, June 10, 2024. The pilot, identified as Captain Anindya Khajuria, was reportedly on a solo flight when the incident occurred.

Following the accident, Captain Khajuria received immediate first aid at the airfield before being transported to a local hospital in Guna. He was subsequently referred to a larger medical facility for further treatment, where his condition has been reported as stable. The Directorate General of Civil Aviation (DGCA) has launched an investigation into the matter.

The incident took place shortly after Captain Khajuria had landed the aircraft. Preliminary reports indicate that the pilot exited the plane while its engine remained operational and the propeller was still in motion. The Diamond DA40 is a commonly used aircraft for flight training purposes.

Such incidents underscore the critical importance of adherence to strict aviation safety protocols, particularly concerning propeller-driven aircraft. Standard operating procedures typically mandate that pilots ensure the engine is shut down and the propeller has come to a complete stop before exiting the cockpit to mitigate risks. The DGCA's investigation will focus on understanding the precise sequence of events, assessing adherence to established safety guidelines, and identifying any contributing factors.

Key details of the incident include:
*   **Date:** Monday, June 10, 2024
*   **Location:** Mach Aviation's Guna Airfield, Guna, Madhya Pradesh, India
*   **Individual Involved:** Trainee pilot Captain Anindya Khajuria
*   **Aircraft Type:** Diamond DA40 (two-seater, propeller-driven)
*   **Nature of Incident:** Struck by running propeller upon exiting the aircraft post-landing
*   **Injuries:** Head injuries, currently reported as stable
*   **Investigating Authority:** Directorate General of Civil Aviation (DGCA)

The ongoing DGCA investigation will be crucial in determining the exact cause of the incident and evaluating the circumstances surrounding Captain Khajuria's exit from the aircraft. The findings are expected to inform potential recommendations for enhancing safety procedures and training protocols within flight schools to prevent similar occurrences in the future. Further updates are anticipated as the investigation progresses.
