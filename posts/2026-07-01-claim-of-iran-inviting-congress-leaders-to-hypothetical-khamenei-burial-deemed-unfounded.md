A speculative claim suggesting Iran has extended invitations to prominent Indian National Congress leaders, including Mallikarjun Kharge, Salman Khurshid, and Pawan Khera, for the burial ceremony of Supreme Leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei, has recently circulated in various media contexts. However, public information and official sources unequivocally confirm that Ayatollah Ali Khamenei remains alive and actively continues his duties as the Supreme Leader of the Islamic Revolution. This factual status renders any reports of a current burial ceremony invitation fundamentally unfounded.

The circulation of such a claim highlights the persistent challenge of misinformation in digital and international discourse. Supreme Leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei, 85, has been Iran's head of state since 1989. His public appearances and statements are regularly reported by official Iranian media and international news agencies, affirming his ongoing leadership. Any official events related to the Supreme Leader's health or succession would be announced through established state channels, which have not reported any such developments.

The Indian political figures named in the unverified claim hold significant positions within the Congress party:
*   **Mallikarjun Kharge:** Serves as the President of the Indian National Congress, making him a leading figure in India's principal opposition party.
*   **Salman Khurshid:** A veteran Congress leader and former Union Minister, who has held portfolios such as External Affairs and Law and Justice.
*   **Pawan Khera:** A prominent national spokesperson for the Indian National Congress, often representing the party on public platforms and in media discussions.

Their inclusion in the unsubstantiated report underscores the potential for such claims to involve high-profile political figures, regardless of factual accuracy. No official statements from the Indian National Congress or the individual leaders have confirmed receipt of such invitations, nor would such confirmations be expected given the current factual situation regarding Ayatollah Khamenei.

Relations between India and Iran have historically been complex but marked by cooperation in various sectors, including trade, energy, and connectivity projects like the Chabahar Port. Diplomatic exchanges between the two nations occur regularly, focusing on mutual interests and regional stability. However, official diplomatic communications adhere to established protocols and are typically publicised through government channels, providing verifiable information on high-level engagements. The absence of any official communique from either the Iranian or Indian foreign ministries regarding such an unprecedented invitation further reinforces its unverified nature.

Journalistic best practices mandate the verification of information, especially concerning sensitive geopolitical figures and events. The propagation of unconfirmed reports can lead to misunderstandings, disrupt diplomatic relations, and erode public trust in information sources. In this instance, the core premise of the claim contradicts verifiable reality.

As of current reports, Ayatollah Ali Khamenei remains the Supreme Leader of Iran, and there is no factual basis for the circulating claim of invitations being extended for his burial ceremony. The situation serves as a reminder for both media consumers and platforms to exercise vigilance and prioritize fact-checking to ensure accurate reporting and to mitigate the spread of unverified information in an increasingly interconnected global landscape.
