All regular teaching staff at two Delhi government schools have been assigned to "Special Investigation Report" (SIR) duty, leading to significant disruption in regular academic instruction. The affected institutions are Sarvodaya Kanya Vidyalaya (SKV) in Molarband and Government Boys Senior Secondary School (GBSSS) in Sangam Vihar. This assignment has prompted concerns among parents and educational stakeholders regarding student learning, particularly as schools rely heavily on contractual and guest teachers to maintain operations.

The directive, which has been in effect, has drawn approximately 60 regular teachers from their primary classrooms across both schools. Teachers on SIR duty are tasked with gathering data, conducting inquiries, and preparing detailed reports on various administrative and educational matters, diverting them from their core teaching responsibilities. This development comes as students are in crucial learning phases, with upcoming examinations for many classes.

Impact and Details:
*   **Teacher Deployment:** At SKV Molarband, approximately 15 regular teachers are reportedly on SIR duty. At GBSSS Sangam Vihar, between 20 to 25 regular teachers have been assigned similar tasks.
*   **Educational Void:** The absence of regular faculty has resulted in larger class sizes for remaining guest and contractual teachers, and in some instances, subjects being taught by less specialized staff.
*   **Parental Concern:** Parents have expressed anxiety over the quality and continuity of education for their children, citing a lack of consistent teaching.
*   **Directorate of Education Stance:** The Directorate of Education (DoE) of the Government of National Capital Territory of Delhi (GNCTD) issued a statement on May 20, 2024, clarifying that only some teachers are assigned non-teaching duties and that teaching activities are not disrupted, or that alternative arrangements are put in place. However, ground reports from the affected schools suggest a different reality.
*   **School Administration Confirmation:** The principal of SKV Molarband confirmed the assignment of regular teachers to SIR duty, highlighting the challenges in maintaining a full teaching schedule.

This situation is not unprecedented, with teachers in Delhi previously being assigned various non-teaching roles, including election duties, census work, and responsibilities related to public health crises such such as the COVID-19 pandemic. However, the current assignment involving all regular teachers at specific schools for an extended period highlights a potential strain on educational resources. Teacher associations have frequently advocated for policies that prioritize teaching assignments, emphasizing the direct impact on student outcomes when educators are diverted.

The long-term implications of these assignments on teacher morale and the academic progress of students remain a point of ongoing discussion. Educational authorities are expected to continue addressing the balance between administrative requirements and the fundamental mandate of providing uninterrupted, quality education. The duration of the current SIR duties and potential future policy adjustments regarding non-teaching assignments for educators are yet to be clearly defined.
