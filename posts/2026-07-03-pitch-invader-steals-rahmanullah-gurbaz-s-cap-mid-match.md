During the ICC Men's T20 World Cup 2024 group stage match between Afghanistan and Uganda on Tuesday, June 4, 2024, at the Providence Stadium in Guyana, play was briefly interrupted by a pitch invader. The individual approached Afghan wicketkeeper-batsman Rahmanullah Gurbaz and took his cap before attempting to evade security personnel.

The incident occurred early in Uganda's innings while Gurbaz was positioned behind the stumps. An individual ran onto the field of play, made direct contact with Gurbaz, removed his cap, and then ran towards the boundary. Security personnel at the venue quickly responded, pursuing the invader across the field. The individual was apprehended within moments, and play resumed shortly after the disruption. Gurbaz retrieved his cap and continued with the match without further incident.

This security breach, occurring during a high-profile international tournament, highlights ongoing concerns regarding player safety and stadium security protocols. While the incident was brief and appeared to be without malicious intent beyond the act of theft, it underscores the vulnerability of athletes on the field. International cricket venues are typically expected to maintain stringent security measures to prevent unauthorized access to the playing area.

*   **Event:** ICC Men's T20 World Cup 2024, Group C match.
