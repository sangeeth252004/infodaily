Eight senior officials from the School Education Department (SED) in Jammu and Kashmir have been suspended following the discovery of a book deemed to contain "anti-national" content in government school libraries. The suspensions, ordered by Chief Secretary Atal Dulloo, were announced recently after an internal inquiry identified significant administrative negligence in the book selection and vetting process. The controversial book, titled "Jammu & Kashmir: The Untold Story" by Wajahat Ahmad, has been ordered to be immediately withdrawn from all schools.

The government's action comes after a high-level review revealed that the book contained "highly objectionable material," including what authorities described as "factual inaccuracies and misrepresentation of historical facts." It was also alleged to promote "secessionist ideas," leading to its blacklisting and the initiation of a comprehensive probe.

Key details surrounding the incident include:
*   **Book Distribution:** The book was distributed to approximately 1,760 higher secondary schools across Jammu and Kashmir as part of a centrally sponsored scheme aimed at upgrading school libraries. This scheme allocated Rs 8 crore for the purchase of 72 different types of books.
*   **Content Allegations:** Authorities stated the book contained content deemed "profoundly disturbing and harmful," asserting it presented an inaccurate historical narrative and ideological positions contrary to national integrity.
*   **Suspended Officials:** The eight officials suspended for "gross negligence and dereliction of duty" include the Director School Education Jammu, along with the Chief Education Officers (CEOs) of Ramban, Kishtwar, Rajouri, Udhampur, Poonch, Doda, and a District Programme Officer (DPO) of Samagra Shiksha.
*   **Vetting Failure:** An internal committee, formed to vet books for school libraries, reportedly failed to adequately scrutinize the content of "Jammu & Kashmir: The Untold Story" before its distribution. The CEO of J&K Samagra Shiksha was specifically tasked with overseeing this vetting process.

The incident highlights a significant lapse in the administrative oversight of educational material procurement and distribution within the union territory. The presence of such a book in school libraries has raised serious concerns within government circles regarding the diligence of officials responsible for safeguarding the educational environment and national interests.

The inquiry initiated by Chief Secretary Atal Dulloo is expected to delve deeper into the circumstances that led to the book's inclusion in school libraries. This probe aims to ascertain the full extent of culpability and recommend robust future safeguards to prevent similar occurrences. Further administrative or legal actions are anticipated based on the findings of this high-level investigation. The immediate future will see the complete removal of all copies of the book from schools and a likely review of the entire book selection policy for educational institutions in Jammu and Kashmir.
