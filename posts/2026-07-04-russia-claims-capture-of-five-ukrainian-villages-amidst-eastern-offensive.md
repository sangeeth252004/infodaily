The Russian defense establishment identified the captured villages as Ocheretyne, Semenivka, Berdychi, Novobakhmutivka, and Keramik. These locations are situated predominantly within Ukraine's Donetsk Oblast, an area that has witnessed some of the most intense fighting since Russia's full-scale invasion in February 2022. The announcement follows a period of intensified Russian ground attacks and shelling in eastern Ukraine, particularly after the fall of the strategic town of Avdiivka to Russian forces in February of this year.

These reported advances, if independently verified, represent localized tactical gains in a conflict characterized by incremental shifts in territorial control and attritional warfare. The area around Avdiivka, a former Ukrainian stronghold, has been a flashpoint for months, with both sides reporting heavy combat and significant casualties.
