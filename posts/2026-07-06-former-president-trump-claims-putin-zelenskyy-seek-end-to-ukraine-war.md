Former U.S. President Donald Trump recently asserted that both Russian President Vladimir Putin and Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelenskyy wish for an end to the ongoing conflict in Ukraine, suggesting that a resolution is "closer than people realise." The statement was made by Trump during a recent public appearance, adding to his consistent rhetoric about his capacity to quickly resolve the war if re-elected to the presidency.

The conflict in Ukraine commenced in February 2022 with a full-scale Russian invasion, leading to significant humanitarian and geopolitical consequences. It has resulted in widespread destruction, millions displaced, and substantial global economic disruption. International efforts to mediate a lasting peace have faced considerable challenges, with both Kyiv and Moscow maintaining distinct positions regarding the terms of a potential settlement.

Mr. Trump, a leading Republican candidate for the 2024 presidential election, has repeatedly claimed he could bring the conflict to a swift conclusion, often stating he would resolve it within 24 hours of taking office. While he has not publicly detailed the specific mechanisms or concessions that would facilitate such an agreement, his latest remarks suggest an underlying readiness for peace on both sides of the conflict. He did not provide evidence or further details to substantiate his claim regarding the leaders' private sentiments.

The former president's comments carry particular weight given his past presidency and his current standing as a prominent figure in American politics, influencing both domestic and international discourse. His suggestions about the proximity of a resolution, however, have not been publicly corroborated by either the Kremlin or the Ukrainian presidential office. Neither President Putin nor President Zelenskyy has issued public statements that jointly affirm a desire for an immediate end to hostilities on terms implying a rapid, unstated agreement.

*   **Context of the Conflict:** Ukraine demands the complete withdrawal of Russian forces from all occupied territories, including Crimea, and the restoration of its 1991 borders, alongside accountability for war crimes and security guarantees.
*   **Russia's Stated Demands:** Russia, conversely, insists on Ukraine's non-alignment, demilitarization, and the recognition of its annexations, particularly of regions it claims to have incorporated into the Russian Federation.
*   **International Diplomacy:** Numerous diplomatic initiatives have been launched by various international actors, including the United Nations, Turkey, and China, but none have yet yielded a comprehensive peace agreement acceptable to both warring parties.
*   **U.S. Stance:** The official stance of the current U.S. administration, under President Joe Biden, has been unwavering support for Ukraine's sovereignty and territorial integrity, providing extensive military and financial aid to Kyiv since the full-scale invasion.

Mr. Trump's continued focus on ending the Ukraine war underscores it as a significant foreign policy issue within the upcoming U.S. election cycle. Observers will continue to monitor statements from all parties involved, including future details, if any, regarding Mr. Trump's proposed path to peace as the conflict progresses.
