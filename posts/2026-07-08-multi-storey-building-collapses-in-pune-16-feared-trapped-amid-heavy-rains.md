Pune, Maharashtra – August 2, 2024 – A multi-storey residential building collapsed in the Kondhwa area of Pune late Thursday night, August 1, leaving an estimated 16 individuals feared trapped under the debris. The incident, which occurred around 11:30 PM, has prompted a major rescue operation by multiple agencies amidst continuous heavy rainfall in the region.

The four-storey structure, reportedly an older residential complex, gave way suddenly. Local residents reported hearing a loud noise before the building crumbled. Emergency services, including the Pune Fire Brigade, Pune Police, and teams from the National Disaster Response Force (NDRF) and State Disaster Response Force (SDRF), were immediately dispatched to the site. Initial reports indicate that several families were residing in the building at the time of the collapse.

The rescue efforts are being significantly hampered by the ongoing heavy monsoon rains and the unstable nature of the remaining debris. Authorities have cordoned off the area to ensure the safety of rescue personnel and bystanders. Medical teams and ambulances have been stationed near the site to provide immediate assistance to those rescued.

Key details regarding the incident and ongoing response:
*   **Location:** Kondhwa area, Pune, Maharashtra.
*   **Time of Collapse:** Approximately 11:30 PM, Thursday, August 1, 2024.
*   **Building Type:** Four-storey residential structure.
*   **Estimated Trapped:** 16 individuals, based on preliminary assessments and resident information.
*   **Rescue Agencies:** National Disaster Response Force (NDRF), State Disaster Response Force (SDRF), Pune Fire Brigade, Pune Police, and local civic body teams.
*   **Challenges:** Heavy rainfall, limited visibility, and the precariousness of the debris pile.
*   **Equipment Deployed:** Specialized search and rescue equipment, including hydraulic cutters, lifters, and sniffer dogs, are being utilized.
*   **Personnel:** Over 100 rescue workers are engaged in the operation.

Pune District Collector Rajesh Deshmukh confirmed the deployment of all available resources, stating, "Our priority is to safely extract those trapped. We are working around the clock, despite the challenging weather conditions." He added that an official inquiry into the cause of the collapse would be initiated after the rescue operation concludes. While heavy rainfall is noted as a contributing factor, structural integrity issues will be a focus of the subsequent investigation.

As of early Friday morning, rescue operations are ongoing, with efforts concentrated on locating survivors and safely removing debris. No official figures on casualties or confirmed rescues have been released at this time. Authorities are appealing to the public to stay away from the collapse site to facilitate the rescue efforts. Further updates are expected as the operation progresses throughout the day.
