Global Market Insights (GMI), a prominent financial research firm, today, July 10, 2026, released its comprehensive market outlook, indicating an anticipated bullish movement for gold prices in the near term. The report, issued from the firm's headquarters in London, highlighted several key macroeconomic factors and technical indicators contributing to this positive forecast for the precious metal.

According to GMI's analysis, the ongoing global economic uncertainties, coupled with anticipated shifts in central bank monetary policies, are expected to bolster gold's appeal as a safe-haven asset. The firm's lead analyst for commodities, Dr. Evelyn Sharma, stated in the report that "persistent inflation concerns in major economies and a potential easing cycle by central banks later in the year are creating a favorable environment for gold." The report specifically pointed to an expected softening of interest rates by the U.S. Federal Reserve and the European Central Bank in late 2026, which historically tends to support gold prices by reducing the opportunity cost of holding non-yielding assets.

GMI’s outlook projects that domestic gold futures, traded on the Multi Commodity Exchange (MCX), could test levels around ₹75,000 per 10 grams in the upcoming quarter. Internationally, the report anticipates COMEX spot gold prices to challenge the $2,500-$2,520 per ounce range. These targets reflect a potential increase of approximately 2-3% from current market levels, which saw MCX August gold futures closing at ₹73,280 on July 9, 2026, and COMEX spot gold at $2,435 per ounce.

Key details from the Global Market Insights report include:
*   **MCX Gold Target:** August futures are projected to test resistance at ₹75,000 per 10 grams.
*   **COMEX Gold Target:** Spot prices are forecast to challenge the $2,500-$2,520 per ounce level.
*   **Primary Drivers:**
    *   **Geopolitical Tensions:** Continued instability in various regions is increasing demand for safe-haven assets.
    *   **Inflationary Pressures:** Elevated inflation rates globally contribute to gold's appeal as a hedge against currency devaluation.
    *   **Central Bank Activity:** Expectation of a dovish pivot from major central banks, reducing bond yields and strengthening gold.
    *   **Physical Demand:** Steady buying interest from central banks for reserve diversification and sustained consumer demand, particularly from Asian markets.
*   **Support Levels:** GMI identified immediate support for MCX gold around ₹73,800 and for COMEX gold at $2,420 per ounce, indicating a relatively stable base for potential upward movement.

The report also highlighted an increase in open interest for gold futures on both MCX and COMEX platforms over the past week, suggesting growing market participation and confidence in the metal's trajectory. This increased investor activity, according to GMI, underpins the technical strength supporting the bullish view.

Market participants are now closely monitoring upcoming economic data releases, including inflation reports from the U.S. and Eurozone, along with official communications from central bank policy meetings, for further cues. Investors typically consider such comprehensive outlooks from reputable firms like GMI when evaluating their portfolio strategies amidst evolving global economic landscapes.
