Manchester, UK – October 26, 2024 – England’s middle-order batter Harry Brook created a new national record on Saturday, striking the fastest Twenty20 International (T20I) century by an English player. The historic innings occurred during the decisive 5th T20I against India at Old Trafford, Manchester, where Brook reached his century off just 42 deliveries. His performance was central to England's commanding total and subsequent series victory.

Brook’s explosive batting surpassed the previous record held by Jos Buttler, who scored a century in 46 balls against South Africa in 2016. Coming to the crease with England at 88 for 3 in the tenth over, Brook accelerated the scoring rate significantly, displaying a blend of power-hitting and precise stroke play. He ultimately finished his innings unbeaten on 108 runs from 47 balls, an innings punctuated by 10 fours and 8 sixes, contributing significantly to England's formidable total of 222 for 4.

The record-breaking innings marked a pivotal moment in the bilateral series, with the 5th T20I serving as the decider after the series stood level at 2-2. Brook’s century not only propelled England to a strong position in the match but also underlined his growing stature in international white-ball cricket. His ability to perform under pressure in a high-stakes encounter provides further evidence of his potential as a key player for England in upcoming tournaments.

Key details from Brook’s record-breaking performance include:

*   **Fastest T20I Century for England:** Achieved in 42 balls, surpassing Jos Buttler's 46-ball effort.
*   **Match Context:** Delivered in the series-deciding 5th T20I against a strong Indian side.
*   **Impact on Match:** Contributed 108 not out from 47 balls to England's total of 222/4.
*   **Venue:** Old Trafford, Manchester, a ground known for its receptive batting conditions.
*   **Overall Series Performance:** This century capped a consistent performance by Brook throughout the series, solidifying his role in the England setup.

This achievement follows a period of strong domestic and international performances for the 25-year-old Yorkshireman. Since his T20I debut in 2022, Brook has shown consistent form, establishing himself as a versatile batter capable of scoring rapidly across different match situations. His century against India is anticipated to further cement his place in England's T20 World Cup plans for the next cycle.

Following this record-breaking match, England secured a 3-2 series victory over India. Harry Brook was subsequently named Player of the Series for his cumulative batting efforts, which included two half-centuries in earlier matches in addition to his historic century. The England squad will now prepare for their next international assignment, with attention turning to the selection process for the forthcoming ICC T20 World Cup, where Brook is expected to be a prominent figure.
