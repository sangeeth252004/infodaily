Chandan Kumar, a 25-year-old Indian tourist from Supaul district, Bihar, drowned on Tuesday, May 28, 2024, while swimming in Phewa Lake, a prominent tourist destination in Pokhara, Nepal. Local authorities launched a search operation following reports of his disappearance in the lake, ultimately recovering his body.

According to reports from the District Police Office Kaski, Mr. Kumar had reportedly entered the lake for a swim when the incident occurred. Police, alongside local divers, initiated recovery efforts. His body was retrieved from the lake several hours after the initial alarm was raised. Following the recovery, the body was transported to the Western Regional Hospital in Pokhara for post-mortem examination, a standard procedure in such cases. Local police have commenced an investigation to ascertain the full circumstances leading to the drowning.

Phewa Lake is the second-largest lake in Nepal and a central attraction in Pokhara, drawing a significant number of domestic and international tourists annually, including a substantial contingent from India. The lake is popular for its scenic beauty, boating activities, and views of the Annapurna mountain range. While popular for recreation, the lake features varying depths and can present hazards, particularly to those unfamiliar with its conditions or who venture into deep waters without adequate safety precautions.

Key details regarding the incident and the location include:
*   **Victim's Origin:** Chandan Kumar hailed from Supaul district in the Indian state of Bihar.
*   **Location:** Phewa Lake, situated in the city of Pokhara, Gandaki Province, Nepal.
*   **Lake Characteristics:** Phewa Lake has an average depth of approximately 8.6 meters (28 feet), with a maximum depth of around 24 meters (79 feet).
*   **Tourism Significance:** Pokhara is a major tourist hub, and Phewa Lake is central to its appeal, attracting visitors for activities like boating, fishing, and lakeside leisure.
*   **Safety Measures:** Although boating typically requires life jackets, swimming in unregulated areas or without supervision can pose significant risks.

The incident highlights the importance of adhering to safety guidelines when engaging in water-based activities in natural environments. Authorities frequently advise tourists to exercise caution, be aware of local conditions, and utilize safety equipment, such as life jackets, especially when boating or swimming in unfamiliar waters.

The Nepal Police are continuing their investigation into the exact sequence of events that led to Mr. Kumar's drowning. Following the completion of the post-mortem, arrangements will be made for the repatriation of his body to India, a process that typically involves coordination between Nepalese authorities and the Indian Embassy or Consulate in Nepal. This incident serves as a reminder for both visitors and local operators regarding the perpetual need for vigilance and adherence to safety protocols in popular tourist areas.
