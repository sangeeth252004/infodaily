Mumbai's iconic Taj Mahal Palace Hotel was subjected to a bomb threat call on Friday, May 24, 2024, initiating a significant security alert and a comprehensive search operation by Mumbai Police. The anonymous call, received by the hotel's management, demanded a sum of Rs 5 crore to avert an alleged bombing. Authorities swiftly mobilised, deploying specialised units to secure the premises and ensure the safety of guests and staff.

Following the receipt of the threat, the hotel immediately notified Mumbai Police. Multiple units, including the Bomb Detection and Disposal Squad (BDDS) and the Sniffer Dog Squad, were dispatched to the luxury hotel located in Colaba. Security personnel commenced an exhaustive search across the entire property, including public areas, guest rooms, and back-of-house facilities, to ascertain the credibility of the threat. The operation was conducted meticulously, with a focus on minimising disruption to hotel operations where possible, while prioritising safety.

The incident underscores the heightened state of alert maintained by key establishments in Mumbai, a city with a history of terror attacks. The Taj Mahal Palace Hotel itself was a prominent target during the November 2008 Mumbai terror attacks, making such threats particularly sensitive. Hotel management released a statement confirming the receipt of the threat and affirming their full cooperation with law enforcement agencies. They also assured guests and the public that all necessary protocols were activated and that safety remained their utmost priority.

Key details of the incident and response include:
*   **Date of Threat:** Friday, May 24, 2024.
*   **Location:** Taj Mahal Palace Hotel, Colaba, Mumbai.
*   **Nature of Threat:** Anonymous caller claimed a bomb had been planted and demanded Rs 5 crore.
*   **Law Enforcement Response:** Mumbai Police, Bomb Detection and Disposal Squad (BDDS), and Sniffer Dog Squad immediately deployed.
*   **Search Operation:** An extensive search was conducted throughout the hotel premises.
*   **Outcome:** No suspicious devices or materials were found during the multi-hour search operation.
*   **Hotel Statement:** Management acknowledged the threat, confirmed cooperation with police, and emphasised the safety of guests and staff.

As of Friday evening, the Mumbai Police confirmed that the extensive search concluded without the discovery of any explosive devices or suspicious items, allowing the hotel to return to normal operations. However, investigations are ongoing to trace the anonymous caller responsible for the threat. Authorities are utilising technical surveillance and other investigative methods to identify the individual, with charges likely to include making false threats and causing public alarm. The incident served as a critical test of the city's security preparedness and response mechanisms for high-profile targets. Security measures at the hotel and surrounding areas remain reinforced as a precautionary measure.
