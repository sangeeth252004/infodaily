A serious hit-and-run incident occurred recently in Chandigarh, involving a speeding Mercedes-Benz vehicle that struck two individuals outside a local nightclub before its driver fled the scene. The incident has prompted a police investigation into the circumstances surrounding the collision and a city-wide search for the driver responsible.

The event unfolded during the late hours outside an establishment in Chandigarh, though specific details regarding the exact location and time are part of the ongoing police inquiry. Initial reports indicate that a luxury sedan, identified as a Mercedes-Benz, was being driven at a high speed when it collided with two pedestrians. Eyewitness accounts are being collected by authorities to reconstruct the sequence of events leading up to the collision, which is critical for the investigation.

In the immediate aftermath, the driver of the Mercedes-Benz reportedly abandoned the vehicle and fled on foot, leaving the two injured individuals at the site. Emergency services were reportedly alerted to the scene, and the injured parties received medical attention. The current condition or extent of their injuries has not been officially disclosed by authorities as the investigation is in its preliminary stages. Such incidents raise significant concerns regarding driver accountability, adherence to traffic laws, and overall road safety, particularly in areas frequented by pedestrians during late hours.

This process typically involves collecting forensic evidence from the scene, examining available CCTV footage from the vicinity, and interviewing potential witnesses who may have observed the incident.
