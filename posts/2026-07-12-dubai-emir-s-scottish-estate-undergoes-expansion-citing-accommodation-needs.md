Sheikh Mohammed bin Rashid Al Maktoum, the Emir of Dubai and Vice President of the United Arab Emirates, has reportedly visited his expansive 63,000-acre Inverinate Estate in the Scottish Highlands only five times over the past two decades. The infrequent visits are attributed to the existing Inverinate House, which possesses 14 bedrooms, lacking sufficient space to adequately accommodate his full family and staff entourage. In response, detailed plans for significant new accommodation facilities on the estate have recently received approval from local authorities.

The Inverinate Estate, situated near Kyle of Lochalsh in the Scottish Highlands, is managed through Smech Management Ltd., a company controlled by the Sheikh's family. Planning documents submitted to the Highland Council outlined the need for increased capacity to host the Sheikh's retinue, which reportedly includes family members and a substantial number of support staff. The current main residence, Inverinate House, was deemed insufficient for these requirements, leading to extended periods without visits from the owner.

To address the spatial constraints, the approved development includes the construction of new lodges designed to provide expanded living and staff quarters. Central to these plans is a proposed 8-bedroom lodge named Kinloch Lodge, intended to serve as a primary residence for the royal family during their stays. This new structure is described in planning applications as a "grand country house," indicating a substantial addition to the estate's residential capacity.

Further details from the submitted applications highlight the comprehensive nature of the expansion:

*   **Estate Size:** The Inverinate Estate spans approximately 63,000 acres, encompassing diverse landscapes of the Scottish Highlands.
*   **Existing Main Residence:** Inverinate House, the primary dwelling on the estate, contains 14 bedrooms.
*   **Proposed Family Accommodation:** An 8-bedroom Kinloch Lodge is planned specifically for the royal family.
*   **Proposed Staff Accommodation:** The development includes provisions for staff, with space for 17 staff members near the Kinloch Lodge, and two additional smaller lodges designed to house up to 14 staff.
*   **Location:** The estate is situated near Kyle of Lochalsh, a prominent gateway to the Isle of Skye in the West Highlands.
*   **Estate Management:** Operations are overseen by Smech Management Ltd., an entity associated with the Sheikh's family interests.
*   **Planning Authority:** The Highland Council granted approval for the proposed construction, following assessment of the detailed plans.

Beyond residential facilities, the Inverinate Estate is known for its extensive recreational and sporting activities, including deer stalking, hunting, and fishing, catering to the interests of its owner and guests. The multi-million-dollar property serves as a private retreat, with its new developments aimed at enhancing its capacity to host large parties comfortably.

With planning permission now secured, construction of the new Kinloch Lodge and associated staff accommodations is anticipated to proceed. This expansion is expected to alleviate the current accommodation issues, potentially paving the way for more frequent and extended visits by Sheikh Mohammed bin Rashid Al Maktoum and his family to their Scottish estate in the future.
