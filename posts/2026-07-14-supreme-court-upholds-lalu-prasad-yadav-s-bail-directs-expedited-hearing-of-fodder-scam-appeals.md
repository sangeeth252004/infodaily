The Supreme Court of India on Monday upheld the bail granted to Rashtriya Janata Dal (RJD) chief Lalu Prasad Yadav in four cases related to the multi-crore fodder scam. A bench of Justices Aniruddha Bose and Bela M. Trivedi concurrently directed the Jharkhand High Court to expedite the hearing of pending appeals filed by Yadav against his convictions in these cases.

The ruling came in response to petitions filed by the Central Bureau of Investigation (CBI), which had challenged the Jharkhand High Court's decision to grant bail to Yadav. The CBI argued against the bail, contending that similar co-accused individuals had not been granted bail and that bail should not be automatically granted solely on the basis of having served half the sentence. The Supreme Court's decision maintains Yadav's freedom while emphasizing the need for timely resolution of his appeals.

The fodder scam involves fraudulent withdrawals of government funds allocated for livestock feed from various treasuries in the Animal Husbandry Department of undivided Bihar during the 1990s. Lalu Prasad Yadav, a prominent political figure and former Chief Minister of Bihar, was convicted in multiple cases stemming from the scam. His convictions led to periods of incarceration.

Key details of the case include:
*   **Convictions:** Yadav was convicted in several fodder scam cases, including those related to fraudulent withdrawals from the Chaibasa, Deoghar, Dumka, and Doranda treasuries.
*   **Bail History:** He was granted bail by the Jharkhand High Court in April 2021, primarily on the grounds that he had served more than half of his prescribed sentences in the various cases.
*   **CBI Challenge:** The CBI challenged this bail, asserting that the principle of serving half the sentence should not be the sole criterion for granting bail, especially given the gravity and scale of the corruption involved.
*   **Supreme Court's Directive:** The apex court’s decision to uphold the bail means Yadav will remain out of custody. The crucial aspect of the directive is the instruction for the Jharkhand High Court to prioritize and expeditiously hear the appeals against his convictions. This aims to bring a conclusive legal resolution to the long-standing cases.

This directive from the Supreme Court shifts the immediate focus to the Jharkhand High Court, which is now mandated to accelerate the appellate process. The expedited hearing will address the merits of Yadav's challenges against his convictions, potentially leading to a final judicial determination in these high-profile corruption cases. The move underscores the judiciary's commitment to ensuring both individual liberty and the timely administration of justice in complex legal matters.
