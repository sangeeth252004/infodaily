The Delhi High Court has recently sought a response from the Union Government regarding a plea that seeks medical intervention for renowned educationist and environmental activist, Sonam Wangchuk. Mr. Wangchuk has been on a hunger strike in Leh, Ladakh, advocating for constitutional safeguards for the region.

The court's directive, issued on Thursday, March 28, 2024, came in response to a public interest litigation (PIL) filed by a petitioner expressing grave concerns over Mr. Wangchuk's health. The plea highlighted the deteriorating physical condition of Mr. Wangchuk, who commenced his "climate fast" on March 6, 2024, and has reportedly been without food for over 20 days. The petitioner emphasized the urgent need for medical examination and care given the prolonged nature of his protest.

Sonam Wangchuk, a prominent figure known for his innovative educational and environmental initiatives in Ladakh, initiated the hunger strike to press for several key demands. Central to his protest is the immediate implementation of the Sixth Schedule of the Constitution for Ladakh. This constitutional provision aims to grant autonomous status to tribal areas, providing for the administration of tribal areas as per local customs and protecting indigenous cultures and land rights. Mr. Wangchuk and his supporters contend that this status is crucial to safeguard Ladakh's fragile ecosystem and unique cultural identity from potential threats posed by industrialization and unregulated development. Other demands include statehood for Ladakh and the establishment of a public service commission for the Union Territory.

The High Court bench, after hearing the arguments, issued a notice to the Ministry of Home Affairs, the Union Territory of Ladakh Administration, and other relevant government bodies. The court has granted these respondents a period of two weeks to file their replies outlining their stance and any actions being considered regarding the plea. The court's move underscores the judicial system's role in addressing humanitarian concerns that arise from citizen protests, particularly when an individual's health is at significant risk.

Mr. Wangchuk's protest has garnered considerable attention, both locally and nationally, drawing focus to the environmental and administrative issues faced by Ladakh. He has been conducting his fast outdoors in extreme cold weather conditions, further intensifying concerns about his well-being. The government's response to the Delhi High Court's directive will be a crucial development in this ongoing situation.

The next hearing in the matter is scheduled for April 12, 2024, by which time the Union Government is expected to have submitted its detailed response to the court, outlining its position on the plea for medical intervention.
