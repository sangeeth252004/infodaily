New Delhi, India – The Citizens for Justice and Peace (CJP) protest at Jantar Mantar, New Delhi, entered its 25th day on September 17, 2024, drawing renewed attention with the participation of prominent public figures. Comedian Kunal Kamra joined the ongoing demonstration, while Bhim Army Chief Chandrashekhar Azad Ravan had previously extended his support on the 24th day of the protest. The sustained demonstration aims to highlight the alleged poor living conditions and lack of dignified rehabilitation for migrant workers following a recent incident of harassment in a shelter.

The CJP-led protest originated in response to what the organization describes as government apathy towards the welfare of migrant workers. Activists and citizens participating in the protest are advocating for improved standards of living, proper rehabilitation, and accountability from authorities responsible for the reported conditions. The specific allegations include "jam-packed" shelters described as housing cockroaches and lacking basic sanitation facilities, according to statements from protestors.

The involvement of individuals like Kunal Kamra and Chandrashekhar Azad Ravan has significantly amplified the visibility of the protest and its core demands. Kamra, upon joining the demonstration on its 25th day, stated his solidarity with the protestors, emphasizing the duration of the peaceful demonstration. His presence is expected to draw broader public and media attention to the grievances of the migrant worker community. Chandrashekhar Azad Ravan's earlier participation on the 24th day also brought considerable focus to the cause. The Bhim Army Chief had underscored the importance of collective action in securing justice for marginalized communities.

The issue of migrant worker welfare remains a critical concern in India, with large populations often facing precarious living and working conditions. Protests such as the one organized by CJP serve as a platform to voice these concerns and pressure governmental bodies to address systemic issues. The demonstration at Jantar Mantar brings together a diverse group of participants, including student activists, human rights advocates, and concerned citizens, all united by the common goal of ensuring justice and dignified treatment for migrant workers.

Key details of the ongoing protest include:
*   **Location:** Jantar Mantar, New Delhi.
*   **Duration:** As of September 17, 2024, the protest has been ongoing for 25 days.
*   **Organizers:** Citizens for Justice and Peace (CJP), a non-governmental organization.
*   **Key Figures:** Comedian Kunal Kamra (joined Day 25), Bhim Army Chief Chandrashekhar Azad Ravan (joined Day 24).
*   **Core Demands:** Dignified rehabilitation for migrant workers, improved living conditions, accountability for alleged harassment and substandard shelter conditions, and an end to perceived government apathy.
*   **Allegations:** Reports of "jam-packed" shelters, infestations (specifically "cockroaches"), and inadequate sanitation for migrant workers.

CJP has stated its unwavering commitment to continue the protest until their demands are met and tangible steps are taken to address the plight of migrant workers. The sustained nature of the demonstration indicates the resolve of the organizers and participants to keep the issue in the public discourse, prompting a potential response from relevant authorities regarding worker welfare policies and shelter management. The situation underscores the ongoing challenges related to labor rights and human dignity in the country.
