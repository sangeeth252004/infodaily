A prominent billboard featuring former U.S. President Donald Trump depicted inside a coffin appeared in Tehran, Iran, on Wednesday, June 26, 2024. The display included text in Persian stating, "We will kill Trump," alongside a message referencing the assassination of Iranian General Qassem Soleimani. This public exhibition intensifies ongoing rhetoric from certain Iranian factions targeting the former American leader.

The large-scale visual installation, reported by local media and shared across social media platforms, was erected in a visible location within the Iranian capital. The imagery prominently featured a rendered likeness of Donald Trump inside an open casket. Accompanying the central image was the additional phrase: "Soleimani's killer will get his revenge," directly linking the display to the January 2020 U.S. drone strike that killed General Soleimani in Baghdad, Iraq. That strike was ordered by then-President Trump.

This recent billboard follows a pattern of similar public displays in Iran, particularly around the anniversaries of Soleimani's death. Such installations serve as a consistent reaffirmation of vows of retribution against those perceived responsible for the general's killing. In January 2023, similar billboards emerged across Tehran, conveying comparable messages of revenge and defiance. These public assertions reflect a sustained hostile posture from elements within Iran towards the former U.S. administration and its key figures.

The targeting of Donald Trump in these displays underscores the deep-seated animosity stemming from the 2020 incident. General Soleimani, who commanded the Quds Force of the Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps (IRGC), was a highly influential figure in Iran's military and foreign policy apparatus. His death was a significant event that escalated tensions between Iran and the United States, prompting immediate vows of "harsh revenge" from Iranian leadership.

The appearance of this billboard occurs in a period of continued geopolitical sensitivity in the Middle East, with indirect negotiations and proxy conflicts remaining points of friction between Iran and Western powers. Such public statements, though symbolic, are intended to convey a resolute stance both domestically and internationally.

As the United States approaches a presidential election where Donald Trump is a leading candidate, the timing of this display may carry additional implications for future U.S.-Iran relations. The persistent nature of these threats indicates that the issue of General Soleimani's assassination remains a significant point of contention for certain Iranian elements, irrespective of current political landscapes. The international community continues to monitor these developments for their potential impact on regional stability.
