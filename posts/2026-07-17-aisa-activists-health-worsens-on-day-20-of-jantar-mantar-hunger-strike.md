The prolonged protest, initiated by AISA to press various demands related to student welfare and educational policies, has seen participants abstain from food for nearly three weeks. Medical personnel monitoring the situation have noted a decline in vital signs among several key strikers as the protest enters its third week. The deteriorating health has amplified calls for intervention and resolution of the students' demands.

The hunger strike represents a form of non-violent civil disobedience aimed at drawing government attention to the perceived grievances of the student community. As the physical toll on the activists mounts, the urgency of their demands and the potential humanitarian concerns associated with their deteriorating health have become central to the unfolding situation.
