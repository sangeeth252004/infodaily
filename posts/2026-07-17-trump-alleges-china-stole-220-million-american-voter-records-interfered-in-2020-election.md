Former President Donald Trump recently asserted that China was responsible for stealing the voter data of 220 million Americans and actively interfered in the 2020 United States presidential election. The claims, made publicly by Trump, point to a massive breach of U.S. voter information as a component of alleged foreign meddling.

Trump's accusation specifically details the theft of records belonging to 220 million U.S. citizens, a significant portion of the American electorate. According to the former president, this alleged data theft by Beijing aimed to influence the outcome of the 2020 election. The nature of the "voter data" was not further specified in public statements beyond its designation as information pertaining to American voters.

The allegations by the former president underscore ongoing concerns regarding election security and the potential for foreign adversaries to impact democratic processes. Such claims, if substantiated, would represent a substantial cybersecurity breach with profound implications for national security and the integrity of U.S. elections. As of the time of this report, official U.S. intelligence agencies or federal law enforcement bodies have not publicly corroborated the specific claims made by Mr. Trump regarding the alleged theft of 220 million voter records by China in relation to the 2020 election.

However, these past assessments have generally focused on broader efforts to sow discord and undermine public confidence, rather than confirming a specific incident of 220 million voter records being stolen by China to directly interfere with a presidential election outcome.
