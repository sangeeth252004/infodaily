Former U.S. President Donald Trump recently made statements linking the upcoming FIFA World Cup to his claims regarding the 2020 presidential election. During his remarks, Mr. Trump also offered praise for FIFA President Gianni Infantino in connection with what he referred to as the "Balogun decision." These comments were part of a broader address where the former president reiterated his long-standing assertions about the integrity of the previous election cycle.

Mr. Trump's remarks included a direct juxtaposition of his 2020 election claims with the achievement of securing the FIFA World Cup hosting rights. He was quoted saying, "'They rigged election, & I got FIFA WC'," suggesting a personal role in the United States, Mexico, and Canada being awarded the hosting duties for the 2026 FIFA World Cup. The tripartite bid was officially selected by FIFA in 2018, during Mr. Trump's presidency, and it marked the first time the tournament would be hosted across three nations. The 2026 event will be the largest FIFA World Cup to date, featuring 48 national teams and 104 matches.

A significant portion of Mr. Trump's comments focused on acknowledging FIFA President Gianni Infantino. The praise was specifically directed toward Mr. Infantino concerning the "Balogun decision." While the former president did not elaborate on the specific details of this decision, the reference is understood to pertain to American-born professional footballer Folarin Balogun. Balogun, a prominent striker, recently declared his intention to represent the United States Men's National Team (USMNT) in international competition, having been eligible to play for England and Nigeria. His decision to commit to the USMNT was a notable development for U.S. soccer, particularly as the country prepares to co-host the World Cup.

*   **Key Aspects of Trump's Statements:**
    *   Reiterated claims of the 2020 U.S. presidential election being "rigged."
    *   Asserted personal credit for the North American bid securing the 2026 FIFA World Cup.
    *   Expressed appreciation for FIFA President Gianni Infantino regarding the "Balogun decision."

The former president has consistently maintained that the 2020 election, in which he lost to current President Joe Biden, was subject to widespread fraud. These claims have been repeatedly challenged and disproven by various state and federal officials, as well as multiple court rulings. Despite these findings, Mr. Trump has continued to voice these allegations in public forums and campaign events, often using them to frame his political narrative.

The 2026 FIFA World Cup is anticipated to be a major international event, bringing significant economic and cultural impact to the host cities across the United States, Mexico, and Canada. The tournament will expand from its previous 32-team format, increasing the number of participating nations and matches. The United States is slated to host 11 of the 16 host cities.

Mr. Trump's recent comments align with his pattern of connecting his political platform to major national and international events. His remarks regarding Mr. Infantino and the "Balogun decision" underscore a perceived positive trajectory for U.S. soccer, an area he appears to be linking to his broader political messaging. As the 2024 U.S. presidential election cycle continues, such statements are likely to remain part of his public discourse.
