Olympic medallist Manu Bhaker has publicly addressed the ongoing student protests across India concerning alleged paper leaks in national examinations, stating it is "heartbreaking to see students suffer." Her comments add a prominent voice to the growing calls for accountability and systemic reforms in the examination process following widespread student unrest.

The protests stem from reported irregularities in major national entrance and eligibility tests. The University Grants Commission-National Eligibility Test (UGC-NET) for 2024 was cancelled by the Ministry of Education on June 19, following intelligence inputs suggesting potential compromise of the examination's integrity. Separately, the National Eligibility-cum-Entrance Test (NEET-UG) for 2024, which qualifies students for undergraduate medical courses, has faced widespread allegations of question paper leaks and unfair grace marks, leading to ongoing investigations and legal challenges.

Bhaker, a multi-time Olympic medallist in shooting, emphasized the severe impact these incidents have on students' futures. "It's heartbreaking to see the students suffer like this, all the hard work they put in for years is wasted because of some people," she reportedly stated. She further stressed the need for a transparent and accountable system, asserting that those responsible for compromising the integrity of examinations must face consequences. Bhaker's public stance aligns with the sentiment of millions of students and parents across the country who are demanding justice and fairness.

In response to the controversies:
*   **UGC-NET:** The Ministry of Education cancelled the exam, reiterating its commitment to ensuring the sanctity of public examinations.
*   **NEET-UG:** The Supreme Court is currently hearing petitions regarding the alleged irregularities. The central government has initiated a Central Bureau of Investigation (CBI) probe into the matter, promising strict action against any individuals found culpable.
*   **National Testing Agency (NTA):** The NTA, responsible for conducting these examinations, is currently under scrutiny, with the government outlining plans for reforms aimed at enhancing its operational transparency and security protocols.

The continuous revelations of alleged paper leaks have eroded trust among students and parents in the national examination system. Students invest years in preparation, often incurring significant financial and emotional costs, only to face uncertainty, potential re-examinations, and the fear that their efforts may be undermined by malpractices. This situation not only causes immense mental stress but also disrupts academic timelines and career aspirations for millions of young individuals across the country.

As investigations continue into the alleged irregularities and the government pledges comprehensive reforms, the focus remains on restoring faith in the examination process. Students, educators, and various stakeholders await the outcomes of the ongoing probes and the swift implementation of robust measures designed to prevent future instances of paper leaks and ensure fair, transparent evaluations for all aspirants.
