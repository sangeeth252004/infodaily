**Protests Erupt in Dhaka Following Arrest Over Unauthorized Lord Ram Statue Construction**

Dhaka, Bangladesh, experienced demonstrations on October 31, 2023, sparked by the arrest of Nikhil Chandra Das, a prominent leader within the Hindu community. Mr. Das was apprehended by police in connection with the construction of an 81-foot statue of Lord Ram in Cumilla district without official authorization. The incident has led to calls from minority organizations for his immediate release and an assurance of religious minority rights.

Nikhil Chandra Das, who also serves as the joint general secretary of the Bangladesh Puja Udjapan Parishad (BPUP), was taken into custody by authorities. Police stated the arrest was made due to concerns that the unauthorized construction of the large religious effigy could potentially "create anarchy and disrupt peace" in the region. The statue, depicting Lord Ram, was being erected in the Debidwar upazila area of Cumilla district, a detail that has garnered significant attention due to its scale and religious symbolism.

Following Mr. Das's arrest, multiple minority rights organizations mobilized to protest the action. Demonstrations were reported in various locations across Dhaka, with participants demanding the prompt release of Mr. Das and greater protection for religious minorities within the country. The protests highlighted growing concerns among these communities regarding their freedoms and security.

Key organizations involved in the protests include:
*   **Bangladesh Puja Udjapan Parishad (BPUP):** Mr. Das's own organization, which actively condemned the arrest.
*   **Bangladesh Hindu Buddhist Christian Oikya Parishad (BHBCOP):** A major interfaith minority rights group.
*   **Bangladesh Hindu Mohajot:** Another prominent Hindu community organization.

Leaders from these groups issued statements criticizing the arrest. J.L. Bhowmik, President of the Bangladesh Puja Udjapan Parishad, asserted that the arrest of Nikhil Chandra Das constituted a violation of constitutional rights guaranteed to citizens. Rana Dasgupta, General Secretary of the Bangladesh Hindu Buddhist Christian Oikya Parishad, echoed these sentiments, expressing strong condemnation of the arrest and indicating that the government's actions failed to adequately protect the nation's minority communities. Advocate Gobinda Chandra Pramanik, representing the Bangladesh Hindu Mohajot, reportedly cited the arrest as another instance in a series of persecutions faced by religious minorities in Bangladesh.

The situation underscores ongoing discussions surrounding religious freedom and the regulatory framework for large-scale construction projects, particularly those with significant religious implications, in Bangladesh. While authorities cite the lack of proper authorization and potential for unrest as reasons for the arrest, minority leaders interpret the event as a further erosion of their rights and an act of harassment. The incident continues to be a focal point for advocates of minority rights, drawing attention to the delicate balance between community expressions of faith and governmental regulations. As of the time of reporting, details regarding Mr. Das's legal proceedings and potential release remain under observation.
