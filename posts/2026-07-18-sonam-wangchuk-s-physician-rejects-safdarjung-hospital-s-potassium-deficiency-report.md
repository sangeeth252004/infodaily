LEH, Ladakh – The personal physician of renowned climate activist and innovator Sonam Wangchuk has publicly rejected a medical report from Delhi's Safdarjung Hospital, which cited potassium deficiency as the cause of Wangchuk's health concerns during his recent 21-day "climate fast" in Ladakh. The treating doctor reportedly labeled the findings as "suspicious," raising questions about the official medical assessment.

The dispute emerged after the Ladakh Union Territory Administration requested a medical check-up for Wangchuk, who concluded a significant hunger strike on April 24, 2024. Wangchuk's fast was a prominent protest aimed at advocating for statehood for Ladakh and its inclusion in the Sixth Schedule of the Indian Constitution, a provision designed to protect tribal populations and their lands. During the fast, Wangchuk experienced various health issues, leading to the administration's intervention and subsequent examination by a team from Safdarjung Hospital.

**Details of the Disputed Report:**
The report from Safdarjung Hospital, a prominent government medical institution, reportedly concluded that Wangchuk's symptoms were primarily attributable to a deficiency in potassium levels. This finding suggested that the activist's prolonged fast might have led to an electrolyte imbalance, a common physiological response to restricted food intake.

However, Wangchuk’s personal physician has presented a contrasting view. The doctor reportedly argued that the symptoms observed were not consistent with typical potassium deficiency and that other factors might have been at play or that the diagnosis itself was questionable under the circumstances. This direct challenge to a report from a national medical institution underscores a significant disagreement over the activist's health status during a politically sensitive protest.

*   **Activist's Stance:** Sonam Wangchuk himself has publicly stated that he believes his health was compromised by the extreme cold conditions in Ladakh during his fast, rather than solely by dietary deficiencies. He has also highlighted the significant public support for his demands.
*   **Context of the Fast:** Wangchuk’s fast, conducted at an altitude of approximately 11,500 feet above sea level in Leh, brought national attention to the environmental and constitutional concerns of Ladakh. Thousands gathered in support, enduring harsh weather conditions alongside him.
*   **Demands:** Key demands include:
    *   Statehood for Ladakh
    *   Inclusion of Ladakh under the Sixth Schedule of the Constitution
    *   Protection of Ladakh's fragile ecosystem from industrial exploitation

The conflicting medical assessments introduce a layer of complexity to the ongoing discourse surrounding Wangchuk’s protest and the health monitoring of individuals engaged in hunger strikes for political or social causes. The official administration-requested report and the activist's personal medical team are now at odds regarding a critical health diagnosis.

As discussions continue between the Ladakhi leaders and the central government regarding the region's demands, the health of Sonam Wangchuk remains a point of public interest. The differing medical interpretations of his condition during the fast are likely to be debated further, potentially impacting public perception of both the protest and the medical assessments involved. No further immediate medical examinations have been announced, but public scrutiny of Wangchuk’s health and the official medical reporting is anticipated to persist.
