Abhijeet Dipke, founder of the Citizens for Justice and Peace (CJP) organization, recently made a serious accusation against the Delhi Police, alleging the "kidnapping" of prominent education reformer and environmental activist Sonam Wangchuk. Dipke's statement, which he termed a "big mistake" by the authorities, has brought renewed attention to Wangchuk's ongoing advocacy for the constitutional safeguards of Ladakh.

The accusation stems from concerns regarding Sonam Wangchuk's movements and his high-profile activism. Wangchuk, renowned for his educational reforms in Ladakh and his environmental initiatives, has been a vocal proponent for the inclusion of Ladakh under the Sixth Schedule of the Indian Constitution. This provision would grant special protections to the region, preserving its unique culture, land, and environment, and ensure greater autonomy for its tribal populations.

Wangchuk's recent activities have included a series of climate fasts and protests aimed at drawing national and international attention to Ladakh's environmental fragility and the demands for its inclusion in the Sixth Schedule. His advocacy highlights the need for sustainable development practices in the Himalayas, particularly as the region faces increasing threats from industrialization and climate change. The alleged police action, as described by Dipke, comes at a time when Wangchuk's public profile and the movement for Ladakh's protection have gained significant traction.

Abhijeet Dipke's statement emphasized the gravity of the alleged incident, framing it as an unwarranted interference in the democratic right to peaceful protest and expression. The CJP, an organization focused on human rights and constitutional values, has historically campaigned against actions perceived as infringements on civil liberties. The allegation of "kidnapping" suggests a serious breach of protocol and a potential misuse of authority by law enforcement, prompting calls for clarification and accountability.

*   **Key Demands of Sonam Wangchuk:**
    *   Inclusion of Ladakh under the Sixth Schedule of the Indian Constitution.
    *   Protection of Ladakh's fragile ecosystem from industrial overdevelopment.
    *   Safeguarding the unique cultural identity and land rights of the indigenous people of Ladakh.
    *   Implementation of democratic and autonomous governance structures for the region.

The context of this accusation is set against a backdrop of increasing awareness regarding environmental conservation and the rights of indigenous communities in sensitive ecological zones. Wangchuk's hunger strikes and public appeals have resonated with various segments of society, placing pressure on governmental bodies to address the demands from Ladakh.

The Delhi Police have not yet issued an official statement or response to Abhijeet Dipke's specific accusation regarding the "kidnapping" claim. Further developments are anticipated as civil society organizations and media outlets continue to monitor the situation concerning Sonam Wangchuk and the broader movement for Ladakh's constitutional protection. The incident underscores the ongoing tensions between state actions and activist movements advocating for environmental and indigenous rights in India.
