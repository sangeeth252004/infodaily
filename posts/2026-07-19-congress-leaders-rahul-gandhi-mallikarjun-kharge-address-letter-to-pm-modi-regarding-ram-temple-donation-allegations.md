Senior Congress leaders Rahul Gandhi and Mallikarjun Kharge have formally addressed a letter to Prime Minister Narendra Modi, calling for an urgent investigation into alleged financial irregularities and "theft" concerning donations collected for the construction of the Ram Temple in Ayodhya. The communication, sent recently, emphasizes a demand for greater transparency and accountability regarding the substantial public contributions.

The letter, reportedly containing the phrase "Your silence is unacceptable," highlights a critical concern among the opposition regarding the management of funds contributed by millions of devotees. This development brings the issue of financial oversight for the high-profile temple project into the national political discourse, pressing for a governmental response.

The Ram Temple project in Ayodhya has garnered significant public support and financial contributions from across the country. The fundraising drive was extensive, involving widespread campaigns to collect donations from individuals, families, and organizations. The scale of these contributions, reportedly amounting to crores of rupees, underscores the importance of stringent financial management and transparent reporting, as advocated by the Congress leaders.

Key points raised in the letter and related context include:
*   **Allegations of Misappropriation:** The letter specifically references claims of "theft" and financial misconduct, urging the Prime Minister to acknowledge and address these serious allegations.
*   **Call for Accountability:** Gandhi and Kharge have called upon the Prime Minister to ensure that the donations are thoroughly accounted for and that those responsible for any malpractices are held answerable.
*   **Trust and Public Faith:** The communication underscores the sentiment that the alleged irregularities could undermine public trust in the institutions managing the revered temple project and impact the faith of donors.
*   **Role of Temple Trust:** The Ram Janmabhoomi Teerth Kshetra Trust is the designated body responsible for overseeing the construction and financial administration of the temple. The letter implicitly or explicitly calls for scrutiny of the trust's financial operations.

This direct appeal to the Prime Minister by prominent opposition figures elevates the issue from isolated claims to a matter requiring high-level governmental attention. It places renewed pressure on the central government and the temple trust to provide a comprehensive public account of the funds received and disbursed for the temple's construction.

As of the time of reporting, there has been no official public statement from the Prime Minister's Office or the Ram Janmabhoomi Teerth Kshetra Trust in response to the letter from Rahul Gandhi and Mallikarjun Kharge. The political and public ramifications of these allegations are expected to continue evolving, potentially leading to further demands for transparency or calls for official inquiries into the management of the Ram Temple donations.
