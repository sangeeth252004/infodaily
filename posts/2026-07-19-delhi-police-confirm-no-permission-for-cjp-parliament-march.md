New Delhi, India – Delhi Police have confirmed that no official permission was sought or granted for a proposed march by the Citizens for Justice and Peace (CJP) organization towards the Indian Parliament. The announcement from police authorities clarifies the regulatory status of the planned public assembly, which intended to proceed in the national capital.

The statement from Delhi Police underscores the established protocols for organizing demonstrations and marches in high-security zones like the Parliament House vicinity. Organizations planning such events are typically required to obtain prior clearance from relevant police departments, outlining the route, number of participants, and purpose of the assembly. The absence of such permission can lead to law enforcement interventions if a gathering proceeds.

Details regarding the CJP's proposed march indicate it was intended to draw attention to specific issues, though the exact agenda was not publicly detailed by police. Marches towards Parliament are often organized by various groups to voice concerns on policy, human rights, or social justice issues directly to lawmakers and the public. However, the area surrounding Parliament House is designated as a high-security zone, and stringent regulations are in place to manage public gatherings and protests to ensure public order and safety.

*   **Police Stance:** Delhi Police maintain that without proper authorization, any attempt to conduct a march towards Parliament would be considered unlawful.
*   **Permit Requirements:** Organizers are typically required to submit an application detailing the date, time, route, estimated number of participants, and the objective of the protest or march.
*   **High-Security Zone:** The area around Parliament is subject to enhanced security measures, making unauthorized assemblies particularly sensitive.

The declaration by Delhi Police serves as a directive to potential participants and organizers about the legal framework governing public demonstrations in New Delhi. Historically, authorities have taken measures ranging from diverting marches to detaining participants if gatherings proceed without the necessary official clearances, especially in sensitive areas. The police assertion that no permission was granted indicates a proactive measure to manage potential public order issues before they arise.

Moving forward, the situation will likely be monitored closely by law enforcement. Any attempt by CJP or its supporters to proceed with the march without the requisite permissions could result in actions by Delhi Police to disperse the gathering and maintain order, adhering to established legal procedures. Further statements from CJP regarding their intentions or adjustments to their plans are anticipated as the situation develops.
