The Delhi Subordinate Services Selection Board (DSSSB) has opened a fresh application window, effective March 19, 2024, specifically for candidates who previously applied for the DASS Grade II examination in 2009. This initiative aims to fill 606 vacancies for the position of Assistant Section Officer (ASO) within various departments of the Government of NCT of Delhi and autonomous local bodies. The application window is scheduled to close on April 17, 2024.

This development offers a renewed opportunity for a specific cohort of applicants whose earlier examination process dates back to Advertisement No. 02/2009 for DASS Grade II posts. The current recruitment drive, under Advertisement No. 04/2024, targets the Assistant Section Officer role, which is classified as a Group 'B', Non-Gazetted, Non-Ministerial position. The move by DSSSB provides a pathway for eligible candidates from the 2009 batch to apply for these administrative posts, addressing a long-standing recruitment matter.

The 606 Assistant Section Officer vacancies are crucial for strengthening the administrative framework of the Delhi government. The ASO position is designated with a pay scale at Level 7, ranging from Rs. 44,900 to Rs. 1,42,400, reflecting the responsibilities and qualifications required for the role. The Post Code for these vacancies is 802/2024.

Key details regarding the application process and eligibility are as follows:

*   **Eligibility:** Only candidates who applied for DASS Grade II under Advertisement No. 02/2009 are eligible to apply for these ASO vacancies.
*   **Application Period:** The online application portal opened on March 19, 2024, and will close on April 17, 2024.
*   **Application Method:** Applications must be submitted online exclusively through the official DSSSB website, dsssb.delhi.gov.in.
*   **Post Classification:** Assistant Section Officer (ASO), Group 'B', Non-Gazetted, Non-Ministerial.
*   **Pay Scale:** Level 7 (Rs. 44,900 - 1,42,400).
*   **Age Limit:** The upper age limit is generally 30 years, with applicable relaxations for reserved categories as per government norms.
*   **Educational Qualifications:** Specific educational qualifications, consistent with those for an Assistant Section Officer, will be required, aligning with the standards set for the 2009 DASS Grade II applicants.

The DSSSB's decision to reopen this application window underscores its commitment to completing recruitment processes, particularly those with historical context. This initiative is expected to facilitate the efficient filling of government positions, ensuring the continued operation and service delivery of various departments within the National Capital Territory of Delhi. Candidates are advised to review the detailed official advertisement on the DSSSB website for comprehensive information regarding eligibility, selection procedure, and application guidelines. Further stages, including examination schedules and subsequent selection processes, will be communicated by the DSSSB after the application window closes.
