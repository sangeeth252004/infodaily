Muhammad Muqaddam Wani, a youth from Handwara, Jammu and Kashmir, has been repatriated to India following a three-month detention in Saudi Arabia. Wani was arrested by Saudi authorities for a social media post featuring Iran's Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei. His return was facilitated through diplomatic efforts by Indian authorities.

Wani, who was employed in Saudi Arabia, reportedly shared a photograph of Ayatollah Ali Khamenei on a social media platform, an action that led to his arrest and subsequent detention. The exact date of his arrest has not been publicly specified, but his detention lasted approximately three months before his release and return to India. Such postings are viewed with strictness under Saudi Arabian laws, which prohibit content deemed politically sensitive or disrespectful to public order.

Upon learning of Wani's detention, his family in Handwara, located in Jammu and Kashmir's Kupwara district, appealed for assistance. The Indian Embassy in Riyadh and the Consulate General in Jeddah engaged with Saudi authorities to secure consular access and facilitate his release. These diplomatic channels worked in coordination with India's Ministry of External Affairs to ensure Wani's safe return home. His family confirmed his arrival back in India, expressing relief after a period of uncertainty regarding his whereabouts and well-being.

The incident underscores the stringent regulations governing online conduct and expression in Saudi Arabia, particularly concerning content with political or religious undertones. The Kingdom has robust cybercrime laws designed to maintain public order and security, which apply to both its citizens and foreign residents. Social media activities that might be considered innocuous in other jurisdictions can lead to severe penalties, including detention and deportation, within Saudi Arabia. This case highlights the importance for expatriate workers to adhere strictly to local laws and cultural sensitivities, especially regarding digital communications.
