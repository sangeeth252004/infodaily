A medical expert associated with the All India Institute of Medical Sciences (AIIMS) has advised urgent medical intervention for renowned environmental activist and innovator Sonam Wangchuk, who is currently engaged in a 21-day "climate fast" in Leh, Ladakh. This recommendation, reportedly based on a recent health evaluation, suggests a need for immediate care to address potential health complications. However, representatives for Mr. Wangchuk have publicly refuted the findings, asserting that the activist remains in stable condition and continues his fast voluntarily.

The recommendation from the AIIMS expert highlights concerns over Mr. Wangchuk's health parameters, following an examination conducted amidst his ongoing protest. While specific details of the medical report have not been fully disclosed, the assessment reportedly indicated conditions that could deteriorate without prompt medical attention. The advisory emphasizes the potential risks associated with prolonged fasting, urging a cautious approach to his well-being.

Conversely, Mr. Wangchuk's team has issued statements vehemently denying any immediate health crisis. According to his spokespersons, Mr. Wangchuk undergoes regular medical monitoring, and his vital signs, including blood pressure, heart rate, and oxygen saturation levels, remain within normal ranges. They have presented this as evidence that his health is stable and that he is fully capable of continuing his protest. Some statements from his camp have suggested that the AIIMS expert's assessment might be exaggerated or potentially influenced by external pressures.

*   **AIIMS Expert's Stance:** The medical professional, reportedly part of a team observing the situation, has emphasized the need for urgent care based on clinical observations and preliminary reports, citing concerns over Mr. Wangchuk's sustained energy deficit.
*   **Activist's Team's Counter:** Representatives for Sonam Wangchuk maintain that he is in good spirits and health, supported by periodic checks from local medical professionals, and that he is not experiencing any life-threatening symptoms.
*   **Context of the Fast:** Mr. Wangchuk began his "climate fast" on March 6, 2024, in Leh, Ladakh. His protest advocates for the implementation of the Sixth Schedule of the Constitution for Ladakh, aimed at protecting the region's fragile ecosystem and the rights of its indigenous people. He is also demanding statehood for Ladakh and the creation of separate parliamentary constituencies for Leh and Kargil.
*   **Location:** The ongoing fast and the medical assessment are taking place in Leh, Ladakh, drawing significant local and national attention.

The contrasting medical opinions have introduced an additional layer of complexity to the ongoing protest. Mr. Wangchuk’s demands, particularly the Sixth Schedule status, have been a long-standing point of contention between local activists and the central government. The activist has gained national recognition for his work in education and environmental conservation, making his health a matter of public and political interest.

As the situation unfolds, observers await further clarification on the differing medical assessments. The central government has previously engaged in discussions with representatives from Ladakh regarding their demands, though no conclusive agreement has been reached. The focus remains on Mr. Wangchuk's health and the potential implications of these medical reports on the future trajectory of his protest and the dialogue surrounding Ladakh's constitutional status.
