Rinchen Dolma, wife of renowned innovator, education reformist, and environmental activist Sonam Wangchuk, has filed a legal challenge against a Delhi High Court order, asserting that it amounts to "illegal confinement" of her husband at Safdarjung Hospital in New Delhi. The petition, filed on April 23, 2024, urges the court to revoke the earlier directive and ensure Mr. Wangchuk’s immediate release.

The challenge brings into focus the circumstances surrounding Mr. Wangchuk's presence at the Delhi-based hospital. Mr. Wangchuk, widely recognized for his work in sustainable development and advocacy for constitutional protections for Ladakh, had reportedly been admitted for medical observation following a recent hunger strike. This strike was undertaken to advocate for the inclusion of Ladakh under the Sixth Schedule of the Indian Constitution, aiming to safeguard the region’s environment and indigenous culture. The Delhi High Court’s order, issued on April 18, 2024, mandated his continued stay at Safdarjung Hospital for medical monitoring, a decision his wife now contests.

Ms. Dolma's petition, filed through her legal representatives, characterizes the High Court's previous order as an infringement on Mr. Wangchuk's fundamental rights, including personal liberty and freedom of movement. It argues that while medical monitoring might be advisable under certain circumstances, confining an individual without their explicit consent, especially when their medical condition does not necessitate critical care or pose an immediate threat, constitutes unlawful detention. The petition specifically seeks a writ of habeas corpus, a legal recourse used to ascertain if a person's detention is lawful and to secure their release if it is found to be unlawful.

Legal counsel for Ms. Dolma contended that Mr. Wangchuk, despite his recent hunger strike, is medically stable and capable of making his own decisions regarding his medical care and place of residence. The petition suggests that the hospital's administration, acting under the court's earlier directive, has restricted his ability to leave, effectively confining him against his will. This situation has drawn attention from civil rights advocates who question the extent of judicial authority in mandating a citizen's prolonged hospital stay without clear medical necessity or robust legal precedent for such confinement.

Key details surrounding the legal challenge include:
*   **Petitioner:** Rinchen Dolma (Wife of Sonam Wangchuk)
*   **Respondents:** Expected to include the Union of India, Safdarjung Hospital administration, and potentially the Delhi High Court (as the issuer of the challenged order).
*   **Date of HC Order Challenged:** April 18, 2024
*   **Date of Challenge Filing:** April 23, 2024
*   **Hospital:** Safdarjung Hospital, New Delhi
*   **Legal Basis of Challenge:** Writ of Habeas Corpus, alleging violation of fundamental rights under Article 21 (Protection of Life and Personal Liberty) of the Indian Constitution.
*   **Context:** Mr. Wangchuk's prior activism, including a 21-day hunger strike, for environmental protection and Sixth Schedule status for Ladakh.

The Delhi High Court is expected to hear Ms. Dolma's petition in the coming days, with a preliminary hearing anticipated on April 26, 2024. The outcome of this legal challenge could have implications for judicial oversight concerning personal liberty, especially in cases involving prominent activists or individuals under specific court directives for medical observation. Further details regarding the court's deliberation and subsequent orders are awaited.
