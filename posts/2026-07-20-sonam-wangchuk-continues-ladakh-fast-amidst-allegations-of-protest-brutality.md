Education reformist Sonam Wangchuk announced on April 8, 2024, his resolve to continue his ongoing hunger strike in Ladakh. Mr. Wangchuk's statement followed allegations of what he described as "brutality" against peaceful protesters participating in a recent march advocating for constitutional safeguards for the region. He emphasized his commitment to the fast until the demands for Ladakh's protection are met and accountability for the alleged actions against demonstrators is established.

Mr. Wangchuk, known for his environmental activism, initiated his "climate fast" on March 6, 2024, in Leh. The protest campaign, which has gathered significant local support, seeks two primary objectives for Ladakh: full statehood and inclusion under the Sixth Schedule of the Indian Constitution. Supporters argue these provisions are crucial for protecting Ladakh's unique ecological environment and its indigenous culture and demography.

Following a major protest march towards Changthang, Mr. Wangchuk stated that authorities had allegedly used force against peaceful demonstrators. He asserted that such actions constituted an attempt to suppress the democratic and non-violent movement. "I will continue my fast," Wangchuk declared, expressing hope that the government would address the alleged use of force and hold responsible parties accountable. He underlined the peaceful nature of the protests and the citizens' right to express their demands.

The movement, spearheaded by the Leh Apex Body (LAB) and the Kargil Democratic Alliance (KDA), has seen widespread participation from various sections of Ladakhi society. The demands for Sixth Schedule status aim to grant autonomous administrative powers to the region, providing a legal framework for the protection of land, resources, and cultural identity. The call for statehood seeks greater self-governance and direct representation for the region's population.

*   **Key Demands:** Statehood for Ladakh and inclusion in the Sixth Schedule of the Indian Constitution.
*   **Protest Leader:** Sonam Wangchuk, Magsaysay Award-winning educationist and environmental activist.
*   **Location:** Leh, Ladakh.
*   **Timeline:** Wangchuk's "climate fast" began on March 6, 2024, with the latest announcement of continuation made on April 8, 2024.
*   **Allegations:** Use of force and brutality against peaceful protesters during a march towards Changthang.

As Mr. Wangchuk reaffirms his commitment to the indefinite fast, the situation in Ladakh remains under close observation. Local stakeholders and national observers await a response from the central government regarding both the long-standing constitutional demands and the recent allegations of protest suppression. The ongoing events highlight escalating tensions between civil society groups in Ladakh and government authorities concerning the region's future administrative and environmental policies.
