Former Swedish international Zlatan Ibrahimović has recently voiced strong criticism regarding Argentine midfielder Leandro Paredes' conduct during a heated moment in the 2022 FIFA World Cup quarter-final match between Argentina and the Netherlands. Ibrahimović, known for his outspoken personality, stated in a recent interview that his personal reaction to Paredes' actions would have been aggressively physical, additionally questioning why opposing players "just stood and watched" the situation unfold.

The incident in question occurred during the high-stakes match on December 9, 2022, which saw Argentina ultimately defeat the Netherlands in a penalty shootout after the game ended 2-2 following extra time. In the 89th minute of regular play, Paredes, then playing for Argentina, committed a foul on Netherlands defender Nathan Aké. Following the foul, Paredes proceeded to forcefully kick the ball towards the Netherlands' technical area, an action that immediately escalated tensions on the field. This sparked a significant on-field altercation involving players and staff from both teams, with benches emptying as personnel from both sides converged.

Ibrahimović reportedly commented on this moment, expressing a stark view on Paredes' behaviour and the broader context of player reactions. His strong language included the statement, "I would have headbutted him!" referring directly to Paredes' action of kicking the ball towards the opposing bench. Beyond his personal hypothetical response, Ibrahimović also criticised the perceived inaction of players from the Dutch national team during the ensuing confrontation. He questioned why they "just stood and watched," implying that a more assertive response was warranted from the opposing side in the face of what he considered provocative conduct.

The quarter-final match itself was notable for its intensity and numerous contentious moments. Referee Antonio Mateu Lahoz issued a total of 18 yellow cards during the fixture, setting a new World Cup record for bookings in a single game. This included a yellow card for Leandro Paredes as a direct result of the incident involving the ball being kicked towards the bench. The confrontational nature of the match continued through extra time and into the penalty shootout, where Argentina emerged victorious, eventually progressing to win the tournament.
