Members of Parliament (MPs) have raised concerns in a recent session, questioning the government's communication strategy regarding the introduction of E20 fuel. The inquiry specifically addresses why vehicle owners whose cars are not designed for E20 fuel were not adequately informed about the potential for mileage loss when operating with the higher ethanol blend.

The query underscores a critical consumer protection issue following the nationwide rollout of E20 fuel, which contains 20% ethanol and 80% petrol. While the government has championed E20 as a strategic move to reduce the country's crude oil import bill and lower carbon emissions, the implications for existing vehicle fleets have become a point of contention. Many vehicles manufactured before approximately 2023 are not "E20-ready" or "E20 compliant," meaning their engines and fuel systems were not designed to handle the higher ethanol concentration without potential operational changes or reduced efficiency.

The MPs' questioning highlights a perceived gap in public awareness campaigns. It is understood that non-E20 compliant vehicles may experience a reduction in fuel efficiency, with industry estimates often suggesting a mileage drop of between 3% and 6% when running on E20 fuel compared to E10 (10% ethanol blend) or pure petrol. This loss in mileage can translate into increased running costs for millions of vehicle owners across the country.

The Indian government officially launched E20 fuel in February 2023, with a target to achieve widespread E20 availability across the nation by 2025. The move is part of the National Policy on Biofuels, which aims to boost domestic ethanol production and blend it with petrol, aligning with broader environmental and economic objectives. While new vehicles are increasingly being manufactured with E20 compatibility, the existing fleet constitutes a significant portion of the total vehicles on Indian roads.

Key aspects of the parliamentary query include:
*   **Lack of Prior Communication:** MPs are seeking an explanation for why specific warnings or advisories regarding mileage loss were not widely disseminated to owners of non-E20 compliant vehicles prior to or during the E20 rollout.
*   **Consumer Impact:** The inquiry focuses on the financial burden on consumers who may unknowingly be incurring higher fuel costs due due to reduced efficiency without adequate pre-notification.
*   **Government Responsibility:** The questioning probes the Ministry of Petroleum and Natural Gas regarding its responsibility to ensure comprehensive public awareness campaigns covering all potential impacts of policy changes.
*   **Automaker Role:** While not directly addressed to automakers, the parliamentary discussion implicitly raises questions about the clarity provided by manufacturers regarding the E20 compatibility of their older models.

The government's response to these parliamentary questions is anticipated, with implications for future consumer information policies regarding fuel standards. The outcome may influence how changes in fuel formulations are communicated to the public, particularly concerning older vehicles and their operational characteristics. Stakeholders await the official clarification on whether additional measures will be implemented to inform and support vehicle owners potentially affected by the shift to higher ethanol blends.
