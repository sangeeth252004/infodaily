New Delhi, [Current Date]: The Prime Minister today announced the establishment of fast-track courts dedicated to adjudicating cases related to alleged irregularities in the National Eligibility cum Entrance Test (Undergraduate) 2026 examination. This significant government response follows ongoing widespread student protests across various regions, demanding greater transparency and fairness in the medical entrance exam process.

The Prime Minister stated that these specialized courts would ensure swift judicial review and resolution of grievances, underscoring the government's commitment to upholding the integrity and sanctity of competitive examinations. The announcement directly addresses mounting concerns from students, parents, and educational stakeholders regarding alleged paper leaks, discrepancies in scoring, and other procedural anomalies reported during the NEET UG 2026 cycle.

Thousands of aspiring medical professionals have been participating in demonstrations in major cities, calling for a comprehensive investigation or, in some cases, a re-conduct of the NEET UG 2026 examination. Their primary grievances center on reports of question paper leaks circulating on social media platforms prior to the exam, concerns over the awarding of grace marks to certain candidates that allegedly impacted overall rankings, and perceived inconsistencies in examination center administration. Students have also voiced demands for increased accountability from the National Testing Agency (NTA), the body tasked with conducting the nationwide test.

Prior to the Prime Minister's announcement, the Ministry of Education had initiated steps, including forming a high-level committee to review the examination process and recommend improvements. The NTA had also issued statements defending its established protocols and confirming that identified cases of malpractice were under investigation, with arrests made in connection with alleged leaks. Several legal challenges had already been filed in various high courts and the Supreme Court, seeking intervention and redressal for the alleged irregularities.

Key allegations cited by protesting students and petitioners include:
*   Reports of question paper leaks occurring in certain examination centers, leading to calls for re-examinations in affected regions.
*   Concerns regarding the methodology for awarding grace marks, which some candidates argue provided an unfair advantage and altered rank distributions.
*   Claims of administrative lapses and unfair practices observed at specific examination venues, potentially compromising the integrity of the test.

The establishment of fast-track courts signals a significant escalation in the government's strategy to address the growing controversy, shifting towards a legally expedited process for resolution. While initial reactions from student groups have acknowledged the judicial intervention as a positive step, many continue to advocate for broader systemic reforms or a full re-examination, maintaining that fundamental issues related to exam security and fairness require more extensive solutions. The coming weeks are expected to see these newly constituted courts begin processing the numerous complaints and petitions related to the NEET UG 2026 examination, setting a precedent for handling future large-scale examination controversies.
