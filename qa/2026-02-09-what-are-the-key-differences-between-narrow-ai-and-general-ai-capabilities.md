### Narrow AI (Weak AI)

Narrow AI refers to artificial intelligence systems that are programmed and trained to perform a single, well-defined task. These systems excel at their designated function but lack the ability to operate outside of their specific domain. Their intelligence is specialized, akin to a highly skilled tool designed for one purpose.

**Example:**
A spam filter in an email client is a form of narrow AI. It is trained to identify and categorize unwanted emails based on patterns and characteristics learned from vast amounts of data. It cannot, however, write an email, play chess, or diagnose a medical condition.

**Limitations:**
The primary limitation of narrow AI is its lack of flexibility. If a task requires a slightly different approach or involves a completely new context, the narrow AI system will likely fail. It cannot transfer knowledge or skills learned in one area to another.

### General AI (Strong AI)

General AI, often referred to as Artificial General Intelligence (AGI), represents a theoretical form of AI that would possess human-level cognitive capabilities. This means it could understand, learn, and apply knowledge to solve any intellectual task that a human being can. AGI would be capable of reasoning, problem-solving, planning, abstract thinking, and learning from experience in a generalizable way.

**Example:**
A hypothetical AGI could potentially learn to cook a new recipe by reading instructions, understand the nuances of human emotion to engage in a conversation, and then apply its learned understanding of physics to build a complex structure, all within a short period.

**Limitations/Edge Cases:**
General AI is currently a theoretical concept, and its development faces significant scientific and engineering challenges. Achieving true AGI would require breakthroughs in understanding consciousness, complex reasoning, and common sense. There are ongoing debates about how to define and measure "human-level" intelligence and whether it is even achievable.
