### Supply Chain Management

Blockchain can create a transparent and tamper-proof ledger of a product's journey from origin to consumer. Each step, from sourcing raw materials to manufacturing, shipping, and retail, can be recorded as a transaction. This improves traceability, helps in identifying counterfeit goods, and can automate processes through smart contracts.

*   **Example:** Tracking the origin and journey of ethically sourced coffee beans. Each handover from farmer to cooperative, roaster, distributor, and retailer can be logged, allowing consumers to verify the coffee's provenance.

### Healthcare

In healthcare, blockchain can securely store and share patient records. It can give patients more control over who accesses their medical data and maintain an auditable history of all interactions. This can streamline interoperability between different healthcare providers and reduce administrative overhead.

*   **Example:** A patient's medical history, including diagnoses, treatments, and allergies, is stored on a blockchain. The patient can grant temporary access to a specialist for a consultation.

### Digital Identity and Verification

Blockchain offers a decentralized approach to digital identity management. Individuals can have a self-sovereign digital identity, meaning they control their personal data and can selectively share verified credentials without relying on central authorities. This can enhance security for online accounts and services.

*   **Example:** Verifying educational qualifications. A university could issue digital diplomas on a blockchain, allowing employers to instantly verify a candidate's degree without contacting the institution directly.

### Voting Systems

Blockchain technology can be utilized to create more secure and transparent voting systems. Each vote could be recorded as a transaction on a blockchain, making it difficult to tamper with or alter results, while also potentially allowing for public verification of the tally.

*   **Limitations:** While promising, widespread adoption in voting faces challenges related to user accessibility, the cost of implementation, and ensuring the anonymity of voters.

### Intellectual Property Management

Protecting intellectual property rights can be improved with blockchain. Creators can timestamp and record their work on a blockchain, establishing an immutable record of ownership and creation date, which can be useful in copyright disputes.
