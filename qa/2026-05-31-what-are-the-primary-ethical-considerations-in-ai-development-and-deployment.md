### Fairness and Bias

AI systems learn from data, and if that data reflects existing societal biases, the AI will likely replicate and potentially amplify them. This can lead to discriminatory outcomes in areas like hiring, loan applications, or criminal justice.

*   **Example:** An AI used to screen job applications trained on historical data where men were predominantly hired for certain roles might unfairly disadvantage female applicants, even if they are equally qualified.

### Transparency and Explainability

It is important to understand how AI systems arrive at their decisions. "Black box" models, where the internal workings are opaque, make it difficult to identify errors, biases, or unintended consequences. Explainable AI (XAI) aims to make AI decisions more interpretable.

*   **Example:** If an AI denies a loan application, the applicant and the lender should be able to understand the specific reasons for the denial.

### Accountability

When an AI system makes an error or causes harm, it's essential to determine who is responsible. Establishing clear lines of accountability—whether it lies with the developers, deployers, or the system itself—is a complex challenge.

*   **Example:** In the case of an autonomous vehicle accident, determining legal and ethical responsibility among the car manufacturer, software developers, and the owner requires careful consideration.

### Privacy

AI systems often require vast amounts of data, including personal and sensitive information. Protecting this data from misuse, breaches, and unauthorized access is a critical ethical imperative.

*   **Example:** AI-powered facial recognition systems raise significant privacy concerns regarding surveillance and the potential for misuse of biometric data.

### Safety and Security

Ensuring that AI systems operate safely and are resistant to malicious attacks is vital, especially for systems deployed in critical infrastructure or autonomous applications.

### Autonomy and Human Control

As AI capabilities grow, questions arise about the appropriate level of human oversight and control. Over-reliance on AI without human judgment can lead to unforeseen problems.
